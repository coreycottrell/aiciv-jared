How to prompt ChatGPT for PowerPoint:

1. Go to ChatGPT > Explore "GPTs".
2. Search for "PPT Builder for Gamma".
3. Then, click START.

ChatGPT will ask what your presentation topic is and if you have any length or audience preferences.

Here's what you'll get:
→ Full research based on your topic.
→ Content for your slides based on the length.
→ Searches from the web, backed by data.

Iterate until you get full research for your slides.

Then, go a step further & create your deck with AI:

1. Go to gamma .app
2. Click on ‘create with AI'
3. Select 'Paste in text'
3. Paste your content from ChatGPT.
4. Customize it & click on generate.

It's fully customizable. Combining ChatGPT and Gamma is a game-changer.

Here is how Google Nano Banana Pro helps you create infographics for free with AI in minutes with simple text prompts.

Follow these steps:
1. Go to gemini.google.com
2. Sign up for free
3. Select the ‘Thinking’ model.
4. Enable the ‘Create Images’ tool.

6. Follow up if you want any changes in it

Use this prompt template:
Create a high quality infographic titled “[TITLE]” that visually explains “[TOPIC]” using a modern, minimal, and professional style with clean typography, balanced spacing, clear section dividers, simple icons, and a cohesive color palette based on [COLOR PALETTE]. Include well structured sections for “[SECTION 1]”, “[SECTION 2]”, “[SECTION 3]”, and any supporting data or visuals that highlight the key points. Use a layout that is easy to read, visually appealing, and optimized for social media sharing at [ASPECT RATIO].

Do’s:
✅ Provide a clear, detailed prompt describing layout, data, and style so Nano Banana Pro can render accurate visuals.
✅ Use its improved text-rendering feature to embed actual, legible text in infographics (titles, labels).
✅ Combine AI output with human review/editing, check facts, refine design, and ensure clarity and brand consistency.
✅ Maintain visual consistency (colors, fonts, layout) across images if you make a series of infographics, benefit from Nano Banana Pro’s creative-control tools.
✅ Use infographics for data-driven or educational content where real-world knowledge and clarity are more important than “artistic style”.

Don'ts:
❎ Don’t rely blindly on AI; avoid publishing without verifying the correctness of data and labels. AI may hallucinate or misplace details.
❎ Don’t assume AI output is “original” with no copyright concerns; avoid using copyrighted images, logos or protected assets without clearance
❎ Don’t use low-detail or vague prompts; that often leads to messy, unclear, or irrelevant visuals unsuited for infographics.
❎ Don’t ignore brand style or design guidelines when using AI; AI might produce visually inconsistent results if unchecked.
❎ Don’t treat AI-generated images as final.

How to create (viral) sketch infographics with AI: 

1. Go to YouTube.
2. Find an actionable video. Copy the URL.
3. Paste it in Gemini with this prompt:
4. Use this prompt guide: https://lnkd.in/dHFz8wY5

"Analyse this youtube video about [topic]: [YT URL]. Summarise this into direct, actionable steps to follow along."

Let Gemini process the video.

5. Click Tools → Create images.
6. Prompt Gemini again:

"Visualise the summary of these notes. Create a realistic photograph of a dry-erase whiteboard with a light wooden frame. The content should be presented as a hand-drawn sketchnote using 'graphic recording' style. The layout should be in 9:16 format.

Style & Layout Guidelines:

Medium: Whiteboard surface with dry-erase markers (not paper).
Colors: Use Black for outlines, boxes, and main text. Use Red, Blue, and Green for headers and specific accents.
Structure: Place the title "[TITLE]" at the top in large, open lettering. Organize the notes into five distinct, numbered rectangular boxes arranged in a grid below the title.
Visuals: Include relevant simple line-drawing doodles for each point.
Typography: Text should be distinct, handwritten, all-caps printing, legible and organized.
Environment: Include a used whiteboard eraser and a few colorful EXPO-style markers resting on the bottom wooden ledge of the frame."

This is how you turn any video into visual notes.
