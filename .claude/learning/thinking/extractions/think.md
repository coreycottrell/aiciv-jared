## Core Thesis

For 2,400 years, human thinking has been controlled by the Greek Gang of Three (GG3: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle). Their system - argument, truth-seeking, and box logic - is excellent but deeply incomplete. We've been complacent about this for millennia, and it's causing catastrophic problems.

> "This is not a nice book. It is not intended to be a nice book. You cannot shift complacency with niceness."

> "We have paid no attention to thinking for 2,400 years."

## The Brain as Asymmetric Patterning System

### Why Creativity Is Logically Necessary

The brain is not a logical computing machine. It is an **asymmetric, self-organizing patterning system**. This is the foundational insight.

**The Tree Asymmetry Model:**
- Standing at trunk and walking toward leaves: at each branch point, probabilities diminish (1:8,000 chance of reaching a specific leaf)
- Standing at the leaf and walking toward trunk: 100% chance of reaching trunk
- Starting from a different point = completely different path

> "The chances of reaching a creative idea through logic may be very slight. But once the idea has been reached, it is obvious and logical in hindsight. That is the nature of asymmetric patterning systems."

**This explains why random word technique works**: The random word comes from direction C, not the habitual A-to-B path. It provides a new starting point with guaranteed access to trunk.

### Humour as the Key Evidence

> "Humour is by far the most significant behaviour of the human brain, far more significant than reason."

In humour:
1. We're led along the main A-to-B path
2. Suddenly shifted to the endpoint of a side path (B)
3. We immediately see that endpoint IS logical from another angle
4. We laugh at the surprise shift

This is IDENTICAL to creativity - except in creativity the shift is permanent (insight), in humour it's momentary. Both reveal the brain makes **asymmetric patterns** that allow legitimate alternative routes.

## The GG3 Legacy: What's Missing

### What We Got (Excellent But Incomplete)
- Argument and critical thinking (Socrates)
- Truth-seeking via removing untruth (Plato)
- Box/category logic - "is" and "is not" (Aristotle)

### What's Missing Entirely
- **Design thinking** - constructing ways forward
- **Perceptual thinking** - how we see things in the first place
- **Creative thinking** - generating genuinely new possibilities
- **Exploratory thinking** - mapping what exists without judgment

> "If perceptions are faulty then perfect processing of those faulty perceptions can only give an answer that is wrong, and sometimes dangerous."

### The "Rear Left Wheel" Problem
Traditional thinking methods are excellent - like an excellent rear left wheel. But a car needs all four wheels. Having one excellent wheel doesn't make the car functional.

## Why Argument Fails as Exploration

When two intelligent people argue:
- Each makes a "case" and defends it
- Energy goes to attack and defense
- No constructive generation occurs
- Argument is terrible at exploring new territory

> "You can argue about which road to take on a road map, but argument cannot create the road map."

**Faults of argument in exploration:**
- Destructive and negative by nature
- Concerned only with attack
- No generative qualities
- Uses known aspects, can't create new ones
- Time wasted in defense
- Polarizes parties

**What argument IS good for:** Establishing truth, working in science/technology where information can be evaluated objectively.

**What it can't do:** Explore a subject, generate new ideas, design ways forward.

## Parallel Thinking vs. Argument

Parallel thinking = the fundamental alternative to argument.

Instead of two people facing each other across a table (adversarial):
- All people stand together on the SOUTH side of the building
- Then all move together to the EAST side
- Then NORTH, then WEST
- Each person looks honestly at what they see from EACH position

**The Six Thinking Hats** (invented 1984) makes this practical:

| Hat | Represents | Function |
|-----|------------|----------|
| White | Paper/Information | What data exists? What's missing? |
| Red | Fire/Warmth | Feelings, emotions, intuition - no justification needed |
| Black | Judge's Robes | Caution, risks, why it won't work |
| Yellow | Sunshine | Benefits, value, why it WILL work |
| Green | Vegetation | Creativity, new ideas, alternatives, PO |
| Blue | Sky | Meta-thinking - organizing the thinking process |

**Results:**
- ABB Finland: 30 days of multinational discussions → 2 days
- Siemens: Product development time reduced 50%
- IBM top laboratory: Meeting times reduced to 1/4
- J.P. Morgan Europe: Meeting times reduced to 1/10

**Why it works:** Removes ego from disagreement. "Try the yellow hat" vs. "Stop being so negative" - one is actionable, one is offensive.

## Formal Tools of Lateral Thinking (As Presented in Think!)

### Tool 1: Challenge
Creative challenge ≠ critical challenge.

Critical challenge: "This is wrong, prove why"
Creative challenge: "This may be perfect. But for a moment we block it to seek alternatives."

Challenge NEVER implies attack. It implies exploration.

> "Oil wells were normally drilled vertically for over 80 years and it worked very well. There was no problem with it. At a seminar for Shell Oil in London in 1970, I suggested, as the result of a challenge, that at a certain depth the drill should be turned horizontal."
> Result: Shell began horizontal drilling, yielding 3-6x more oil.

### Tool 2: Focus (Three Types)
1. **Purpose focus**: Clear destination - "We want ideas to speed up checkout"
2. **Area focus**: General region - "We want ideas about car parks"
3. **Idea-sensitive point**: Where a new idea would make a big difference

### Tool 3: Concepts
> "Concepts are a very important part of thinking in general and an even more important part of creative thinking."

Extract the concept → find alternative ways to deliver it.

Car insurance concept = "covering financial risk of car ownership" → alternative delivery: insurance tax on petrol sales.

**The Concept Fan:**
Start at purpose → identify broad concepts/directions → for each concept, find specific ideas.

Traffic example:
- Purpose: Reduce congestion
- Broad concepts: Reduce traffic, Improve flow, Increase travel space
- For "reduce traffic": restrict vehicles, discourage traffic, reduce need to travel
- For each of those: specific concrete ideas

### Tool 4: Provocation (PO)
"PO, cars should have square wheels."

**Movement techniques from provocation:**
- Extract the concept (what's interesting about a square wheel's behavior?)
- Focus on the difference (how does it differ from round wheels?)
- Pick out positive aspects
- Imagine it in action and trace what happens

PO square wheels → rise and fall is cyclical and predictable → if suspension adapted in advance → **anticipatory suspension** → now commercially available.

### Tool 5: Random Input
Use random word (from watch-second-hand + numbered list) to get new entry point.

Steel mill: Using just this one technique, 21,000 new ideas generated in one afternoon. Took 9 months to sort through them.

## The Perception Priority

> "It is perceptions that control emotions and emotions control behaviour. Changes in perception will change emotions and therefore behaviour. If your perception changes, you have no choice: your emotions and behaviour change too."

> "Logic will never change emotions and behaviour. Trying to persuade people logically to change emotions is useless in practice."

This is why perception is the key battleground. Argument addresses logic. Design addresses perception.

---

## Knowledge vs. Thinking

> "Information is excellent - but it is not enough."

China 2,000 years ago was far ahead of the West in technology. Lost the advantage. Why? Lack of creative thinking culture, not lack of information.

> "The development of computers with their superb ability to handle information has made matters worse. What happens when very young children are given computers? They develop the habit of searching for the answer they need. They no longer have to think - they just search for the answer."

## Language Problems

> "Language is an encyclopaedia of ignorance."

Words freeze perceptions. The word "enemy" prevents seeing nuance. We need:
- A word for "neither friend nor enemy but with whom we must deal"
- A word for "possible" (stronger than "may be", weaker than "is")
- Words for the states between the boxes we have
