## CoRT Thinking Tools (Core Content of This Book)

The CoRT programme consists of practical thinking tools, each named as an acronym. These form the practical framework for thinking as a learnable skill.

### Group 1: Breadth Tools (Widening Perception)

**PMI: Plus, Minus, Interesting**
The foundational three-phase scan. Before evaluating any idea:
1. **Plus**: What's good about this? (Force yourself to find value)
2. **Minus**: What's bad, problematic, or worrying?
3. **Interesting**: What's curious, worth noting, or worth further exploration?

PMI prevents premature judgment. Without it, we evaluate instantly based on first impression. With it, we see more before deciding.

Application: De Bono found that students who applied PMI to ideas they initially hated often changed their view - just by being forced to find Plus first.

**CAF: Consider All Factors**
Before deciding or acting, list ALL factors that are relevant:
- Not just the obvious ones
- Not just the ones that support your preferred view
- Not just what others have considered

CAF disciplines the thinking to be comprehensive before moving.

**C&S: Consequence and Sequel**
Thinking through the chain of consequences:
- Immediate consequences (now to 1 year)
- Short-term consequences (1-5 years)
- Medium-term consequences (5-25 years)
- Long-term consequences (25+ years)

Many decisions that seem good in the short term are disastrous long term. C&S forces multi-horizon thinking.

**AGO: Aims, Goals, Objectives**
What are we actually trying to achieve?
- Aims: The broad purpose (direction)
- Goals: The medium-term targets
- Objectives: The specific measurable outcomes

When thinking is confused, clarifying AGO often resolves the confusion.

**OPV: Other People's Views**
Before concluding, construct the perspectives of:
- All parties affected
- All stakeholders
- Even parties you disagree with

De Bono proved that fights often end when both parties are required to do OPV on each other. You cannot sustain hatred when you've genuinely constructed the other's perspective.

### Group 2: Organization Tools

**APC: Alternatives, Possibilities, Choices**
After finding the "right" answer, deliberately generate alternatives:
- What are the other ways?
- What options exist?
- What choices are available?

Even when you know the right answer, generating alternatives reveals if it really is best.

**FIP: First Important Priorities**
After generating many ideas and factors, identify what matters most. Prioritization forces the mind to distinguish important from interesting.

**HV LV: High Value vs. Low Value (Decision Making)**
The explicit value-weighting of options. Not all choices are equal. Some matter much more than others.

### Group 3: Interaction Tools

**EBS: Examine Both Sides**
Explicitly construct both sides of any argument before forming view.

**PoV: Point of View**
Identify your own point of view. Name it explicitly. Recognize it as ONE perspective, not objective truth.

### Group 4: Creativity Tools

**ATL: Alternatives To Logic**
Specifically directed at finding creative alternatives when logic says "there's only one way."

**NHI: Not Handled Intelligence**
What am I not thinking about? What's outside my current frame? What am I missing?

## Core Teaching Philosophy of the Course

The CoRT programme was designed on three principles:

**1. Thinking is a skill, not intelligence**
You don't need high IQ. You need trained habits. A 10-year-old with CoRT tools can outthink a genius without them.

**2. Tools must be simple and memorable**
If a tool is too complex, it won't be used under pressure. PMI, CAF, OPV - these are memorable because they're simple acronyms for simple operations.

**3. Practice creates habits**
Reading about PMI doesn't help. Applying PMI repeatedly, until it becomes automatic, is what creates lasting benefit.

The CoRT programme ran for 45 minutes per week for 20+ weeks. Thinking cannot be improved by a one-day workshop.

## Evidence of Effectiveness

Multiple studies showed significant improvements:

**South Africa school**: One class got 4 CoRT tools, the other didn't. By year end:
- CoRT class: $45,000 profit from business activities
- Control class: $10,000 profit

**Venezuela**: Thinking made mandatory in all schools after proven results.

**Research** (Atkey organization): Teaching De Bono thinking as separate subject improved performance in every other subject by 30-100%.

## The Three Main Reasons Behind Any Decision

Simplified from the book:
1. **Fear** (of consequences, of loss, of being wrong)
2. **Greed** (of gain, advantage, pleasure)
3. **Hassle** (avoiding complexity, effort, controversy)

Verbalizing which is dominant in any decision gives you enormous insight and honesty.

## Applications for AI Collective

1. **PMI as standard protocol**: Before any architectural decision, run PMI. Prevents premature adoption/rejection.

2. **CAF as coordination tool**: When tackling complex problems, CAF the factors before delegating. Ensure comprehensive coverage.

3. **C&S for system design**: Long-term consequence thinking is essential for infrastructure decisions. "What does this lead to in 2 years?"

4. **OPV for agent conflicts**: When two agents diverge, require each to perform OPV on the other. Often resolves apparent conflicts.

5. **AGO clarity**: Before any mission, state AGO explicitly. Clear aims prevent confused work.

6. **Fear/Greed/Hassle**: Organizational decisions often driven by hassle avoidance. Naming this enables more honest evaluation.
