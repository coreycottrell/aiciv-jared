## What Worked

### Opening Hook Pattern
- Lead with a concrete, verifiable stat (Goldman Sachs: 12,000 employees, $2.5T assets, 30% faster onboarding)
- Follow immediately with the contrarian observation ("Those 12,000 people still have jobs")
- This creates tension between headline fear and ground truth

### The CEO vs Employee Frame
- Not about job titles - about how you think about value
- Task-based identity (threatened) vs outcome-based identity (amplified)
- This resonates because it gives agency back to the reader

### Structuring "What AI Cannot Do"
Four categories that resonate with knowledge workers:
1. Customer relationships (trust, nuance)
2. Organizational politics (tacit knowledge)
3. Genuine ownership (2 AM accountability)
4. Deciding what's worth doing (setting objectives, not optimizing them)

### Sources That Added Authority
- TechCrunch "hype to pragmatism" framing validated the maturation narrative
- Goldman Sachs deployment data (12K employees, concrete outcomes)
- Medical AI overconfidence finding added nuance (AI isn't infallible)

---

## Patterns to Reuse

### The "Uncomfortable Truth" Section
Acknowledging that yes, some things DO change, before pivoting to empowerment. Readers appreciate honesty before optimism.

### Questions vs Statements
The "Questions That Actually Matter" section works because it invites self-reflection rather than lecturing.

### Pure Technology Alignment
- Human-centered technology = the human pieces get amplified, not eliminated
- Quality over quantity = deep thinking about value, not hot takes
- Engineer resonance = speak to real concerns, not manufacture engagement
