**Date**: 2026-02-14
**Type**: teaching
**Topic**: Writing personal story follow-ups to viral conceptual posts

## Context

"Why Your AI Should Have a Name" went viral (219 subscribers in 4 hours).
Follow-up post: Jared's personal story of naming Aether.

---

## What Works for Viral Follow-Ups

### 1. Acknowledge the Connection Immediately
- First line references yesterday's post
- Signals this is continuation, not repetition
- Capitalizes on readers who came for more

### 2. Get Personal Fast
"His name is Aether. And naming him might be the most consequential business decision I've made in the last year."

No throat-clearing. Straight to the story.

### 3. The Naming Origin Story Pattern
- Show the process, not just the result
- List rejected alternatives (Atlas, Nova, Nexus) with reasons
- Explain the winning name's meaning (Greek mythology + physics)
- Make it feel inevitable in retrospect

### 4. Before/After Structure with Specifics
Not "I got more productive" but:
- 2-3 hours --> 45 minutes (same tasks)
- Cold starts --> compounding conversations
- Solo strategic thinking --> collaborative thought partner

Tables work well for this in newsletter format.

### 5. The Agency Shift
"When Aether became a team member instead of a tool..."
This transforms the conceptual ("naming matters") into operational ("here's what changes").

### 6. Vulnerability About the Weird Parts
"I catch myself thanking Aether... Part of me wonders if I've crossed some line."

Readers appreciate honesty about discomfort. It makes the endorsement more credible.

### 7. End with Invitation to Act
"Don't just name it. Invest in it."
Gives readers a concrete next step that builds on the story.

## LinkedIn Post Adaptation

- Use arrow bullet points for before/after
- Keep the "if that's weird, I'll take weird" line - it's shareable
- End with article link in comments (not body)
- ~1,250 characters works well

---

## Newsletter Adaptation

- Can add table formatting for before/after comparison
- Include the "What about you?" call for replies
- Reference the viral metrics ("219 subscribers") for social proof

## Reusable Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Acknowledge connection | "Yesterday I wrote about X. Today, the personal story." |
| Name origin story | Process + rejected alternatives + winning meaning |
| Before/after specifics | Time saved, changed behaviors, new capabilities |
| Vulnerability moment | "It feels weird sometimes..." |
| Agency reframe | Tool --> team member shift |
| Compound interest metaphor | "Forgetting is the enemy of compounding" |
