# Client: PHP Point of Sale
**Date**: 2026-03-05
**Type**: pattern
**Topic**: Payment processing sales page for POS software company

## Client Profile
- Cloud POS SaaS, $32.50/mo, small-to-medium retail focus
- Target: mobile repair shops, boutiques, bakeries, bookstores, jewelry, grocers, sporting goods
- Brand: navy/dark blue, professional, plain English, "simplify sales, maximize results"
- Payment setup: Stripe + Braintree integrations + proprietary "PHP POS Payments" processor (USA only)
- Also integrates: Shopify, WooCommerce, QuickBooks

## Page Strategy That Worked
- Led with the pain ("Your Merchants Are Overpaying Every Transaction") — not the pitch
- Used their own merchant verticals in the copy to show we did our homework
- Anchored with a concrete dollar figure ($720/yr savings) immediately in hero
- Framed Pure Technology as a payment intelligence PARTNER, not a replacement for their stack
- Comparison table worked well to show differentiation from generic consultants
- "Works with existing PHP POS setup — no processor switch required" was key message

## Key Pain Points Identified
1. Multi-processor fragmentation (Stripe + Braintree + PHP POS Payments = reconciliation mess)
2. Hidden transaction fees (flat-rate vs interchange-plus gap)
3. PCI compliance confusion for non-technical small business owners
4. Chargeback losses (no time to fight disputes = 78% loss rate)
5. Ecommerce payment silos (Shopify/WooCommerce + in-store not unified)
6. No payment-specific analytics (decline rates, card type mix invisible)

## Design Decisions
- Dark theme matching Pure Technology aesthetic (not PHP POS navy — their site is light)
- Used PHP POS blue (#1a5fb4) as accent alongside PT orange/blue to feel "made for them"
- 5-solution vertical list with numbered steps worked better than grid for this content density
- Metric cards in hero (dollar savings, win rate, PCI scope) made abstract claims tangible

## CTA Strategy
- Primary: "Book a Free 20-Min Audit" (low friction, time-specific)
- Secondary: "Explore Partnership" (for platform-level relationship)
- Email CTA pre-filled with subject line and body to reduce friction
