## Key Strategic Decisions in the Plan

**Primary positioning**: Toast-specialized marketing agency, not a general restaurant agency. The Toast expertise and future API integration are the moat.

**Primary sales tactic**: Free 30-minute Toast Marketing Audit — low barrier, high conversion, demonstrates expertise on the prospect's own data.

**Revenue model**: Three streams: (1) managed retainers ($750-$5K/month), (2) Toast API integration SaaS ($149-$249/month/location), (3) Toast corporate relationship (18-24 month horizon).

**Year 1 revenue projection**: ~$282K total, ~$228K net pre-owner comp.

**Integration product**: The high-leverage play. Toast API enables automated lapse re-engagement, loyalty tier campaigns, slow-day boosters. At 1% penetration of 127K Toast locations = $3M+ ARR. No general agency can replicate this without the integration.

## Key Risk Flags

- First clients must generate measurable, documented results — proof-of-concept pricing (50% discount) is intentional and necessary
- Toast partner program approval is helpful but not required; direct AM relationships are the real channel
- Integration MVP should be scoped to single workflow minimum (lapse re-engagement) to avoid development scope creep

## ICP Summary

Single-location or 2-3 location full-service independent restaurants on Toast, $600K-$3M revenue, urban/suburban US markets, no current marketing infrastructure, owner-operator with budget authority.

## What Toast's Analysis Revealed About the Gap

- Toast has email, loyalty, and guest data tools built in — operators do not know how to use them
- Toast's own marketing content tells operators WHAT to do but never shows HOW using their own platform
- No managed marketing services layer exists in Toast's ecosystem — this is the whitespace
- 127,000+ locations, est. 89,000+ independent operators, est. 9,000-15,000 who would pay for marketing services = $54M-$450M TAM at scale
