## Context

Created a full week of content (Feb 17-21, 2026) for PureBrain.ai, continuing momentum from viral LinkedIn newsletter "Why Your AI Should Have a Name."

## Content Architecture Pattern

### Theme Progression (Emotional Arc)

| Day | Theme | Emotion | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|---------|
| Monday | Psychology/Science | Curiosity | Establish credibility |
| Tuesday | Stories/Proof | Recognition | Build social proof |
| Wednesday | Enterprise | Authority | Expand relevance |
| Thursday | Self-discovery | Insight | Personal engagement |
| Friday | Challenge/CTA | Activation | Drive action |

This arc moves from **education** (Mon-Wed) to **engagement** (Thu-Fri).

### Cross-Platform Adaptation

**LinkedIn**: Full narrative (1,100-1,350 chars), includes newsletter CTA, 5 hashtags
**Twitter**: Condensed insight (under 280 chars), no links in main content
**Bluesky**: Thread format (4 posts), conversational, includes CTA in final post

### Key Insights Applied

1. **User naming patterns** (from pattern-detector): People choose mythological names (Atlas, Nexus) over human names. Incorporated this into Thursday's "5 Types" content.

2. **Relationship framing** (from carousel strategy): Position AI as "thinking partner" not tool. Consistent throughout all pieces.

3. **Newsletter CTA placement**: End of each LinkedIn post, never disruptive, always contextually relevant.

## Successful Frameworks Used

### 5 Types Classification Framework
Categories that reveal self-identity:
1. Mythological (power seekers)
2. Guide (navigators)
3. Companion (warmth seekers)
4. Functional (capability focused)
5. Abstract (non-human intelligence)

This framework is highly shareable and drives self-reflection.

### Before/After Story Structure
- Pain point (before naming)
- Transformation moment (naming)
- Positive outcome (after naming)
- Universal lesson

### Weekend Challenge Format
- Clear deadline (Sunday night)
- Simple steps (1-4)
- Low friction entry
- Report back mechanism
