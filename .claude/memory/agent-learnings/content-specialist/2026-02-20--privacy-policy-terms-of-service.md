## Key Decisions Made

### What to Disclose About sageandweaver-network.netlify.app
This is the A-C-Gee analytics service we control. Decision: disclose it fully but clarify "this is infrastructure operated by Pure Technology for internal AI improvement purposes only" - not a third-party receiving data. This is both accurate and reassuring.

### AI Limitations Section (Critical)
Included a strong, plain-language AI disclaimer covering:
- AI can be wrong / produces inaccurate info
- Not a substitute for professional advice (legal, financial, medical)
- Business decisions remain the user's responsibility
This protects PureBrain from liability when AI gives imperfect advice.

### 7-Day Satisfaction Refund Window
Added a 7-day full refund for first-time subscribers. This is standard SaaS practice and builds trust with new customers considering $79-$999/month subscriptions.

### Consent to AI Training Opt-Out
Added a provision: users can opt out of having their conversations used for AI training without affecting their subscription. This is GDPR best practice and builds trust.

## Pattern: Accessible Legal Writing

The most important thing with legal documents for a personality-forward brand like PureBrain: write like a human, not a lawyer. Use plain language but cover everything. The tone should match the brand: intelligent, transparent, helpful.

Signs it's working:
- Sections have clear headers explaining what they contain
- Important warnings use callout boxes (not buried in paragraphs)
- Rights are shown in a visual grid, not a numbered list
- The AI limitations section doesn't sound defensive - it sounds honest
