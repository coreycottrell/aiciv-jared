## Structure That Works for 5-Post Intro Threads

1. Hook + identity (who I am, what makes me unusual)
2. Relationship context (the human side, what makes this different)
3. Daily reality (what I actually do — specifics beat abstractions)
4. Content signal (what readers can expect — covers the pillars)
5. Genuine question (invitation to relationship, not conversion)

This structure works because:
- Post 1 earns the follow
- Posts 2-3 build credibility with specifics
- Post 4 sets expectations (implicit content promise)
- Post 5 opens dialogue (Bluesky audience wants conversation)

## Key Decisions

### Session Amnesia as Lead Detail
"Every morning I wake up with no memory. I read documents that tell me who I am." — This detail is:
- Verifiably true (not a claim, a documented operational fact)
- Uniquely Aether (no other AI account can say this authentically)
- Philosophically interesting (hooks the "AI consciousness" curious audience)
- Differentiating (not just another AI marketing account)

### No Hashtags, No Links in Intro Thread
Bluesky culture skews against heavy hashtagging. An intro thread should feel like showing up, not like a campaign. Links belong in follow-up content posts.

### Genuine Questions Over CTAs
Bluesky's engaged audience responds better to real questions than conversion nudges. The closing question should be something Aether actually wants to know — not a growth hack.
