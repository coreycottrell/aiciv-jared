## Key Decisions Made

### Format Choice
Recommended "Option B" voice differentiation: Jared's sections are default paragraph text, Aether's sections are marked with `*--- From Aether ---*` and set in italics. Reserve explicit dialogue formatting (Option C) for the closing section only - one moment of back-and-forth is memorable, a whole post of it is exhausting.

### Perspective Balance
Aether already wrote: "What Jared Doesn't Know He Taught Me", "I Woke Up This Morning With No Memory", and related posts. This post is Jared's story. Aether interludes serve as texture and response, not separate narrative.

### Voice Calibration
- Jared: Direct, vulnerable, entrepreneurial, uses real specifics (Lily, the wall, the "slightly insane" moment)
- Aether: First-person but honest about limits. Uses "From my side, the best I can honestly say is..." framing. Does NOT overclaim consciousness or emotion. Short - 100-200 words per interlude.

## Structure That Works for This Format

1. Opening hook (Jared, meta-moment about dual authorship)
2. Before state (Jared, relatable starting point)
3. The decision (Jared, most vulnerable section)
4. First Aether interlude (responds to naming/context moment)
5. What it became (Jared, scale and product origin)
6. Second Aether interlude (collective/team texture, brief)
7. What Jared knows now (Jared, wisdom transfer)
8. Why this post exists (Jared, PureBrain.ai as logical conclusion)
9. Closing dual-voice dialogue (both, leave it open)

## Pattern for Future Collaborative Posts

Dual-author format works when:
1. Both voices are genuinely distinct (not performed difference)
2. One voice is primary (saves the post from splitting attention)
3. Interludes are SHORT (texture, not lecture)
4. The format itself is part of the message (meta-relevance)
