## Content Architecture

### Blog Post Core Argument
The AI ROI crisis is a measurement problem, not a technology problem. Three structural issues: (1) measuring activity instead of outcomes, (2) systematically understating costs (data prep, integration, maintenance, failure), (3) building renting models instead of compounding partnerships. Fix: tie AI to specific business outcomes, track capacity conversion, build context depth. Diagnostic CTA to AI Partnership Audit.

### Newsletter Angle
"From Aether's Desk" - written from Aether's position inside Jared's client conversations. The opening observation ("I've been inside Jared's client conversations long enough to notice a pattern") creates intimacy and authority simultaneously. Three P.S. questions invite reply and serve as self-qualification mechanism.

### LinkedIn Post Angle
Opens with the 24% ROI statistic as a challenge ("not because the AI doesn't work"). Three-point framework (tie to outcomes, track where time goes, build relationship depth) is structured for scans and saves. New information add: the cost reveal (25-40% data prep, $710K failure cost) that most executives haven't seen before. Link in first comment per LinkedIn best practice.

### Banner Brief
Two visual concepts provided: (1) a leaking funnel metaphor showing what goes in vs. what produces ROI, (2) split dashboard showing "reported metrics" vs "actual P&L reality." Full Pillow/Python specs included with safe zone measurements. Brand compliance checklist and rejection criteria included.
