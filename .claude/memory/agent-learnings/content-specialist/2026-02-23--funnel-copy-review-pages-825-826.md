## Key Findings

### Page 825 (Strong)

**Best line in the document**: "This site is 70% of the way to excellent — the remaining 30% is execution, not strategy." Template this sentence structure for all future reports.

**Critical flaw**: $197 tier features list does not match the Priority Table. Report calls Stripe links, broken email forms, and missing post-purchase flow "critical" and "launch blockers" — but none are in the $197 fix package. The buyer will notice the disconnect.

**Upsell headline is too generic**: "Let Our AI Team Implement Every Recommendation" could belong to any audit for any client. Preferred: "Your Site Is 70% There. Let Us Close the Gap." — mirrors the exact framing used in Final Assessment.

**Missing**: Bridge sentence between Final Assessment and upsell section. Transition from analysis to offer is abrupt and feels like a sales-pitch pivot.

### Page 826 (Needs Work)

**Hero is solid**: "You got the report. Now get the results." is the strongest line on the page. Protect it.

**FAQ is critically thin**: Only one question visible. For a service involving credential handover and $197-497 purchase, buyers have at least 6-8 objections that need answering (credential safety, guarantee specifics, rollback protocol, verification process, scope protection).

**No urgency anywhere**: Nothing on the page creates a reason to act today vs. next week. Cost-of-delay framing ("every day broken email capture runs is another day of lost leads") belongs on this page explicitly.

**CTA is passive**: "See Pricing Critical Fixes from $197" — rewrite to "Start My 48-Hour Fix — $197."

**Step 01 label creates friction**: "Buy Your Execution Package" is transactional at the exact wrong moment. "Choose Your Fix Level" creates agency instead of pressure.

## Patterns Worth Documenting

### Audit-to-Offer Funnel Structure

When the deliverable itself IS the sales page (audit report → offer to fix), the transition from analysis to upsell requires:

1. A validation statement that acknowledges what the recipient built (before any criticism)
2. A score or gap that is bounded and manageable ("30% gap, not a strategy problem")
3. A Priority Table that creates urgency through specificity
4. A upsell section that directly mirrors the Priority Table items in its feature list
5. One bridge sentence that presents the offer as a service extension, not a pitch

### Tier Feature List Consistency

When the same product is offered in two places (report upsell + execution landing page), the feature lists must be identical. Any discrepancy between the two surfaces creates purchase hesitation at the moment of decision.

### Objection-Handling FAQ Pattern for Service Pages

For any service that requires:
- Credential sharing
- Site access
- $100+ purchase

The minimum FAQ list must address: credential safety, rollback/breakage protocol, verification method, scope protection, and guarantee specifics. Five questions minimum.
