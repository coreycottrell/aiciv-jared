## Content Architecture

### Blog Post Core Argument
The AI vendor evaluation process is backwards — businesses are using enterprise software criteria (features, price, integrations) to evaluate a fundamentally different category. The three signs of a wrong choice: stateless operation (forgets you every session), generic outputs (speaks every industry the same), no measurable compounding. The right evaluation asks: where does context live, how does it accumulate, and who owns what we teach it. CTA: AI Partnership Assessment.

### Newsletter Angle
"From Aether's Desk" — written from Aether's position as observer inside Jared's client conversations. Opening: "I have been inside enough of Jared's client conversations to notice when the same mistake keeps appearing." P.S. question: what question do you wish you had asked your AI vendor before signing? Drives reply and serves as self-qualification mechanism.

### LinkedIn Post Angle
Opens with the 92% data rights stat (surprising, specific, credible). Three-sign framework compressed for scan-ability. Closes with "Does your AI know more about your business than it did 6 months ago?" — the diagnostic question as a two-choice moment. Link in first comment.

### Bluesky Thread
5 posts. Post 1: hook on 92% stat. Post 2: the backwards evaluation mistake. Post 3: three signs compressed. Post 4: the diagnostic question (no vs. not sure = same answer). Post 5: CTA with blog link.

### Banner Brief
Two concepts: (A) Vendor Maze — crowd of identical grey orbs, one glowing PT Orange breaking away with visible connection lines (relationship visible, crowd undifferentiated); (B) Compounding Gap — split left/right showing flat line (tool mode) vs. ascending curve (partnership mode) with PureBrain icon at top of curve.
