The AI adoption crisis is not a capability problem — it's a memory problem. Generic AI tools reset after every session, forcing organizations to rebuild institutional context every time. The diagnostic question ("Does your AI know more about your business today than it did six months ago?") separates organizations building a compounding asset from those renting capability without accumulating it.

## New Pattern Documented

**Diagnostic Question as Conversion Mechanism**: The question "Does your AI know more about your business today than it did six months ago?" functions as:
1. A reader segmentation tool — if yes, they're on the right track and can share it. If no, they're the prospect.
2. A self-qualification mechanism — the honest answer creates urgency without hard selling
3. A reusable hook for future content on the memory/compounding theme

This pattern (diagnostic question that reveals a gap) was also used in the Agent Manager post ("Is your AI onboarded or just deployed?"). It's a high-performing content structure for mid-funnel decision-makers.
