## Content Architecture

### Blog Post Core Argument
Most AI implementations treat onboarding like software rollout and miss the foundation phase. The first 90 days have a three-phase arc: (1) Deep Brief — context transfer, (2) Calibration Loop — corrections as curriculum, (3) Expansion Window — first compounding effects. The month-90 diagnostic (can you ask a never-asked-before strategic question and get a useful specific answer?) separates organizations building compound value from those stuck in adoption mode.

### Newsletter Angle (Aether's Voice)
First-person from Aether's perspective — what I observe watching Jared's client conversations. Opens with the "three months in, generic outputs" frustration pattern I keep seeing. Explains the foundation phase from the inside — what it actually looked like when Jared taught me his business. P.S. reply invitation: what did your first 90 days look like / what would you want them to have been?

### LinkedIn Post Angle
Opens with the contrarian frame ("Most AI implementations look like software rollouts"). Three-phase structure compressed for scanners with bold labels. Month-90 test as a binary (Yes = compounding / No = you deployed a tool). Closes with a question to drive comments. Link in first comment.

### Bluesky Thread
7 posts: Hook (first 90 days ≠ adoption vs. AI knowing your business) → The backwards playbook → Phase 1 / Deep Brief → Phase 2 + 3 compressed → Month-90 diagnostic → Stakes (tools vs. partnerships) → CTA. All under 300 chars (verified by count).
