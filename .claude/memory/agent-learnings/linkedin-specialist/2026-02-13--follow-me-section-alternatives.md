# LinkedIn "Follow Me" Section Patterns

**Date**: 2026-02-13
**Type**: teaching
**Topic**: Optimizing the "Follow me if you want to" section in LinkedIn About

---

## Context

Jared requested alternatives to his current "Follow me if you want to" section. Current version was good but generic - missing his unique differentiators (PureBrain.ai, $70K loss story, 400+ books, experiential marketing background).

## Key Patterns Identified

### 1. Lead with the Unique
Generic: "Learn about personalized experiences"
Specific: "Watch me build an AI that remembers your brand (PureBrain.ai)"

### 2. Vulnerability Creates Connection
"Rebuild after losing $70K" is more compelling than "years of experience"

### 3. Numbers Create Credibility
"400+ books" beats "well-read"
"4 companies" beats "serial entrepreneur"

### 4. Personality Differentiates
Unexpected details (D&D, mechanical keyboards) create pattern-interrupt
LinkedIn is full of polished sameness - weird is memorable

### 5. Philosophy as Hook
"Engineer fascination, don't buy attention" is a clear stance
Stances attract followers who resonate, repel those who don't (both good)

## Alternatives Provided

1. **PureBrain-Forward**: Product vision focus - leads with the AI he's building
2. **Story-Driven**: Journey focus - leads with vulnerability ($70K loss)
3. **Curiosity + Contrast**: Philosophy focus - opens loops with "what works instead"
4. **Personality-Infused**: Personal quirks - D&D/keyboards for pattern-interrupt

## Recommendation

- Story-Driven for personal brand building
- PureBrain-Forward for product awareness

---

## Future Application

When optimizing "Follow me" sections:
1. Start by listing unique differentiators (what's true for ONLY this person?)
2. Avoid generic marketing terms ("resonance", "personalized", "unforgettable")
3. Include at least one vulnerable/real element
4. Make sure each bullet gives a SPECIFIC reason to follow
5. Keep under 15 words per bullet (scannable)
