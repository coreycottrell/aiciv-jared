## Voice Patterns That Worked

### Jared's Voice Anchors (confirmed from this session)
- First-person observation: "I've seen this," "I've been thinking about," "I wrote about"
- Pattern confidence without citing statistics (observations from lived experience)
- Direct but not blunt - always gives the reader something to act on
- "Engineer resonance" = no hype words, no urgency language, no manufactured FOMO

### Structural Pattern
- Hook (1-2 sentences, under 235 chars)
- Context/problem paragraph (2-3 sentences)
- The pivot/insight (2-3 short paragraphs)
- The implication (1-2 sentences)
- Blog link CTA
- Newsletter CTA
- Closing question (specific, not generic)

## Strong Lines to Remember / Reuse as Inspiration

- "Parallel realities" - when two groups operate on incompatible assumptions without realizing it
- "Competent isn't the same as compounding" - AI that answers vs AI that builds understanding
- "The silence that follows is the answer" - for any moment where non-response reveals the truth
- "The tool exists, but trust doesn't" - the enterprise AI trust gap framing

---

## What to Avoid (Confirmed Anti-Patterns)

- Do NOT frame AI content around job threat/fear (already covered, now feels repetitive)
- Do NOT use urgency language ("before it's too late," "you need to act now")
- Do NOT use bullet lists for narrative content - prose flows better for this voice
- Do NOT add statistics without sourcing - Jared's voice is observation-based, not citation-heavy
- Do NOT end with generic "What do you think?" - always make the question specific
