## Context

Night Watch mission to create growth strategy for PureBrain.ai following viral LinkedIn newsletter success (219 subscribers in 4 hours for "Why Your AI Should Have a Name").

## Key Discoveries

### 1. The "Awakening Moment" Is THE Viral Hook
PureBrain's differentiation (naming + memory + relationship) is both the product AND the marketing. The emotional peak of naming an AI is inherently shareable - we just need to make it easy to share.

**Tactic**: Generate a shareable "AI Birth Certificate" image post-naming. One-click share to social. Template: "I just awakened [AI Name]. My AI remembers me now."

### 2. Two-Sided Referral Programs Work Best for SaaS
Research shows two-sided incentives (both referrer AND referee benefit) close growth loops faster. Structure:
- Referrer: 30 days free per successful referral
- Referee: First month 50% off

**Key timing**: Surface referral prompts at emotional peaks (post-naming, first workflow success, 7-day anniversary).

### 3. Community-Led Growth > Cold Outreach in 2026
Private Slack/Discord communities outperform cold outreach for B2B SaaS. Users trust peer recommendations in curated spaces.

**Target communities identified**:
1. Solopreneur/Creator: Indie Hackers, Ship 30, Buildspace
2. Marketing/Growth: Demand Curve, Exit Five
3. AI Power Users: OpenAI Discord, r/ClaudeAI
4. Productivity: Notion Community, Obsidian Discord
5. CPG/Marketing Directors: CPG Insiders, Modern Retail

### 4. Session Bug Blocks All Growth Tactics
The session management bug (92% of sessions = "unknown" ID) breaks:
- User journey tracking
- Referral attribution
- Returning user reconnection

**This is P0 before ANY growth scaling.**

### 5. K-Factor Target for Viral SaaS
Target K-factor of 0.3 (each user brings 0.3 new users on average). This creates compounding growth:
- 1,000 users -> 300 referrals -> 90 second-order referrals

## Future Application

When planning viral growth for AI products:
1. Identify the emotional peak moment in user journey
2. Make that moment shareable with one click
3. Use two-sided referral incentives
4. Time prompts at emotional highs, not random
5. Target communities where ICP already congregates
6. Fix tracking infrastructure before scaling
