## Context

Created comprehensive LinkedIn commenting strategy for Jared Sanborn (PureBrain.ai) to drive 10-20 newsletter subscribers per week through 20 minutes of daily targeted engagement.

## Key Decisions Made

### 1. Three-Tier Target Structure (24 People)

- Tier 1 (daily): Ethan Mollick, Pascal Bornet, Allie K. Miller, Andrew Ng, Klarna CEO, Arvind Krishna, Dario Amodei, Sam Altman
- Tier 2 (3x/week): Bernard Marr, Zain Kahn, Liz Ryan, Sangram Vajre, Scott Brinker, J.P. Gownder, Rashim Mogha, Chip Conley, Gary Vaynerchuk, Ryan Breslow, Tobias Lutke, Cassie Kozyrkov
- Tier 3 (1x/week): Karim Lakhani, Mike Volpe, Bret Taylor, Reid Hoffman

### 2. 10 Comment Templates Created

1. Real Example - most authentic, uses PureBrain experience
2. Contrarian Angle - respectfully challenges premises
3. Missing Piece - adds dimension post didn't cover
4. Question That Makes People Think - genuine curiosity driver
5. Data Point - adds statistics
6. Story - brief narrative with specificity
7. Framework - mental model offer
8. Connection - links to broader trend
9. Case Study - external example
10. Bridge to PureBrain - max 1 in 5-8 comments, never a pitch

### 3. Strict 20-Minute Protocol

- Min 1-3: Scan for candidates
- Min 3-5: Select 3 best
- Min 5-12: Comment 1 (80-150 words, most important)
- Min 12-16: Comment 2 (50-100 words)
- Min 16-19: Comment 3 (30-60 words)
- Min 20: Reply loops from yesterday

### 4. Six PureBrain-Specific Authority Topics

1. AI memory and persistent context
2. Naming AI and building AI relationships
3. Enterprise AI governance
4. AI pilot purgatory
5. CEO vs. employee AI lens
6. Autonomous AI workflows and multi-agent systems

## Strategic Insights Worth Remembering

- Early comments (within first 2 hours of post) get 10x profile visits of late comments
- The mechanism: good comment -> profile visit -> newsletter discovery -> subscription
- Never put links in comments (destroys the curiosity loop)
- Template 10 should be 1 in 8 comments, not 1 in 5 (true authentic ratio)
- 3 quality comments beat 10 generic ones every time
- Gary Vee's comment section is noise-heavy - only engage with genuinely substantial points
- Sam Altman posts infrequently on LinkedIn (more active on X) - monitor but don't chase

## Future Application

For any LinkedIn engagement strategy:
1. Prioritize early commenting (within 2 hours of post) over volume
2. The indirect mechanism (comment -> profile visit -> newsletter) requires patience
3. Templates should be patterns, not scripts - voice must remain authentic
4. Track which thought leaders actually convert to profile visits and subscriptions
5. Reply loops (responding to replies on your comments) create second notification wave
