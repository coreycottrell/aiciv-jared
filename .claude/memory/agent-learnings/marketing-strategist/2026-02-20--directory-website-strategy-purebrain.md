## Key Discoveries

### 1. Industry Affinity Framework for "Living AI Partner" Products

For AI products that emphasize persistent memory and relationship continuity, the highest-affinity industries are those where the human relationship IS the product:
- Legal (attorneys managing client context)
- Real Estate (agents tracking buyer preferences)
- Financial Advisory (advisors tracking client goals over years)
- Marketing Agencies (managing multiple client brand voices)

These score 8-9/10 on affinity vs. industries where AI is a transactional tool.

### 2. Directory Website Strategy Pattern (For Future Reference)

Best practice for niche directory sites driving traffic to a core product:
1. Each site has ONE industry focus - do not try to combine verticals
2. First article is always the pillar: "Best AI tools for [Industry]" (targets main keyword)
3. Every article includes 5+ FAQ pairs with FAQ schema markup
4. Internal links point both within the directory site AND to the core product (purebrain.ai)
5. UTM parameters on all links: `utm_source=[domain]&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=directory`
6. AEO and SEO are optimized together - structure for extraction, not just readability

### 3. AEO vs SEO Content Structure Differences

AEO (AI Answer Engine Optimization) requires:
- Direct answer in first paragraph (AI systems scan for this)
- FAQ sections mandatory (Perplexity/Claude pull directly from these)
- FAQ schema markup (structured data that machines can parse)
- Data with citations in-article (Perplexity favors cited statistics)
- Headers phrased as questions ("How Can Lawyers Use AI?" not "How Lawyers Use AI")
- Definitions of key terms ("What is an AI with persistent memory?")

Key stat: Overlap between AI citations and Google top-10 results is only 12-28%. Must optimize separately.

### 4. Publication Contribution Timing and ROI

Directory sites take 3-6 months for SEO traction. Publication contributions deliver traffic faster (days to weeks after publication). Best sequence:
1. Build niche sites (foundation)
2. Publish on sites (establish content)
3. Pitch publications referencing the site content as authority
4. Publication placements link back to core product

### 5. Industry-Specific Publication Targets

For legal: Above the Law, National Law Review, Law360 Pulse (contributor-friendly, AI-forward)
For real estate: Inman News (has active contributor network), HousingWire, REALTOR Magazine
For financial services: Financial Planning Magazine, ThinkAdvisor, InvestmentNews
For marketing: MarTech, Search Engine Journal, Content Marketing Institute

### 6. Outreach Template (Proven Pattern)

- Subject: "Guest pitch: [Specific headline] - [Name], CEO of PureBrain.ai"
- Under 200 words
- Reference a specific recent article they published
- Lead with the counterintuitive claim, not the product
- 15-20% response rate is normal for cold outreach to established publications

## PureBrain-Specific Positioning Insight

The "memory problem" is the universal hook across all industries:
- Law: "Your AI forgets your client's case history between sessions"
- Real Estate: "Your AI doesn't remember what your client is actually looking for"
- Finance: "Your AI resets the client relationship every conversation"

In every vertical, the angle is the same: generic AI starts from zero every time. PureBrain doesn't. Lead with the problem, land on the solution.
