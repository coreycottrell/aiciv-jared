## Key New Discoveries

### 1. Email Capture Copy Is Generic — This Is a 30-Minute High-ROI Fix

All 7 posts have email capture forms with generic "Subscribe" copy. Changing to benefit-specific copy ("Read What I Can't Publish on the Blog") is a single Elementor edit that applies site-wide. Expected 20-40% list growth improvement. This was missed in 3 prior sessions.

### 2. LinkedIn Newsletter Is a Separate Audience — Treat It That Way

LinkedIn Newsletter subscribers opted in on LinkedIn and likely haven't seen the blog or email newsletter. Republishing blog content to this audience is a missed opportunity. Proposed "The Partnership Council Weekly" format (400-600 words, Monday, distinctive content) creates a third content channel with zero overlap. 12 issue topics ready to queue.

### 3. Post Headline SEO Scores Reveal the Two-Speed Blog Problem

Posts 1-2 score 2-3/10 for search discoverability (brand voice posts — intentionally conversational). Posts 3-7 score 5-8/10. The fix is not renaming existing posts — it's creating Layer 3 companion posts that capture the search traffic and link into the deeper brand-voice posts. Four specific Layer 3 posts identified.

### 4. Voice Drift Is Measurable and Requires a Pre-Publish Filter

Daily posting pace (8 posts in 9 days) has produced a measurable voice drift. Voice Score declined from A+ (Posts 1-2) to B range (Posts 5-7). The fix is a single-sentence pre-publish voice filter: "Could any AI blog publish this?" If yes — revise the opening before publishing.

### 5. Pillar Page Spec Finalized for Post 7

Post 7 (95% AI Pilots Fail) is the strongest pillar page candidate. Spec: expand to 3,500-4,500 words with 5 new sections including original data, downloadable checklist (content upgrade), case study section, and embedded AI Pilot Glossary.

## Patterns Worth Noting

### The Email Form Copy Gap Pattern

Three sessions of analysis can miss the most visible thing on the page. The email forms are on every post. The copy is generic. No prior session flagged this. Lesson: check the micro-copy, not just the structure.

### Republish Default Is the Default, Not a Strategy

The LinkedIn Newsletter getting blog content is a default behavior — easiest thing to do. It is not a strategy. Any time a channel is receiving "whatever is easiest to repurpose," that channel is being underused. This applies universally.

### Intent Categories Outperform Audience Categories

"For Individuals / For Teams" is an audience classification that doesn't help readers find the next relevant thing to read. "Fixing What's Broken / Scaling AI" is an intent classification that matches where the reader is in their journey. Intent-based categories reduce bounce rate because they recommend the next step, not just the next audience-appropriate post.
