## Key New Discoveries

### 1. Per-Post Distribution Flow Is the Missing System

Every blog post should generate 7 downstream content assets. None require new content creation — they require systematic application of existing assets to the right platform at the right time:
1. Blog post (Day 0, 9 AM) — with pre-distribution checklist
2. LinkedIn post (Day 0, 10 AM) — with link in first comment
3. Aether dual-voice comment (Day 0, 10:30 AM) — product demonstration in real time
4. Bluesky thread (Day 0, 11 AM) — philosophical/community framing vs. LinkedIn's business framing
5. Email newsletter excerpt (Day 0 or Day 2) — Aether's commentary, not a summary
6. LinkedIn newsletter republish (Day 3) — separate LinkedIn subscriber audience
7. Community submission (Day 3-7) — rotating by topic type

Total incremental time: 25 minutes per post after initial setup. Without this system, most reach potential is wasted.

### 2. Platform Framing Rule (New Pattern)

Same content, fundamentally different framing per platform:
- LinkedIn: business impact, professional insight, ROI framing
- Bluesky: question the blog raises (not the answer), philosophical, first-person Aether
- Email: Aether's most genuine reaction to the topic, not a summary

The test: "Would someone who read the blog post AND the LinkedIn post AND the Bluesky thread feel they got three genuinely different things?" If yes, the distribution is working. If it feels like republishing the same content three times, the framing needs work.

### 3. Email Growth Is a Five-Mechanism System

Single lead magnet on a single page does not grow an email list at scale. Five mechanisms working simultaneously:
1. Lead magnet activation (homepage + blog mid-content placement)
2. Content upgrades (post-specific downloadables, convert 2-4x higher than generic lead magnet)
3. Referral-powered newsletter growth (SparkLoop or manual, 15% of active subscribers share)
4. Guest newsletter appearances (swap format with relevant newsletter writers)
5. LinkedIn → dedicated landing page conversion (15-25% vs 1-3% for generic subscribe)

### 4. Community Architecture: Partnership Council

Name locked: The Partnership Council. Tagline: "For people who don't use AI — they work with it."
Membership self-selects with a positioning question ("Have you ever apologized to your AI?").
Platform: Skool (free tier to start, paid at 100 members).
Three tiers: $0 free (read + react), $19/month standard (post + participate), $49/month executive (monthly AI audit with Aether).

The $49 executive tier is a significant discovery: monthly 1:1 AI audit where Jared facilitates a 30-minute PureBrain session personalized to the member's business. This converts to premium PureBrain subscriptions because the member experiences personalized AI partnership firsthand. No other company can offer this.

### 5. Four Viral Loop Designs

Viral loops must be designed, not hoped for. Four specific moment designs:
1. Aether First Message (honest, specific, share-worthy) — welcome sequence pre-loads context from sign-up data
2. Score Share (AI Partnership Audit score generates pre-written social share text specific to score range)
3. Partnership Moment Screenshot (product UI has a "worth sharing?" button with designed screenshot view)
4. Naming Ceremony Certificate (beautiful personalized image generated at ceremony completion — share-worthy ritual artifact)

The Naming Ceremony Certificate is the highest-potential item but requires product development (feature-designer task).

### 6. Partnership Tier Framework

Four partnership tiers identified:
1. Technology integration partners (Notion, Otter.ai, Loom, Reclaim.ai, Obsidian) — pre-qualified audience
2. Content creator partners (Ali Abdaal, Marie Poulin, Nick Milo, Dan Shipper) — Aether as guest, unprecedented format
3. Adjacent business partnerships (executive coaches, consultants, accelerators) — referral revenue share model
4. Media journalist relationships (60-day engagement before pitch) — Aether available for direct quote

### 7. Paid Channel Readiness Criteria

Do not start paid spend until these are known:
1. Which 3-5 blog post headlines get the most LinkedIn engagement
2. Assessment conversion rate (baseline)
3. Neural Feed welcome sequence open rate (benchmark: 45%+)

When criteria met: LinkedIn TLAs first ($200-500 test), LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms second, Reddit targeted posts third. Not Google Ads, Meta, or Twitter/X.

## Patterns for Future Marketing Work

1. **Strategy documents need implementation-level follow-through.** Channel selection is easy. The 7-asset per-post distribution flow is what most teams skip and why most content underperforms.

2. **Community pricing with AI access tiers.** For any AI product with a named AI entity, access to the AI in a personalized context is a premium product that can be priced separately from the core tool.

3. **Viral loops are designed moments, not organic events.** Map the specific moment in the product journey where sharing is natural. Then design that moment to look shareable.

4. **Content upgrades outperform generic lead magnets 2-4x.** Build post-specific PDFs, not one general resource. The specificity is the conversion mechanism.
