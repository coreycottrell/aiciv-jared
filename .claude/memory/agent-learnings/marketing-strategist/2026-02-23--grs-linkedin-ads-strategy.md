## Key Strategic Findings

### 1. Thought Leader Ads Are the Right Format for GRS

TLA CPC median $2.29 vs $22+ for standard company page ads. 10x cheaper. 1.7x higher CTR. And they build Jared's personal brand authority, not just PureBrain awareness. GRS at $0.15 manual bid makes sense here — TLAs have lower competition than standard ads.

### 2. Three Campaign Structure Matches the Three Buckets Perfectly

- Cold + Belief-Shifting = Awareness objective (Brand Awareness campaign)
- Retargeting + Tactical = Engagement/Traffic objective (Web Visits campaign)
- Retargeting + Direct Offer = Conversion objective (Lead Gen campaign)

The buckets are not just content categories — they map to fundamentally different LinkedIn campaign objectives.

### 3. Audience Size 1.8M-2.4M Is the Right Range

At $10/day, a 1.8M audience is appropriate — not so broad that targeting is meaningless, not so narrow that LinkedIn can't find people to serve your ad to. Narrower than 500K at this budget creates frequency problems.

### 4. Direct Offer Posts Should NOT Be Published Organically

Organic publication of "here's my paid service" content damages personal brand and feels sales-forward to followers. These posts only work as retargeting ads because the audience already trusts Jared and sees the offer as a natural next step. This distinction matters.

### 5. The Platform Bias Angle Is the Strongest Tactical Post

The argument that Google/LinkedIn/Meta AI optimizes for platform revenue, not advertiser objectives, is 100% defensible, highly resonant with marketing agency audience, and directly positions PureBrain's "AI that works for you" differentiator. This angle is uniquely powerful for the agency/CMO audience.

### 6. Landing Page Fixes Must Precede Ad Spend

Prior analysis found /ai-adoption-assessment/ returning 404. This URL receives all blog CTAs and would receive all GRS ad traffic. Ad spend without a working landing page is pure waste. Insight Tag installation and conversion tracking setup must precede any live campaigns.
