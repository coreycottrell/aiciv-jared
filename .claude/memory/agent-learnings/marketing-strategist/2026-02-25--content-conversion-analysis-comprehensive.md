## Patterns Worth Preserving

### The Trust Bootstrapping Pattern

Converting customers requires trust. Trust requires testimonials. Testimonials require customers. Breaking this loop requires direct outreach to existing customers with a specific format request — not a generic "leave a review" ask. The most effective format: "What changed about how you work since you started?" Offer to draft for their approval. Gets 3-5x more responses than open-ended requests.

### Assessment Score Mismatch Pattern

Any scored lead capture tool (quiz, assessment, audit) that routes all score buckets to the same CTA is losing conversion from low-intent and high-intent visitors simultaneously. Low-intent visitors (low scores) need a value-first path (free resource, nurture sequence). High-intent visitors (high scores) need a direct purchase path. One CTA for all scores wastes both segments.

### Ungated Long-Form Content Pattern

Long-form guides (7+ sections) are the highest-intent content on any site. Visitors who read a 7-section guide are not casually browsing. Gate the guide at section 1-2 preview, deliver rest via email series. Expected capture rate: 15-25% of guide visitors. Email series delivery creates 7 touchpoints instead of one anonymous pageview.

### Competitive Moat Framing

PureBrain's clearest moat vs. general AI tools: permanent memory. This cannot be replicated without architectural investment. Framing: "They forget you. We don't." vs. "Your Brain. Your AI." is a concrete proof claim vs. brand aesthetic claim. Concrete claims outperform aesthetic claims in conversion for unfamiliar products.
