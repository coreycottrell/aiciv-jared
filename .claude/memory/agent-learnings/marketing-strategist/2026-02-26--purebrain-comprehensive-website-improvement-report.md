## Patterns Worth Noting

### "Compound Trust Gap" Pattern
When a site has zero trust signals (no testimonials, no social proof, no subscriber count, no press), the compound effect is worse than the sum of individual gaps. A visitor encountering multiple empty trust sections interprets this as early-stage/risky rather than just "missing proof." Getting to 3-4 trust elements simultaneously is more important than incrementally adding one at a time.

### "Score-Matched CTA" Pattern (Reconfirmed)
Any assessment or quiz that routes all score buckets to the same CTA is losing both ends simultaneously — low scorers bounce from premature purchase asks, high scorers receive no validation reinforcement. Score-matching is a 3-4 hour development task with the highest expected ROI of any single site element beyond trust signal addition.

### "Quick Win Math" Pattern
Quick wins (< 4 hours each) collectively generate 15-20% conversion improvement within 7 days when deployed as a batch. This creates measurable proof of progress before strategic changes (which take weeks) show results. The batch matters — deploying quick wins individually produces isolated effects that are harder to attribute and harder to motivate.
