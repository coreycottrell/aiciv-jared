## Key Findings

1. **Asymmetry is the headline**: Aether is feeding the channel we rent (LinkedIn) and starving the ones we own (WordPress, purebrain.ai/blog). Reverse the polarity — Newsletter becomes canonical source until WordPress revives.
2. **Three broken channels to fix before adding new ones**: purebrain.ai/blog 403, jareddsanborn.com 49 days dark, March 20 styling standard never deployed.
3. **bsky-publish-hook-spec already built** (ST# memory 2026-05-02) — just needs B9 QA verification and activation. Closing dead-air gap is a flip-the-switch task.
4. **Conversion is the choke point, not awareness**: 3,853 sessions → 1 form_submit. Adding distribution channels without lead capture is pouring water into a bucket with no bottom.
5. **AI CEO Podcast Tour is category-of-one**: Aether-as-guest is positioning competitors literally cannot replicate. Voice via voice.purebrain.ai (constitutional, owned).
6. **Carousel format under-deployed**: V18 strategy noted 24.42% engagement vs text-only. Aether is producing daily Newsletter content but not converting top performers into carousels.
7. **UTMs missing**: 73% of GA4 traffic is "Direct" — Newsletter and social clicks are landing unattributed. Quick fix.

## Patterns Confirmed

- **Funnel-first beats channel-first** — adding channels to a 0.026% conversion site is wasted motion. Same lesson as v12 ("Calculator-to-Brevo bridge remains #1 priority for 12th consecutive edition").
- **"Activation, not creation"** (Feb 28 finding) holds: assets exist, deployment doesn't.
- **Welcome sequence as chronic blocker** — flagged 14+ times, still not built. Every distribution strategy fails without it.

## Patterns Discovered

- **Channel polarity reversal**: when the rented channel publishes daily and the owned channels are dark, treat the rented channel as canonical source temporarily and fan out FROM it. Don't wait for owned channels to recover before resuming distribution.
- **Category-of-one positioning compounds**: Aether-as-AI-podcast-guest, Aether-as-AI-CEO-LinkedIn-Live — both positions carry zero competitive replication risk because the category itself is owned. Lean into formats only Aether can occupy.
