## What I Learned

### YouTube Shorts B2B Reality (2026)

**Discovery Advantage:**
- 74% of views from non-subscribers = highest cold-audience reach of any short-form platform
- Shorts benefit from YouTube's search engine (TikTok/Reels don't have durable search discoverability)
- Average engagement rate 5.91% (beats TikTok, IG Reels, FB Reels)

**B2B Conversion Path:**
- Short-form demos drive 38% of demo requests (proven B2B conversion)
- BUT: Shorts alone don't generate B2B leads—they build brand familiarity and test hooks
- Best B2B use = clip high-performing moments from existing long-form content
- If no long-form library, invest there first (Shorts without foundation = views with no conversion path)

**PureBrain Application:**
- We HAVE long-form (blog posts with full audio)
- Shorts act as "interest hooks" driving viewers to blog/site
- Our ICP (operators, founders, AI-curious seniors) research on YouTube for tools/case studies
- Shorts surface to interest-based audiences (not just subscribers), perfect for net-new reach

### Hacker News Reality Check

### The Repurpose vs. Net-New Framework

This task forced clarity on what "under-utilized" really means:

**NOT:**
- Platforms where we could post more if we had more content
- Channels requiring new content creation disciplines

**YES:**
- Assets we already own (YouTube channel, blog audio library)
- Content we're already producing (weekly blog with audio)
- Distribution we're missing (clips we could extract but aren't)

**YouTube Shorts wins because:**
1. Asset exists (YouTube channel ✅)
2. Raw material exists (20-30 blog audio files ✅)
3. Extraction is mechanical (FFmpeg timestamps, not creative writing ✅)
4. Cadence rides existing rhythm (1 blog/week = 3 Shorts/week ✅)

### 30-Day Falsifiability Design

**Why Views-to-CTR, not vanity views:**
- 10,000 views means nothing if nobody clicks through
- B2B audiences scroll differently than entertainment seekers
- ICP match proven by action (click to learn more), not passive consumption

**Why 500 views + 1% CTR threshold:**
- 500 views = baseline to confirm algo distribution (proves we're not shadowbanned)
- 1% CTR = 5 people per 500 views clicking through (low bar, but proves qualified interest)
- If we can't hit that in 30 days (12 Shorts total), the hook format isn't working

**Kill criteria clarity:**
- Prevents sunk cost fallacy ("but we built the pipeline, let's keep posting")
- 30 days = 4 weeks of A/B testing (different hook types, different b-roll styles)
- If algo learning curve doesn't kick in by then, audience mismatch confirmed

### Risk Assessment Honesty

**Why Shorts could flop:**
- YouTube algo optimized for entertainment (dance trends, comedy skits, viral challenges)
- B2B educational content fights uphill against algo preference
- "Talking head AI clips" may not hook scrollers the way product demos or founder stories do
- Vanity views (people watch 3 seconds, swipe) inflate metrics without qualified traffic

**Mitigation:**
- Test 3 Shorts styles in Week 1 (pure audio + text, b-roll + voiceover, founder on-camera intro)
- Track CTR obsessively (not just views)
- If Week 2 CTR < 0.5%, pivot Shorts style before Week 3
- If Month 1 fails threshold, kill experiment and redirect effort to proven channels (LinkedIn, Bluesky)
