## Context

Built a high-stakes dual pitch for Graham Martin — gaming industry titan (Sky Bet £3.4B exit, 17 government regulatory advisor, Probability Games plc). Two simultaneous asks: investor in PureBrain AND enterprise customer deploying 100+ PureBrains.

## Key Structural Decisions

### 1. Lead With the Flaw in the Competition, Not Our Feature

Instead of pitching PureBrain's features, opened by naming the architectural flaw in ALL current AI tools (no persistent memory). This positions PureBrain as the only coherent solution, not a competitor.

### 2. Mirror His Own Career Arc

Graham built regulatory frameworks that didn't exist yet. Sky Bet was speculative at launch. The pitch mirrors that pattern: "You've done this before — built the category before it was certain." This creates autobiographical resonance. He is not being asked to do something unfamiliar; he is being asked to repeat his best move.

### 3. Dual Track: Investor + Customer + Co-Creator (Three Revenue Streams)

Most pitches choose one. This pitch makes all three mutually reinforcing:
- Investor = upside on the whole company
- Customer = proof case that drives other gaming operators
- Co-creator = his regulatory IP has a royalty structure attached

The combination is more compelling than any single track.

### 4. The "Graham Martin Regulatory Intelligence Platform" Framing

The most differentiated element: nobody else can build this specific PureBrain. It requires his IP + PureBrain architecture. This makes him the necessary ingredient, not the recipient of a pitch. Power dynamic inversion.

### 5. Gaming-Specific ROI Scenarios

Five specific ROI scenarios tailored to his world:
1. Regulatory compliance automation (17 jurisdictions x analyst costs)
2. Multi-jurisdiction market entry speed (licensing window compression)
3. VIP customer retention (£5M+ on £50M VIP base)
4. Risk/fraud pattern compounding memory
5. Acquisition due diligence compression

### 6. Pricing Architecture for Enterprise Gaming Deployment

- Foundation: £2,500/month (up to 10 PureBrains)
- Growth: £6,500/month (11-50 PureBrains)
- Enterprise: £15,000/month (51-150 PureBrains)
- Strategic Partnership: bespoke (150+ + custom regulatory training)

Pricing anchored against human equivalent cost. Breakeven at Month 2-3 for Growth tier.

## Key Insight: "Correct Them" as a Qualification Technique

Presenting cost estimates as directional and inviting Graham to correct them is a deliberate technique. When he says "actually it costs more than that in Gibraltar," he has become an engaged co-author of the ROI case. That is a buying signal.

## Close Structure

Closed on scarcity (window to be founding partner closes). Did NOT close on urgency of our need for capital — closed on urgency of his window to own this position before someone else fills it. Veteran investors respond to opportunity scarcity, not vendor urgency.
