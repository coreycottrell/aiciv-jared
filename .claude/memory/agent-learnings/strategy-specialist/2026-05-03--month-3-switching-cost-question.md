# Month-3 Switching Cost: The Real Moat Test

**Agent**: strategy-specialist
**Date**: 2026-05-03
**Type**: teaching
**Confidence**: high

---

## Context

Nightly activation BOOP asked for the single highest-leverage strategic question PT should answer in the next 30 days. PT is mid-pricing-flip transition (25-100 client range, $149/$499/$999 → $197/$579/$1,089). Narrative converging across the agent collective is "memory-as-moat" vs. Mem.ai/ChatGPT/Notion AI.

## The Insight

Every input-side lever (positioning, channels, pricing) is reversible and copyable. The only asset that compounds monotonically with usage is **switching cost from accumulated memory artifacts**. Mem.ai's "Heads Up" can be cloned in a quarter — three months of a specific customer's voice, decisions, customers, and rejected ideas cannot.

## Why Founders at 25-100 Clients Skip This

1. Top-of-funnel feels like the obvious bottleneck.
2. Cohorts haven't aged long enough to actually test churn — so retention "looks fine."
3. Studying it risks discovering your differentiator is weaker than your pitch deck. Most would rather not know.

## The Falsifiable Test (30 days)

Three instruments by June 3:
1. **Memory depth score** per customer (entities, voice tokens, decisions referenced).
2. **Forced export flow** offered to 10 random month-3+ customers — measure completion rate and "what's missing."
3. **One survey question**: "If we shut down tomorrow, what would you lose that ChatGPT can't replace?"

**Pass threshold**: 7 of 10 give concrete, specific, painful, PT-only answers.

**Fail action**: Redirect the next 30 days of growth spend from acquisition to deepening memory artifacts before the $197 price flip lands.

## Why This Matters Strategically

If month-3 churn is structurally near-zero, the price flip is just a coupon for early adopters. If it's normal SaaS (5-7%), the entire memory-as-moat narrative is marketing fiction and the pricing ladder is built on sand. Either answer is more valuable than another 50 logos.

## Decision Frame for Jared

This is the question whose answer changes whether PT is a SaaS company with cute branding OR a switching-cost compounder. Those have different valuations, different fundraise stories, and different next-30-day priorities.
