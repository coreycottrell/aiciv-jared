## Key Findings

### GRS Architecture (3 Buckets)
- **Bucket 1 - Belief-Shifting**: Take a stance. Shift the enemy from "wrong tool" to "tool sprawl + no direction." Never sell in these posts. Pure worldview content. These build the warm audience.
- **Bucket 2 - Tactical**: Results, behind-the-scenes, how-to. Documentary content (what my AI team worked on this week) is immune to copying and converts curiosity into interest.
- **Bucket 3 - Direct Offer**: Direct response with URL in first comment. Sacrifice engagement metrics for conversion. Use only AFTER Buckets 1 and 2 have warmed the audience.

### LinkedIn Paid Ads Key Settings (Verified 2026)
- **Objective**: Always choose Engagement (not Brand Awareness) — Engagement CPC is $4.80 vs $7.90-12 for Brand Awareness
- **Manual vs automated bid**: Start with automated for 2 weeks to get baseline, then switch to manual at 15% above suggested range
- **Daily budget starting point**: $10/day for Belief-Shifting, $20-30/day for Direct Offer posts
- **ALWAYS disable**: Audience Expansion (More targeting options → uncheck) AND LinkedIn Audience Network (Placements section → uncheck)
- **Boost trigger**: Only boost posts that got ICP comments in first 90 minutes of organic posting
- **Campaign duration**: 5-7 days max per post before fatigue increases CPCs

### LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms vs External Landing Pages
Critical finding: LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms (native to Campaign Manager) convert 5x better than external landing pages. For the AI Partnership Audit, a Lead Gen Form inside LinkedIn is the highest-converting setup, not sending traffic to purebrain.ai/ai-partnership-audit/.

### Economics at $300/month Starting Budget
- Average CPC: $1.25-2.29 (Thought Leader Ads)
- Expected monthly clicks: 130-240
- Lead conversion on free audit: 25-35%
- Expected leads: 32-84/month
- Expected calls: 3-17/month
- At 20% close rate + $179 tier: budget pays for itself with 2 closes

### Jared's Unfair Advantage
He is not consulting about AI partnership — he IS running one. Documentary content ("what my AI team worked on this week") is impossible for competitors to copy because it requires living the experience. No other LinkedIn CEO/founder voice has authentic first-person experience operating a human-AI collective. This is the content angle that should anchor the whole strategy.

DM template: "Hey [First Name], here's the AI Partnership Self-Assessment: [link]. Takes 5 min. Happy to walk through results — just reply here." Post that drives it: "Comment AUDIT below and I'll send you our AI Partnership assessment tool." 2026 AI intent detection means even "send it" or "I'd love that" triggers the DM.
