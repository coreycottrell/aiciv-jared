# Research: College ROI vs. AI Education
**Date**: 2026-03-20
**Topic**: Higher education economics, skills-based hiring, AI learning outcomes for PureBrain parent targeting
**Confidence**: High

## Key Findings

### College Cost Reality
- True 4-year cost: $108,584 (in-state public) to $261,880 (private nonprofit) including all expenses
- Average student loan debt: $43,333 total (including private); $39,547 federal
- 44.6 million Americans carry federal student debt totaling $1.84 trillion
- Average repayment time: 20 years; monthly payment ~$434
- Opportunity cost of 4 years not working: ~$140,000 in foregone wages

### Employment Crisis for Graduates (2025)
- 42.5% underemployment rate for recent graduates — near record high
- 52% of Class of 2023 underemployed a year after graduating
- Unemployment for young recent grads (23-27): 4.59%, up from 3.25% in 2019
- Only 30% of graduates work in their field of study
- 48% of 2025 grads felt unprepared for entry-level positions

### Employer Direction of Travel
- 72% of employers now prioritize skills over degrees
- Skills-based hiring up 63% year-over-year
- Google, Apple, IBM, Tesla, Bank of America, Dell, Walmart all dropped degree requirements
- IBM: no degree for half of U.S. roles
- Google: own certificates treated same as degrees; certificate holders outperform grads

### AI Skills Premium
- 56% wage premium for AI skills jobs (PwC 2025) — up from 25% one year prior
- 7x growth in AI fluency job requirements (2023-2025)
- 1.6 million unfilled AI positions globally
- AI engineer average salary: $206,000 (2025)
- $5.5 trillion projected economic loss from AI skills gaps

### AI Learning Effectiveness
- AI personalized learning: 54% higher test scores, 30% better outcomes, 10x engagement
- AI tutoring outperforms in-class active learning (2025 RCT, effect size 0.73-1.3 SD)
- Squirrel AI students: 456% higher scores in less time
- Learning effectiveness: 4.6/5 vs traditional 3.2/5

### Parent/Public Sentiment (Key for Marketing)
- 59% of Americans say college NOT worth cost (I&I/TIPP March 2026)
- 63% of registered voters say degree not worth cost (NBC 2025)
- Only 35% rate college as "very important" — down from 70% in 2010 (Gallup)
- 51% of Gen Z graduates say degree was "waste of money"
- BUT: 59% of parents still hope child attends college — cognitive dissonance, marketing opportunity

### PureBrain Value Proposition Math
- 4 years of college: $108,584-$261,880
- 4 years of PureBrain at $197/mo: $9,456
- Savings: $99,128-$252,424 (91-96% cheaper)
- Zero debt on "graduation"
- Skills aligned with market demands vs. outdated curricula
