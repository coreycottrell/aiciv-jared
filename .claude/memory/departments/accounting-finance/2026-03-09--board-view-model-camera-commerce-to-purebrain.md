# AF Memory: Board View Model — Camera Commerce Era to PureBrain Era

**Date**: 2026-03-09
**Type**: synthesis | financial-model
**Topic**: Complete board view financial model update from hardware to SaaS

## What Was Done

Read all 43 sheets of the original Board View Excel model (FP-001-230927), extracted key numbers, and built a full updated board-view financial model replacing the Camera Commerce / hardware business model with PureBrain SaaS + platform ecosystem reality.

## Key Numbers from Original Model (Camera Commerce Era)

- FY5 Revenue: $22.32B
- FY5 Gross Margin: 65.4% (limited by hardware COGS)
- FY5 EBITDA: $12.09B (54.1% margin)
- FY5 Net Income: $7.98B (35.7% margin)
- Hardware COGS FY5: $7.72B
- Customer Rewards FY5: $3.89B (huge liability)
- Firm Valuation (original): $14.37B via discounted FCF at 0.1% discount rate

## Updated Model Structure

11 revenue streams mapped from 6 original streams:
1. PureBrain Subscriptions → replaces Hardware Subsidies
2. Market Research/Data Services → evolved Core Model (same market, AI-enhanced)
3. Pure Influence → retained + AI layer added
4. CPG Model + PMG Agency → retained + existing PMG revenue
5. Enterprise Custom Builds → replaces Camera Commerce (higher value)
6. Pure Research → retained + IP-generating
7. Licensing & White-Label → NEW (biggest lever)
8. API/Marketplace → NEW
9. Training & Certification → NEW
10. Pure Capital → NEW
11. Pure Digital Assets → NEW

## Key Model Insights

**Structural advantage**: Updated model has no hardware COGS, no inventory, no customer rewards liability. Gross margin goes from 65.4% to 84.5% (Base) or 93.6% (Vision) — no hardware business can compete with this.

**Scenario architecture**: Original model presented one scenario as "the" number. Updated model has 4 scenarios. Vision/Board case = $7.57B FY5 (not the $22B original, because $22B was built on 7M phone deployments which is a different TAM capture than PureBrain's).

**The Vision lever**: Stream 7 (Licensing) at $4.2B in Vision FY5 requires 2–3 Fortune 500 white-label deals. P&G alone at 100K employees at $85/seat royalty = $102M ARR. Three such deals = $300M+. These are warm pipeline (Jared's existing relationships).

**Break-even timing**: Month 2 EBITDA positive. PMG revenue + enterprise pilots cover Month 1 gap. This is structurally superior to original model which had negative FCF through Year 2 due to hardware inventory buildup.

## Notable Gotchas

- Original model discount rate was 0.1% (nearly no discounting) — this is why terminal value was $14.2B and total firm value $14.37B. Very aggressive valuation assumption baked in.
- Original model's "Market Research Services / Core Model" FY5 = $14.94B (67% of total revenue from one stream) — high concentration risk. Updated model distributes across 11 streams.
- Customer Rewards was $3.89B FY5 in original (18% of revenue going to phone users as incentive) — eliminated entirely in PureBrain model.
