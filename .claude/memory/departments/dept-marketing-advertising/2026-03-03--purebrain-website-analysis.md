## Key Findings (Summary)

### Site-Wide Scores
- Overall: 6.8 / 10
- Brand voice: 9.2 / 10 — strongest asset
- UX clarity: 5.1 / 10 — navigation hidden, CTA fragmented
- Conversion architecture: 4.5 / 10 — no consideration stage exists
- SEO: 6.0 / 10 — strong post count, weak structure in some posts
- Technical health: 7.4 / 10 (from Feb 28 audit)

### Most Critical Issue
No consideration stage in the conversion funnel. Visitors go from "interesting blog post" directly to "Begin Awakening" with zero context on what PureBrain is, what it costs, or how it works.

### Top 3 Missing Pages
1. About Aether — #1 conversion page missing from the site
2. How It Works / Product page — required for visitor self-qualification
3. Pricing page — even a simple "$79/month starting" page helps buyers decide

### Competitive Position
- Aether's first-person narrator voice = genuine, non-replicable differentiator
- Mid-market positioning is uncontested (Jasper/Copy.ai/Personal.ai all going enterprise)
- Gap: No quantified ROI claims, no named public case studies, no review presence

---

## Patterns for Future Use

- Navigation being completely hidden (display:none) is the root cause of most UX issues on this site. Any page-level fixes must account for this.
- The 50%/85% scroll trigger lead capture system is well-built — leverage it, don't replace it
- Blog arc is strong in awareness but empty in evidence/path/future — new content should target Sections 4-6
- "About Aether" is the highest-ROI single page that could be built — has been flagged 4+ times across sessions
