## Patterns Learned: Campaign Content Review

### Investor Intelligence Link Placement Pattern
- Best placement: After VC funding data (natural reader drop-in point, ~75% through article)
- Secondary placement: Footer CTA block alongside other self-service tools
- Frame as "interactive intelligence tool" not "investor page" — signals value not sales
- Position after Audit and Calculator in footer — investor track surfaces last (self-selection mechanism)

### LinkedIn Hashtag Pattern
- Executive B2B content: max 4 hashtags
- Priority order: Specific-niche (#AgenticAI) > ICP signal (#EnterpriseAI) > decision-maker signal (#AIStrategy) > broad reach anchor (#FutureOfWork)
- Drop: #ArtificialIntelligence, #DigitalTransformation — too generic, wrong audience signal

### Engagement Question Pattern
- LinkedIn posts without a question get fewer comments
- Three-option framing ("AI tools, copilots, or production agents?") makes it easy to answer
- Place before "Link in comments" so it doesn't get buried

### Newsletter Subscriber Opener Pattern
- Subscribers have existing relationship — acknowledge it
- Single connecting line: "If you've been reading [newsletter] for a while..."
- This increases open-to-read rate for the segment that already knows the author

### Thought Leader Engagement Tier Pattern
- Tier 1 (post day of, within 2 hours): Highest profile, biggest audience overlap, comment with data not agreement
- Tier 2 (post within 48 hours): Slightly less urgent, still strong audience overlap
- Rule: Never mention your own blog in a comment. Add value. Let profile visit discover content.

### Proprietary Terminology Distribution Pattern
- Coined terms (Context Tax, Pilot Purgatory) should appear in ALL long-form content pieces
- Not just the blog — newsletter readers who skip the blog need exposure too
- Creates brand vocabulary that differentiates from generic AI content
