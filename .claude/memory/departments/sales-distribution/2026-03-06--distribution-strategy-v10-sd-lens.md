## Key Frameworks Introduced

### Pipeline Stage Definitions
7 stages: Aware → Engaged → Assessed → Qualified → Trial Start → Retained → Expanded
With target conversion rates at each transition.

### Channel Economic Audit Template
CPL + CPA tracking per channel. Without this, channel investment decisions are guesses.

### Channel Tiering (March Priority)
- Tier 1: Email, Referral, LinkedIn (Jared), Assessment Funnel
- Tier 2: Consultant network, Bluesky funnel, Occupation pages
- Tier 3: Do not invest yet — LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads, YouTube, Podcast

### Revenue Target Math (Q2)
243 total customers across 4 tiers = ~$112K MRR possible. Conservative target: $50K MRR by June 30.

### Partner Ecosystem Architecture
Three partner types: Referral (passive, 20% recurring), Reseller (wholesale pricing), Integration (technical, API-level distribution).

### Paid Distribution Gate
Trial-to-paid conversion must exceed 60% before introducing paid channels. Below 60% = fix the funnel, not the traffic.

### Jared Time Budget
~2.5 hours/week on high-trust, un-automatable distribution. Everything else delegated.

## Patterns for Future Revenue Work

1. **Sales lens vs. marketing lens**: Marketing optimizes reach. Sales optimizes conversion. Both are needed, but the frameworks are different. Next time a "distribution strategy" request comes in, confirm which lens is needed — or produce both separately.

2. **CRO before CAC**: The fastest path to revenue at current scale is converting more of the people already in the funnel, not acquiring more people. Fix conversion before investing in acquisition.

3. **Enterprise changes the math**: One enterprise deal at $5,000-$12,000/mo has more revenue impact than 10-80 SMB customers. At current scale, 3 enterprise contracts = $126K-$432K ARR. Do not treat enterprise as a future-state strategy.

4. **Paid distribution is last, not first**: Paid amplifies what is already working. If conversion is broken, paid makes the problem more expensive. Gating paid behind a 60% trial-to-paid conversion threshold is the right discipline.

5. **Referrals are the highest-quality channel**: Referred customers have 25-40% higher LTV and 50-60% lower CAC than any paid channel. This is always true in SaaS. Build the referral infrastructure before anything else in the acquisition stack.

6. **Influencer accountability**: Aether's brand value as a distribution asset is only provable with UTM tracking. Without measurement, it is an expense. With measurement, it becomes a quantified channel with a CAC that can be compared to other channels.
