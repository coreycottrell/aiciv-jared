## Context

Built a custom enterprise AI deployment proposal for Hunden Partners, a hospitality/destination real estate consulting firm. They had already produced their own AI org chart and agent summary document — sophisticated buyer with architectural thinking.

---

## Key Patterns Learned

### 1. Sophisticated buyers need architecture mapping, not feature lists

Hunden already knew what they wanted (Hunden OS, client mode vs studio mode, three specific pilots). The proposal's job was not to explain AI — it was to map their existing vision onto PureBrain's deployment architecture. Skip the education section, go straight to the mapping.

### 2. Their own document is the proposal outline

Their AI org chart document contained: 5 operational domains, 3 recommended pilots with names, a shared OS concept, and a list of expansion areas. Every section of the proposal came directly from their language. Mirror the client's own framework back to them.

### 3. Enterprise pricing requires a phased entry point

Full deployment came to $12,000/mo ($144K/year). The objection management is built into the proposal via phased pricing: $3,500/mo for pilot validation. If pilots fail within 30 days, client walks with no obligation. This removes the risk that blocks enterprise decisions.

### 4. ROI framing must be conservative and specific

Analyst time recovery: 4 analysts x 60% reduction x $75/hr = $187K/year. This is not aspirational — it is conservative. The proposal explicitly calls it conservative. Enterprise buyers trust low-end honest estimates more than high-end projections.

### 5. CCW (Claude CoWork) comparison handled proactively

Client mentioned CCW as an alternative. Address it directly. The distinction: CCW is a tool framework, PureBrain is a deployed civilization. Same way Hunden distinguishes between a research tool and a research analyst. Use the client's own language to explain the differentiation.

### 6. Brain stream count anchors the scale of the recommendation

Total brain streams: 34 across 6 civilizations. That number is credible and specific. Vague proposals lose. Count everything.
