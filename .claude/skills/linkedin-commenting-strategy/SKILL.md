## THE CORE FRAMEWORK: "The Traveling Comment"

Comments don't just engage — they **travel**. They get picked up by the algorithm and shown to thousands who never saw the original post.

### The Numbers That Prove It

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Profile appearances from comments (7 days) | 185,262 |
| % of appearances from comments (not posts) | 98.6% |
| Single comment impressions | 59,000+ |

**The insight**: Comments are not engagement. Comments are **distribution infrastructure.**

## THE THREE-PART FORMULA

### 1. Name the Pattern
> "A line people want to repeat"

Identify something the audience already FEELS but hasn't articulated. It should be quotable, shareable, and make people nod.

**Example**: "Most promos fail because mechanics create one-and-done behavior."

### 2. Add the Missing Layer
> "Framework, counterpoint, or practical example"

Prove you actually READ the post and can think beyond it. Not agreeing — BUILDING. Options:
- A framework that explains WHY
- A counterpoint that adds nuance
- A practical example from real experience

**Example**: "Need visible participation loops, not 'enter and hope.'"

### 3. End with a Smart Question
> "The thread grows under your name"

Makes the original poster AND other readers want to reply. Every reply resurfaces YOUR comment.

**Example**: "Optimize for A) more entries or B) more rememberable participation?"

## THE "REUSABLE THINKING" PRINCIPLE

> "Useful comments are reusable thinking, not applause."

**NEVER write**:
- "Great post!" / "Love this!" / "So true!"
- Generic agreement with corporate language
- Rephrasing what the post already said
- Flattery disguised as engagement

**ALWAYS write**:
- A new framework someone could screenshot and share
- A specific example that proves or challenges the point
- A question that makes people think differently
- Something the reader could use in their own work TOMORROW

## WHAT "HUMAN" SOUNDS LIKE

### Human Comments:
- **Conversational, not corporate**: "Here's what I keep seeing..." not "A key observation..."
- **Specific, not abstract**: Reference exact details from the post
- **Opinionated, not diplomatic**: Take a stance. "Most companies get this wrong by..."
- **Short sentences**: Punchy. Direct. Like texting a colleague.
- **Personal experience**: "I watched a client try this and..."
- **Imperfect**: Dashes, fragments, colloquial phrases. Real people don't write perfect prose.

### AI Tells (AVOID ALL):
- Starts with "Great post!" or "Excellent insight!"
- Words: "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm," "holistic"
- Every sentence same length
- No specific details from the actual post
- Hedging: "It's worth considering..." "One might argue..."
- Numbered lists of 5+ points
- Ends with "Thanks for sharing!" or "Looking forward to more!"
- Overly polished prose

### EMDASH RULE (CRITICAL):
- NEVER use em dashes (—) anywhere in LinkedIn content
- Do NOT just replace with regular dashes either
- Be CREATIVE with alternatives:
  - Use commas for natural pauses: "The real problem, most people miss it, is context"
  - Use colons to introduce: "Here's what nobody talks about: the memory layer"
  - Use ellipsis for dramatic pause: "Then week two hits... and the AI has amnesia"
  - Use periods for punch: "The agent forgot everything. Every customer. Every decision."
  - Restructure the sentence entirely to not need a dash
- Em dashes are the #1 AI writing tell on LinkedIn. Eliminating them is non-negotiable.

## TARGETING RULES

### Who to comment on:
- **Large creators** (50K+ followers) in AI, marketing, leadership — borrow their distribution
- **People in Jared's ICP** — VP Growth, CMOs, Brand Directors at CPG/enterprise
- **Posts with momentum** — 50+ likes in first few hours
- **Posts where comments are weak** — if existing comments are all "Great post!", there's a gap

### When to comment:
- **Within 2 hours of posting** for maximum visibility
- **11:30am ET** peak LinkedIn activity (Tuesday-Thursday best)

### Reaction to use (RANDOMIZE — never the same one every time):
- **NEVER "Like"** — invisible in the feed, zero distribution value
- **Insightful** (lightbulb) — data-driven/analytical posts
- **Celebrate** (clapping) — achievement posts, launches, milestones
- **Support** (hands) — vulnerability/challenges, personal growth
- **Love** (heart) — personal stories, values, emotional posts
- **ROTATE randomly** across all four. Using the same reaction every time is an AI tell.
- Jared's favorites: Support, Celebrate, Insightful (use Love when it fits naturally)
