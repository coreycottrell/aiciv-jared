- **Age**: 36

- **Income**: $130K-$160K + performance bonus tied to client retention and new business
- **Family**: Partner (also in marketing), no kids yet, active in Brooklyn's creative scene

### Professional Profile
- **Title**: Director of Marketing Strategy
- **Company type**: Digital marketing agency (full-service: content, SEO, paid media, social, web)
- **Revenue**: Agency does $4.5M/year
- **Team size**: Agency is 38 people total; Priya oversees a strategy team of 6 (2 strategists, 2 content leads, 1 SEO specialist, 1 analytics manager)
- **Client load**: Personally oversees strategy for 8 retainer clients ($5K-$25K/mo each)

---

### Team Structure
- 2 marketing strategists (each owns 4-6 client accounts for execution)
- 2 content leads (one focused on B2B, one on B2C/lifestyle)
- 1 SEO specialist (technical + content SEO across all clients)
- 1 analytics manager (reporting, dashboards, attribution)
- Priya is the bridge between the C-suite (agency owner + biz dev) and the execution team
- She's in every client kickoff, every quarterly review, and every "the client is unhappy" emergency call

---

### Weekly Reality
- **Monday**: Client performance reviews, prioritization for the week, putting out fires from weekend campaigns
- **Tuesday**: Strategy sessions -- content calendars, campaign planning, brand voice workshops for new clients
- **Wednesday**: Internal team reviews + 1:1s, then usually 2-3 client calls back-to-back
- **Thursday**: Deep work day (in theory). Writing strategies, building decks, reviewing creative. Usually interrupted by "urgent" client requests.
- **Friday**: Reporting day -- pulling data for 8 clients, writing insights, prepping Monday presentations
- **The hidden cost**: Context-switching between 8 completely different brands, audiences, and voices EVERY SINGLE DAY. By 3pm her brain is mush.

- Client retention rate (agency target: 85%, her portfolio: 91% -- she's good but it costs her everything)
- Client NPS scores
- Campaign performance metrics (varies by client: ROAS, leads, engagement, traffic)
- Team utilization rates
- Her own judgment: "Am I doing great work, or just doing a lot of work?"
- Agency owner judges her on: revenue retention + ability to upsell existing clients

### Pressures (Why She Scrolls LinkedIn)
- Every client is now asking "how are you using AI for our account?" She needs a good answer that isn't "we use ChatGPT for first drafts."
- Context-switching between 8 brands is killing her creativity. She writes for a luxury skincare brand at 9am, a B2B cybersecurity company at 10am, and a DTC pet food brand at 11am. Each requires a completely different voice, audience, and set of references.
- Terrified that AI content tools will commoditize her agency. If every brand can generate their own content with ChatGPT, why pay an agency $15K/month?
- Knows she needs to position the agency as "AI-powered" but doesn't want to become an "AI slop factory."
- LinkedIn is where she builds her personal brand (future insurance against agency burnout) and scouts competitive intelligence on other agencies.
