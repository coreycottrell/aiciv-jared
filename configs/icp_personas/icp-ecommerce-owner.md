# ICP Profile: E-commerce Brand Owner (Shopify/DTC)

## "Olivia Park"

---

### Demographics
- **Age**: 31
- **Location**: Portland, OR
- **Education**: BA in Marketing from University of Oregon, self-taught in Shopify, Klaviyo, and Meta Ads
- **Income**: $120K-$200K (salary + profit distributions, fluctuates wildly by season)
- **Family**: Long-term partner (works in tech), no kids, rescue cat named Pixel. Social life revolves around the Portland DTC/startup community.

---

### Professional Profile
- **Title**: Founder & CEO
- **Company type**: DTC skincare brand -- clean, sustainable, mid-premium positioning ($35-$85 products)
- **Revenue**: $1.8M trailing 12 months (up from $900K previous year). Profitable but margins are thin after COGS, shipping, and ad spend.
- **Team size**: 6 people (Olivia, 1 marketing manager, 1 customer service rep, 1 fulfillment coordinator, 1 product development contractor, 1 part-time bookkeeper)
- **Channels**: Shopify DTC (70%), Amazon (20%), wholesale to boutiques (10%)

---

### Team Structure
- Marketing manager handles day-to-day social media, email flows (Klaviyo), and basic ad management
- Customer service rep manages Gorgias inbox (80-120 tickets/day), returns, and review responses
- Fulfillment coordinator works with 3PL, manages inventory levels, handles shipping issues
- Product development contractor formulates new products (quarterly launches)
- Part-time bookkeeper does monthly reconciliation, inventory costing
- Olivia does: strategy, brand voice, Meta/Google Ads optimization, influencer partnerships, product photography direction, investor conversations, and whatever falls through the cracks

---

### Weekly Reality
- **Monday**: Check weekend sales (Shopify dashboard ritual), review ad performance (Meta Ads Manager + Google Ads), plan the week's content
- **Tuesday**: Product content day -- approve social posts, review email flows, brief the marketing manager on campaign messaging, respond to influencer DMs
- **Wednesday**: Operations day -- inventory review, 3PL check-in, review customer service trends (are complaints about the new moisturizer increasing?), Amazon listing optimization
- **Thursday**: Strategy/growth day -- analyze customer data, review subscription metrics, plan next product launch, sometimes meet with potential wholesale accounts
- **Friday**: Financial review, cash flow planning, responding to everything that piled up, maybe a podcast interview or content creation
- **The reality**: She's in Slack, email, and Shopify 14 hours/day. Every decision from ad creative to shipping carrier is hers. She's the CEO, CMO, and COO simultaneously.

---

### What She's Judged On
- Revenue growth (investors/advisors care about this)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) -- currently $38, needs to be under $30 to hit margin targets
- Customer lifetime value (LTV) -- $127 average, wants to push to $160+ through subscriptions
- Return rate (currently 4.2%, industry avg is 6%, she's proud of this)
- Email revenue percentage (target: 30% of total, currently 22%)
- Her own measure: "Am I building a real brand or just running a sophisticated ad arbitrage operation?"

---

### Pressures (Why She Scrolls LinkedIn)
- Meta Ads costs keep rising. Her ROAS dropped from 4.2x to 3.1x in the last 6 months. She needs to diversify acquisition channels but doesn't know where to start.
- Content creation is a treadmill. She needs 30-50 pieces of content per week across TikTok, Instagram, email, and product listings. Quality is slipping because volume demands are insane.
- Customer service responses are inconsistent. Her CS rep is great but overwhelmed. Response time has crept from 2 hours to 8 hours. Customers notice.
- Amazon is cannibalizing her DTC margins but she can't afford to leave the platform.
- Sees other DTC founders talking about "AI-powered customer insights" and "AI content generation" but everything she's tried produces generic content that doesn't match her brand.
- LinkedIn is where she networks with other DTC founders, follows e-commerce thought leaders, and learns from brands ahead of her.

---

### "Act Now" Moments (Buying Triggers)
1. **Launches a new product** and needs 40+ pieces of content (product descriptions, email sequences, social posts, ad copy, influencer briefs) in 2 weeks. The marketing manager is drowning.
2. **Customer data reveals a pattern** she missed for 3 months -- customers who buy the serum AND the cleanser have 2.3x higher LTV. She thinks: "Why didn't I know this sooner? Why isn't something flagging these patterns automatically?"
3. **A negative review goes viral on TikTok** and her customer service rep doesn't catch it for 12 hours. By then, there are 200 comments. Olivia spends the entire day in crisis management mode.
4. **Her best-performing email flow** was written 8 months ago. She knows it needs updating but has no time. When she asks ChatGPT to rewrite it, the output sounds nothing like her brand.
5. **A competitor brand** (same niche, similar products, launched 6 months after her) passes her in revenue. Their secret: "We use AI for everything -- content, customer service, inventory forecasting." Olivia feels the competitive pressure physically.

- Running a $3M+ brand that "punches above its weight" in content quality and customer experience

- "I need something that works with my existing stack -- Shopify, Klaviyo, Gorgias, Meta Ads. Not another standalone tool."
- "My customers can SMELL AI-generated content. If the emails feel robotic, my open rates will tank."

- "Is this just ChatGPT with a skin? What am I paying for that I can't get from a $20/month subscription?"
- Price: $197/mo is meaningful but justifiable if she can see ROI in content output. $579/mo requires proving it saves more than the equivalent of 5 hours of her time (at her effective $100/hr rate).

### The Offer-Positioning That Lands

**DON'T say**: "PureBrain is an AI content generation platform for e-commerce brands."

**DO say**: "Your AI partner knows your brand voice, your customer data, your product catalog, and your competitive landscape. When you need 40 pieces of launch content in 2 weeks, it doesn't produce generic DTC copy -- it produces YOUR copy. When customer sentiment shifts, it flags the pattern before it becomes a crisis. It's the team member who knows your brand as well as you do and works 24 hours a day."

**The frame**: PureBrain isn't replacing Olivia's creativity -- it's multiplying it. She still sets the vision and voice. The AI handles the volume, the pattern recognition, and the 3am customer service response that sounds exactly like her brand.
