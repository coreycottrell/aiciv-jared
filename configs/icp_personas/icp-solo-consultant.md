# ICP Profile: Solo Consultant / Fractional Executive

## "David Kim"

---

### Demographics
- **Age**: 47
- **Location**: Charlotte, NC (but works with clients nationwide, mostly remote)
- **Education**: MBA from Duke Fuqua, Six Sigma Black Belt, PMP
- **Income**: $250K-$400K (varies by year and client load)
- **Family**: Divorced, two teenagers (shared custody). Freedom and flexibility are non-negotiable -- he left corporate specifically for this.

---

### Professional Profile
- **Title**: Fractional COO / Operations Consultant
- **Company type**: Solo practice (LLC). Serves as fractional COO for 3-4 companies simultaneously.
- **Revenue**: His own practice does $300K-$400K/year
- **Client companies**: Mid-market companies ($10M-$100M revenue) that need operational leadership but can't justify a full-time COO
- **Engagement model**: Typically 10-15 hours/week per client, $250-$350/hour, 6-12 month engagements

---

### Team Structure
- It's just him. That's the point and the problem.
- Uses a VA (Philippines-based, 20 hours/week) for scheduling, expense reports, and basic research
- Occasionally subcontracts to other fractional executives when he finds adjacent needs at a client
- Has a loose "brain trust" of 5-6 other fractional executives he texts for advice

---

### Weekly Reality
- **Monday**: Deep day with Client A (manufacturing company, $35M revenue). Reviews operational dashboards, meets with their leadership team, works on their supply chain optimization project.
- **Tuesday**: Split between Client B (SaaS company, $12M ARR) in the morning and Client C (professional services firm, $22M) in the afternoon. Two completely different worlds.
- **Wednesday**: Client D (e-commerce brand, $8M) plus admin day -- invoicing, proposals for new clients, LinkedIn content.
- **Thursday**: Follow-up calls, document writing, strategy work. His "thinking day" that keeps shrinking.
- **Friday**: New business development + the work that didn't fit anywhere else.
- **The hidden killer**: He maintains 4 completely separate mental models simultaneously. Every client has different systems, different team dynamics, different metrics, different politics. The context-switching is BRUTAL.

---

### What He's Judged On
- By clients: Speed of impact. "What did David improve in the first 90 days?"
- By himself: Revenue per hour (wants to stay above $300/hr effective rate), client retention, referral rate
- By the market: His reputation is his business. One bad engagement and the referral network dries up.
- Privately: Whether he can maintain this pace for another 10 years, or if he needs to build something more scalable

---

### Pressures (Why He Scrolls LinkedIn)
- LinkedIn IS his sales channel. 80% of his clients come from LinkedIn content + referrals. He posts 2-3x/week because he has to, not because he wants to.
- Feels the Context Tax more acutely than almost anyone. He walks into Client A on Monday morning and has to mentally reconstruct where they left off last Tuesday. His notes help, but the cognitive load is enormous.
- Uses ChatGPT heavily but is FRUSTRATED. Every client conversation starts from zero. He's tried creating separate GPTs for each client -- it works okay but the quality ceiling is low and maintenance is annoying.
- Sees younger consultants using AI aggressively and worries about being commoditized. His edge is 20 years of experience -- but if AI can give a 28-year-old consultant 80% of his judgment, his rates are in danger.
- Knows he needs to "scale himself" but doesn't want to hire employees (tried it, hated managing people, went back to solo).
