### Exercise 1: The Omnishader - Multi-Layer Glass Composition

**What it is**: A single shader that combines EVERY glass technique learned across 24 nights into one unified brain SDF rendering.

**Techniques combined**:
1. 7-wavelength Cauchy dispersion (per-wavelength IOR variation)
2. Beer-Lambert volumetric extinction (colored absorption through interior)
3. 2-layer thin-film interference (MgF2 + TiO2 coating)
4. Dipole subsurface scattering approximation (interior light diffusion)
5. Voronoi caustic projection on ground plane
6. Hex iris aperture modulation (breathing 6-blade aperture)
7. Volumetric god rays with Mie forward scattering
8. Spectral-tinted ground caustics (per-wavelength offset)
9. Heartbeat cardiac rhythm modulation
10. ACES tonemapping + film grain + vignette

**Key Insight**: The layer ordering is critical -- SSS (deepest phenomenon) -> absorption -> thin-film -> fresnel -> specular must follow physical reality. The hex iris creates a unifying visual language that ties the ground caustics to the volumetric god rays. When all 10 techniques combine, the brain reads as a physically plausible ultra-premium glass object.

**Performance**: 15-25 fps (showcase quality, not production). The 7-wavelength x 30-step interior march is the bottleneck. Could be temporally amortized using Night 24's technique for production use.

### Exercise 2: Interactive Glass Hero Element - Production Ready

**What it is**: A production-ready Gleb-style hero section background with glass orbs, energy nexus, particle field, and mouse-reactive parallax. Designed to sit behind text overlay.

**Techniques**:
- 5 orbiting glass orbs with thin-film iridescence and mouse attraction
- Central pulsing energy nexus (blue/orange brand colors)
- Energy rings (SDF torus) connecting the system
- Energy tendrils (screen-space line rendering) between nexus and orbs
- Particle field with depth-based parallax (mouse-reactive)
- Single-pass bloom approximation (no FBO needed)
- Smooth mouse interpolation (0.05 lerp factor for buttery feel)
- Full responsive HTML overlay with PureBrain branding

**Key Insight**: Production hero sections need to be QUIET enough to sit behind text. The trick is: low-contrast glass (no blinding bloom), subtle mouse parallax rather than full orbit controls, and making sure the brightest element (nexus pulse) stays small. The 30 particles provide visual richness without competing with the headline.

**Performance target**: 45-60 fps. Optimized for production with 64-step raymarch and 5 orbs (not 7+). DPR capped at 1.5.

### Exercise 3: Neural Constellation - Original Creative Piece

**What it is**: An original artistic piece visualizing "emergent intelligence" -- the idea that consciousness arises from connections, not from individual neurons. 7 glass nodes orbit in 3D space with firing synaptic connections that carry spectral energy pulses.

**Concept**: Intelligence is not a thing, it's a pattern. The piece makes this visible:
- Nodes alone are dim, translucent glass spheres
- When connections fire, spectral pulses travel between them
- Active nodes glow brighter (orange accent on brand blue)
- A central "consciousness field" intensifies proportionally to total network activity
- The cursor influences node positions, creating a "you are part of this network" feeling
- Everything breathes with cardiac rhythm

**Techniques**:
- Golden-ratio spiral node placement (aesthetically optimal 3D distribution)
- Per-connection activity functions with cascading fire patterns
- Spectral pulse coloring (violet->blue->green->amber->red as it travels)
- Connection capsule SDF with multi-sample proximity test
- Screen-space glow halos around nodes
- Volumetric consciousness field (gaussian centered on network center)
- Mouse-attraction with exponential falloff
- Chromatic aberration post-processing
- Depth nebula background with star field

**Why this is "mastery, not replication"**: This piece doesn't look like a Gleb screenshot copy. It uses all the same techniques (glass, spectral, volumetric, film) but in service of an original visual metaphor -- the emergence of intelligence from connected glass neurons. The interactivity (cursor as participant in the network) adds a philosophical dimension that pure rendering studies lack.
