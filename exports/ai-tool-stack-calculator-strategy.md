## Optimization Notes

### A/B Test Opportunities
1. **Default preset**: "Startup Stack" is the default (pre-selected). Test "Enterprise Stack" as default for B2B audiences — higher total → bigger savings demonstration
2. **CTA copy**: "Start Your AI Partnership" vs "Replace Your Stack Today" vs "See How It Works"
3. **Savings threshold**: Currently shows confetti when their stack > $299. Could also show a "warning" animation when stack hits $150 (halfway point) to build anticipation

### Conversion Boosters to Add Later
- "Share my stack" button (generates a shareable image or URL with their selection)
- Testimonial blurb below the calculator from someone who switched
- "Annual savings" calculator variant (multiply monthly by 12)
- Exit-intent popup triggered when user tries to leave: "You're paying $X/month. See if PureBrain fits."

### Tracking (GA4 Events to Set Up)
- `calculator_tool_toggled` — fires when any tool is turned on/off
- `calculator_preset_applied` — fires with preset name
- `calculator_savings_threshold_crossed` — fires when total > $299 (high-intent signal)
- `calculator_cta_clicked` — fires on "Start Your AI Partnership" click
- `calculator_total_reached` — fires with ranges ($0-$99, $100-$299, $300-$499, $500+)

## Key Messaging Notes

### The Strategist Is the Hook
The single most powerful comparison is human strategist vs PureBrain. When someone selects "Human Strategist (light)" at $1,500/month, their total immediately jumps past $299 by $1,200+. The savings number becomes undeniable.

Lead with this in sales conversations: "What do you currently pay for strategic guidance?"

### "Connects them" Is the Differentiator
The calculator shows COST. The differentiator is context. The copy note at the bottom covers this:
> "PureBrain doesn't just replace these tools — it connects them. One AI that knows your business, remembers your preferences, and improves over time."

This should be emphasized in every social post and email that promotes the calculator.

### Free Tools Are Intentional
DeepSeek ($0) and NotebookLM ($0) are included. This builds credibility — the calculator isn't trying to make every tool look expensive. It's honest. This increases trust in the overall comparison.
