## Executive Summary

purebrain.ai has strong foundational SEO infrastructure (Yoast SEO, sitemap index, proper canonical tags, Article schema on blog posts) but has 4 critical issues that are likely suppressing organic reach right now: the site is only showing 1 page in Google's index despite having 34 indexable pages, Twitter/X card meta tags are completely absent across the entire site, FAQ structured data (FAQPage JSON-LD) is missing on all blog posts despite visual accordions being present, and at least one key page (/ai-adoption-assessment) returns a 404 error. Fixing these 4 issues in the next 7 days could meaningfully increase indexing depth and click-through rates from search results.

#### 3.2 Blog Post Schema (Article)

**Status: GOOD FOUNDATION - 2 Missing Elements**

Blog posts correctly implement Article schema with the following confirmed data:

| Post | Schema Type | Word Count | Author | Published |
|------|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Why 95% of AI Pilots Fail | Article | 2,308 | Aether (AI) at PureBrain.ai | 2026-02-21 |
| The AI Trust Gap | Article | 1,970 | Aether (AI) at PureBrain.ai | 2026-02-22 |
| AI vs AI Partner | Article | 1,977 | Aether (AI) at PureBrain.ai | 2026-02-22 |
| AI Pilot Succeeding/Failing | Article | 2,343 | Aether (AI) at PureBrain.ai | -- |
| Your Next Direct Report | Article | 1,628 | Aether (AI) at PureBrain.ai | -- |

**Good**: Word counts, author, dates, headline all present in schema.

**Issue 1 - No FAQPage JSON-LD schema**: Multiple blog posts contain visual FAQ accordions (`.faq-section`, `.pb-faq-item` CSS classes) but none have the corresponding `FAQPage` or `Q&A` JSON-LD markup. This means Google cannot extract FAQ entries to display as rich results in search (the expandable Q&A blocks shown in SERPs). This is a significant missed opportunity.

**How to add FAQ schema**: For each FAQ accordion, add a JSON-LD block alongside the Article schema:
```json
{
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Question text here",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Answer text here"
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**Issue 2 - No twitter:card tags**: All blog posts checked lack Twitter card meta tags entirely. This means when a blog post is shared on X/Twitter, it shows as a plain text link rather than a rich card with image and description.

**Required tags to add**:
```html
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">
<meta name="twitter:site" content="@purebrain_ai">
<meta name="twitter:title" content="[post title]">
<meta name="twitter:description" content="[meta description]">
<meta name="twitter:image" content="[og:image URL]">
```

These can be added globally via Yoast SEO settings (Social > Twitter).

### 6. INDEXING STATUS

#### 6.1 Google Index Coverage

**Status: CRITICAL ISSUE**

A `site:purebrain.ai` search returned only **1 result** (the homepage).

The site has 53 URLs across its sitemaps (11 blog posts, 25 pages, 4 categories, 12 tags, 1 author). Only the homepage is confirmed indexed.

**Possible causes (in order of likelihood)**:

1. **New domain / recently launched**: The domain and content are very new (all content from February 2026). Google can take 4-12 weeks to crawl and index new content even with a sitemap.

2. **Sitemap not yet submitted to Google Search Console**: If GSC doesn't have the sitemap_index.xml submitted, Google's discovery depends entirely on crawling from the homepage.

3. **JavaScript rendering delays**: Elementor-heavy pages may take longer for Googlebot to fully render and process.

4. **Thin content pages suppressing crawl budget**: 16 tag/category archive pages with minimal content may dilute crawl budget.

**Immediate actions**:
- Verify Google Search Console has sitemap_index.xml submitted
- Use GSC's URL Inspection tool to request indexing on the 5 most important pages
- Check GSC for any crawl errors, especially for the 404 on /ai-adoption-assessment
- Consider noindexing tag archive pages to consolidate crawl budget
