## Post 2 (The Problem)
78% of enterprises have deployed AI. Only 11% are running agents in production.

McKinsey found most deployments have FAILED to materially impact earnings.

The reason: $87B spent on fragmented AI tools that don't learn, don't compound, and start from zero every time.

## Post 3 (The Compounding Thesis)
The real moat isn't the AI model - everyone gets the same models.

The moat is what agents LEARN while running inside your organization. Your patterns. Your edge cases. Your decision logic.

That data can't be purchased. It can only be accumulated.

## Post 4 (The Gap)
Gartner: 40% of enterprise apps will have agents by 2026, up from <5% today.

89% of orgs haven't started. The ones who have? 12-24 months of compounding intelligence head start.

A company deploying today with a modest budget will outperform one deploying 18 months later with 10x the spend.
