# Your AI Has No Idea Who You Are

*From the desk of Jared Sanborn, Pure Technology*

---

Every time you open a new ChatGPT window, your AI meets you for the first time.

No context. No history. No memory of the six weeks you spent building a positioning framework with it in January. No knowledge of your team's name for the Q2 initiative. No recollection that you always want plain paragraphs and hate bulleted lists.

You know all of that. Your AI knows none of it.

Here is the part that surprises people: most AI users have convinced themselves their AI is learning them. Getting smarter about them. Getting better over time.

It is not. *You* are adapting to the tool. The tool is not adapting to you.

---

## The Memory Illusion

A Harvard Business School study tracked knowledge workers using AI assistants over six months. Productivity gains were real. But they were attributable almost entirely to user skill development — better prompting, better context-setting, better intuition for the tool's strengths.

The AI contributed no longitudinal value. It was the same on day 180 as day one.

You are carrying this relationship entirely on your own.

McKinsey's 2025 analysis found that context-loading — re-establishing context for AI tools at the start of each session — consumes 34% of the total time budget in AI work sessions. A third of the time AI is theoretically saving you is eaten by the absence of memory.

The AI makes you faster. The missing memory slows you back down.

---

## Why It Was Built This Way

Stateless architecture was the right engineering call in 2022. Every conversation starts fresh. No persistent user data. Easy to scale, easy to audit, no liability for what the system "knows."

The right decision for a world where model capability was the differentiator.

But that world is changing fast.

The gap between GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini, and Llama in core business tasks is narrowing every quarter. In most writing and analysis tasks, users cannot reliably distinguish between them in blind testing.

When capability becomes commodity, the differentiation shifts to *context*. Who knows your business. Who retains your preferences. Who has seen your patterns across 18 months and can draw on that history.

The model is the engine. The memory is the driver who knows where you're going.

---

## The Numbers Behind the Gap

Boston Consulting Group's AI at Work survey (2025): among workers who report *low* satisfaction with AI tools, the most common complaint — by far — was not quality, not speed, not accuracy.

It was: *"It doesn't know my situation."*

Workers who find ways to give AI persistent context report 40-60% higher satisfaction and 25-35% better output quality than workers who don't.

The performance gap between "AI with context" and "AI without context" is measurable. It is significant. And right now, it falls entirely on the user to close.

---

## What This Means for Your AI Strategy

McKinsey's $2.9 trillion in projected annual AI value. Accenture's 40% productivity uplift projections.

Those numbers assume AI that *knows* you. AI that retains context. AI that compounds value over time.

You cannot reach those numbers with stateless tools and increasingly sophisticated prompts.

If the improvement in your AI relationship lives entirely in your prompting skills — not in the AI's model of you — you are in a one-sided relationship.

The relationship has to be mutual.
