Section 01

Why Delegation Matters

  • Most people use their AI. Power users delegate to it.
  • The difference: asking your AI to do something vs giving it ownership
  • Delegation = compound returns. Every task you delegate once saves you hundreds of hours over time.
  • A CEO does not do the work — a CEO defines the outcome and trusts the team to deliver
  • Your AI is your team. Treat it like one.
The #1 skill that separates casual users from power users is NOT better prompts — it's better delegation.

Section 02

The CLEAR Framework

  • CContext — Give background. What's the situation? What have you tried? What matters?
  • LLimitations — Set boundaries. Budget, timeline, tone, things to avoid.
  • EExamples — Show what good looks like. Past work, competitors, reference material.
  • AAudience — Who is this for? Customers, investors, your team, the public?
  • RResult — Define the outcome. What does "done" look like? Be specific.
Every delegation that fails can be traced back to a missing CLEAR element. Before you delegate, run through all five. It takes 30 seconds and saves hours.

Section 04

Context Loading — Feed Your AI

  • Your AI is only as good as the context you give it
  • Share your business docs, SOPs, brand voice, past decisions, client history
  • The more your AI knows, the less you have to explain per task

Without Context

"Write me a follow-up email to a client." — Generic, robotic, needs 3 rounds of revision. You spend more time editing than writing it yourself.

With Context

"Follow up with Sarah from Apex Corp. She was interested in our enterprise tier. Use my casual-professional tone. Reference our last call about their Q3 timeline." — Done in one shot.

Context is an investment, not overhead. Every piece of context you load today saves you explanation time on every future task.

Section 05

Ownership vs Instructions

  • Stop giving step-by-step instructions. Start giving outcomes.
  • When you micromanage the how, you cap your AI at your own efficiency
  • When you delegate the outcome, your AI can surprise you with better approaches

Instructions (Linear)

"Write a 500-word blog post about X. Use Y format. Start with a hook. Include 3 subheadings. End with a CTA." — You're doing the thinking. Your AI is just typing.

Ownership (Exponential)

"I need a blog post about X. You know my voice, my audience, and what performs. Own it." — Your AI applies everything it's learned about what works for YOU.

Instructions scale linearly. Ownership scales exponentially. The best delegation sounds like you're talking to a trusted VP, not reading from a script.

Recurring Delegation

  • Set-and-forget tasks your AI handles automatically
  • The "Delegate Once" principle: If you do it more than twice, delegate it permanently
  • Your AI gets better each time it runs a recurring task — it learns your patterns
● Morning Briefings — Daily status on your business, inbox, and priorities
● Email Drafts — Pre-written responses waiting for your approval
● Meeting Prep — Background research and talking points before every call
● Social Content — Drafted posts aligned with your brand calendar
● Client Follow-Ups — Timely touchpoints that never fall through the cracks
● Weekly Reports — Metrics, highlights, and action items compiled automatically

Section 07

The 4 Delegation Killers

✗ Over-Explaining — Micromanaging your AI with step-by-step instructions. Trust the context you've built. Let it execute.
✗ Under-Explaining — Vague requests with no context. "Write something good" is not delegation. Use the CLEAR framework.
✗ Not Checking Output — Trust but verify. Review the first 2-3 outputs of any new delegation. Then let it run.
✗ Doing It Yourself — The biggest waste. If your AI can do 80% of a task, delegate 100% and edit the last 20%.
The fix for all four: Start with CLEAR, give ownership not instructions, verify early, then trust the process. Your AI improves every single time.
