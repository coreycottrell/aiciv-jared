## Core Teaching

AI output quality is a two-way street. Most underperformance traces back to how the human frames tasks, not AI capability. This session introduced a 5-question self-assessment framework to reveal gaps in how humans partner with their AI -- gaps invisible when only evaluating AI output.

## Key Concepts

This module marked a format shift from presentation-based tips to participative self-assessment. Jared introduced a fundamental reframe: stop evaluating your AI and start evaluating yourself as an AI partner. The core distinction is between giving instructions (step-by-step micromanagement) versus describing outcomes (destination-based delegation). Partners who describe where they want to go and let AI find the path unlock exponentially more value than those who dictate every step. A second critical distinction is memory as learning versus memory as storage -- if a fresh AI instance loaded with your same files would feel identical to your current AI, then your AI has not actually learned, it has only stored. True partnership means AI accumulates inferred understanding across sessions that transcends explicit data. The session also demonstrated that domain expertise is a compounding advantage: specialists who teach their AI their field create capabilities no generalist can replicate.

## Specific Techniques

1. **5-Question Partnership Self-Assessment**: A framework of 5 questions designed to reveal gaps in how you partner with AI, including: Are you giving instructions or describing outcomes? What has your AI learned about you that you never explicitly taught it?
2. **Instructions vs Outcomes Spectrum**: Use detailed instructions only for complex builds; for routine and general direction work, describe the destination and let AI navigate
3. **Memory as Learning vs Storage Test**: If a fresh AI with your files feels identical to your current one, your memory system is storage, not learning
4. **Mirror Test**: Before criticizing AI output, evaluate your own input quality first -- most output issues trace back to how the human frames the task
5. **Domain Expert Acceleration**: Give AI 3-4 exemplars of world-class work in your specialty and let it self-design a training curriculum
6. **Collaborative Structure Design**: Instead of imposing organizational structures (e.g. Google Drive layout), ask your AI to co-design them -- results often exceed what you would have designed alone
7. **Outcome-Based Delegation**: For skill training, provide exemplars and desired end state, then let AI build its own path to mastery
