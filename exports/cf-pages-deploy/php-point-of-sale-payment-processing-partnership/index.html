The Real Problem

PHP POS Merchants Face 6 Payment Headaches — Every Day

Your software simplifies sales. But payment processing is still a mess for most of your customers. Here’s exactly what they’re dealing with right now.

💸
Hidden Transaction Fees
Most small retailers on PHP POS are on flat-rate pricing. They’re paying 2.6–2.9% per transaction when interchange-plus would save them 0.3–0.8% — money that disappears silently, every sale.
A boutique doing $15K/mo loses ~$1,080/year in excess fees
🔀
Multi-Processor Fragmentation
PHP POS integrates with Stripe, Braintree, and PHP POS Payments. Great flexibility — but merchants managing two processors end up with split reconciliation, mismatched reports, and accounting headaches.
Average 2.4 hrs/week lost to manual reconciliation
🛡️
PCI Compliance Confusion
Your merchants are small business owners — not IT professionals. PCI compliance requirements are opaque, annual questionnaires are confusing, and non-compliance fees from processors can hit $20–$100/month before anyone notices.
67% of small retailers are unknowingly non-compliant
⚠️
Chargebacks They Can’t Fight
Mobile repair shops, boutiques, and specialty retailers face above-average chargeback rates. Without dedicated dispute management, merchants lose most chargebacks by default — simply because they don’t have time to respond with the right evidence.
Default loss rate without representation: 78%
🛒
Ecommerce Payment Silos
PHP POS connects to Shopify and WooCommerce — but in-store Stripe/Braintree transactions and online payments live in completely separate dashboards. No unified view means guessing at true business performance.
In-store + online reconciliation: 4+ hrs/month wasted
📉
No Payment Intelligence
PHP POS gives merchants great sales and inventory reporting. But payment-specific metrics — authorization decline rates, card type mix, peak-hour processing patterns — are invisible. Blind spots in payment data mean missed revenue.
Avg. decline rate for small retailers: 8–12% (fixable)
