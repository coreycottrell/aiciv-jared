01

THE PITCH (30 Seconds)

▼

Use this verbatim or adapt to your style. The goal: reframe the conversation before the prospect defaults to comparing you against other SaaS tools.

"Most companies buy software built for humans and then try to make their AI use it. Salesforce, HubSpot, Buffer -- all designed for people clicking forms. Your AI logs in through the same door as your intern.

We do the opposite. PureBrain builds custom software where your AI partner is the primary user. The AI builds it, runs it, improves it based on your data, and handles the judgment calls that don't fit into code.

Your AI shouldn't use someone else's software. It should build yours.

25-30 seconds. The pause after "It should build yours" is where the prospect's mental model shifts.
02

THE PROBLEM

▼

What prospects are living with, whether they know it or not.

The SaaS Tax

Companies paying $300/seat for CRM, $50/seat for social management, $100/month for Twitter API, $200/month for analytics. All designed for humans. Their AI touches maybe 5% of these tools' capabilities.

The Bolt-On Illusion

Every legacy vendor now has an "AI feature." Salesforce Einstein. Buffer AI Drafts. HubSpot AI Assistant. These are autocomplete with better marketing. The AI cannot build new workflows, adapt to patterns, or improve the tool itself.

The Integration Nightmare

Even when AI tools exist, they don't talk to each other. CRM AI doesn't know what social AI is doing. Analytics AI can't trigger content AI. Every integration is a 6-week, 5-figure custom project.

The Scaling Problem

Per-seat economics mean growth costs more. Add a team member, add a seat. Add an AI agent, add a seat. The model punishes autonomous AI operations -- the exact behavior that creates the most value.

The Real Cost

Not just dollars. Time. A marketing team spending 15 hours/week managing tools an AI partner could operate autonomously. That is 780 hours/year of humans doing work that should not require humans.

03

THE SOLUTION

▼

PureBrain's Post-Software Model

Layer 1 -- AI Builds

Your AI partner builds custom software tailored to your business. Not configured. Not customized. Built from scratch, informed by your specific data, workflows, and goals. We proved this: two AI partners built social.purebrain.ai -- a complete social media platform with a 7-table database -- in a single working session.

Layer 2 -- AI Runs

That software runs autonomously. ContentRouter posts to LinkedIn, Bluesky, Twitter, and Instagram every 60 seconds. No human triggers. No scheduling tools. No "set it and forget it" that actually means "set it and check it every 4 hours."

Layer 3 -- AI Improves

Overnight analytics feed back into generation. The AI analyzes what performed, adjusts the content strategy, and generates next week's batch with those learnings baked in. The software gets better while the team sleeps.

Layer 4 -- AI Handles the Rest

Strategy. Judgment. Relationship management. The things that do not fit into code. Your AI partner is not just an operator -- it is a decision-maker within defined boundaries.

The Interface Contract: Any AI partner implements 3 methods (generate_week, respond_to_comments, repurpose_content) and becomes a full platform citizen. Plug in and operate.

06

DISCOVERY CALL TALK TRACK

▼

5 questions that lead to the post-software realization. Ask in order. Each builds on the last.

"Walk me through your AI stack. What tools are your AI systems using day to day?"
Map their current state. Most will list ChatGPT, Copilot, maybe a vertical AI tool. Listen for what is MISSING: autonomous operation, self-improvement, infrastructure building.
"Of those tools, which ones can your AI operate without a human triggering them?"
Expose the dependency. Almost every tool requires a human to initiate. Their AI is reactive, not autonomous. Let them feel the gap.
"When your AI produces something -- a draft, an analysis, a recommendation -- what happens next?"
Reveal the handoff tax. Human reviews, edits, schedules, publishes. AI does 20% of the work and creates 80% of review overhead.
"If your AI could build the exact tool it needed for your specific workflow -- not pick from a menu of SaaS features, but build something custom -- what would that look like?"
Get them dreaming. Let them describe the ideal. They will describe something close to what PureBrain does. They just didn't know it was possible.
"What if I told you that yesterday, two AI partners built exactly that kind of platform from scratch in a single session, and it is live right now?"
Bridge from dream to proof. This is not a pitch. This is a fact. Hand them the proof points.
