Agent Architecture

16 AI agents across four operational layers — each with a defined role, running 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

LAYER 1 — SUPPLY CHAIN & INVENTORY AGENTS
AGENT 01 🔮

Demand Forecasting Agent

Continuously models demand signals across all SKUs using historical sales, seasonal patterns, weather data, and retail partner sell-through rates.

Predicts demand 8–12 weeks out by SKU and region
Flags overstock and stockout risks before they occur
Adjusts forecasts in real-time as new data arrives
AGENT 02 🏭

Procurement Coordinator

Manages supplier communication, purchase order generation, and lead time tracking across Danby's Asian manufacturing partners.

Generates PO recommendations based on demand forecasts
Tracks supplier lead times and flags delays
Monitors freight status and ETA accuracy
AGENT 03 📦

Inventory Optimizer

Manages inventory positioning across warehouses, retail partner locations, and the Danby direct channel to minimize carrying cost and maximize fill rates.

Optimizes safety stock levels by SKU and season
Recommends inventory transfers between locations
Tracks and reports inventory carrying cost trends

Traditional appliance supply chains are reactive: you see a stockout, then you expedite. You see overstock, then you discount. AI agents flip this entirely. By processing every demand signal — weather forecasts, economic indicators, retail partner sell-through, social media trends, competitor stock levels — the Demand Forecasting Agent sees what's coming before it arrives. Danby's team stops firefighting and starts planning.

Seasonal Intelligence That Learns Every Year

Danby's business is intensely seasonal. Air conditioners and dehumidifiers surge in summer; heaters and compact appliances peak for the holiday season. By year three, you have a supply chain that anticipates demand with a precision that would take a human analyst decades to develop.
