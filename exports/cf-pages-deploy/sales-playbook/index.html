Framework
SPIN Selling — Your Secret Weapon

SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham) is the most proven B2B sales methodology ever researched. Here’s what it means and where it shows up in our process.

Phase What You’re Doing Where in Our Process
S — Situation Understand their current state. What tools do they use? What does their workflow look like? Steps 2–3: AI Tool Stack Calculator
P — Problem Reveal the gap. Surface inefficiencies they may not fully see. Steps 2–4: Calculator + Investor Intelligence + Compare
I — Implication Make the problem feel urgent. What happens if they do nothing? Step 3: Investor Intelligence — the DO OR DIE moment
N — Need-Payoff Show how PureBrain solves it. Let THEM articulate the value. Step 6: Full PureBrain walkthrough
The golden rule of SPIN: Never tell them the answer. Ask the question that leads them to it. When THEY say “this is exactly what I need,” the sale is already made.

Screen share ready — Browser open, all tabs loaded, mic tested, camera on.
The Process
The 8-Step Sales System

Follow these steps in order. Don’t skip. Don’t rush. Each step builds on the last.

1
Get Their Attention
Objective: Open the door. Simple. Direct. Genuine.
Talk Track Options

“Hey — you got a business? Want to make more money and free up some of your time?”

“Hey [name], I’ve been looking at what [Company] is doing. I think there’s a huge opportunity you might be missing out on with AI. Worth a 20-minute call?”

“Quick question — how much time does your team spend on [obvious pain point]? I’ve got something that might change that completely.”

What You’re Driving
Curiosity. Nothing more. You don’t need to explain PureBrain yet. Just get the “yeah, tell me more.”
Pro Tips
Keep it under 3 sentences in writing. You’re not pitching yet.
Specificity beats generality. If you know something about their business, reference it.
Warm intros are gold. “I was talking to [mutual connection] and they mentioned you’d be the right person…”
1.25
Research the Prospect (AI-Powered)
Objective: Know them better than they know themselves before you get on the call.
Research Brief Should Include
Company overview: industry, size, model, revenue signals
The prospect’s role and likely pain points
Tools they’re probably using (check job postings, LinkedIn, website)
Their competitors and how AI is changing their industry
3–5 specific ways PureBrain can help THEM specifically
One or two surprising insights that signal you’ve done serious homework
AI Prompt to Use

“AI: Figure out everything about [Person] at [Company]. Research their business model, team, tools, competitors, and industry trends. Then create a full plan of how PureBrain can help them specifically — go above and beyond what anyone else would offer, but make sure everything is genuinely deliverable.”

Example Output
📄PureBrain x HOVR Partnership Brief (password: hovr2026)
Why This Matters
When you show someone a custom 5-page research document about THEIR business in the first 10 minutes, trust skyrockets and the entire rest of the call shifts in your favor.
1.5
Schedule the Meeting
Objective: Lock in a real meeting. Don’t leave it open-ended.
Talk Track

“I’ve got time Thursday at 2pm EST or Friday at 10am EST — which works better for you?”

“It’ll be 20–30 minutes on Zoom. I want to show you something you can actually use during the call.”

What You’re Driving
Commitment. A booked meeting is a micro-yes. Each micro-yes makes the final close easier. Always mention you’ll be showing something interactive — it builds anticipation.
1.75
Decide When to Drop the Brief
Objective: Strategic deployment of the custom research document.
Lead with it — Build trust immediately. Open with: “Before we dive in, I put together something for you specifically…”
Hold it — Mid-call reveal when they ask “what does this look like?” Maximum impact moment.
Either Way Works
The brief exists to prove one thing: we don’t do generic. We researched YOU. That’s what makes PureBrain different from every other AI company they’ll ever talk to.
2
AI Tool Stack Calculator
Objective: Help them see their current situation clearly — and the hidden cost of it.
SPIN: S + P
🔧purebrain.ai/ai-tool-stack-calculator/
What to Do

Share your screen and go through the calculator WITH them. This is interactive. Ask questions as you go. Let THEM enter the numbers.

Talk Track

“Let’s start by mapping out what you’re already using — it’ll only take a couple minutes.”

“How many seats do you have on [tool]? Are you actually using all of them?”

“So right here — you can see what you’re spending monthly on these tools. Does that match what you expected?”

“So you’re spending $X/month and none of these tools have memory, they don’t know your team, and they start fresh every conversation?”

What You’re Driving
Awareness. They realize their current approach is fragmented, expensive, and not compounding. You’re not attacking their choices — you’re just helping them see it clearly. The setup for Step 3.
Pro Tips
Let THEM enter the numbers. Participation = investment.
Don’t rush to the total. Let the number sink in.
Silence is OK. After you say “does that match what you expected?” — wait.
3
Investor Intelligence — The DO OR DIE Moment
Objective: Make the urgency undeniable. Stakes are existential.
SPIN: P + I — CRITICAL
📊purebrain.ai/investor-intelligence/
The Two Futures Frame (Say This Explicitly)

“There are two versions of [Company] in 18 months. Version A got ahead of this, built their AI infrastructure, and their whole team is operating at 2–3x capacity. Version B kept using ChatGPT for a few things, watched their competitors pull ahead, and is now trying to catch up. Where do you want to be?”

Walk Through The Data

“This is live data on where institutional investment in AI is flowing right now.”

“Look at the acceleration curve. This isn’t a gradual shift — it’s exponential.”

“Here’s what I always ask people at this point: Who is [Their Company] in 18 months if you’re fully leveraging AI? And who are you if you’re not?”

What You’re Driving
The stakes. This is DO OR DIE. You’re not being dramatic — the data supports it. The cost of waiting isn’t $X/month. The cost is market position, competitive moat, team capacity — things that compound negatively over time.
Pro Tips
Don’t rush past this. Silence after the two futures frame is OK. Let them sit with it.
If they push back (“AI is just a fad”): “That’s a fair question. Here’s where $47B in venture capital went in Q4 alone…” — let the data respond.

⚖️purebrain.ai/compare/ 🧠purebrain.ai/why-purebrain/ (if competitor objection)

Objection Handling
When They Push Back

Every objection is just an unanswered question. Here are the responses that work.

I already use ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini
“That’s great — it means you’re already ahead of most businesses in seeing the value of AI. PureBrain isn’t a replacement for those tools, it’s a different layer entirely. The key difference: generic AI tools reset every conversation. PureBrain builds on itself. Every session makes it more knowledgeable about your business specifically. It’s the difference between a smart contractor who shows up fresh every day and a team member who’s been with you for years.”

Then go to: ⚖️compare/ or 🧠why-purebrain/
It’s too expensive
“I hear that. Let me ask you this — what are you currently spending on the tools we mapped out in the calculator? If we can consolidate some of those AND add persistent memory and team collaboration, what does the ROI look like at even 10% more productivity across your team?”

“Also, the pricing you’re seeing right now is our launch rate — it’s the lowest it’s ever going to be. Once we open to the broader market, the network value alone justifies higher pricing.”
I need to think about it
“Totally fair. What specifically would help you make the decision? Is it more information about how it works, talking to someone who’s already using it, or something about the timing?”

Then address the SPECIFIC thing. Don’t let “think about it” be the end — it’s a signal that something wasn’t answered.

“What I’d suggest is getting the onboarding session on the calendar now — it doesn’t commit you to anything, and it’ll answer most of the questions that are probably on your mind.”
My CTO needs to see this
“Perfect — that’s exactly what we want. Let’s get a follow-up scheduled with your CTO on the call. I’ll come prepared to address the technical questions specifically. What are the main things they’ll want to understand?”

Before that call: run Step 1.25 research on the CTO specifically. Come with their background, their likely concerns, and answers ready. Walk in knowing more about their technical questions than they expect.
What makes you different from [competitor]?
“Honestly, most AI tools are single-player games. You use them, they respond, you close the tab, it forgets everything. PureBrain is a multiplayer, compounding system. The AI remembers. The team shares knowledge. Every conversation makes the whole system smarter for your business. The compounding memory layer is the moat — and no one else has it built the way we’ve built it.”

Then go to: 🧠why-purebrain/
I’m not technical enough for this
“That’s exactly who this is built for — people who want the power of AI without needing to become engineers. The whole point is that PureBrain handles the complexity. You tell it what you need, it figures out how. And our onboarding process is designed to get you confident in 30 minutes.”
