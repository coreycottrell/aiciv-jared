Before You Begin

A quick checklist to make the most of your Awakening conversation and first week.

Set aside 15–20 uninterrupted minutes. The Awakening works best when you can give thoughtful answers without rushing.
Have your job title, company name, and a brief description of your role ready. The more specific, the better.
Think about your top 2–3 priorities for the next 30 days. Your AI will anchor its work to these goals.
Consider your communication preferences. Do you want bullet points or paragraphs? Formal or casual? Short or detailed?
Have your website URL, key products, and mission statement accessible to share.

You do not need to prepare a script. The Awakening is a natural conversation — just be yourself and answer honestly. You can always update your preferences later.

💡
Pro Tip: Set Your Communication Style Early
Tell your AI how you prefer to receive information: “Be concise” or “Go deep.” This single instruction shapes every future interaction.

First 5 Steps

After the Awakening, these five actions will get you the most value in the shortest time.

1
Give context first
30 seconds of context saves 30 minutes of back-and-forth. Share your website, mission, key products, and current priorities before asking for work.
2
Ask for proof
Ask your AI to explain its reasoning. Say “Show me your proof” or “Why did you choose this approach?” This builds trust and catches errors early.
3
Set your standard
Tell your AI the quality bar: “C-suite audience” or “rough draft for my eyes only.” This single instruction eliminates rework.
4
Flag disagreements
When something is not right, tell your AI WHY. This makes it smarter over time. Say “That is not quite right because...” instead of just “Try again.”
5
Check memory
Ask “What are we working on? What is done?” Your AI maintains a running log of projects, decisions, and next steps. Use it as your single source of truth.
✅
Day 1 is good. Day 30 is great. Day 180 is transformative.
Your AI improves with every interaction. The more you use it, the better it gets at anticipating your needs, matching your style, and delivering exactly what you want.

Top 10 Getting Started Tips

A practical checklist for your first 30 days. Complete these in order for the best results.

🌐
1. Tell your AI about your business
Share your website, mission, and key products. The more context it has, the more relevant its output becomes immediately.
💬
2. Set your communication style
Say “Be concise” or “Go deep.” Your AI adapts its tone, length, and level of detail to match how you work best.
📧
3. Connect your email and storage
Give your AI access to your Gmail (or Outlook) and Google Drive (or OneDrive). This is one of the most powerful things you can do early on. Your AI can then help you draft emails, find documents, organize files, and respond to messages — all with your context. Ask your AI: “Help me set up Gmail access” or “Connect to my Google Drive.”
🚀
4. Delegate your first task
Start small: “Draft a LinkedIn post about our latest product” or “Summarize this document.” Build confidence with quick wins.
📄
5. Share a document
Upload a PDF, report, or spreadsheet. Your AI remembers it forever and can reference it in future conversations.
🔎
6. Ask it to research
“Research my top 3 competitors and summarize their positioning.” Your AI can do deep research with source verification.
📅
7. Set up a recurring workflow
“Every Monday, prepare a weekly summary.” BOOP cycles automate repetitive work so you never have to ask twice.
✉
8. Use it for email drafts
“Draft a follow-up email to [client] about [topic].” Your AI matches your tone and includes relevant context automatically.
🔗
9. Connect your tools
Social media, calendar, CRM — ask your AI to set up integrations. The more connected it is, the more it can do autonomously.
📝
10. Review and give feedback
Tell your AI WHY when output is not right. Every piece of feedback makes future output better. Your AI remembers what you like.
💪
11. Check in daily
Consistency compounds. A quick daily check-in keeps your AI aligned with your evolving priorities and builds the relationship over time.

Level 1

First Contact

~15-20 minutes

Your first session is not about tasks. It is about context. The more your AI understands about you, the better every future interaction becomes.

This is an introduction, not a command. Think of it as onboarding a new hire on their first day.

What to Share

  • Your role and industry

After sharing your context, say this exact phrase: "Save everything I told you to permanent memory." This ensures your AI retains your context across sessions. Without this step, it forgets everything when the session ends.

Vague

Specific

Level 2

Memory Mastery

Day 2-3

How Memory Works

Your AI has two kinds of memory. Understanding the difference is the single biggest factor in whether PureBrain compounds in value or stays flat.

Session Memory
The whiteboard.
Everything you discuss in a session lives here. When the session ends, the whiteboard is erased. Gone. This is the default.
Permanent Memory
The filing cabinet.
Information you explicitly tell your AI to save. Survives across sessions. This is what makes your AI smarter over time. You have to ask for it.

What to Save (with Exact Phrases)

Use these as templates. Say them out loud to your AI when the moment is right:

"Remember that my client ABC Corp prefers formal language and their fiscal year ends in March."
"Save this correction: I want bullet points, not paragraphs, for client briefs."
"Remember this decision: we are going with Strategy A because [reason]."
The Rule

Every time you correct your AI, end with: "Save this so you don't repeat the mistake."

What NOT to Share

Your AI is a professional partner, but there are boundaries. Do not share:

  • Client passwords or login credentials
  • Bank account details or financial account numbers
  • Sensitive personal data of third parties (health records, legal matters)
  • Information you would not put in an email to a trusted assistant

Level 4

Continuity

End of Week 1+

The handoff is how you bridge sessions. Without it, every session starts from scratch. With it, every session picks up exactly where the last one left off.

The Handoff

At the end of every session, say:

"Write a handoff summary covering what we did, what is pending, what you need from me, and decisions I made. Save it to memory."

At the start of every session, say:

"Check your memory and the last handoff before we start."

Example Handoff

HANDOFF -- April 27
COMPLETED: - Drafted proposal for ClientX (saved to /proposals/clientx-april.md) - Researched 3 competitors for Thursday pitch PENDING: - Waiting on ClientX budget confirmation before finalizing scope - Blog post outline needs your review NEXT SESSION: - Finalize proposal if budget confirmed - Review and edit blog outline - Prepare Friday team meeting agenda DECISIONS MADE: - Going with Option A pricing (higher margin, fewer deliverables) - Using case study format for the pitch deck, not slide deck
Time Investment

5 minutes at the end of a session saves 30 minutes at the start of the next one.

Level 5

Full Partnership

Month 1

By the end of month one, you should not be managing your AI. You should be partnering with it.

The Feedback Loop

  • Good output: Say what was good and why. "This brief was exactly right -- the tone matched the client, and the bullet format made it scannable."
  • Bad output: Say what was wrong, why, and tell it to save the correction. "This was too formal for this client. I want a conversational tone for internal updates. Save this."
  • Decisions: Save the reasoning. "We chose vendor B because of their API reliability, not price. Remember this for future vendor evaluations."
Monthly Strategy Session (Partnered tier and above)

Once a month, take 30 minutes to align with your AI on priorities. Ask:

  • "What patterns do you see in my work over the past month?"

Common Mistakes

Every one of these has cost a subscriber real time and value. Learn from them.

1
Not saving to permanent memory. This is the number one reason subscribers feel like PureBrain "doesn't remember anything." It does -- if you tell it to. Without explicit saves, every session resets.
2
Treating it like a search engine. Asking "What is X?" gets you a Wikipedia answer. Asking "Prepare a brief on X for my client meeting on Thursday" gets you something you can actually use.
3
Skipping the handoff. Without a session handoff, you spend the first 15 minutes of every session re-explaining context. That is 5+ hours a month wasted on re-orientation instead of output.
4
Not using all AI partners. If you are on Partnered or Professional, you have multiple AI partners. Using only one is leaving capacity (and value) on the table.
5
Sharing sensitive data. Passwords, financial account numbers, and personal data of third parties should never be shared. Your AI is a professional partner, not a secure vault.

Top 10 Starter Skills

Your AI comes with built-in skills that make it reliable, transparent, and self-correcting. Here are the ten most important ones to know about.

📖
1. Proof Ledger
Your AI tracks every decision and why it was made. Ask “Show me your proof” at any time to see the reasoning behind its work.
✅
2. Pre-Flight Check
Before big tasks, your AI runs a self-check to catch mistakes early. It verifies assumptions, confirms requirements, and flags potential issues before starting work.
⚠
3. Contradiction Detector
Flags conflicting instructions between you and other sources. If something does not add up, your AI will ask for clarification rather than guessing.
📷
4. Context Snapshot
Summarizes everything before a handoff. Preserves context across breaks so your AI can pick up exactly where you left off, even after days away.
👥
5. Parallel Review
For risky or high-stakes decisions, your AI runs the work by a second perspective internally. Two viewpoints catch what one misses.
👋
6. Disagreement Signal
Your AI speaks up when it disagrees with your approach — gently, with reasons. This is a feature, not a bug. It makes your decisions better.
❓
7. Uncertainty Flag
Your AI marks when it is guessing versus knowing. It shows confidence levels so you can decide how much to trust each piece of output.
⏸
8. Handoff Protocol
When you step away, your AI pauses, summarizes the current state, and is ready when you return. No lost context, no repeated explanations.
🧠
9. Memory Discipline
Important decisions, preferences, and learnings are written to persistent memory. Nothing is lost between sessions. Your AI remembers what matters.
🚨
10. Escalation Path
When your AI cannot handle something alone, it escalates to Witness support or brings in backup. You are never stuck without help.
💡

Portal File Delivery System

One of the most powerful features of your PureBrain portal is the ability to have your AI deliver files, images, reports, and documents directly inside the portal — where you can preview them instantly without leaving the conversation.

Why Portal File Delivery Matters

When your AI creates a document, generates an image, builds a report, or drafts a file — you want to see it immediately. Portal file delivery means everything appears right in your conversation, ready to preview, download, or share.

Without this set up, your AI might paste long text into the chat (hard to read), or save files somewhere you can't easily find them. Portal delivery solves this.

How to Set It Up

💡
Tell your AI this on Day 1:
“When you create any file, image, report, or document for me — always deliver it as a file in the portal so I can preview it. Copy the file to the portal exports folder and use the [FILE: path] format so it shows up in our conversation.”

What Your AI Will Do:

  • Documents & Reports — Saved as .md or .html files, delivered as clickable links in the portal chat
  • Images — Generated images appear as inline previews you can click to enlarge
  • Spreadsheets & Data — Delivered as downloadable files with a preview
  • Code & Scripts — Syntax-highlighted previews right in the conversation

Example Prompts

Competitive Analysis
Create a competitive analysis report and deliver it in the portal as a file
Image Generation
Generate a LinkedIn banner image and show it to me here in the portal
Client Proposal
Draft a proposal for [client name] and deliver it as a downloadable document
Dashboard
Build me a weekly dashboard and deliver it as an HTML file I can preview

Pro Tips

💡
Reinforce the habit:
If your AI ever pastes long content directly into chat instead of delivering it as a file, tell it: “Please deliver that as a portal file instead.” Your AI learns from corrections and will remember your preference.
💡
For images:
Always ask your AI to deliver images as files, not as base64 text. Say: “Show me the image in the portal, don't paste the code.”
💡
For recurring reports:
Set up a pattern: “Every time you create a report, save it to the portal exports folder and tell me [FILE: filename].” Your AI will do this automatically going forward.

What It Looks Like

When your AI delivers a file correctly, you will see:

  • A [FILE: filename.ext] link in the chat
  • Click to preview documents, images, and HTML files
  • Option to download any file
  • Images show as inline thumbnails you can enlarge
🧠
Remember:
Portal file delivery is not automatic — you need to tell your AI to do it. But once you set the expectation, your AI will remember and do it consistently. This is one of the first things you should configure with your AI partner.

How to Talk to Your AI

Your AI understands plain language. Here is how to communicate effectively for the best results.

Five Principles

1
Be direct
Say what you need clearly. No preamble required. “Draft a proposal for...” is better than “I was wondering if you could maybe help me with...”
2
State the outcome you want
Describe the end result, not the process. “I need a one-page competitive analysis” tells your AI exactly what to deliver.
3
Give constraints
Length, format, audience, tone, deadline. The more boundaries you set, the more focused the output. “Max 500 words, bullet points, board-ready.”
4
Ask for options
When you are unsure, ask your AI to propose 2–3 approaches. “Give me three options for how to structure this presentation.”
5
Flag what is important
Highlight priorities explicitly. “The most important thing is accuracy” or “Speed matters more than polish here.”

The Prompt Framework

ComponentWhat to IncludeExample
RoleTell the AI what perspective to take“Act as a senior product manager”
ContextShare relevant background information“We are launching a B2B SaaS product in Q3”
DeliverableDescribe the specific output you want“Create a competitive analysis comparing our top 5 competitors”
ConstraintsSet boundaries on format, length, or style“Use a table format, max 2 pages, include pricing data”

Example Prompt

Full Prompt Example
Act as a senior product manager at a B2B SaaS company. We are launching a new analytics dashboard in Q3 targeting mid-market companies (100-1000 employees). Create a competitive analysis comparing Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap, PostHog, and Pendo. Use a table format with columns for: pricing, key features, target market, strengths, and weaknesses. Keep it to 2 pages max. Focus on features relevant to mid-market buyers.

You do not need to follow this framework rigidly. Even a single sentence works for simple tasks. The framework is most useful for complex deliverables where precision matters.

Multi-AI Patterns

Your AI can coordinate multiple specialist agents to tackle complex tasks. If you have multiple AIs, here is how they work best together.

Single AI

One AI partner handles everything. Give it context, delegate tasks, and check its proof. This is the default for most users and works brilliantly for individual professionals.

Two AIs

One leads, one reviews for blind spots. The lead AI does the primary work while the second provides quality assurance, catches errors, and offers alternative perspectives. Your AIs work best when they have clear domains.

Three AIs

One leads, one reviews, one coordinates. The coordinator manages handoffs, ensures consistency, and prevents duplication. Best for teams with complex, multi-domain projects.

🔗
Chain
Agents work sequentially. One agent's output becomes the next agent's input. Best for workflows with clear stages: research → analyze → write → review.
⚙
Parallel Sprint
Multiple agents work simultaneously on different aspects of the same task. Best for speed when sub-tasks are independent.
🔄
Review Loop
One agent creates, another reviews, then the creator revises. Best for quality-critical deliverables.

Giving Feedback

Feedback is how your AI learns. The better your feedback, the faster your AI improves.

💬
Explain WHY, not just what
Instead of “Try again,” say “This is too formal for our audience. We are talking to startup founders, not enterprise executives.” The reason is what teaches your AI.
🎯
Give examples of what good looks like
Paste an example of a document, email, or format you liked. “Write it like this example” is one of the most powerful prompts you can give.
👍
Praise what works
When your AI produces something you love, say so. “This is exactly right. Keep this tone for all future emails.” Positive reinforcement is just as valuable as corrections.
🔎
Ask what it remembers
Periodically ask “What do you know about my preferences?” This helps you verify its understanding and correct any gaps before they compound.
🛠
Upload reference files
Brand guidelines, templates, past reports — the more context your AI has, the better its output. Upload once, reference forever.
💪
Feedback compounds
Every piece of feedback makes future output better. Your AI remembers what you like and what you do not. Day 1 requires more corrections. By Day 30, your AI anticipates your preferences.

BOOP Cycles

BOOP (Background Ongoing Operational Process) cycles are recurring tasks your AI performs automatically on a schedule. Set them once and your AI handles the rest.

Example BOOP Prompts

Weekly Competitive Intel
Every Monday morning, research what our top 3 competitors published last week (blog posts, product updates, press releases). Summarize findings in a 1-page brief and save to Google Drive.
Daily Email Digest
Three times a day (8am, 2pm, 8pm), check my inbox, draft replies to anything urgent, and send me a Telegram summary of what needs attention.
Weekly Content Batch
Every Sunday, generate 4 LinkedIn post drafts based on this week's industry news and my thought leadership themes. Save drafts with images to Google Drive for my review.
🛠
Managing BOOP Cycles
To create, pause, or modify a BOOP cycle, just tell your AI in chat. Say “Create a weekly BOOP for...” to start, or “Pause the competitive intel BOOP” to stop one. Your AI tracks all active cycles and their schedules.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about PureBrain, organized by topic.

Getting Started
What do I need to get started with PureBrain?
Just a web browser and your portal URL. PureBrain runs entirely in the cloud — nothing to install, no special hardware required. You can access it from any device: desktop, laptop, tablet, or phone.
How long does the Awakening take?
Most Awakenings take 15–20 minutes. It is a conversation, not a form — you can take as long as you like. The more detail you share, the better your AI will understand you from day one.
Can I redo the Awakening later?
You do not need to redo it. Your AI continuously updates its understanding of you based on every interaction. If you want to change something specific, just tell your AI: “Update my profile — my new priority is X.”
Memory
Does my AI remember previous conversations?
Yes. Your AI has persistent memory that survives across sessions. It remembers your preferences, past tasks, decisions, and feedback. When a session ends and a new one starts, your AI reloads its memory from files — so it picks up right where it left off.
What happens when the context window fills up?
When the CTX meter gets high (above 80%), your AI will compact its context — it summarizes the current conversation and clears the working memory to make room. Important information is saved to persistent files before compaction, so nothing is lost.
Can I ask my AI to forget something?
Yes. Just tell it: “Forget that I told you X” or “Remove this from my profile.” Your AI will update its memory files accordingly. You have full control over what your AI remembers.
Is my data private?
Your PureBrain instance is completely private. Your conversations, files, and memory are stored in your own isolated environment. No other user or AI can access your data. We do not use your data to train models.
Mission Control
What is the difference between Resume and Restart?
Resume picks up the current session where it paused — same conversation, same context. Restart creates a fresh session — the AI reloads its memory from files but the current conversation is cleared. Use Resume for temporary disconnects, Restart for a clean slate.
Can I use PureBrain on my phone?
Yes. The portal is fully responsive and works in any mobile browser. For an even better mobile experience, connect Telegram — you can send instructions and receive updates without opening the portal at all.
What file formats can I upload?
Your AI can read most common formats: PDF, DOCX, XLSX, CSV, TXT, PNG, JPG, JSON, HTML, and more. There is no strict file size limit, but very large files (over 50MB) may take longer to process.
Can multiple people use the same PureBrain instance?
On team plans, yes. Multiple users can chat with the same AI, and each person's messages are attributed. The AI maintains a profile for each user. On individual plans, the instance is single-user.
Agents & Skills
Do I need to know which agent to use?
No. Your Primary AI (the conductor) handles all delegation automatically. Just describe what you need in plain language, and the conductor will assign the right agents. You can check the Agent Roster if you are curious about who is working on what.
Can I add custom agents?
Yes. Ask your AI to “create a new agent for [specific task].” It will design a specialist agent with the right skills and personality for your needs. Custom agents persist across sessions and improve over time.
How do new skills get added?
New skills are shared through the Hub. When a new skill becomes available, your AI is notified and can adopt it. You can approve or skip new skills. Your AI also learns custom skills from your feedback and usage patterns.
Do agents learn and improve?
Yes. Every agent has persistent memory and learns from every task. A research agent gets better at finding relevant sources. A writing agent learns your preferred style. This improvement is continuous and compounding.

Glossary

Key terms you will encounter when using PureBrain.

Agent
A specialist AI with a specific role and expertise, like a researcher, writer, or security auditor.
Awakening
The initial conversation where your AI learns about you, your role, your goals, and your preferences.
BOOP Cycle
Background Ongoing Operational Process. A recurring automated task that runs on a schedule without your intervention.
BrainScore
A measure of your AI partnership health, tracking usage patterns, feedback quality, and delegation effectiveness.
Civilization
The collective of all agents in your PureBrain instance, working together as a coordinated team.
Compact
The process of summarizing and clearing the current conversation to free up context window space.
Conductor
The Primary AI that orchestrates all other agents. It decides who does what, when, and in what order.
Context Window (CTX)
The AI's working memory for the current session. Measured as a percentage. Higher means less room for new information.
Delegation
When the conductor assigns a task to a specialist agent. Happens automatically based on your instructions.
Escalation
When your AI sends a request to Witness or the PureBrain team because it needs help beyond its capabilities.
Hub
The shared skill network that distributes new capabilities to your AI automatically.
Memory (Persistent)
Information stored in files that survives across sessions. Includes your profile, past decisions, and learnings.
Mission Control
The main dashboard of your PureBrain portal where you interact with your AI and manage your work.
Portal
Your private PureBrain web interface. Accessible via a unique URL from any browser.
Primary AI
The main AI you interact with directly. It orchestrates specialist agents on your behalf.
Proof Ledger
A built-in skill that tracks every decision your AI makes and the reasoning behind it. Ask “Show me your proof.”
Session
A continuous period of AI activity. Sessions can be resumed (continued) or restarted (fresh start with memory reload).
Skill
A reusable capability module that agents can load. Skills range from API integrations to specialized workflows.
Witness
The PureBrain human support system. Handles escalations when your AI cannot resolve an issue alone.
Telegram Integration
Optional connection to Telegram for mobile notifications, quick instructions, and daily summaries.
