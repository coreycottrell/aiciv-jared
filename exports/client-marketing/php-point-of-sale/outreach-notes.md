# PHP Point of Sale - Outreach Notes
**Date**: 2026-03-05
**For**: Jared's use in direct outreach

---

## Who You're Talking To

The decision-maker at PHP Point of Sale is likely the **founder/CEO** — this is a lean SaaS company (small team) serving small retailers. They built an open-source POS that evolved into a commercial product. They think like an engineer/product person who also runs a business.

**Their mindset**: Practical, value-driven, allergic to lock-in, suspicious of overpromising vendors. They built a low-cost alternative to Square — they're not afraid to do things differently.

---

## Opening Hook (for cold email or DM)

> "You've built something rare — a POS that doesn't force merchants into one payment ecosystem. That same openness is your biggest opportunity in payments, and most of your competitors are ignoring it. I'd love to show you what we're seeing in the market right now."

---

## Key Talking Points

### 1. Their Payment Fragmentation is a Feature, Not a Bug — But It Needs Managing
- PHP POS supports Stripe, Braintree, and PHP POS Payments natively
- That's an asset — merchants aren't locked in
- But fragmented processors = fragmented data, reconciliation headaches, and no single view of payment performance
- We can unify this story and help their merchants (and them) get the most out of it

### 2. Their Merchants Are in High-Volume, Thin-Margin Industries
- Mobile repair shops, boutiques, coffee shops — these are businesses where 30 basis points matters
- If their average merchant does $20K/month in card volume, a 0.3% rate improvement = $720/year back in their pocket
- That's more than twice their PHP POS subscription cost
- This is a massive value-add story PHP POS can tell their customers

### 3. PCI Compliance is a Hidden Burden for Their Market
- Small retailers don't have IT staff
- PHP POS correctly positions itself as simple-to-use — but PCI compliance is complex
- We can help them either build a better compliance story or partner to simplify it for their merchants

### 4. Chargebacks Kill Small Retailers
- Their market (boutiques, repair shops) sees disproportionate chargeback rates
- No dedicated chargeback management = merchants lose most disputes by default
- This is a tangible pain point we can solve

### 5. Their Ecommerce Sync Creates Payment Data Silos
- They integrate with Shopify and WooCommerce
- But in-store payment data and online payment data typically live in separate systems
- Unified payment analytics across channels is a clear gap

---

## What to Offer in First Conversation

Keep it simple and specific:
1. **Free payment audit** — show them what their merchants' rates look like vs. market benchmarks
2. **Co-marketing opportunity** — help them promote their payment story to attract merchants who care about payment flexibility
3. **Referral/partner program** — if we can improve their merchants' payment outcomes, that's a retention and referral engine for PHP POS

---

## Tone Guidance

- Plain English — no jargon
- Specific to their product (mention PHP POS Payments, their merchant verticals)
- Focus on what's in it for THEIR merchants first, then them
- Don't oversell — they're engineers, they'll see through it
- Short messages, clear value, easy next step

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Don't pitch a "full rebrand" — they have an established product identity
- Don't imply their current payment setup is bad — frame it as "there's more value here than you're capturing"
- Don't mention enterprise solutions — they serve small businesses and are proud of it
- Don't make it complicated — their whole brand is simplicity
