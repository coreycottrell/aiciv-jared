## Executive Summary

Toast, Inc. (NYSE: TOST) is the dominant restaurant-focused point-of-sale and management platform in the United States. Founded in 2012 and headquartered in Boston, MA, Toast has grown to serve approximately 127,000+ restaurant locations as of 2024, generating over $1.3 billion in annualized revenue. Unlike general-purpose POS players (Square, Clover), Toast is purpose-built exclusively for the food service industry — a strategic focus that has made it the market share leader in full-service restaurant technology.

Toast's marketing is primarily oriented around inbound lead generation through content, direct sales outbound to restaurant owners, and a growing partner/referral channel. Their brand is functional and operator-centric (not aspirational), and their content strategy centers on restaurateur education.

This analysis identifies Toast's strengths, competitive weaknesses, marketing gaps, and specific opportunities where Pure Marketing Group (PMG) could provide value to Toast's restaurant operator clients.

### What Toast Actually Is

Toast is not just a POS system. It is a full restaurant operating system encompassing:

- **Point of Sale**: Tableside ordering, quick service terminals, kitchen display systems (KDS)
- **Online Ordering**: Branded direct ordering via Toast's TakeOut app and web integration
- **Payments Processing**: Integrated payments at negotiated flat rates (typically 2.49% + $0.15)
- **Payroll and HR**: Toast Payroll (direct competitor to Gusto, ADP for restaurants)
- **Marketing Tools**: Email marketing, loyalty programs, guest data capture
- **Inventory Management**: Recipe costing, waste tracking, vendor integrations
- **Scheduling**: Shift scheduling and labor management
- **Reporting and Analytics**: Real-time sales, labor cost, menu performance dashboards
- **Toast Capital**: Revenue-based financing for restaurant operators
- **Hardware**: Proprietary Android-based terminals, handhelds, KDS units, kiosks

The strategic moat is vertical integration. Once a restaurant is on Toast, switching costs are extremely high because payroll, loyalty data, inventory, and revenue data all live in one ecosystem.

### Market Position

Toast holds the largest market share among full-service restaurant POS providers in the US. Their closest restaurant-specific competitors are:

- TouchBistro (stronger in Canada, table service focus)
- Lightspeed Restaurant (stronger internationally, upscale/fine dining)
- Revel Systems (enterprise/franchise focus, iPad-based)
- SpotOn (aggressive growth challenger, similar all-in-one positioning)

General-purpose competitors (Square, Clover) compete heavily in the quick-service and small/micro-restaurant segment but lose ground at full-service table management complexity.

### Revenue Model

Toast generates revenue through three primary streams:

1. **Financial Technology (FinTech) Solutions** (~75% of revenue): Payment processing fees on every card transaction processed through Toast terminals. This is the primary revenue driver and is why Toast's software can sometimes be offered at low or no monthly cost.

2. **Subscription Services** (~20% of revenue): Monthly SaaS fees for POS software, payroll, scheduling, inventory, and add-on modules.

3. **Hardware** (~5% of revenue): Sale of proprietary terminals, handhelds, KDS, and kiosks. Hardware margins are typically thin or negative — it is a land-grab strategy.

## Section 2: Pricing Tiers and Target Segments

### Software Pricing Tiers (as of 2025)

Toast structures pricing into four main tiers:

**Tier 1: Starter Kit (Free / $0/month)**
- Designed for single-location quick-service operators
- Includes 1 POS terminal, basic reporting, online ordering
- Payment processing required through Toast at standard rates
- Target: Food trucks, pop-ups, very small cafes, first-time operators

**Tier 2: Point of Sale ($69/month)**
- Full POS capabilities for single location
- Includes tableside ordering, kitchen display
- Does not include payroll, inventory, or advanced marketing
- Target: Small to mid-size restaurants, independent operators

**Tier 3: Build Your Own (Custom)**
- A la carte module selection
- Base POS + choose from: Payroll, Scheduling, Marketing Suite, Loyalty, Online Ordering, Inventory
- Pricing scales with each module added (typically $50-$150/module/month)
- Target: Growth-stage operators who want specific capabilities

**Tier 4: Restaurant Basics / Pro (Custom Enterprise)**
- Full platform bundle with volume discounts
- Dedicated account management, onboarding support, priority support SLA
- Custom payment processing rate negotiations possible at scale
- Target: Multi-location operators, regional chains, franchise groups (50+ locations)

### Hardware Pricing

Toast hardware is proprietary (Android-based, not off-the-shelf iPad). Pricing:
- Toast Go 2 (handheld): ~$409 retail or leased
- Toast Flex (terminal): ~$627 retail
- Toast Kiosk: ~$999-$1,200
- Kitchen Display System (KDS): ~$627
- Hardware leasing programs available (typically $100-$150/terminal/month)

### Transaction Fee Structure

Standard processing: 2.49% + $0.15 per transaction (card present)
Online orders: 2.99% + $0.15 per transaction
Custom rates available at enterprise/high-volume tier

## Section 3: Marketing Strategy Deep-Dive

### 3.1 Marketing Channels

**Inbound / Content Marketing (Primary)**
Toast operates one of the most aggressive content marketing operations in the restaurant tech space. Key assets:
- Restaurant management blog (100+ articles/year on topics from food cost control to hiring tips)
- "Menu Templates" and "Restaurant Business Plan" downloadable guides (lead magnets)
- Restaurant industry statistics and annual "Voice of the Restaurant Industry" survey report
- Recipe and menu costing calculators

The content strategy is educational, not product-pitchy. They teach restaurant owners how to run a better business — and the product is the implied answer.

**Direct Sales / Outbound (High Investment)**
Toast operates a large direct sales team (reported 1,000+ sales reps) using:
- Cold calling to restaurants (territory-based reps)
- LinkedIn outreach to restaurant owners and managers
- Restaurant industry trade show presence (National Restaurant Association, state associations)
- Demo-first sales model ("Come see the product, we'll handle the rest")

**Partner / Referral Channel (Growing)**
- Restaurant consultants and accountants refer Toast for commissions
- Integration partners (food delivery platforms, accounting software) cross-sell
- Toast for Accountants program

**Paid Search and SEO**
Toast aggressively bids on "restaurant POS" and competitor-name keywords (searches for "Square restaurant POS," "Clover restaurant," etc. frequently serve Toast ads). Their SEO investment in informational content is extremely high.

**Social Media**
- LinkedIn: Corporate/B2B content, hiring, industry stats
- Instagram: Restaurant lifestyle and partner restaurant success stories
- YouTube: Product tutorials, webinar replays, customer testimonials
- Facebook: Local restaurant community engagement, ads targeting restaurant owners by interest

### 3.2 Messaging and Brand Voice

Toast's brand voice is:
- **Direct and practical** (not inspirational, not flashy)
- **Operator-empathetic** ("We understand restaurants because we only do restaurants")
- **Data-forward** (references to percentage improvements, time saved, revenue lifted)
- **Accessible** (avoids jargon, writes for owner-operators not tech buyers)

Core messaging pillars:
1. "Built for restaurants, by restaurant people"
2. "Everything you need, all in one place"
3. "More time for hospitality, less time on back-office chaos"
4. "Grow your revenue with insights you can actually use"

What is MISSING from their messaging:
- Almost no emotional resonance or aspirational narrative
- Minimal social proof at scale (testimonials feel templated)
- Weak differentiation on their marketing tools specifically
- No coherent content strategy around their payroll/HR modules (undersells this capability)
- Their "restaurant marketing" content is generic industry advice — not differentiated thought leadership

### 3.3 Content Strategy

Toast's content strategy is volume-oriented and SEO-driven. They publish frequently on:
- Restaurant management how-tos
- Food industry trends and statistics
- Hiring guides for restaurant roles
- Menu pricing and food cost calculators
- Seasonal campaign ideas for restaurants

**The Gap**: Their content tells restaurant operators WHAT to do with marketing (run loyalty programs, post on Instagram, collect emails) but almost never shows HOW well these things work when powered by Toast's own marketing tools. They are leaving a massive proof point on the table.

## Section 4: Competitive Landscape

### 4.1 Primary Competitors

**Square for Restaurants**
- Strength: Brand recognition, no monthly software fee on free tier, easy setup
- Weakness: Loses badly at table management complexity, inventory, and labor management for mid-to-large restaurants
- Pricing: Free tier available; Plus plan at $60/month/location
- Target: Very small restaurants, food trucks, cafes, pop-ups
- Marketing approach: Consumer-brand-led (Square the payment processor first, restaurant second)
- Toast wins vs Square: Any restaurant with tables, a waitstaff, or more than one terminal

**Clover (Fiserv)**
- Strength: Distribution through banks and ISOs (huge sales channel), hardware flexibility, non-restaurant versatility
- Weakness: Not purpose-built for restaurants; table management, kitchen display, and delivery integration are afterthoughts
- Pricing: Varies widely by ISO/dealer; typically $39-$99/month for restaurant plans
- Target: General small business including restaurants (not restaurant-specific)
- Marketing approach: Banking/financial services distribution channel; minimal direct-to-restaurant brand presence
- Toast wins vs Clover: Any operator who needs restaurant-specific workflows

**Lightspeed Restaurant**
- Strength: Stronger internationally (Europe, Australia, Canada), fine dining feature depth, multi-venue hotel capability
- Weakness: Higher price point, less US market presence than Toast, smaller direct sales team in US
- Pricing: $69-$399/month depending on plan
- Target: Upscale/fine dining, hotels, enterprise multi-venue
- Marketing approach: Premium positioning, strong design/hospitality brand aesthetic
- Toast wins vs Lightspeed: Mid-market US, quick-service, casual dining

**SpotOn**
- Strength: Aggressive pricing, strong in sports/entertainment venues, growing fast
- Weakness: Smaller partner ecosystem, less brand awareness, fewer integrations
- Pricing: Comparable to Toast, sometimes lower upfront
- Target: Similar to Toast (full-service restaurants, multi-location)
- Marketing approach: "We're the underdog fighting for your margins"
- Toast wins vs SpotOn: Brand trust, larger ecosystem, more integrations

**TouchBistro**
- Strength: iPad-based (more familiar hardware), strong in Canada, solid table service UX
- Weakness: US market presence limited, no integrated payroll, no built-in payment processing
- Pricing: $69/month base; add-ons for reservations, loyalty, online ordering
- Target: Canadian casual dining, US boutique restaurants
- Toast wins vs TouchBistro: Full platform integration (payments, payroll, marketing built-in)

## Section 5: Toast's Marketing Pain Points and Gaps

This section identifies where Toast's current marketing falls short — which points to either where Toast itself could benefit from PMG's services, or where Toast's restaurant clients need support that Toast isn't providing.

### 5.1 Toast's Own Marketing Pain Points

**Pain Point 1: Content is Educationally Deep but Commercially Shallow**
Toast's blog is excellent at teaching restaurant operators how to succeed. But the leap from "here's how to run a loyalty program" to "here's how Toast's loyalty tool delivers measurable ROI" is rarely made compellingly. They have proof points (customer case studies) but these are buried and feel generic.

**Pain Point 2: Weak Social Proof on Specific Features**
Toast features like payroll, inventory management, and the marketing suite are significantly undermarketed relative to their POS. A restaurant owner evaluating Toast might not even know Toast does payroll. This is a marketing failure for a product suite that could extend average contract value significantly.

**Pain Point 3: Multi-Location and Enterprise Marketing is Underdeveloped**
Toast is visibly trying to move upmarket toward regional chains and franchises (where the big ARR lives), but their marketing materials are still primarily oriented toward single-location independent operators. Enterprise-facing content (ROI calculators for chains, franchise management case studies, multi-location analytics demos) is thin.

**Pain Point 4: Toast's "Marketing Suite" for Restaurants is Marketed Poorly**
Toast has email marketing, SMS, loyalty, and guest data tools built in. But their marketing of these marketing tools is almost entirely generic. They could be running campaigns that say "Toast's loyalty program drives X% more repeat visits on average" with specific, named customer proof. They don't. They leave PMG-type marketing firms to fill the education gap.

**Pain Point 5: The International Expansion Story is Muddled**
Toast expanded to the UK and Ireland and is likely targeting further international markets. Their marketing for international operators is thin, generic, and fails to account for local market nuances (VAT handling, local labor law for HR, regional payment preferences).

### 5.2 Toast Restaurant Clients' Marketing Pain Points

This is the bigger opportunity. Toast serves 127,000+ restaurants. These operators are NOT marketers. They are cooks, owners, operators. Their marketing capabilities are primitive in most cases:

**Restaurant Client Pain Point 1: Social Media is Inconsistent or Nonexistent**
Most independent restaurants post on Instagram sporadically, have no content calendar, and have zero paid social strategy. They have beautiful food and zero visibility strategy.

**Restaurant Client Pain Point 2: Zero Email Marketing Leverage**
Toast captures guest email data through online ordering, loyalty sign-ups, and reservation integrations. But the vast majority of restaurant operators have no idea how to turn that data into revenue. Toast provides basic email tools — operators need someone to actually run them.

**Restaurant Client Pain Point 3: Local SEO is Invisible**
Most independent restaurants have unclaimed Google Business Profiles, no review response strategy, inconsistent NAP data, and zero local SEO content. A PMG-style marketing agency that specializes in restaurant local SEO would find a massive, underserved market here.

**Restaurant Client Pain Point 4: No Seasonal or Event-Driven Marketing**
Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, New Year's Eve, NFL season openers — these are the most important revenue days for restaurants. Most operators have no marketing plan for peak season events beyond maybe posting on Instagram the day before.

**Restaurant Client Pain Point 5: Brand Differentiation is Non-Existent**
"We serve great food in a welcoming atmosphere" describes every restaurant. Most independent operators have no real brand story, no differentiated position, and no marketing that makes them memorable versus the restaurant next door.

## Section 6: Opportunities for Pure Marketing Group

### 6.1 Opportunity Architecture

There are three distinct ways PMG could position relative to Toast:

**Option A: Toast Restaurant Client Agency (Most Actionable)**
Become a marketing agency that specializes in serving Toast's restaurant client base. Market to restaurant operators directly, using Toast integration as a selling point ("We connect directly to your Toast data to power smarter campaigns").

**Option B: Toast Partner / Integration (Medium-Term)**
Apply to Toast's partner program, build a marketing integration that reads Toast guest data (email lists, loyalty, purchase history) and powers automated campaigns. Pitch this as a Toast-approved add-on.

**Option C: Toast Corporate Marketing Vendor (Longest Horizon)**
Pitch PMG's services directly to Toast, Inc. as a vendor for content creation, social media management, or campaign execution for their own brand marketing. Enterprise sales cycle, long lead time, but high-value contract.

### 6.2 Option A Deep-Dive: Toast Restaurant Client Agency

**The Pitch to Restaurant Operators:**
"You're using Toast — which means you already have the data, the tools, and the audience. We turn that into reservations. We handle your email marketing, social content, review management, and seasonal campaigns. You focus on the food."

**Services to Lead With:**
1. Monthly social media content and management (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
2. Email marketing using Toast guest data (post-visit follow-up, loyalty campaigns, seasonal promotions)
3. Google Business Profile optimization and review management
4. Seasonal event marketing (Valentine's Day, Mother's Day packages, etc.)
5. Menu photography and brand content creation
6. Local SEO content and citation building

**Target Client Profile Within Toast's Base:**
- Single-location or 2-3 location independent operators
- Annual revenue: $500K-$5M (mid-market independent)
- Operating in competitive urban/suburban markets
- Owners who feel overwhelmed by marketing and know they're losing to chains with marketing budgets

**Pricing Positioning:**
- Entry: $500-$1,000/month (social media management + email)
- Growth: $1,500-$2,500/month (full-service marketing retainer)
- Premium: $3,000-$5,000/month (multi-location, custom campaigns, content production)

**Acquisition Strategy for Restaurant Clients:**
- Partner with local restaurant associations (NRA state chapters, city hospitality groups)
- Referral program with Toast account managers and Toast-certified accountants
- Content marketing specifically for restaurant owners (blog, YouTube: "Restaurant Marketing 101")
- LinkedIn/Instagram ads targeting restaurant owners/GMs in target geographies
- Toast community forums and restaurant owner Facebook groups

### 6.3 Option B Deep-Dive: Toast Integration Product

**The Product Concept:**
A marketing automation layer that connects to the Toast API and enables:
- Automated post-visit email sequences based on visit frequency
- Loyalty tier-based offer personalization
- Revenue-predictive campaign timing (send offers on historically slow days)
- Review request automation (ask happy frequent guests to review on Google)
- Churn prevention (identify guests who haven't visited in 60+ days, send win-back offers)

**Why This Works:**
Toast has the data. PMG has the marketing expertise. The integration is the bridge that turns raw POS transaction data into marketing intelligence.

**Toast API Capabilities (Publicly Documented):**
Toast's API allows third-party applications to read:
- Order history and guest spend data
- Loyalty program membership and activity
- Online ordering data
- Reservation data (through integration partners)

**Path to Market:**
1. Build the integration (lightweight, focus on email/SMS automation first)
2. Apply to Toast's ISV Partner Program
3. Launch in Toast's Marketplace (visible to all 127,000+ Toast locations)
4. Charge restaurant operators $100-$300/month for the marketing automation layer
5. Upsell to managed services (PMG team runs the campaigns)

### 6.4 Option C: Toast Corporate Marketing Vendor

**The Entry Point:**
Toast's content marketing team is large but stretched. They publish broadly across restaurant management, technology, industry trends, payroll, and more. There are specific gaps:
- Video content and YouTube channel is under-developed relative to their text content
- Podcast presence is minimal (a big miss in the restaurant owner audience)
- Case study production quality is uneven
- Their "Toast for Enterprise" and "Toast for Franchises" content is thin

**PMG's Pitch to Toast Corporate:**
"You're the #1 restaurant OS in America. Your content tells operators what to do. We can help you show them you're the platform that makes it happen — with case studies, video content, and campaigns that convert awareness into demos."

**Realistic Assessment:**
This is an 18-24 month play. Toast has a large in-house marketing team. Getting in the door requires either a warm referral through their partner network or a very targeted outreach that leads with a specific gap (e.g., "We specialize in restaurant video content and have produced for [X similar tech brand]").

## Section 8: Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-90 Days)
1. **Define the ICP**: PMG's target restaurant client — size, geography, cuisine type, pain profile. Start narrow (e.g., "full-service independent restaurants in mid-Atlantic metro areas using Toast, $800K-$3M revenue").
2. **Build the case study**: Sign 2-3 restaurant clients at reduced/free rates. Execute a 90-day marketing program. Document the measurable revenue/traffic impact. This is the proof point that generates all future sales.
3. **Apply to Toast's partner program**: Get visible in Toast's ecosystem. Even as a "recommended marketing agency" designation creates inbound from Toast account managers.
4. **Create Toast-specific content**: Publish "How to Get the Most Out of Toast's Marketing Tools" content. This ranks for searches Toast operators conduct, and it positions PMG as the expert on Toast's own platform.

### Medium-Term Actions (90-180 Days)
5. **Launch the referral program**: Partner with Toast account managers and restaurant accountants. Pay referral fees for restaurant client introductions.
6. **Develop the integration proof-of-concept**: Build a lightweight Toast API connection that demonstrates automated email marketing using guest visit data. Even a manual version (export data, import to Brevo/Mailchimp, run campaign) proves the concept.
7. **Produce a "State of Restaurant Marketing" report**: Annual research targeting restaurant operators. Gets media coverage, builds credibility, generates leads.

### Long-Term Actions (6-18 Months)
8. **Scale the agency vertically**: Once 20-30 restaurant clients are on retainer, build a Toast-specialized restaurant marketing team with templates, playbooks, and workflows that make scaling efficient.
9. **Launch the integration product**: When the market fit is validated, productize the Toast data-to-marketing-automation layer.
10. **Pursue Toast corporate relationship**: Use client success stories and integration product as proof points to open a conversation with Toast's partnerships team.
