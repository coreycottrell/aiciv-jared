## Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

Toast serves 127,000+ restaurant locations in the United States. These operators have purchased a sophisticated all-in-one platform — POS, payments, email marketing, loyalty, payroll — and the vast majority are using approximately 20% of its capability. The marketing tools alone represent a dormant revenue engine for every restaurant on Toast's platform.

We are going to build the definitive marketing agency for Toast restaurant operators.

### What We Are Building

A vertically specialized marketing agency that speaks Toast's language, connects to Toast's data, and delivers measurable revenue impact to independent and small-chain restaurant operators. We enter as an agency, build proof of concept with 2-3 clients in the first 90 days, then systematically scale while simultaneously developing a Toast API integration product that automates the marketing intelligence layer.

The endgame is a three-revenue-stream business:
1. Managed marketing retainers (agency fees, $500-$5,000/month per client)
2. A Toast-connected SaaS marketing automation product ($100-$300/month per location, scalable to Toast's entire 127,000+ base)
3. Potentially a Toast corporate content/marketing services relationship (18-24 month horizon)

### Financial Snapshot

| First paying client | Day 45 | $6,000-$18,000 |

### Why This Wins

- Toast has no managed marketing services layer. We fill a gap they have explicitly chosen not to fill.
- The 89,000+ independent restaurant operators on Toast are not marketers. They are overwhelmed owner-operators who need execution, not advice.
- Our Toast expertise is a durable competitive advantage — most marketing agencies are generalists. We become the specialists restaurant operators trust because we understand their stack.
- The integration product creates compounding moat: we turn Toast POS data into marketing intelligence that no general agency can replicate manually.

## Phase 2: Proof of Concept (Days 31-90)

### Objective

Sign first 2-3 paying restaurant clients. Execute a 90-day marketing program for each. Document measurable results that become the proof points for all future sales. During this phase, execution quality matters more than speed of acquisition.

### 2.1 Client Acquisition (Days 31-60)

**Primary Acquisition Channels:**

**Channel 1: Direct Outreach to Restaurant Owners**

Email sequence for cold leads:
- Email 1 (Day 1): Value-led introduction. Subject: "Your Toast data is making you less money than it should." Body: 3-paragraph email about the unused marketing potential in their Toast guest list, ending with a specific question ("Do you have 15 minutes to see what your email list could be generating?").
- Email 2 (Day 4): Case/proof point lead. Share a specific example of what a similar restaurant did with email marketing (even a hypothetical built from industry data if no case study yet).
- Email 3 (Day 10): Soft offer. Offer a free 30-minute Toast marketing audit — we look at their actual platform and tell them what they're missing. Zero sales pressure.
- Email 4 (Day 17): Final follow-up. Quick one-liner asking if they want the audit.

**Channel 2: Restaurant Owner Facebook Groups**

Join 5-10 local and national restaurant owner Facebook groups. Do not immediately pitch. Spend 2 weeks contributing genuinely helpful answers to marketing questions. Then introduce your agency when appropriate or when directly asked.

**Channel 3: Local Restaurant Associations**

Contact the state chapter of the National Restaurant Association and local hospitality associations (NYC hospitality coalition, Chicago Restaurant Association, etc.). Introduce as a Toast-specialized marketing resource. Ask if they have newsletters or partner directories for members.

**Channel 4: Toast Account Managers and Certified Partners**

Toast has account managers assigned to restaurant clients. Approach LinkedIn:
- Connect with Toast account managers in target geographies
- Send a positioning message: "I run a marketing agency that specializes in helping Toast restaurants get more out of their platform. If you ever have clients who are struggling with marketing, I'd love to be a referral resource — no strings attached."
- Offer to pay referral fees ($200-$500 per closed client, or first month revenue split)

**The Free Audit Offer (High-Conversion Tactic):**

A "Toast Marketing Audit" is the easiest first step for restaurant owners. It costs them nothing, requires 30 minutes of their time, and gives us a chance to demonstrate our knowledge of their specific platform. Structure:

1. Ask them to share their Toast backend (screen share or read-only access)
2. Review: Guest email list size, last email sent, loyalty enrollment rate, online ordering conversion, Google Business Profile status
3. Deliver a 1-page findings report with 3-5 specific recommendations and the revenue impact of each
4. At end: "We can implement all of this for you. That's what we do."

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] 100 outreach emails sent in first 3 weeks
- [ ] 15-20 discovery calls completed
- [ ] 10 free Toast audits delivered
- [ ] First signed client by Day 45

### 2.2 Onboarding First Clients (Days 45-60)

**First client pricing strategy:** Offer first 3 clients at 50% standard rate in exchange for:
- 90-day case study permission
- Willingness to share revenue/traffic data
- Video testimonial if results are strong

This is not charity — it is buying proof points that will convert future clients at full price.

**Client Onboarding Process:**

1. **Kickoff call** (Day 1 of engagement): Establish goals, access to platforms, identify key dates and events in next 90 days
2. **Platform access**: Toast (read access to analytics, email tools, loyalty data), Google Business Profile, Instagram/Facebook, any existing email platform
3. **Brand audit**: Review all existing marketing materials, photos, tone of voice
4. **90-day marketing calendar**: Build and present for client approval (seasonal events, promotions, content themes)
5. **Week 1 deliverables**: First email campaign draft, first week of social posts, Google Business Profile updated

### 2.3 Executing the 90-Day Marketing Program

For each client in this phase, the core program is:

**Email Marketing (Powered by Toast Data):**
- Month 1: Set up properly segmented email list from Toast guest data. Send welcome sequence to subscribers who have never received an email. Send re-engagement campaign to lapsed guests (60+ days since last visit).
- Month 2: Launch loyalty-tier email program (different messaging for top 20% spenders vs. first-timers). Execute seasonal campaign for next major holiday or event.
- Month 3: Measure repeat visit rate change vs. baseline. A/B test subject lines. Document results.

**Social Media Content:**
- 3-4 posts per week on Instagram and/or Facebook
- Content mix: 60% food/experience content, 20% behind-the-scenes/story, 20% promotion/offer
- Month 1: Establish aesthetic consistency and voice
- Month 2: Run one paid promotion (even $150-$300 budget) for a specific offer or event
- Month 3: Evaluate what content drove the most engagement and referral traffic

**Google Business Profile:**
- Claim and fully optimize (photos, description, hours, attributes)
- Set up weekly post cadence
- Implement review response template and respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Submit for Google local SEO best practices (consistent NAP, keyword-rich description)

**Seasonal Event Marketing:**
Identify 1-2 major restaurant events in the 90-day window (Mother's Day, local food festival, back-to-school, summer kickoff, etc.) and build a full campaign: email sequence, social posts, PR angle if relevant.

**Measurement:**
Track against baseline (establish in Week 1):
- Email list size and growth
- Email open rate and click rate
- Repeat guest rate (from Toast analytics)
- Google Business Profile views and actions
- Social followers and engagement rate
- Revenue on key event days vs. prior year

### 2.4 Documenting Results (Days 75-90)

The case study is the most important deliverable of Phase 2. Structure:

**Case Study Format:**
- Restaurant name and profile (with their permission, or anonymized)
- The situation before (specific numbers: email list size, last email sent, Google review status)
- What we did (90-day program summary — not marketing fluff, just the specific tactics)
- The results (specific numbers: repeat visit rate change, email-driven revenue, review count change)
- A quote from the owner

Even modest results told with precision are compelling. "Restaurant X had never sent a marketing email. We built their guest list to 847 contacts, ran 4 campaigns in 90 days, and their Tuesday-Thursday cover count increased 18% in month 3" is a winning case study.

### 3.2 Toast API Integration Proof of Concept (Days 100-150)

**Goal:** Build a working prototype of a Toast-connected marketing automation that demonstrates the product concept, even if it is not yet a polished SaaS product.

**Phase 3 Integration Scope (MVP):**

The MVP integration should accomplish one thing exceptionally well: **automated re-engagement email based on guest lapse data from Toast.**

How it works:
1. Read Toast guest order history via Toast API
2. Identify guests who visited within the last 6 months but have not returned in 45+ days
3. Automatically trigger a personalized re-engagement email via the email marketing platform (Brevo, Mailchimp, or similar)
4. Track whether those guests return within 30 days (close the loop with Toast order data)

This single workflow — which no general marketing agency can execute without the API integration — is a killer demo feature and a genuine revenue driver for clients.

**Technical Steps:**
- [ ] Review Toast API documentation at dev.toasttab.com
- [ ] Obtain API credentials (requires applying for developer access)
- [ ] Build data extraction script: pull guest visit history, identify lapsed segments
- [ ] Connect to email platform API to trigger automated sends
- [ ] Build simple reporting dashboard (even a Google Sheet with formulas is sufficient at MVP stage)
- [ ] Test with one client's live Toast data (with their written permission)
- [ ] Document results from first automated campaign

**Technical Roles Needed:** One developer or technical co-founder to build the integration. This can be outsourced or contracted for Phase 3 MVP. Full-time technical hire is Phase 4.

**Estimated Development Cost (MVP):** $3,000-$8,000 for a contracted developer to build the initial API connection and email trigger workflow.

## Sales Playbook

### Overview

The sales process for restaurant clients is short when leads are warm (Toast AM referral, restaurant association intro) and medium when cold (direct outreach). Target sales cycle: 7-21 days from first contact to signed agreement.

### Step 1: Lead Qualification

**Qualifying questions for every prospect:**

1. "How long have you been using Toast?" (Want 12+ months — they have enough data for us to work with)
2. "How are you currently handling your marketing?" (Listen for: nothing, or doing it myself poorly)
3. "Are you actively using Toast's email and loyalty features?" (If yes, great — we build on it. If no, even better — there is more opportunity.)
4. "What does a slow Tuesday look like for you, and what do you do to drive traffic on a night like that?" (Reveals pain and sophistication level)
5. "If marketing was working for you — really working — what would that look like in your business?" (Reveals their success picture, which we use in the proposal)

**Disqualify if:**
- On a non-Toast POS (we cannot deliver our core value)
- Below $500K revenue (budget constraint)
- Chain with a marketing department (wrong target)
- Looking for a one-time website build or logo (not our service)

### Step 2: The Toast Marketing Audit (Free)

Offer every qualified prospect a free 30-minute Toast Marketing Audit over video call. During the call:

1. Ask them to share their screen or grant you view access to their Toast backend
2. Review the following (takes 10-15 minutes):
   - Guest email list size and engagement status
   - Last marketing email sent (date, subject, open rate if visible)
   - Loyalty program enrollment count (% of guests enrolled)
   - Google Business Profile status (claimed? Review response rate? Recent posts?)
   - Online ordering conversion metrics
3. Deliver your findings live, conversationally — not a formal presentation
4. End with: "You have more marketing potential here than you're using. We can help you activate it. Want to see a proposal?"

**The audit converts at high rates because:**
- It is free, so the barrier is zero
- Seeing their own platform through an expert's eyes is compelling
- The conversation naturally identifies their pain points in their own words, which we mirror back in the proposal

### Step 3: The Proposal

Deliver proposals within 24-48 hours of the audit. Format:
- 2-3 pages maximum
- Open with their specific situation ("You have 847 guests who ordered online in the last 6 months and have never received an email from you. That is your first campaign.")
- Recommend a specific tier with rationale
- Show projected results (conservative, based on industry benchmarks and comparable case studies)
- Price with all fees itemized
- Clear next step: "Sign the agreement and we start your onboarding call within 48 hours"

### Step 4: Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We don't have the budget right now" | "What if we could show you that this program pays for itself? Our typical client sees enough incremental repeat visits in 60 days to cover the monthly fee. Let's start with our Tier 1 and prove the model." |
| "I've worked with marketing agencies before and they didn't deliver" | "That is the most common thing I hear. Most agencies don't understand restaurants — they apply the same tactics they use for dentists and HVAC companies. We only work with Toast restaurants. We know your metrics, your seasonality, your margin constraints." |
| "I can do this myself" | "You can. The question is whether you will. Restaurant operators I work with are managing kitchens, staff, and customers 12 hours a day. Marketing keeps falling to the bottom of the list. We handle it so you don't have to think about it." |
| "Can we start smaller?" | "Absolutely. Our Tier 1 at $750/month is designed for exactly that. We prove the model in 90 days, then discuss next steps." |
| "Toast already has marketing tools built in" | "They do — and we use them for you. The tools are there; the execution is the hard part. Toast can give you a kitchen, but it doesn't cook the food." |

### Step 5: Closing and Contract

- Use a simple 1-2 page service agreement with clear scope, monthly fee, payment terms, and termination notice
- Payment: Invoice first month + setup fee (if applicable) before work begins
- Minimum commitment: 3 months for Tier 1-2; 6 months for Tier 3 (gives time to show results and prevents churning before ROI is visible)

## Toast API Integration Roadmap

### Overview

The Toast API integration is the product that separates this agency from every generalist competitor. It turns raw POS transaction data into automated, personalized marketing. Built in phases, it becomes a standalone SaaS product by Month 12.

### Phase 1: Research and Access (Days 100-110)

- [ ] Review Toast's developer documentation at dev.toasttab.com
- [ ] Understand available API endpoints: Orders, Guests, Loyalty, Menu, Payments
- [ ] Apply for Toast developer access (requires business registration)
- [ ] Understand data privacy requirements (PCI compliance, guest data handling)
- [ ] Identify which client(s) will serve as the integration pilot (requires written permission)

**Key Toast API Endpoints for Marketing Use:**
- `GET /orders` — Full order history by guest, including spend, items, visit frequency, and recency
- `GET /loyalty/accounts` — Loyalty program enrollment and point/tier status by guest
- `GET /guests` — Guest profiles with contact info and visit history
- `GET /menus` — Menu structure and item performance data (useful for content creation)

### Phase 2: MVP Build (Days 110-150)

**MVP Scope:** One automated workflow, fully functional.

**Workflow: Guest Lapse Re-Engagement**

```
[TRIGGER] Guest has not visited in 45 days
     |
[TOAST API] Read last visit date + average spend + typical order type
     |
[SEGMENT] High-value lapsed (avg spend $50+) vs. standard lapsed
     |
[EMAIL PLATFORM] Trigger personalized email
  - High-value: "We miss you. Here's 20% off your next visit."
  - Standard: "Come back this week — here's a treat on us."
     |
[TRACKING] 30-day window: Did they return? Log outcome in reporting.
     |
[REPORT] Monthly: How many lapsed guests were re-engaged? Revenue generated from campaign?
```

**Technical Stack (Recommended):**
- Toast API: Python or Node.js wrapper for API calls
- Email platform: Brevo (already in use) or Mailchimp via API
- Database: SQLite or PostgreSQL for storing guest segments and campaign history
- Reporting: Google Sheets or simple web dashboard

**Development timeline:** 4-6 weeks for contracted developer

### Phase 3: Expansion Features (Months 6-9)

After MVP is validated:

| Feature | Description | Development Estimate |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| Slow-day revenue boost | Identify historically slow days from order history; trigger promotions automatically | 2-3 weeks |
| Review request automation | Post-visit email to high-satisfaction guests requesting Google review | 1-2 weeks |
| New guest welcome sequence | Trigger 3-email welcome series after first visit detected in Toast | 2-3 weeks |
| Loyalty tier campaigns | Automated emails when guest crosses loyalty point threshold | 2-3 weeks |
| Menu item recommendation | Based on past orders, recommend new menu items in email content | 3-4 weeks |

### Phase 4: Product Launch (Months 10-12)

- Productize the integration as a standalone SaaS product
- Build simple onboarding flow (restaurant owner connects their Toast account via OAuth)
- Create subscription billing infrastructure
- Submit to Toast Marketplace
- Price: $149-$249/month standalone; included in Tier 3 managed services

### Integration Compliance Notes

- Toast partner program approval likely required for full API access and Marketplace listing
- Guest data handling must comply with applicable privacy laws (CAN-SPAM, state privacy laws)
- Restaurant operators must provide written consent to connect their Toast data to third-party marketing tools
- PCI compliance considerations for any systems that touch payment data (avoid — use only guest/order data, not payment card data)
