## Post 2 (Insight)
Building agents used to require a dedicated engineering team.

Now it's a configuration step. Low-code platforms. Managed runtimes. Pre-built tool connections.

This is classic infrastructure commoditization. Same pattern that turned web hosting from a specialized skill into a dropdown menu.

## Post 3 (The Gap)
Here's what every "build your first agent" tutorial skips:

They all stop at deployment.

Nobody asks: is this the right task for an agent? Does this decision improve the business or just automate a process nobody examined?

The strategy gap is real.

## Post 4 (Framework)
Before deploying any agent, three questions:

1. What decision does this agent make, and who currently owns it?
2. What does failure look like, and who notices?
3. What changes when this works?

If you can't answer all three, you're automating on autopilot.
