Project Estimate Rationale & Comparables
Tarlac Data Center
Philippines / Indo-Pacific
$300M–$500M
MAKR-owned site in New Clark City, Tarlac, DFC's most active Indo-Pacific market. The Philippines is now the fastest-growing data centre market in Southeast Asia, with construction costs averaging $6.5–7.5M/MW. A sovereign-aligned, DFC-backed facility on owned land would target 50–75MW initial capacity with multi-phase expansion rights. The site's proximity to Narra Technology Park positions it within an emerging hyperscale corridor that is attracting $2.7B+ in committed investment.
Comparable: Narra Technology Park (also Tarlac) at $890M per 100MW phase, $2.7B total across 300MW. Philippine DC market projected to reach $1.6B annually by 2034.
Philippine News Agency, Jan 2026
Baltic Subsea Cable Infrastructure
Europe / Baltic
$500M–$800M
Multi-country NATO-allied cable network with European Commission and European Investment Bank support in principle. This is not a commercial cable. It is security-grade critical digital infrastructure for NATO allies, designed in direct response to the Baltic Sea cable sabotage incidents of 2024–2025. Security-grade subsea cables require hardened landing stations, redundant routing, surveillance integration, and military-standard encryption, all of which command a 2–3x premium over commercial equivalents. NATO launched "Baltic Sentry" specifically to protect these assets. The EU has allocated €533M for submarine cable projects under CEF Digital (2024–2027), with €267M in CPEI funding specifically for 2026–2027.
Comparable: WorldLink (Abu Dhabi to Europe via Iraq) at $700M. Google's Umoja (Africa) at $1B+. EU CEF Digital total allocation €533M. GlobalConnect domestic Baltic ring at €40M (commercial grade, much smaller scope).
EU Commission / NATO / Atlantic Council, 2025-2026
AI-Enabled Emergency Response
GCC / Middle East
$150M–$300M
Multi-city AI command platform with predictive analytics, multi-sensor fusion, edge processing, and automated threat detection for civilian protection across one or more GCC states. The GCC is the world's most aggressive investor in AI-enabled public safety. Abu Dhabi alone is spending $3.5B+ on government automation by 2027. Saudi Arabia committed $200M in a single smart city vision JV with Dahua, and is coordinating a $100B national AI initiative. MAKR's proposal specifies "disaster resilience and predictive analytics" which implies a full operational intelligence layer, not just cameras and sensors, but the command architecture that connects them across emergency services, environmental monitoring, and civilian alert systems.
Comparable: Saudi/Dahua smart city JV at $200M (vision only, single partner). Abu Dhabi government automation at $3.5B (of which emergency response is one vertical). Stargate UAE Phase 1 at $200MW scale.
Digital Bricks / MEI / Introl, 2025
Subsea Cable Infrastructure (Bypass)
GCC / Middle East
$600M–$1B
Alternative data routes securing global internet traffic at critical chokepoints. This is the single most urgent infrastructure priority in the Gulf right now. The Red Sea cable disruptions from the Yemen conflict have severed or threatened 16+ submarine cables carrying an estimated 17% of global internet traffic. Six competing fibre corridors are being financed by Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE through Syria, Iraq, and East Africa as emergency alternatives. Ooredoo alone committed $500M for a single overland bypass route. MAKR's "critical chokepoints" language and the strategic framing as a DFC-relevant project (countering adversarial control of data routes) positions this as a multi-route, multi-landing-station system with sovereign data sovereignty protections.
Comparable: Ooredoo $500M (single overland route, Iraq-Turkey-Europe). WorldLink $700M (Abu Dhabi to Europe subsea). Ooredoo FiG 720-Tbps all-GCC interconnect. Google Umoja $1B+ (Africa subsea). 2Africa cable $3B+ (Meta-led, 37 countries).

Revised Global Pipeline

With TBD values estimated at the upper range of defensible comparables, the MAKR global pipeline moves from $19B+ to approximately $20.5B–$21.6B across 33 projects in 7 regions. The four previously undisclosed lines represent $1.55B–$2.6B in additional pipeline value, concentrated in the GCC and Europe, two regions where DFC is actively expanding its mandate under the BUILD Act reauthorisation.

Methodology

How These Estimates Were Derived

Each estimate was built from three inputs: (1) comparable projects in the same geography and sector, sourced from 2025–2026 market data; (2) internal pipeline consistency checks against regional sub-totals stated in the proposal; and (3) unit economics for each infrastructure type ($/MW for data centres, $/km for subsea cable, platform licensing benchmarks for AI systems).

Estimates are positioned at the upper range of defensible market comparables, reflecting the sovereign-grade, security-critical nature of each project, the DFC co-financing structure which attracts larger capital stacks, and the current geopolitical urgency driving accelerated investment in each sector. These are pre-feasibility estimates. Final values will be refined through engineering, procurement, and co-financing structuring.
