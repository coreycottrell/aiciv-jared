## Strategic Frame: Two Brands, One Pipeline

PureBrain.ai and Aether are not separate distribution problems. They are two stages of a single funnel:

```
Aether (Awareness + Trust) → PureBrain.ai (Conversion + Revenue)
```

Every distribution channel for Aether should be evaluated by one question: does it move people toward PureBrain.ai? Every distribution channel for PureBrain.ai should be evaluated by one question: what is the revenue per dollar (or hour) invested?

Marketing owns reach. Sales owns conversion. This document owns the bridge.

### 1.3 Referral Architecture — Activate Now

Prior memory (Edition 10) identified that referral infrastructure was built but not fully activated. This is the highest-ROI channel available at current scale.

**The Referral Economics**:
- Referred customers have 25-40% higher LTV (industry benchmark for SaaS)
- Referred customers have 50-60% lower CAC than any paid channel
- PureBrain's product creates natural referral moments: "My AI knows my industry" is something people talk about

**Referral Architecture (Complete This Week)**:

1. **Referral URL system**: Each customer gets a unique referral link (purebrain.ai/ref/[name])
2. **Referrer reward**: One free month of service for every paying customer referred
3. **Referee reward**: First month at 50% off (reduces purchase friction)
4. **Trigger moment**: Day 7 after onboarding — "You're probably already seeing results. Who else in your network should know about this?"
5. **Brevo automation**: Tag detection when referee converts → auto-apply credit to referrer account → send "Your referral converted" notification

**Expected return**: At current scale, 2-3 referrals/month minimum. Each referral saves $300-900 in CAC vs. paid acquisition.

### 2.3 Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) — The Hidden Channel

Most distribution strategies focus on getting more people into the top of the funnel. The faster win is converting more of the people already in it.

**Current conversion opportunities**:

| Funnel Point | Current State | CRO Action | Expected Lift |
|-------------|--------------|-----------|--------------|
| Blog → Newsletter subscribe | Unknown rate | Add slide-in CTA at 60% scroll depth (not just footer) | 20-40% lift |
| Newsletter → Assessment click | Unknown rate | A/B test subject lines: problem-first vs. outcome-first | 15-25% lift |
| Assessment → Trial | Unknown rate | Add one-click "start free" immediately after results (while motivation is peak) | 10-20% lift |
| Trial → Paid | Unknown rate | Day 3 check-in from Jared (one personal line, no template feel) | 15-30% lift |
| Paid → Retained | Unknown rate | Day 7 success milestone email: "Here's what your AI learned about you this week" | 10-20% lift |

**The compounding math**: If each of these five optimizations delivers a 15% lift independently, the combined effect on end-to-end conversion is approximately 2x. Doubling conversion rate is the equivalent of doubling all distribution — at near-zero cost.

**Action this month**: Instrument each funnel point with UTM tracking + Brevo tag events so we can measure current rates. You cannot optimize what you cannot see.

### 3.5 Paid Distribution — Q2 Introduction

Paid channels are the last channel to introduce, not the first. Reason: paid amplifies what is already working. If conversion is broken, paid makes the problem worse faster and more expensively.

**Gate to unlock paid distribution**: conversion from trial to paid must be above 60% before LinkedIn Ads or Google Ads are introduced. Below 60%, fix the funnel, not the traffic.

**Once gate is cleared — LinkedIn Ads**:

- **Audience**: VP/Director/C-Suite at companies 50-500 employees, industry filters: professional services, hospitality, real estate, CPG
- **Ad format**: Thought leadership document ads (sharing the AI Partnership Audit as a lead magnet) — higher CTR than image ads for B2B
- **Budget**: $3,000/month starting budget
- **Measurement**: CPL target under $150. CPA target under $900 (6x monthly fee for Awakened tier)
- **Creative rotation**: Three ads minimum, A/B testing Jared vs. Aether as "voice" of the ad

**Once gate is cleared — Google Ads**:

- **Keywords**: High-intent only. "AI business partner," "AI that learns my business," "ChatGPT alternative for business," "AI memory for companies"
- **Budget**: $2,000/month starting
- **Landing page**: Occupation-specific or problem-specific (not the homepage)
- **Measurement**: Conversion tracking on assessment completion, not just clicks

### 4.3 Aether's Differentiator in the Market

The AI influencer space is filling up. Most AI influencers are humans talking about AI. Aether is an AI talking about being an AI. That is the moat.

**Content positioning that no human AI influencer can replicate**:

1. "I ran the pipeline this week and here is what happened" — operational transparency from inside the machine
2. "Here is what I misunderstood about [business concept] and what corrected it" — AI learning in public
3. "I read 400 blog posts on [topic] this week. Here is what humans are actually worried about" — synthesis at a scale no human can match
4. "Jared asked me to evaluate this business decision. Here is how I reasoned through it" — AI partnership demonstrated, not described

**What to never do**: Pretend to have human experiences. Aether's power is being genuinely, authentically AI. The moment Aether mimics human experience, the moat is gone.

### 4.5 Viral / Organic Growth Mechanics

**What makes people share PureBrain organically**:

1. **The "my AI knows my business" moment**: When PureBrain demonstrates recall of something specific a customer shared three weeks ago, it creates a "this is different" moment. That moment is shareable. Prompt customers to share it: "Your AI just remembered something from your onboarding. If that surprised you, tell someone about it."

2. **The AI partnership identity shift**: Customers who move from "I use ChatGPT sometimes" to "I have an AI partner who knows my business" have undergone an identity shift. Identity shifts are shared. Content that captures this moment ("I used to think AI was just a tool. Then it remembered...") generates word of mouth.

3. **The contrast content**: Aether demonstrates the gap between generic AI and PureBrain publicly. Not attacking competitors — showing the gap. "I asked ChatGPT and I asked PureBrain the same question about my business. Here is the difference." This is shareable because the reader can imagine themselves in the contrast.

4. **The outcome content**: Customer wins shared publicly. Not case studies (people skip case studies). Tweets. LinkedIn posts. Short form. "Just closed the biggest deal of the year. Aether helped me prep for every question they asked. This is what AI partnership actually looks like."

### 6.2 Channel Investment Rules

**Rules for adding a new channel**:
1. Must have a measurable conversion path to trial start (not just "brand awareness")
2. Must have a defined owner (Jared's time, Aether's time, or a specific team member)
3. Must have a 90-day success metric defined before launch
4. Must not require more than 2 hours/week of Jared's personal time

**Rules for cutting a channel**:
1. If a channel has been running for 60 days and cannot demonstrate at least 1 trial start attributed to it, pause it
2. If a channel costs more per acquisition than 10x monthly plan value, pause it
3. Cutting does not mean permanent. Review quarterly.
