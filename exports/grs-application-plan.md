## PART 4: LINKEDIN ADS SETUP GUIDE

### Overview

LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads (TLAs) let you pay to show your personal posts to a targeted audience. They appear in the LinkedIn feed as your post, with a "Promoted" label. They get 2.68% median CTR vs 0.42% for standard company ads.

GRS uses these not to run cold ads, but to guarantee that your best organic content reaches your exact ICP. Post organically first. Wait for signals. Then boost.

---

### Step-by-Step Campaign Setup

**Before you start**: You need access to a LinkedIn Campaign Manager account tied to a company page. PureBrain.ai's LinkedIn company page should already have this. If not, create it at business.linkedin.com.

**Step 1: Post Organically First**
- Publish your post normally on your personal LinkedIn profile
- Wait 60-90 minutes for initial organic engagement
- If the post gets comments from ICP-matching profiles in the first 90 minutes, it is worth boosting

**Step 2: Open Campaign Manager**
- Go to business.linkedin.com
- Click "Create campaign"
- Select your Campaign Group (create one called "GRS - Belief Shifting Posts" and one called "GRS - Direct Offers")

**Step 3: Select Campaign Objective**
- Choose "Engagement" as the objective
- Why: Engagement objective produces $4.80 average CPC vs $7.90-12 for Brand Awareness — roughly half the cost for landing page traffic

**Step 4: Define Your Target Audience**

For PureBrain's ICP, use these targeting parameters:

Audience 1 — SMB Decision Makers (Primary)
- Job Titles: CEO, COO, President, VP Operations, Chief Digital Officer, VP Technology, Director of Operations
- Company Size: 50-500 employees
- Industries: Professional Services, Management Consulting, Financial Services, Healthcare Administration, Technology (non-enterprise)
- Geography: United States

Audience 2 — Growth-Stage Companies (Secondary — higher conversion intent)
- All of the above, plus:
- Member Groups: Companies that have recently raised funding (Series A-C)
- Seniority: Director and above only
- Skills: Digital Transformation, Business Process Improvement, Strategic Planning

Audience 3 — Retargeting (Activate when you hit 300+ engagers)
- LinkedIn will build this audience automatically from people who engaged with your posts
- Use this to run Direct Offer posts (Bucket 3) to people who already know your content

**Step 5: Disable Audience Expansion and LinkedIn Audience Network**
- In the targeting section, click "More targeting options"
- Uncheck "Enable Audience Expansion" — this prevents LinkedIn from expanding beyond your defined audience
- In the Placements section, uncheck "LinkedIn Audience Network" — this keeps your ads only on LinkedIn, not third-party sites
- Why: You want precision, not scale. Every dollar should reach your specific ICP.

**Step 6: Select Your Ad Creative**
- Click "Browse existing content"
- Select the member post you want to promote (this must be a post from your personal LinkedIn account)
- LinkedIn will use the exact post as the ad creative
- Only posts from the last 12 months are eligible

**Step 7: Set Budget and Bidding**
- Daily budget: Start with $10/day for Belief-Shifting posts, $20-30/day for Direct Offer posts
- Bidding: Start with automated bidding for the first two weeks to get baseline data
- After week two, if you want tighter cost control, switch to manual bidding
- Manual bid starting point: Set your bid at 15% above LinkedIn's suggested range
- Why above the range: LinkedIn distributes budget to highest bidders first. Going 15% above suggested gets you into the auction without massively overpaying.

**Step 8: Set Campaign Duration**
- Run Belief-Shifting posts: 5-7 days per post
- Run Direct Offer posts: 3-5 days per post
- Do not let campaigns run indefinitely — fatigue sets in after 7 days and CPCs rise

**Step 9: Monitor and Optimize**
- Check results after 48 hours
- If cost per engagement is above $3.00, pause and adjust targeting
- If comment rate from ICP titles is strong, increase daily budget by $5/day
- Use the Companies tab in Campaign Manager to see which companies are engaging with your content
