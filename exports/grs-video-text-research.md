The GRS appears to operate on three interlocking levers:

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards active participation and gives posts a 90% algorithmic boost when the author is actively responding. This is not accidental - it is a scheduled activity.

## Part 2: The Video-to-Text Workflow (Detailed)

Based on Jakub's background at Demandi.co and the broader methodology he developed, here is the reconstructed workflow:

### Step 1: Raw Capture (The Hard Part Made Easy)
Record yourself speaking about your topic. This can be:
- A 2-5 minute voice note on your phone
- A short selfie video
- A longer talking-head video
- Podcast/interview audio

The key insight: speaking is faster and more authentic than writing. CEOs and founders think in voice, not in text. Starting with video/audio captures natural personality and removes writer's block.

### Step 2: Transcription
Use AI transcription tools (Otter.ai, Whisper, or built-in transcription in Descript, etc.) to convert to raw text. The transcript will be rough - filler words, incomplete sentences, stream of consciousness.

### Step 3: AI Prompting Workflow (The "Secret Sauce")
This is where Jakub built significant expertise. The raw transcript is processed through structured AI prompts designed to extract LinkedIn-optimized content. Based on his work at Demandi.co, the workflow involves:

- **Hook extraction**: Pull the most provocative or curiosity-driving statement from the transcript
- **Structure formatting**: Convert stream-of-consciousness into LinkedIn-native formats (short paragraphs, white space, clear flow)
- **Personality preservation**: Maintain the speaker's authentic voice and specific word choices - not generic corporate language
- **CTA integration**: Add a natural question or call-to-action at the end that invites comments

The prompts are designed to produce "high-converting LinkedIn content" - meaning content that generates comments, not just likes.

### Step 4: Format Selection
Based on the content type, choose the format:
- **Short punchy text post**: Personal insight, contrarian take, story-driven point
- **Listicle format**: "X things I learned about Y" - works for tactical content
- **Carousel/Document**: For complex frameworks, step-by-step processes
- **Native video**: Keep the original if it's clean enough (sub-90 seconds performs best)

### Step 5: The Content Atomization Expansion
One video/voice note generates multiple assets:
1. Original text post (primary distribution)
2. 2-3 short follow-up posts expanding on sub-points
3. A carousel summarizing key points
4. Newsletter segment (re-use the content for email list)

This is the "waterfall model" - one raw input becomes a week of content.

### Step 6: Publishing with Golden Hour Protocol
- Post when you can dedicate 60-90 minutes to active engagement
- Respond to every early comment within 15 minutes
- Ask questions back to commenters to extend thread depth
- Do NOT include external links in the post (60% reach reduction penalty)

## Part 7: 2026 LinkedIn Algorithm Context

This section provides the broader platform landscape the GRS operates within.

### What the Algorithm Rewards in 2026

1. **Dwell time**: How long people spend reading/watching your content. Longer = more reach.
2. **Comment quality over quantity**: Thoughtful multi-sentence comments outperform one-word replies 3x for reach amplification.
3. **First-hour engagement velocity**: Fast early engagement triggers broader distribution.
4. **Author response speed**: Replying to comments within 15 minutes gives 90% algorithmic boost.
5. **Native content**: Content that keeps users on LinkedIn rather than clicking away.

### What the Algorithm Penalizes in 2026

1. **External links in posts**: 60% reach reduction. Non-negotiable penalty.
2. **Engagement pods**: LinkedIn now detects and suppresses coordinated inauthentic engagement.
3. **Posting frequency spikes**: Sudden increase in posting frequency with lower engagement quality suppresses all your posts, not just weak ones.
4. **Obviously AI-generated content**: Content lacking human perspective gets deprioritized.

### Format Performance Rankings (2026)

1. **Document/carousel PDFs**: Highest engagement at 6.6% engagement rate. Users spend time scrolling.
2. **Short text posts with strong hooks**: Excellent reach per follower. Clean, minimal formatting.
3. **Native video (under 90 seconds)**: 5x higher interaction for awareness content. Must have captions.
4. **LinkedIn Live**: 29.6% engagement rates but high production barrier.
5. **Single image posts**: Underperforming vs 2024 - 30% less reach than text-only equivalent.

### Posting Frequency Sweet Spot

3-4 high-quality posts per week. NOT daily posting (unless engagement quality remains high). The algorithm tracks your baseline engagement rate - posting more low-quality content drags down performance of all posts.

## Part 9: Templates and Frameworks

### The GRS Post Structure Formula

```
[HOOK - Single provocative or curiosity-driving statement]

[BRIDGE - One sentence connecting hook to the insight]

[BODY - 3-5 short paragraphs, each one idea, max 2-3 sentences]
- Paragraph 1: The problem or contrast
- Paragraph 2: The insight or turning point
- Paragraph 3: The practical implication
- Paragraph 4: The unexpected angle (optional)
- Paragraph 5: The so-what

[CTA - Question that invites a comment, or a provocative closing line]
```

### Voice Note to Post AI Prompt Template

```
You are converting a voice note transcript into a LinkedIn post for [NAME].

Transcript: [PASTE RAW TRANSCRIPT]

Rules:
- Keep [NAME]'s authentic voice and specific phrases
- Strong hook in the first line (provocative, contrarian, or curiosity-driving)
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max each)
- White space between every paragraph
- End with a question or strong closing line
- Under 200 words total
- NO external links
- NO generic corporate language
- The post should make someone stop scrolling

Output the post only, no explanation.
```

### First-Comment Link Strategy
For posts where you want to drive traffic to purebrain.ai:

```
Post: [Value-packed content, no links]

First comment (post immediately after publishing):
"Full breakdown here for those who want to go deeper: [URL]"
```

This preserves reach while still capturing clicks from interested readers.
