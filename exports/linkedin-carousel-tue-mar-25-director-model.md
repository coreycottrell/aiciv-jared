## Slide Content

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**The Director Model**
How to manage AI like a direct report — not an intern

### Slide 2
**The Problem: Intern Mode**

Most teams treat AI like an intern:
- Give it a task
- Wait for output
- Fix what is wrong
- Repeat from scratch tomorrow

Result: You are doing the AI's job for it.
No compounding. No growth. No leverage.

### Slide 3
**The Shift: Director Mode**

Directors do not do the work. They set context, give feedback, and let their team grow.

The Director Model applies the same principle to AI:
- Set context once (deeply)
- Give corrections that persist
- Let the AI compound over time

### Slide 4
**The 3 Layers**

1. CONTEXT LAYER — Who you are, what you sell, how you talk, what you have tried before
2. FEEDBACK LAYER — Every correction becomes permanent training
3. AUTONOMY LAYER — The AI handles execution; you handle direction

Most AI tools only have Layer 1 (barely).
None of them have Layer 2.

### Slide 5
**What Changes**

With the Director Model:
- Monday's corrections make Tuesday's output better
- Your AI knows your brand voice after week 1
- By month 3, it drafts things you would actually send

Without it:
- Every session starts from zero
- You re-explain context every time
- The AI never gets better at YOUR work

### Slide 6 (CTA)
**Your AI should get better the longer you work with it.**

If it does not, you are stuck in Intern Mode.
