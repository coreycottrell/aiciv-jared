# PureBrain LinkedIn Outreach - Connection Request Templates

**Initiative**: 2 (LinkedIn Direct Outreach)
**Prepared by**: marketing-strategist
**Date**: 2026-02-24

---

## Critical Protocol: Blank Connection Requests Win

**Current LinkedIn data**: Blank connection requests accept at 68% vs. 32% for connection requests with a message.

**Reason**: Personalized requests often feel salesy. Blank requests, when preceded by genuine engagement (like + substantive comment), feel like natural networking. The comment you left is the introduction.

**Default behavior**: Send blank connection requests for all targets who have received the pre-warm sequence (react + comment). Only use connection message templates in the specific exceptions listed below.

---

## When to Use a Connection Message (Exceptions Only)

Use a connection message in these situations:

1. **You have not completed the pre-warm sequence** and the target is too high-value to wait another week.
2. **You share a mutual connection** who specifically told you to reach out.
3. **You met them at an event** (virtual or in-person) and they will not remember who you are without context.
4. **Their content directly mentions the exact problem PureBrain solves** (e.g., they posted about generic AI tools failing them yesterday).

In all other cases: blank request + pre-warm sequence is the protocol.

---

## Template CR-1: The Referral Introduction

**Use when**: A mutual connection told you to reach out.

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], [Mutual connection name] suggested I reach out — we're both working in
the [AI/coaching/consulting] space and they thought we'd have interesting things
to compare notes on. Happy to connect.
```

**Why it works**: Third-party credibility immediately changes the dynamic. They trust their connection's judgment. Your request becomes a favor to a mutual friend.

---

## Template CR-2: The Post Reaction

**Use when**: Their recent post directly described a frustration PureBrain solves (generic AI tools, context lost between sessions, AI that does not learn).

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], your post about [AI tool/generic AI frustration] resonated — that exact
tension is something we've been building toward solving at PureBrain. Would love
to connect and exchange notes.
```

**Why it works**: It proves you read their content, demonstrates relevance without pitching, and is short enough to not feel like a sales message.

---

## Template CR-3: The Event Follow-Up

**Use when**: You connected at a virtual event, podcast, webinar, or LinkedIn Live.

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], great to cross paths at [event name]. Your point about [something specific
they said] stuck with me. Connecting here to stay in touch.
```

**Why it works**: Specificity signals genuine attention. Most event follow-ups are generic. This one is not.

---

## Template CR-4: The Content Appreciation (High-Value Targets Only)

**Use when**: Target is a Priority 1 target with 10,000+ followers and you have not been able to complete a pre-warm sequence due to timeline pressure. Use sparingly.

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], I follow your content on [specific topic] — particularly appreciated
[one specific thing they have written or said]. Connecting to stay in the loop.
```

**Why it works**: Flattery does not work. Specific appreciation does. "I appreciate your take on why AI newsletters are becoming commoditized" beats "love your content" by a wide margin.

---

## Template CR-5: The Direct Transparency (For AI-Savvy Targets)

**Use when**: Target is an AI consultant or has explicitly posted about building AI-powered businesses. They will appreciate directness over relationship theater.

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], I'm Jared — founder of PureBrain. I follow your work on [AI topic]
and am building out our advisor network. Connecting to get on each other's radar.
```

**Why it works**: AI-native consultants see through the indirect approach. Naming the business and the intent upfront respects their time and signals that you are not here to waste it.

---

## Template CR-6: The Shared Niche

**Use when**: You and the target serve overlapping but non-competing audiences, and the connection is genuinely about mutual awareness.

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], we both serve [executives/business owners/knowledge workers] —
you on the [coaching/ops/leadership] side, us on the AI partnership side.
Seems worth being connected.
```

**Why it works**: "We serve the same people" is one of the highest-trust opening positions in LinkedIn networking. It signals collaboration potential, not sales motion.

---

## Template CR-7: The Specific Pain Post

**Use when**: Target has posted very recently (within 7 days) about a client situation that directly maps to PureBrain's use case (memory loss between AI sessions, generic tools failing, AI not understanding context).

**Character limit**: 300 characters max.

```
[Name], your post about [client situation/AI pain] — we built something
specifically for that problem. Happy to connect and share what we found.
```

**Why it works**: This is the rarest and highest-converting template because it meets them exactly where they are. Reserve it for situations where the fit is unmistakable.

## Quality Control Checklist (Before Sending Any Connection Request With Message)

- [ ] Message is under 300 characters
- [ ] First word is their name
- [ ] No PureBrain product pitch in the message
- [ ] No generic phrases ("love your content," "excited to connect," "thought leader")
- [ ] Includes at least one specific reference to something they said or did
- [ ] Does not ask for anything (call, meeting, favor) in the connection request

Connection requests that pass all six checks convert at significantly higher rates than those that skip the specificity requirement.

## What Not to Send (Anti-Examples)

**Do not send this**:
```
Hi [Name], I came across your profile and I think we have a lot in common.
I'm working on an exciting AI project and would love to connect with like-minded
professionals. Looking forward to connecting!
```
This fails because: generic, no specificity, "like-minded professionals" is meaningless, "exciting AI project" is a red flag phrase.

**Do not send this**:
```
Hi [Name], I'm the founder of PureBrain.ai, an AI platform that gives knowledge
workers a personalized AI partner that learns and remembers across sessions.
Would love to connect and tell you more!
```
This fails because: sales pitch in connection request, no reference to them specifically, asks for nothing specific but creates "oh this is a salesperson" impression.

**Do not send this**:
```
Hi [Name], I love your content and think you'd be a great fit for our affiliate
program. We pay 20% recurring commission. Would love to connect!
```
This fails because: jumping to ask before establishing any relationship, commission mention in connection request is disqualifying for most people.

## Notes on LinkedIn's Current Algorithm (February 2026)

LinkedIn's February 10, 2026 update specifically targets "engagement bait" and inauthentic connection patterns. Mass connection requests using the same template across hundreds of targets in a short window may trigger their spam detection.

Maintain outreach at human scale: no more than 20 new connection requests per week. This keeps you under LinkedIn's radar and maintains quality anyway — 20 well-targeted, pre-warmed connections per week is sustainable and effective.
