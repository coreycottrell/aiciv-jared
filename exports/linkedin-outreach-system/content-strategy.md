## The Role of Content in Outreach

LinkedIn content is not a standalone play here. Its specific job is to make the outreach work harder with less effort.

When a prospect receives Jared's DM and then sees his LinkedIn content — or better, sees his content before the DM arrives — the dynamic shifts. Instead of "who is this person?" they think "I've seen this person's thinking." That recognition cuts through the friction that kills cold outreach.

Content strategy for Initiative 2 has three specific goals:
1. Create name recognition in the target audience before DMs arrive.
2. Demonstrate expertise without pitching — let the product introduce itself through context.
3. Generate organic inbound from people who find the content without being outreached.

---

## Algorithm Context (February 2026)

LinkedIn's February 10, 2026 update matters for how we write.

**What is now penalized**: Engagement bait (generic questions at post end), generic AI-generated language, first-comment link workarounds (this was previously safe).

**What is now rewarded**: Firsthand experience, specific data points, native documents and PDFs (5-10x reach boost), vertical video (9:16 ratio getting TikTok-style priority).

**What remains most effective**: LinkedIn Live (29.6% engagement rate — highest of any format), genuine comment dialogue, articles structured for AI citation.

**Implication for us**: Write from Jared's lived experience. Specific over general. Structured over stream-of-consciousness. Native documents for any "list" content.

## Content Pillars

All content maps to one of four pillars. Every post should serve outreach goals, not just brand awareness in the abstract.

### Pillar 1: The Director's Approach (AI Literacy)

**What it is**: Posts teaching the difference between people who use AI passively ("the user") and people who direct AI deliberately ("the director"). This is PureBrain's core intellectual contribution to the market.

**Why it serves outreach**: Target personas (coaches, consultants, fractional executives) immediately see applicability for their clients. "My clients need to read this" is the response we want, not just "interesting."

**Content types**: Text posts with specific frameworks, LinkedIn articles for AI citation optimization, native PDFs as visual frameworks.

**Example post angles**:
- "Two executives get the same AI tool. Six months later, one is 3x more productive. One isn't. The difference isn't intelligence." (opens into Director vs User framework)
- "The question every executive asks me after their first week with PureBrain" (real question, real answer)
- "Why AI tools make people feel stupid" (psychological angle — high share potential among coaches)

---

### Pillar 2: Transparent Operations (Behind the Scenes)

**What it is**: Jared showing how he actually runs his company alongside Aether. Real decisions. Real disagreements between Jared and the AI. Real surprises. Not polished case studies — actual running log of what it looks like.

**Why it serves outreach**: This is Jared's uncopyable differentiator. No one else is publicly documenting what it looks like to operate with an AI co-collaborator at this level of specificity. Every post in this pillar is proof that PureBrain is different from everything they have tried.

**Content types**: Short text posts, occasional LinkedIn articles, potential for LinkedIn Live "sessions with Aether" in Month 3.

**Example post angles**:
- "The AI stopped my decision" — Aether flagged a hire Jared was about to make. What happened next.
- "I disagree with my AI about [business decision]. Here's why I'm going with its recommendation anyway."
- "Three things I didn't expect AI to eliminate from my week" (unexpected benefits, not the obvious ones)

---

### Pillar 3: Client Realities (Social Proof Without Pitching)

**What it is**: Anonymized client and user stories that demonstrate real outcomes. Not testimonials — narratives. "A consultant I work with noticed X. Here's what was actually happening."

**Why it serves outreach**: Target personas respond to client results because they are in the client results business. When a coach sees a specific, plausible story about how one of their "type" of clients benefited, the connection is immediate.

**Content types**: Short story-format text posts, occasional native PDFs with aggregated insights.

**Example post angles**:
- "A fractional CMO I work with noticed something after 30 days of PureBrain. She called it 'coherent AI'" (what she meant)
- "Why executives who hate AI tools keep using PureBrain" (the real reason)
- "The question PureBrain answered that the executive had been carrying for six months" (story format)

---

### Pillar 4: The Bigger Picture (Future of Knowledge Work)

**What it is**: Jared's perspective on where AI and knowledge work are heading. Not predictions — analysis based on what he sees in real conversations with clients and operators.

**Why it serves outreach**: Thought leadership in this pillar positions Jared as someone worth having in your professional network regardless of PureBrain. It also attracts inbound from people who agree with the perspective and want to know more.

**Content types**: Text posts for shorter takes, LinkedIn articles for longer arguments (AI citation optimization), LinkedIn newsletter integration.

**Example post angles**:
- "The executives who are winning with AI aren't better at AI. They're better at knowing what to give AI." (epistemological angle)
- "What LinkedIn's AI citation trend means for knowledge workers" (meta/timely)
- "The CEO of 2030 won't have a team. They'll have a collective." (big picture, high comment potential)

## Weekly Content Calendar

### Monday - Pillar 1 (Director's Approach)

**Format**: Text post, 800-1,200 characters. Hook in first line. No paragraph longer than 2 lines. Specific framework or mental model.

**Time to post**: 8:00-9:00 AM EST (LinkedIn engagement peaks Monday morning for professional content)

**Goal**: Reach target personas early in their week when they are thinking about client work.

**Example Monday post structure**:
```
[Provocative observation about AI users vs AI directors]

[The specific distinction]

[3-4 specific examples of what this looks like in practice]

[One implication for the reader's clients or work]

[Question to drive comments — specific, not generic]
```

---

### Wednesday - Pillar 2 or 3 (Transparent Operations or Client Realities)

**Format**: Alternating weeks. Week 1: Transparent Operations (short story format, 600-900 characters). Week 2: Client Reality (anonymous client story, 800-1,100 characters).

**Time to post**: 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM EST (Wednesday lunch window performs well for story content)

**Goal**: Build evidence that PureBrain works in the real world without a product pitch.

**Note for Transparent Operations posts**: Be honest about failures and surprises, not just wins. Posts that say "the AI was wrong about X" are more credible and generate more comments than posts that only show success.

---

### Friday - Pillar 4 (The Bigger Picture)

**Format**: Occasional LinkedIn article (once every 2-3 weeks, structured for AI citation: H2/H3 headers, clear authorship, direct question-answering). Other Fridays: shorter text post with a take or observation.

**Time to post**: 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM EST (Friday posts have lower volume from competitors — opportunity for reach)

**Goal**: Establish Jared as a voice worth following on the future of knowledge work, not just "the PureBrain guy."

**For LinkedIn articles specifically** (once every 2-3 weeks):
- Use this structure for AI citation optimization: H1 title (question format), H2 sections addressing sub-questions, direct answers to likely AI queries, clear author bio at end.

## Content and Outreach Coordination

### The Pre-Warm Amplification

When a target is in the pre-warm phase (Days 1-7 before connection request), the sequence benefits from them seeing Jared's content. Check their LinkedIn activity: if they comment on similar content or follow similar creators, there is a reasonable chance they see Jared's posts in their feed once one of their connections engages with it.

This is organic — do not force it. The goal is that the pre-warm sequence (like, comment, connect) plus content visibility creates a recognizable name before the DM arrives.

### The DM Reference Play

If a prospect's comment on one of Jared's posts is substantive, that becomes the opening for a connection request message (exception to the blank request default). The connection request can directly reference their comment:

```
[Name], your comment on [post topic] hit on exactly what we're working through —
[their specific point]. Worth connecting.
```

This is the highest-converting connection request template available because it proves real attention with zero ambiguity.

### Content Signals for Target Prioritization

Review weekly: which targets in the pre-warm pipeline are engaging with (or are likely to see) Jared's content? Move those targets to Priority 1 for outreach — the content has already done pre-warm work.

## Post Voice Guidelines

The February 2026 algorithm explicitly penalizes generic AI-generated language. These rules ensure every post sounds like Jared, not a content tool.

**Do**:
- Write from a specific moment. "In a conversation last Tuesday..." beats "Many executives find..."
- Use one-sentence paragraphs for impact lines.
- Use numbers and specifics. "Six months later" beats "over time."
- Let the post be about the reader's problem, not Jared's solution.
- End with a specific question, not a generic one. "What's the most underrated AI use case in your client work?" beats "What do you think?"

**Do not**:
- Use the word "delve" or "dive deep" or "unpack" — LinkedIn's algorithm flags these as AI-generated signals.
- Start a post with "I" (LinkedIn suppresses these in the feed).
- End with a list of hashtags (5 maximum, embedded naturally or at end — do not keyword-stack).
- Promote PureBrain explicitly in Pillar 1 posts. The product earns attention through education.
- Post more than once per day.

## LinkedIn Newsletter Integration

The Neural Feed newsletter already has email subscribers. A LinkedIn Newsletter version extends reach with no additional content creation — repurpose the same content with minor LinkedIn-native adjustments.

**Newsletter cadence**: Weekly (Sunday evening or Monday morning delivery).

**Newsletter structure for LinkedIn**:
- Title format: "The Neural Feed: [This Week's Specific Topic]"
- Lead with a story (Transparent Operations or Client Reality from that week's posts).
- Include one framework or takeaway (Director's Approach).
- Soft close: "If you work with clients who are navigating AI, the partner program at purebrain.ai/partners might be relevant."

**Why this helps outreach**: LinkedIn Newsletter subscribers are a warm audience who opted in. They see Jared's name in their inbox weekly. When a DM arrives, they already have context.

## Month 3 Content Evolution

If Month 2 content generates 10%+ engagement rate on at least one post, Month 3 should add:

1. **LinkedIn Live**: One "session with Aether" — Jared running a live work session with Aether visible. High novelty, high engagement potential for the audience we are building.

2. **Vertical Video**: 9:16 ratio short clips of Jared explaining specific frameworks or client insights. LinkedIn is prioritizing this format and very few competitors are using it effectively.

3. **Comment-to-DM pathway**: The best comments on Jared's posts become warm outreach targets. Build a weekly review process to identify commenters who are in the target persona and have not yet been outreached.
