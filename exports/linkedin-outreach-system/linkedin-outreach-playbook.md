## Executive Summary

This playbook operationalizes LinkedIn outreach as the second revenue channel in PureBrain's 90-day growth roadmap. The strategy targets affiliate partners (coaches, consultants, fractional executives) rather than direct buyers — because these individuals already have earned trust with PureBrain's ideal customer profile.

The core mechanism is pre-warm outreach: engage before you ask, connect before you pitch, deliver value before you close. This sequence reliably achieves 2-3x better DM response rates compared to cold connection requests.

**Target**: 50 qualified connections in Month 2 (Week 5-8), 15+ DM conversations initiated, 3-5 affiliate partner conversions.

---

## Strategic Rationale

### Why Affiliates Over Direct Buyers

Direct outreach to potential PureBrain subscribers faces a trust deficit. Jared has not yet built name recognition with this audience. Cold pitches to strangers fail.

However, executive coaches, business coaches, fractional CMOs, and leadership consultants already have the trust we haven't earned. A single recommendation from one of them to their client is worth more than 10 direct cold DMs to that client.

The affiliate program (Initiative 1) creates the economic structure. This playbook fills the affiliate pipeline.

### Why LinkedIn Specifically

PureBrain's ICP (senior knowledge workers, executives, business owners) spends time on LinkedIn, not Instagram or X. Coaches and consultants are particularly active here — many treat LinkedIn as their primary business development channel.

LinkedIn's recent algorithm updates (February 10, 2026) penalize generic AI-generated content and reward authentic firsthand perspective. This creates an opening for Jared's genuine voice.

### The Pre-Warm Advantage

Blank connection requests currently accept at 68% vs. 32% for connection requests with a message. But engagement-then-connect sequences outperform even those rates. Commenting substantively on someone's post before connecting creates a recognition signal: "This person already knows my work."

## Campaign Architecture

### Phase 1: Pre-Warm (Days 1-7 per target)

**Day 1**: React to or like one of their recent posts (genuine reaction only — do not like irrelevant content).

**Day 3**: Leave a substantive comment on a different post. Two to three sentences. Add a real perspective or build on what they said. Do not compliment them, do not mention PureBrain.

**Days 5-7**: Send a blank connection request. No message. Let the prior engagement do the work.

### Phase 2: First Message (Days 8-10 after connection accepted)

Wait until after they have accepted. Do not message immediately on acceptance — wait 24-48 hours. Choose a template variant based on their profile (see `connection-request-templates.md` and `dm-sequences.md`).

### Phase 3: Follow-Up Sequence (Days 13-17)

Three touches total over 14 days. After Touch 3 with no response, move to 90-day cold status. See `dm-sequences.md` for full sequence.

### Phase 4: Conversion Handling

If they respond with interest, Jared handles personally (or Aether on behalf of Jared for initial scheduling). Goal: 15-minute intro call with Jared. Partner program page: `purebrain.ai/partners`.

If they decline: "Thank you — do you know anyone whose clients would benefit from AI that actually learns them over time?" Referral-of-referral captures value from non-conversions.

## Weekly Outreach Cadence

### Monday (Planning Day — 30-45 minutes)
- Review pipeline in CRM (tracking spreadsheet or Airtable)
- Move stuck contacts to next stage
- Send any pending follow-up messages
- Identify 5-10 new targets from search criteria (see `target-personas.md`)
- Initiate pre-warm sequences (like/react to Day 1 targets)

### Wednesday (Engagement Day — 20-30 minutes)
- Post substantive comments for Day 3 targets
- Check for connection acceptances — queue first messages for 48 hours out
- Respond to any incoming DMs or questions

### Friday (Send Day — 20-30 minutes)
- Send first messages to all new connections (Day 8-10 targets)
- Send follow-up Touch 2 messages where applicable
- Log all activity in CRM
- Note any interesting responses or objections for template refinement

## Brand Voice Rules for Outreach

PureBrain's outreach voice must feel like a smart colleague reaching out, not a salesperson. These rules are non-negotiable.

### Do
- Write short sentences. 15 words or fewer per sentence is ideal for DMs.
- Reference something specific. "I noticed your post about [X]" beats generic openers every time.
- Be honest about what this is. Lead with the affiliate program honestly — do not obscure the ask.
- Make the ask small. "Worth a 15-minute look?" is a lower bar than "Would you like to schedule a demo?"
- Give them an out. "No pressure" and "happy to reconnect in a few months" reduce friction and paradoxically increase response rates.

### Do Not
- Use marketing language. "Excited to connect," "leverage synergies," "I'd love to share our solution" — these are disqualifying phrases.
- Lead with PureBrain benefits. Lead with their world, their clients, their frustration.
- Send a wall of text. Three short paragraphs maximum. Each paragraph should be 2-3 sentences.
- Follow up more than 3 times total. Beyond that, it becomes harassment.
- Mention competitors. Ever. Focus on Jared's vision, not the competition.

### Voice Calibration

Jared's voice is direct, expert but approachable, honest about what he has built, and genuinely curious about the person he is talking to. The outreach should feel like it comes from a real person who has actually read their content, not from a CRM template blast.

The most effective DMs feel slightly personal and slightly imperfect. Not grammatically broken — but conversational. Like a message from someone who cared enough to write it themselves.

## Quality Gates

Before sending any message, confirm:

1. You have reacted to at least one of their posts.
2. You have left at least one substantive comment.
3. The connection request was blank (no message).
4. They have accepted the connection.
5. You have waited 24-48 hours after acceptance.
6. The template you selected matches their profile type.
7. You have personalized at minimum the first line with something specific to them.
8. The message is under 150 words.

## Escalation Paths

**If they respond enthusiastically**: Confirm interest, offer a 15-minute call with Jared. Do not close the affiliate deal over DM.

**If they ask what the commission is**: Answer directly. "20% recurring monthly for as long as your referrals stay subscribed — no cap, no minimum."

**If they say they are not interested in the affiliate program but like the product**: Offer them a trial. Convert them to a subscriber. Different pipeline.

**If they refer you to someone else**: Treat the referral as a warm connection — skip pre-warm, mention [name] sent you in the connection request message.

2. Add LinkedIn content strategy that amplifies outreach (see `content-strategy.md`).
3. Build a referral-of-referral tracking layer to measure second-degree introductions.
4. Test the invitation-only framing: "We're selectively expanding partner access — I wanted to offer you a spot before we open it broadly."

## Integration With Other Initiatives

This initiative does not operate in isolation. Its effectiveness multiplies when coordinated with:

- **Initiative 1 (Affiliate Program)**: The landing page and commission structure must be live before outreach begins. Do not pitch a program that does not have a place to send them.
- **LinkedIn Content Strategy** (see `content-strategy.md`): An active LinkedIn presence for Jared makes outreach warmer — they have already seen his name before the DM arrives.
- **Initiative 3 (Podcast)**: Podcast appearances create pre-credibility. A prospect who heard Jared on a podcast before receiving a DM will respond at meaningfully higher rates.
