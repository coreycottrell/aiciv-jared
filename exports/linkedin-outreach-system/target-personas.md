# PureBrain LinkedIn Outreach - Target Personas

**Initiative**: 2 (LinkedIn Direct Outreach)
**Prepared by**: marketing-strategist
**Date**: 2026-02-24

---

## Overview

The primary outreach target is not a PureBrain subscriber — it is a trusted advisor whose clients are PureBrain subscribers. This distinction shapes everything: the search criteria, the message framing, and the conversion goal.

Think of this as recruiting a distribution network built on pre-existing trust, rather than building trust from scratch with each individual buyer.

---

## Persona 1: The Executive Coach

**Who They Are**

An experienced executive who has pivoted to coaching after 15-25 years in senior leadership roles. They work with C-suite and VP-level clients on leadership development, decision-making, and executive presence. Many have corporate backgrounds (consulting, finance, operations) and have built a coaching practice over 5-10 years.

**LinkedIn Profile Signals**
- Title: "Executive Coach," "Leadership Coach," "C-Suite Coach," "Executive Performance Coach"
- 500-10,000 connections
- Posts about leadership, decision-making, executive presence, team dynamics
- Mentions clients (without naming them) in posts — "In a coaching session last week..."
- May have written a book or runs a cohort program
- Likely has testimonials from former C-suite clients

**Their Pain**

Their clients — executives at growth-stage or enterprise companies — are all using AI tools, but none of them are using AI well. They ask their coaches about AI adoption constantly and coaches feel unprepared to advise on it. The best executive coaches want to help their clients with AI without becoming "AI tool explainers."

**Why They Are a Strong Affiliate**

They meet weekly with executives who should be using PureBrain. A single recommendation from a trusted coach is worth more than 100 cold emails. If they believe in the product (give them trial access), they refer naturally in conversation.

**Outreach Template**: Variant B (Client Parallel Hook)

**Qualification Score Threshold**: Must-have criteria 1-5 + at least 3 strong signals (criteria 6-15)

**Targeting Search String** (LinkedIn search)
- Title keywords: "Executive Coach" OR "Leadership Coach" OR "C-Suite Coach"
- Keywords in profile: "executives" OR "leaders" OR "C-suite"
- Content activity: Active in last 30 days
- Connection count: 500+

## Persona 2: The Fractional Executive (CMO/COO/CFO/CRO)

**Who They Are**

A senior executive who now works with 3-6 companies simultaneously in a fractional capacity, typically serving growth-stage startups or SMBs that cannot afford a full-time C-suite hire. They are operators who have "been in the seat" and lend execution capacity along with strategic guidance.

**LinkedIn Profile Signals**
- Title: "Fractional CMO," "Fractional COO," "Fractional CFO," "Fractional CRO," "Interim VP of Marketing"
- Often lists multiple companies in experience section simultaneously
- Posts about their clients' challenges (anonymized), fractional work dynamics, AI for operators
- Active commenter on entrepreneur and startup content
- May have a newsletter or regular posting cadence
- Often shares tools and systems they use with their fractional clients

**Their Pain**

They are managing multiple clients with limited time. They need to scale their own cognitive output and often feel like they are solving the same problems across clients repeatedly. They also want to look ahead — AI is changing the roles they fractional-fill, and they need to stay current or risk becoming obsolete.

**Why They Are a Strong Affiliate**

They have direct, trusted access to business owners and executives who make purchasing decisions. They regularly recommend tools to their clients. When a fractional COO tells a CEO "you should try this," the CEO tries it. They also have strong incentive to test PureBrain for their own workflow.

**Outreach Template**: Variant B (Client Parallel Hook), with emphasis on "persistent context across multiple clients"

**Targeting Search String**
- Title keywords: "Fractional" AND ("CMO" OR "COO" OR "CFO" OR "CRO" OR "VP")
- Keywords in profile: "startups" OR "growth" OR "scale" OR "SMB"
- Activity: Regular posts (2x+ per week preferred)

## Persona 3: The Business Coach (SMB-Focused)

**Who They Are**

A coach who works with small to mid-size business owners, typically helping with revenue growth, operations, sales strategy, or entrepreneurial mindset. Often has a defined niche (e-commerce founders, service business owners, solopreneurs, agency owners). Frequently runs group programs, online courses, or masterminds in addition to 1:1 coaching.

**LinkedIn Profile Signals**
- Title: "Business Coach," "Revenue Coach," "Sales Coach," "Entrepreneur Coach," "Growth Coach"
- Higher follower counts (often 5,000-50,000) due to broader audience appeal
- Posts frequently about business fundamentals, mindset, client wins
- May run or promote courses, challenges, or live events
- More likely than other personas to have a strong personal brand or following
- Often promotes other products/services they have used (affiliate-friendly behavior)

**Their Pain**

Their clients — small business owners — are overwhelmed by the speed of AI change and need practical guidance, not theory. Business coaches who can help clients implement AI systems without becoming "tech consultants" command higher positioning and retention.

**Why They Are a Strong Affiliate**

They have audience infrastructure that other personas lack — newsletters, social followings, and group programs. A single newsletter mention from a business coach with 10,000 subscribers is worth more than individual DM sequences. They are also the most likely persona to have affiliate experience and be comfortable with the model.

**Outreach Template**: Variant C (Direct Value Hook) for content creators with large audiences; Variant B for coaches with smaller, more intimate client relationships

**Targeting Search String**
- Title keywords: "Business Coach" OR "Revenue Coach" OR "Sales Coach" OR "Growth Coach"
- Keywords: "entrepreneurs" OR "business owners" OR "founders"
- Follower count: 1,000+ (LinkedIn followers, not just connections)
- Activity: 2x+ posts per week

## Persona 4: The AI Strategy Consultant

**Who They Are**

A consultant who has positioned themselves specifically around AI adoption, AI strategy, or digital transformation for organizations. Often has a technical background (data science, software, enterprise systems) and is now advising companies on AI implementation at the team or organizational level. May have worked at a major consulting firm before going independent.

**LinkedIn Profile Signals**
- Title: "AI Strategy Consultant," "AI Advisor," "Digital Transformation Consultant," "AI Implementation Specialist"
- Follows and comments on AI thought leaders (must be real engagement, not generic)
- Posts frequently about specific AI tools, use cases, implementation learnings
- Has likely written about AI adoption challenges, change management, or organizational readiness
- May have a newsletter or LinkedIn Newsletter on AI topics
- Commands a premium audience — followers likely include the decision-makers we want

**Their Pain**

They advise companies on AI strategy but their clients end up using generic tools that fail to create lasting habit change. They have credibility as an AI voice but lack a product to point clients to for the "personal AI adoption" layer of the strategy — the human-facing, daily-use component.

**Why They Are a Strong Affiliate**

They are already in conversations about AI adoption with decision-makers. When they recommend PureBrain, it comes with their professional credibility on the specific topic. Their clients will view the recommendation as research-backed. They also have the highest potential to become case-study partners if their clients get strong results.

**Outreach Template**: Variant A (AI Frustration Hook) — they have almost certainly posted about generic AI tool limitations

**Targeting Search String**
- Title keywords: "AI" AND ("Consultant" OR "Strategist" OR "Advisor" OR "Coach")
- Keywords: "AI adoption" OR "AI implementation" OR "digital transformation"
- Recent post content: mentions of specific AI tools, implementation challenges, or AI ROI

## Persona 5: The Organizational Development / HR Consultant

**Who They Are**

An independent consultant or small firm specializing in organizational effectiveness, learning and development, talent management, or HR transformation. Works with mid-market and enterprise companies on workforce capability building. Often has a corporate HR background (CHRO, VP HR, L&D Director) before going independent.

**LinkedIn Profile Signals**
- Title: "OD Consultant," "HR Consultant," "L&D Consultant," "Organizational Effectiveness," "Talent Strategy Consultant"
- Posts about workforce trends, skills gaps, learning culture, team performance
- Active in HR and people-strategy LinkedIn communities
- May be a SHRM member or similar professional association
- Engages with future-of-work and AI-in-the-workplace content

**Their Pain**

AI is rewriting every job description in their client organizations. They are being asked to help companies build "AI-ready" workforces without a clear framework for what that means at the individual level. They need tools that help employees develop genuine AI fluency, not just awareness.

**Why They Are a Strong Affiliate**

They are influencing AI tool selection and deployment decisions at the organizational level. If they recommend PureBrain as part of an "AI onboarding" or "AI skills development" framework, teams adopt it together. This creates potential for the team plan at $3,000 rather than individual subscriptions.

**Outreach Template**: Variant B (Client Parallel Hook), with emphasis on team AI adoption and the "persistent memory across the team's shared context" angle

**Targeting Search String**
- Title keywords: "OD Consultant" OR "HR Consultant" OR "L&D" OR "Organizational" OR "Talent"
- Keywords: "AI" OR "future of work" OR "workforce" OR "skills"
- Activity: Posts about AI in the workplace

## Disqualifying Signals (Do Not Target)

These signals indicate a poor fit regardless of how strong other criteria are:

- No profile photo or incomplete profile (signals low LinkedIn investment)
- Last activity more than 30 days ago (not an active user)
- Posts only promotional content with no genuine perspective
- Less than 500 connections (limited network reach for referrals)
- Based outside English-speaking markets (without a clear reason they serve those markets)
- Bio explicitly focused on individual consumers rather than business professionals
- Title is generic corporate employee title with no coaching/consulting element
- Previous public criticism of AI tools in a way that signals fundamental opposition (not skepticism — opposition)

## Priority Matrix

Score each potential target using this matrix before adding to the outreach pipeline.

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Mentions AI in recent posts | +3 |
| Has published a LinkedIn Newsletter | +2 |
| Runs courses, workshops, or cohort programs | +2 |
| 1,000+ LinkedIn followers (not just connections) | +2 |
| Has affiliate or ambassador history | +3 |
| Engages with Jared's content (or PureBrain content) | +5 |
| Follower base overlaps with PureBrain ICP | +3 |
| Based in North America, UK, or Australia | +1 |
| Has mentioned frustration with generic AI tools | +4 |
| Responds to comments on their posts | +2 |
| Posts 3x+ per week | +1 |

**Scoring**:
- 15+ points: Priority 1 (outreach immediately)
- 10-14 points: Priority 2 (outreach in Week 2)
- 5-9 points: Priority 3 (outreach in Week 3-4)
- Below 5 points: Do not outreach
