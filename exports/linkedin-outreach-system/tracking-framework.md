**Initiative**: 2 (LinkedIn Direct Outreach)
**Prepared by**: marketing-strategist

## Overview

Good tracking does three things: tells you what is working, tells you what to stop, and tells you what to do next Monday. A tracking framework that takes more than 30 minutes per week to maintain will not be maintained. This one is designed for weekly review in under 30 minutes.

The system lives in a Google Sheet (no Airtable required). Two tabs: Contact Pipeline and Weekly Metrics.

## KPI Benchmarks and Thresholds

These benchmarks are based on LinkedIn outreach research for B2B services. They are starting points, not ceilings. Adjust after 3-4 weeks of actual data.

| KPI | Below Target | On Target | Strong |
|-----|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Connection acceptance rate | < 45% | 45-65% | > 65% |
| Touch 1 response rate | < 10% | 10-25% | > 25% |
| Conversation-to-call rate | < 20% | 20-35% | > 35% |
| Call-to-partner rate | < 25% | 25-45% | > 45% |
| Referral-of-referral rate | < 5% | 5-15% | > 15% |

**If connection acceptance rate drops below 45%**: The pre-warm sequence may not be completing before sending the request. Check that like + comment + wait period is consistent.

**If Touch 1 response rate drops below 10%**: Template messaging may not be resonating with the targets being selected. Review the persona-template match and consider whether targeting is accurate.

**If call-to-partner rate drops below 25%**: The product demo or Jared's call may need refinement. This is outside the outreach system's control — escalate to Jared for feedback on call structure.

## Weekly 30-Minute Review Protocol

### Monday Morning (30 Minutes Maximum)

**Step 1 (5 minutes): Update Status Column**
- Review everyone with "Outreach Active" or "Follow-Up Pending" status.
- Move anyone whose Touch 2 or Touch 3 date has passed to next stage.
- Mark anyone who responded over the weekend.

**Step 2 (5 minutes): Calculate This Week's Numbers**
- Fill in the weekly metrics tab for last week.
- Note any template or persona that outperformed or underperformed.

**Step 3 (10 minutes): Identify Action Items**
- Who needs Touch 2 sent this week?
- Who needs Touch 3 sent this week?
- Who has a call scheduled? (Confirm with Jared's calendar)
- Who is a new connection ready for first message?

**Step 4 (10 minutes): Add New Targets**
- Identify 5-10 new targets using LinkedIn search criteria from `target-personas.md`.
- Add to pipeline with "Pre-Warm" status.
- Score each target using the priority matrix. Only add Priority 1 and 2 targets.

**Output**: A clear action list for Wednesday and Friday of that week.

## Monthly Review Protocol (First Monday of Each Month)

This review looks at the full pipeline, not just the current week.

**Questions to answer**:

1. What is the end-to-end conversion rate from pre-warm to partner? Is it improving or declining?

2. Which persona converts to affiliate at the highest rate? Which has the highest response rate? Are these the same persona? (If not, prioritize the higher-conversion persona even if response rate is lower.)

3. Which DM sequence (A, B, C) generates the most conversations? Which generates the most partners? (Again, these may differ.)

4. What objections are coming up repeatedly in conversations? These should be addressed in template refinement.

5. Are there patterns in who says yes vs who says no that are not captured in the persona framework?

6. What is the referral-of-referral yield? Is asking for intros after declines producing results?

**Output**: One-page summary with updated targeting priorities, any template revisions, and revised volume targets for the next month.

## Data Hygiene Rules

1. **Update the pipeline the same day** outreach happens. Do not batch updates — memory fails and timestamps drift.

2. **Never delete rows**. If a contact goes cold or DNR, change the status and leave the row. Historical data matters for pattern analysis.

3. **One person, one row**. If a contact refers you to someone new, the new person gets their own row with a note in the referring contact's row.

4. **Backup weekly**. Export the Google Sheet as .xlsx every Monday. Store in the `exports/linkedin-outreach-system/data/` folder.

5. **Do not track personal information beyond what is listed**. LinkedIn profile data (public), outreach dates, and conversation notes only. No personal details, no sensitive information from their conversations.
