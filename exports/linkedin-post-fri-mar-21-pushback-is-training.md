The best thing you can do for your AI is disagree with it.

Not ignore it. Not replace it. Push back.

Every correction you give AI is a data point. Every "no, that is not how we do it" teaches it something no training dataset ever could.

But here is what most people miss:

Most AI tools throw that correction away the moment you close the tab.

You spent 10 minutes explaining why the tone was wrong. Why the strategy missed the mark. Why the recommendation did not fit your business.

Then tomorrow you open a new session and it has forgotten everything.

That is not a tool problem. That is an architecture problem.

The companies I work with that are actually getting compounding value from AI are the ones where corrections persist. Where pushback becomes training. Where the AI on Friday is measurably better than the AI on Monday — because of every disagreement in between.

Your frustration with AI output is not wasted energy.

It is the most valuable signal you can give.

The question is whether your AI is built to keep it.
