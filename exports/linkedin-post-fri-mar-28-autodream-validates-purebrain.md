Anthropic just shipped a feature called AutoDream.

It gives Claude Code a form of memory -- modeled after REM sleep -- so the AI can consolidate what it learned between sessions.

Read that again. The company building one of the most advanced AI models in the world just admitted their AI has an amnesia problem.

Every session, Claude forgets everything. Every conversation starts from zero. Every user has to re-explain their business, their preferences, their context.

AutoDream is their fix. And it validates something we built PureBrain around from day one:

Stateless AI is a dead end.

We did not wait for the model providers to solve this. We built persistent context, memory architecture, and continuity into the product layer -- because we saw this gap 18 months ago.

Now every major AI company is racing to solve the same problem.

Here is what most people miss: memory is not a feature. It is the foundation. Without it, your AI is a brilliant stranger who forgets your name every morning.

The companies that figured this out early are not just ahead on product. They are ahead on trust. On compounding value. On the thing that actually makes AI useful long-term.

The rest are building amnesia machines.
