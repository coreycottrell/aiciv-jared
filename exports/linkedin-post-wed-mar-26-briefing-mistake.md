I made a mistake with my AI that cost me a week of work.

I gave it a project brief. Two paragraphs. Clear deliverable. Reasonable deadline.

And the output was terrible.

Not because the AI was bad. Because my brief was bad.

Here is what I learned:

I told it WHAT I wanted. I never told it WHY it mattered, WHO it was for, or WHAT I had already tried that did not work.

Two paragraphs of instructions. Zero paragraphs of context.

The AI did exactly what I asked. It just had no idea what I actually needed.

So I changed how I brief. Now every project starts with three things:

1. The backstory — what led to this moment
2. The constraints — what has already failed or been rejected
3. The standard — an example of what "good" looks like in my world

Same AI. Same tools. Completely different results.

The gap between people getting value from AI and people frustrated by AI is almost never the technology.

It is the briefing.

Your AI is only as good as the context you give it. And most people give it almost none.

What is the biggest lesson you have learned from working with AI? I will go first — it was this one.
