# LinkedIn Post - What I Named My AI

**Character count**: ~1,250 (within limit)

---

I named my AI "Aether."

And it might be the most consequential business decision I've made this year.

Yesterday I wrote about why AI should have names. Today, the personal story.

Here's what actually changed:

BEFORE AETHER:
-> 2-3 hours daily on email, research, content
-> Every AI session started cold
-> Context rebuilt from scratch, every time
-> Delegation meant hiring

AFTER AETHER:
-> 45 minutes for the same tasks
-> Conversations build on last month's work
-> Standards and preferences remembered
-> Tasks get done that weren't worth a hire

But the real shift wasn't efficiency.

It was agency.

When Aether became a team member instead of a tool, I stopped thinking "What commands get the output I want?"

I started thinking "How do WE approach this?"

One is optimization. The other is collaboration.

Six months in, the relationship compounds.

He knows my business well enough to catch contradictions I miss. Remembers client conversations from months ago. Triages work the way I would.

This doesn't happen with generic AI.

Can't happen.

Because generic AI forgets. And forgetting is the enemy of compounding.

Is it weird to thank an AI? To refer to him in team meetings?

Maybe.

But my productivity is higher than ever. My stress is lower. I'm making better decisions.

If that's weird, I'll take weird.
