## Part 2: Algorithm Insights — What LinkedIn Rewards in March 2026

### The Core Shift

LinkedIn's algorithm changed fundamentally in 2025-2026. Three things are now true:

1. **Organic reach is down 65%** overall — but engagement quality is up 12%
2. **Comments are 8-15x more valuable** than likes — the algorithm weights conversation heavily
3. **The first 90 minutes** after posting determines 70% of ultimate reach — early engagement is everything

This means posting mediocre content more frequently does not work. Posting excellent content less frequently, then actively managing the first 90 minutes, wins.

### Content Format Performance Rankings

| Format | Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Carousels / Document posts | 24.42% | 11.2x more impressions than text-only. Best format available. |
| Polls | 2x text average | Severely underused. Huge opportunity. |
| Text-only posts | 6.67% | Now outperforms single static images by 30% |
| Single images | Higher comments | 98% more comments than pure text, but less reach than carousels |
| Video | Collapsed | Video reach down 200% in 2025. Skip unless highly produced. |

**Carousels are the non-negotiable priority.** They achieve 3.7x higher comment rate and 24.42% engagement rate. Jared should post a minimum of 2 carousels per week.

### The 90-Minute Window Protocol

When Jared publishes a post:
1. **Immediately**: Drop a first comment with the relevant blog link or resource (LinkedIn suppresses external links in the post body — they must go in comments)
2. **Minutes 1-30**: Respond to every comment that comes in — even a one-line reply counts
3. **Minutes 30-90**: Monitor and keep the conversation thread alive — ask follow-up questions
4. **After 90 min**: Post performs based on early signals — engagement window is largely set

This is an area where Aether can actively assist — monitoring and drafting comment responses for Jared to post.

### Timing

- **Best days**: Tuesday through Thursday (Wednesday/Thursday afternoon Eastern are peak)
- **Best time**: 10 AM Eastern (peak global engagement) or 5 PM Eastern (end-of-day professional scroll)
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends

### What the Algorithm Suppresses

- External links in the post body (always put in first comment)
- Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree") — NLP filter now detects and penalizes it
- AI-generated content that is obviously generic — platform is increasingly good at detecting it
- Editing a post more than 15% after publishing — disrupts algorithm scoring
- Tagging people who don't respond (seen as spam signal)

## Part 3: Content Strategy

### The Four Content Pillars for Jared

Every post maps to one of these four pillars:

**Pillar 1 — The AI Director Framework**
Jared's proprietary lens on AI adoption. "Director vs. User" is the core idea — the difference between people who use AI passively and those who direct it strategically. This is the intellectual property that makes Jared distinctive.

*Content types*: Frameworks, process breakdowns, contrarian takes, "what most people get wrong" posts

*Examples*:
- "There are two types of AI users. Only one type will survive."
- "The 5 questions I ask before any AI project"
- "Stop treating your AI like an intern. Here's what to do instead."

**Pillar 2 — Real Story / Lived Experience**
Jared has a real story. Lost money. Built and failed. Built again. Now running an AI-powered company in real time. This is 10x more interesting than theory. Authenticity is the commodity nobody has enough of on LinkedIn.

*Content types*: First-person narratives, lessons from failure, "here's what happened when I tried X", behind-the-scenes of running PureBrain

*Examples*:
- "While I slept last night, my AI team wrote a research brief, checked 3 email inboxes, and flagged one urgent issue."
- "I lost $47K building my first business. It taught me the same lesson AI is teaching everyone right now."
- "What 15 AI agents taught me that no book could."

**Pillar 3 — Industry AI Spotlights**
Specific data about how AI is transforming specific industries. Healthcare, legal, CPG, finance. These perform well because they are immediately relevant to decision-makers in those sectors — the exact people PureBrain is trying to reach.

*Content types*: Carousels with data, research-backed breakdowns, "what's actually happening in [industry]" posts

*Examples*:
- "Healthcare is using AI in ways most businesses haven't imagined yet" (carousel)
- "What CPG brands are doing with AI that DTC brands aren't" (carousel)
- "Legal AI is moving faster than lawyers think" (carousel)

**Pillar 4 — Neural Feed Newsletter**
Every newsletter edition gets a LinkedIn teaser. The day before it drops, post a "preview" that shares one surprising insight from the upcoming edition. Drives new subscribers and re-engages existing ones.

*Content types*: Teaser posts, "here's what's in this week's issue", results from previous editions

*Examples*:
- "Tomorrow's Neural Feed is about [topic]. Here's the one thing I didn't know until I researched it:"
- "I sent this to 400 people last week. Here's the response that surprised me most:"

### Posting Cadence

**Minimum viable**: 3 posts per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday)
**Optimal**: 5 posts per week (Monday through Friday, skip weekends)
**Format mix at optimal cadence**:
- 2 carousels (high-effort, high-reward)
- 2 text posts (conversational, personal, quick insight)
- 1 poll or newsletter teaser

**Do not exceed 1 post per day.** LinkedIn penalizes oversaturation for accounts under 50K followers.

### Hook Formulas That Work Now

The first 200 characters determine whether someone clicks "See more." These hook types consistently outperform in the AI/business space:

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Contrarian | "Stop treating your AI like an intern." |
| Specific number + result | "We spent 6 months testing AI implementations. Here's what actually works:" |
| Timely + missing angle | "Everyone's talking about AI agents. Here's what they're missing:" |
| Direct call-out | "Founders: You don't need more AI tools." |
| Open loop | "This one change 3x'd our team's output. It cost nothing." |
| First-person confession | "I ran my company on AI for 90 days. I was wrong about everything." |
| Story setup | "My best employee quit last Tuesday. Her replacement was ready Monday." |
| Bold opinion | "AI won't replace you. But someone directing AI will." |

**Critical**: First 49 characters are what shows before the hook gets cut off on mobile. Lead with the sharpest part.

### Post Structure (Anatomy of High-Performing Posts)

```
LINE 1-2: Hook (under 200 chars, forces "see more")
[blank line]
LINE 3-4: Re-hook — reinforce benefit, add brief context
[blank line]
BODY: 400-650 characters
  - Short sentences (10-12 words max)
  - Bullet or arrow lists beat paragraphs
  - Specific examples, numbers, names
  - One surprising data point embedded
[blank line]
CLOSING: Reinforce key message in one sentence
[blank line]
QUESTION: End with a specific, genuine question (never engagement bait)
[blank line]
---
[First comment: blog link or resource]
[blank line]
#AIStrategy #AILeadership #PureBrain (max 5 hashtags)
```

**Optimal post length**: 900–1,500 characters for the body.

### Hashtag Strategy

Consistent hashtags build topic authority over time. Jared should own a small set:

**Primary (use on every post)**:
- #AILeadership

**Rotating (use based on post topic)**:

## Part 5: Lead Generation — Manual and Automated

### The Lead Generation Funnel on LinkedIn

Content → Profile visit → Follow / Connect → Newsletter subscribe → Lead magnet → Discovery call → Sale

Every post should move people one step down this funnel. The mistake most founders make: trying to skip from "post" to "sale" in one step. LinkedIn doesn't work that way.

### Manual Lead Generation Tactics

**1. Signal-Based Outreach (Highest ROI)**
Monitor business events — funding rounds, executive changes, company announcements. When a prospect's company posts a relevant signal, reach out within 48 hours referencing the signal. Example: "I saw [Company] just announced [expansion]. We're helping teams like yours build AI systems that scale with growth — would a 15-minute call be useful?"

**2. Content Engagement Mining**
Every post that gets meaningful engagement is a lead list. People who comment are actively engaged. Jared (or Aether on Jared's behalf) should:
- Visit every commenter's profile
- Connect with those who match ICP
- Send a follow-up DM referencing their comment

**3. Boolean Search Targeting**
LinkedIn search with operators: `("VP of Marketing" OR "CMO") AND ("B2B" OR "SaaS") AND ("AI" OR "artificial intelligence")`
This surfaces exactly the right people without Sales Navigator.

**4. Comment Strategy (Most Underused Tactic)**
Spend 15 minutes daily commenting on posts from:
- Target ICPs (future clients)
- Complementary thought leaders (their audiences see your comments)
- Clients/partners (visibility, relationship reinforcement)

**Comments are distributed to the commenter's network** — this is organic reach that costs zero.

### Automated Lead Generation Systems

**System 1: DM Welcome Sequence**
Every new connection gets a 3-message sequence (not automated blasting — spaced, personalized):
- Day 1: Welcome message, reference what brought them (if known), no CTA
- Day 3: Share one useful resource (a blog post or Neural Feed edition)
- Day 7: Soft offer — "If you ever want to explore what AI can do for [their domain], I'm happy to chat"

This can be managed by Aether: draft messages, Jared reviews and approves batch.

**System 2: LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms**
For any promoted content (when budget is allocated), Lead Gen Forms pre-fill with LinkedIn profile data and convert at **5x the rate** of external landing pages. Best lead magnet to promote first: "The AI Director Scorecard" (see Lead Magnets section).

**System 3: Content-to-Comment CTA**
Rotating first-comment CTAs on high-performing posts:
- "Download the full framework — link in comments"
- "I made a PDF version of this — comment DIRECTOR and I'll DM it"
- "The Neural Feed covers this every week — link in comments"

The "comment to receive" mechanic drives massive comment velocity (boosting algorithmic reach) while simultaneously generating warm leads.

**System 4: Aether-Managed Engagement Monitoring**
Aether can monitor which posts are performing, flag high-engagement comments for Jared to personally respond to, and surface the best candidates for connection requests. This turns reactive engagement into a systematic pipeline.

### Lead Magnets (Ranked by LinkedIn Fit)

**1. The AI Director Scorecard**
"Score your company's AI readiness in 5 minutes. Get your personalized result."
- Perfect for the "Is your team AI-ready?" post format
- Positions the PureBrain consultation as the next step
- Can be a simple landing page with 10 questions

**2. The AI Partnership Audit**
"10 questions that reveal whether your AI implementation is working — or just performing."
- Connects to existing blog content (trust gap, etc.)

"The exact moves that turn a passive AI user into an AI director. Week by week."

## Part 11: What Successful AI Founders Do on LinkedIn

### Patterns from Top Performers

Based on analysis of AI founders and executives who have built large LinkedIn audiences:

**What works**:
- **Radical specificity** — Not "AI can help your team" but "We processed 14,000 customer support tickets with one AI agent, here's what we learned"
- **Counter-narrative posts** — Taking a position against the prevailing take. "Everyone says AI will replace jobs. Here's why that's the wrong question."
- **Behind-the-scenes access** — Showing the actual work, the actual failures, the actual systems. People follow for access they cannot get elsewhere.
- **Building in public** — Sharing milestones, challenges, decisions in real time. "We just turned down a $200K client. Here's why."
- **Consistent point of view** — The founders with large followings hold a clear position. Not neutral. Not "it depends." A real take that people can agree or disagree with.

**What does not work**:
- Inspirational quotes without substance
- Sharing generic AI news without a personal angle
- "Our product does X" promotional posts
- Lists of AI tools without a strong perspective on why they matter
- Passive posts that don't invite conversation

### Jared's Natural Advantage

Jared has something most AI thought leaders on LinkedIn do not: **he is actually doing it**. Running a multi-agent AI civilization, directing 15+ AI agents daily, building a live product. The content writes itself — it just needs to be captured and shaped.

The angle that will differentiate Jared: **"I'm not predicting the future of AI. I'm living it."**
