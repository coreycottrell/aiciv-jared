## Part 1: Algorithm State — March 2026 Update

### What Changed Since March 6

The Dataslayer February 2026 report confirmed a detail that matters significantly for Jared: **the first-comment link workaround no longer works cleanly.**

As of early 2026, LinkedIn's algorithm has extended link suppression to include external links in first comments. The previous best practice (put your blog link in the first comment) now "incurs penalties" per the February Dataslayer analysis. This is a meaningful shift from what we recommended in v1-v6.

**New recommendation**: Do not include links anywhere in the first 90 minutes post-publish. Once the Golden Window closes (first 90 minutes), adding a first-comment link is lower-risk — but the clean approach is to never include links unless you are promoting a paid campaign.

### Algorithm Priority Stack (Current)

| Signal | Weight | Implication |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Dwell time (30+ seconds) | Highest | Write posts people actually finish reading |
| Comment length and quality | 15x vs. likes | End every post with a specific, genuine question |
| Saves | Very high | Educational, framework content gets saved |
| Profile visits after post | New 2026 signal | Profile completeness now affects distribution |
| First-hour engagement | Critical | 2-5% of your network engaging in first hour = broad reach |
| External links in post | -60% reach | Never put links in the post body |
| External links in first comment | Now penalized | Only add links after 90 minutes, if at all |
| Engagement bait ("Comment YES") | Suppressed | Never. Use specific, real questions only |
| AI-generic content | Penalized | LinkedIn's NLP now flags predictable AI structures |

### Format Performance Rankings (March 2026)

1. **Document/Carousel (PDF)**: 6.60% average engagement, 300-600% above text/image. Dominant format. Post at minimum 2 per week.
2. **Short native video (30-90 seconds)**: 5.60% engagement, 36% YoY growth in views. Resurgence — skip YouTube links, upload native only.
3. **Text-only posts**: 2-4% engagement when structured with strong hook. Outperforms single static images.
4. **Polls**: 200%+ above-average reach, severely underutilized. Run one per month minimum.
5. **Single images**: Declining (-30%). Use sparingly.
6. **Video (long form)**: Only if 3+ minutes with strong retention. Otherwise skip.

### One Timing Update

Buffer's 2026 data shows a meaningful shift: **late afternoon and evening (3-8 PM) now compete with morning slots**. The prior recommendation to exclusively post at 8-10 AM was 2025 data. Current data shows:

- Wednesday 4 PM ET: Best overall (confirmed)
- Friday 3-4 PM ET: Strong second
- Tuesday 3-4 PM ET: Solid
- Thursday 4-5 PM ET: Good
- Monday: Skip posting, use for commenting
- Weekends: No data supports this

The practical change: if Jared's workflow makes afternoon publishing more natural, it's now equally valid to Tuesday/Thursday morning.

## Part 4: The Neural Feed — Newsletter Growth Playbook

The March 6 report built out the post-level strategy but didn't address the newsletter as a growth asset. This fills that gap.

### Why LinkedIn Newsletter Matters More Than Email (Right Now)

LinkedIn newsletters get a notification delivered to every connection and follower when you publish. That's a free distribution push that email doesn't get. The tradeoff: less ownership than an email list. The strategy: use LinkedIn newsletter to grow reach, use it to push subscribers toward Brevo/email list.

### Realistic Subscriber Timeline for Neural Feed

| Phase | Timeframe | Subscribers | Open Rate | What's Driving Growth |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------------|
| Cold start | Months 1-3 | 0-300 | 35-45% | Direct message campaign to existing connections |
| Momentum | Months 4-6 | 300-1,000 | 35-45% | Consistent posting + post-level CTA |
| Authority | Months 7-12 | 1,000-5,000 | 40-50% | Collaborations + algorithm compound |
| Scale | Year 2+ | 5,000+ | 40-50% | Organic + reputation |

**Revenue implications for Neural Feed**:
- 500 subscribers: No direct revenue from newsletter itself, but conversion to PureBrain offers begins
- 2,000 engaged subscribers: $500-2,000/month potential (sponsorships possible, PureBrain offers converting at 2-3%)
- 5,000+ subscribers: Sponsorships start at $1,000-3,000/mention. PureBrain lead flow meaningful.

### Neural Feed Growth Tactics (In Priority Order)

**1. The First-Connection DM Campaign (One-Time)**

When launching or relaunching the newsletter, send a personal DM to 200-300 connections asking them to subscribe. Script:

```
Hey [Name],

I've been publishing The Neural Feed — a weekly briefing for business leaders on AI that actually works in practice. Thought of you because [specific reason — their industry, a post they made, something they mentioned].

If it's relevant, here's the link: [newsletter URL]

No hard sell — just thought you'd find the March issues interesting.

[Jared]
```

Do not send to everyone at once. 20-30 per day maximum. Aim for 5-10% conversion. At 250 sends, that's 12-25 new subscribers from one campaign.

**2. The Post-Level Subscribe Push (Every Issue)**

The day before each Neural Feed edition publishes, post a teaser:

```
Tomorrow's Neural Feed is about [topic].

The thing I didn't know until I researched it:

[Most surprising finding from the issue]

Subscribe before it drops: [newsletter link in first comment after Golden Window]

What question would you want this issue to answer?
```

This drives subscriptions AND engagement in one post.

**3. The Collaboration Loop (Months 4-6)**

Identify 3-5 LinkedIn newsletters in adjacent spaces (not direct competitors) with 3,000-10,000 subscribers. Propose a cross-mention: you mention their newsletter to your audience, they mention Neural Feed to theirs. Approach only newsletters where Jared's audience and theirs genuinely overlap.

Target niches: executive coaching, business operations, leadership development, marketing strategy. Avoid AI newsletters that are direct competitors.

**4. The Content-to-Newsletter Funnel**

Every post Jared publishes should have one path back to Neural Feed. Not as a CTA in the post body (link penalty). As:
- The question at the end pointing to a topic covered in an upcoming issue
- A first-comment mention after the Golden Window
- A response to comments that references Neural Feed content

### Neural Feed Content Structure (70-20-10)

- 70% value: Frameworks, data, analysis on AI in business
- 20% personal story: What Jared and Aether are actually doing
- 10% promotional: PureBrain updates, offers, lead magnets

**Issue length for optimal reach**: 1,500-2,000 words per issue. LinkedIn rewards long-form content that keeps readers on-platform. Short newsletters (under 500 words) see 25-35% worse reach.

**Frequency**: Bi-weekly for first 6 months (build the habit before increasing). Move to weekly once Jared has a reliable content pipeline producing material without strain.

## Part 5: Lead Generation — From LinkedIn to Qualified Call

### The 5-Step Pre-Warm Sequence

This is the non-automated version. Run manually for high-value targets.

**Step 1 (Day 1)**: Follow and like their 2 most recent posts. No comment yet.

**Step 2 (Day 3)**: Leave a genuine, specific comment on a relevant post. 2-3 sentences. Add actual perspective, not just "great post."

**Step 3 (Day 7)**: Send connection request with note (keep under 300 characters):
```
[First name] — saw your post on [topic], thought it was insightful. I'm building in a similar space (AI for business ops). Would be good to connect.
```

**Step 4 (Day 10, after connection accepted)**: Send context message (NOT a pitch):
```
Thanks for connecting. Your work on [specific thing] is relevant to what I'm seeing with clients around AI implementation — the gap between "using AI" and "directing AI" is bigger than most realize. No pitch — just genuinely enjoy following people who are thinking carefully about this.
```

**Step 5 (Day 21, only if engagement exists)**: Soft offer:
```
Hey [Name] — I've been building a framework for business leaders on the "AI Director" transition. I'm doing a small group of free strategy sessions this month to pressure-test the approach. If you'd ever want 30 minutes to run through your AI situation, I'm happy to make time. No agenda beyond the conversation.
```

**Conversion benchmark**: 30-40% connection acceptance rate. 15-25% response rate on context message. 5-10% booking rate on soft offer.

### The Comment Trigger DM

When someone comments meaningfully on a Jared post, send within 24 hours:

```
[Name] — your comment on [topic] was one of the sharper observations on that post. I'd be curious what you're seeing in your own organization around [related topic]. Mind if I ask?
```

Wait for response. Never pitch in the first exchange.

### Lead Magnet Activation (Comment Mechanic)

Post specifically designed to drive lead magnet downloads (use sparingly, once per month maximum):

```
I built a 5-question scorecard that tells you exactly where your AI strategy has gaps.

500+ leaders have used it. The most common result: they thought their biggest problem was tools. It's almost never tools.

Want it? Drop "SCORECARD" in the comments and I'll DM you the link.
```

Note: LinkedIn suppresses this less than "Comment YES" because it's tied to a specific action and a genuine resource. Still monitor whether LinkedIn's NLP begins flagging this format in 2026.
