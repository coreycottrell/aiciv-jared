### 1.4 Post Structure (The Anatomy of High-Performing Posts)

Based on Gracker AI analysis of viral B2B posts:

```
LINE 1-2: Hook (under 200 chars, forces "see more")
[blank line]
LINE 3-4: Re-hook — reinforce benefit, add brief context
[blank line]
BODY: 400-650 characters
  - Short sentences (10-12 words max)
  - Bullet or arrow lists > paragraphs
  - Specific examples, numbers, names
  - One surprising data point embedded
[blank line]
CLOSING: Reinforce key message
[blank line]
QUESTION: End with a specific, genuine question (not engagement bait)
[blank line]
---
[First comment: link to resource or blog]
[blank line]
#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 (max 5)
```

**Optimal character count**: 900-1,500 characters for the post body.

**First comment for links**: LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links in the body. Always put blog links, resource links, and CTAs in the first comment.

### 1.5 Post Examples Analysis — Top Patterns in AI/Business Space

**Pattern 1: "AI won't replace you" Variations**
The hook "AI won't replace you. But someone using AI will." has driven millions of impressions across hundreds of posts. It works because it acknowledges the fear without confirming it — and implies action is required. Jared's version: upgrade this to "AI won't replace you. Someone *directing* AI will."

**Pattern 2: Numbered Lessons from Experience**
Posts structured as "X things I learned from [real experience]" with numbered insights and a closing question dominate the thought leadership space. Performs especially well when the author admits mistakes.

**Pattern 3: Old Way vs. New Way**
Before/after comparison tables and "Old way: 5 steps. New way: 1 prompt." structures drive high engagement because they show concrete transformation. Visual format (carousel) amplifies this further.

**Pattern 4: The Framework Reveal**
Posts that name and explain a framework — even a simple 3-step one — get saved at disproportionate rates. Saved posts signal "evergreen value" to the algorithm. Example: "The Director Framework for AI" as a named concept gives Jared intellectual property on LinkedIn.

**Pattern 5: Data + Non-Obvious Insight**
Stats are common. Stats followed by a non-obvious interpretation are rare and powerful. Example: "89% of companies have deployed AI tools. 76% say results are below expectations. The stat nobody talks about: the gap isn't the tool. It's the direction."

**Pattern 6: Case Study Micro-Story**
A specific client transformation story (anonymized if needed) with a before/after result, told in 3-4 short paragraphs. The specificity ("a 47-person logistics company", "$340K in recovered margin") outperforms generic claims by 3-5x engagement.

**AI Agents Business Hook That's Performing Well (2025)**:
"Now when I get an invoice from a contractor, I just forward the email to our AI agent. It reads the PDF, extracts the bank details, checks funds, and queues the transfer for my approval. My CFO thought I hired someone."

This works because it's concrete, surprising, and makes the abstract real. Jared has real versions of this story.

### 1.7 The 90-Minute Golden Window

This is the most underused tactic in Jared's current strategy.

When a post goes live:
1. LinkedIn tests it with your immediate network for 60-90 minutes
2. Early engagement (especially comments) triggers algorithmic amplification
3. 70% of a post's ultimate reach is determined in this window

**Action required within 90 minutes of posting**:
- Reply to every comment with a substantive question or expansion (15+ words)
- Respond to replies on those comments
- Go comment meaningfully on 5-10 other posts in your network

**Commenting on others' posts**: Commenting 10-20 posts daily boosts profile views by 50% and increases your own post engagement by 10%. Longer comments (15+ words) have 2.5x more impact than short reactions.

### 3.3 LinkedIn Newsletter — The Neural Feed

**The Neural Feed is a major asset.** LinkedIn newsletters are indexed by search engines, auto-notified to subscribers, and build compounding authority.

**Current opportunity**: LinkedIn newsletters have subscriber notification built in — this is free reach Jared is not fully exploiting.

**Growth tactics for The Neural Feed**:

1. **Teaser posts before each edition**: Post a 3-bullet preview of the newsletter 24 hours before it publishes. This primes readers and increases open rate.

2. **Cross-platform seeding**: Every newsletter edition should be teased on Bluesky thread AND mentioned in Jared's main post that week.

3. **Featured section placement**: Pin the newsletter subscription link prominently in Jared's profile featured section.

5. **Lead magnet integration**: Create a companion resource for each newsletter edition (checklist, framework PDF) and mention it in the newsletter. Builds subscriber value and list segment data.

**Cadence**: Weekly consistency matters more than frequency. Pick a day (e.g., Thursday morning) and hit it every week. Subscribers begin to expect it — that expectation builds habit and open rates.

**Newsletter content angle**: Each edition should follow The Neural Feed's existing brand angle — AI intelligence that's actually useful for business directors, not just enthusiasts.
