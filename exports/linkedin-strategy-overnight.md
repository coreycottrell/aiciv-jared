## Posting Frequency & Timing

### Cadence Recommendation

**Target: 4-5 posts per week** (not daily - quality over quantity)

Research shows posting daily without quality maintenance triggers algorithmic suppression. Companies and individuals posting consistently 3-5x weekly see 2x more engagement than inconsistent posters.

**Weekly rhythm:**

| Day | Format | Content Focus |
|-----|--------|---------------|
| Monday | Text post | Observation from weekend/prior week - curiosity hook |
| Tuesday | Document carousel | Educational framework or AI transformation insight |
| Wednesday | Text post | Productivity data or counter-narrative |
| Thursday | Video OR text | Behind-the-scenes with Aether, human-AI partnership story |
| Friday | Text post | Weekly reflection, data summary, question to community |

*Note: Tuesday-Thursday posts receive 60% more clicks and 80% more comments than weekend content.*

### Optimal Posting Times

**Primary windows (EST):**
- 8:00-9:00 AM - Before the morning work sprint
- 10:00-11:00 AM - Peak professional browsing
- 12:00-1:00 PM - Lunch break

**Best days:** Tuesday (highest activity), Wednesday, Thursday

**Avoid:** Weekends (25-50% lower engagement), after 7 PM, major holidays

### The First 90 Minutes Rule

This is critical. LinkedIn determines most of a post's reach within the first 90 minutes based on early engagement signals. The algorithm initially shows posts to 2-5% of your network. Strong engagement triggers broader distribution.

**Action required after every post:**
- Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours
- Engage with others' content immediately before posting to prime the algorithm
- Ask follow-up questions to commenters to extend conversation threads
- Target 10 insightful comments and 5 conversation threads in the first 90 minutes - this outperforms 100 generic likes

### The Algorithm's "Depth Score" (New January 2026)

LinkedIn introduced a "Depth Score" metric in January 2026 that accumulates over 24-48 hours and rewards:
- Deep comments (multi-sentence, not "Great post!")
- Save/bookmark actions
- Time spent on post
- Return visits to post

This means a post is not dead after 24 hours. Posts with strong depth signals continue getting distribution.

**Implication:** Reply to comments on posts 24-48 hours after publishing to re-activate the depth score accumulation.

6. Plan next week with learnings applied

## Final Principles to Remember

1. **Depth beats breadth.** One genuinely insightful post per week outperforms daily generic content. LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm was specifically tuned to reward this.

2. **The 95-5 Rule.** Only 5% of your LinkedIn audience is ready to buy right now. The other 95% are building trust. Every post should serve both groups, but the content should primarily be designed for the 95%.

3. **LinkedIn rewards humans, not logos.** Jared's personal profile is the primary asset. Every decision should maximize his visibility and credibility, not the company page's.

4. **Links kill reach.** Always put external links in the first comment, never the post body. This is the single highest-ROI technical adjustment available.

5. **The newsletter is the owned asset.** LinkedIn can change its algorithm tomorrow. The newsletter subscriber list is Jared's to keep regardless of platform changes. Every LinkedIn strategy should ultimately serve newsletter growth.

6. **Authenticity is the moat.** With 53.7% of LinkedIn content now AI-generated, human voice + real data + genuine perspective is the scarcest resource on the platform. Jared has all three.
