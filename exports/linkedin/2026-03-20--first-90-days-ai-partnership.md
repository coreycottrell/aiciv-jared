You signed the AI contract. Set up accounts. Ran test prompts. Declared it "live."

Three months later you have activity data but zero compound value.

Every conversation still starts from zero. Every output is still generic. Nothing has accumulated.

This is not an AI problem. It's an onboarding problem.

Here's what most companies do in the first 90 days:

Week 1-2: Set up accounts, train on features
Week 3-4: Run pilot projects
Month 2: Scale to more users
Month 3: Measure ROI

Looks reasonable. It's backwards.

Notice what's missing: no process for what the AI learns about the business. No structure for capturing institutional knowledge. No deliberate effort to build the context layer that makes outputs sharpen over time.

The first 90 days aren't about adoption. They're about what gets built in the foundation.

Here's how the best teams actually do it:

Month 1: Knowledge transfer. Treat the AI like a new executive hire. Feed it your communication style, competitive landscape, internal terminology, what's been tried before. McKinsey's research confirms — AI implementations fail most often at the integration layer, not the capability layer.

Month 2: Calibration through real work. Every correction is curriculum. The teams that accelerate fastest feed corrections back with context. The teams that stagnate fix the output and move on. Same mistake next time.

Month 3: Compound returns. Work products should feel noticeably different from month one — not because the AI updated, but because it has 90 days of your context behind every output.

Get the foundation wrong and you'll spend two years wondering why your AI keeps producing outputs that don't quite fit.

Get it right and you'll have something most organizations never develop: an AI that actually knows your business.
