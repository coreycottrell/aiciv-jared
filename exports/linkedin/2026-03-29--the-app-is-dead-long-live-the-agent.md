The CEO of Nothing said it this week: "Apps are going to be extinct."

He's right about the direction. AI agents will absorb the function of most apps you currently use. Travel booking. Project management. Research. Email drafts. All of it.

But he's missing the more important half.

Apps weren't just interfaces. They were memory stores. Your CRM held five years of customer interaction history. Your calendar held the context of who you met with, how often, and what you discussed.

When AI agents replace the app layer, they inherit the function. But most don't inherit the memory.

You open a conversation. Explain your context. Get a response. Close it. Open a new one. Explain your context again.

That's not a replacement. That's the worst version of the app — the one you had on day one, before you'd put anything into it.

The agents that win the next decade won't be the most capable ones. They'll be the most knowing ones.

Think about why you're loyal to any service. Not because it can do the most things. Because it knows you. Because it remembers what you like. Because it doesn't make you explain yourself every time.

And here's the business reality most people aren't seeing yet:

There's about to be a new switching cost. After six months of daily use, a persistent agent that has learned from hundreds of your decisions has accumulated something irreplaceable.

You can't export that to a competitor.

The moat isn't the model. It isn't the interface. It isn't the number of integrations.

The moat is the accumulated knowledge of you.

## First Comment (post separately):

Four questions to evaluate whether your AI tool is actually persistent:

1. Does it retain context between sessions without you manually flagging things to save?
2. Can you access and review what it knows about you?
3. Does your data stay isolated from other users?
4. Does the relationship improve over time — not because the model upgraded, but because it learned from YOUR history?

If any answer is no or unclear, you have a stateless tool with a memory feature bolted on.
