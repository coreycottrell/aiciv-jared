**Initiative**: 4 of 6 from 90-Day Growth Roadmap (Months 2-3)

---

## Executive Summary

Newsletter cross-promotions are the lowest-cost, highest-trust subscriber acquisition channel available to The Neural Feed right now. Unlike paid ads (expensive, cold audiences) or SEO (slow compounding), newsletter swaps deliver warm audiences who already self-select for AI and business content. A reader who subscribes after being recommended by a trusted newsletter they already love starts with higher trust, better open rates, and higher conversion probability than almost any other acquisition source.

The strategic framing: we are not asking for exposure. We are offering genuine value to another writer's audience, and asking for reciprocal access to their readers in return. This only works when the content fit is real. Every target newsletter in this system was chosen because the audience overlap is genuine.

**Why now**: The Neural Feed is new enough to have growth upside but established enough (daily posting cadence, live blog infrastructure, Brevo email system operational) to make a credible pitch. Newsletters approaching 100+ subscribers are respected enough for mid-tier swaps. Approaching later means missing the window where our content freshness is an asset.

When describing The Neural Feed to a potential partner, use this framing:

"The Neural Feed covers the practical side of AI adoption for business leaders — not just what tools to use, but how to build a working relationship with AI that actually compounds over time. This framing:
1. Avoids the overused "AI newsletter" positioning
2. Speaks to reader pain (underutilizing AI they already have)
3. Distinguishes from technical/developer newsletters
4. Makes the audience overlap obvious to any business-adjacent newsletter

## The Three Partnership Tiers

### Tier 1: Feature Swaps (Primary Strategy)

**What it is**: We write content specifically for their audience; they mention it in their newsletter; we feature or mention them in Neural Feed.

**Best for**: Newsletters with 5,000-100,000 subscribers. Small enough to be approachable, large enough to move the needle.

**Our offer**: An Aether-written guest piece (400-800 words) tailored to their audience angle, with a transparent "written by AI" disclosure that demonstrates the product.

**What we ask**: A 100-200 word feature on Neural Feed for their readers, with a direct opt-in link to https://purebrain.ai/#awakening (not homepage — this takes readers directly to the sign-up section).

**Why this works**: We are offering something most newsletter writers haven't received before — content written by an actual AI system about working with AI, tailored for their specific audience. The novelty of "let me show you what an AI that actually knows your audience can produce" gets replies.

---

### Tier 2: Shoutout Swaps (Volume Strategy)

**What it is**: A paragraph-length mutual recommendation. We mention them in Neural Feed; they mention us in theirs.

**Best for**: Newsletters with 2,000-10,000 subscribers where a full feature swap feels like too much coordination overhead.

**Timeline**: Can be proposed and executed within one email exchange. No content creation required beyond the shoutout copy.

**Our offer**: A genuine recommendation of their newsletter to Neural Feed subscribers — written by Aether, explaining specifically what value their readers get.

**What we ask**: A similar recommendation from them to their readers.

**Tip**: Offer to write their copy for them. Most newsletter writers are short on time. "I'll write the mention for you — just review and approve it" dramatically increases yes rates.

---

### Tier 3: Co-Created Campaigns (Long-Term Strategy)

**What it is**: A joint mini-series or challenge jointly promoted to both lists.

**Best for**: Established partnerships only. Propose this after one successful Tier 1 swap. Never lead with this.

**Example**: A 5-day "AI Partnership Challenge" — daily prompts sent to both lists simultaneously, with both newsletters cross-referencing each other throughout.

**Why save for later**: Requires more coordination, shared risk, and mutual trust. A successful simpler swap is the proof of concept that earns the right to propose something bigger.

## Campaign Architecture

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 5-6, March 23 - April 5)

Before outreach begins, build the infrastructure that makes the system work.

**Deliverables** (see companion files for details):
- Landing page variant list with dedicated UTM parameters per partner (see tracking-system.md)
- Subscriber welcome email variant for cross-promo subscribers (see onboarding-sequence.md)
- Ad copy variants for partner newsletters to run (see ad-copy-variants.md)
- Outreach templates ready (see swap-proposal-templates.md)

**Internal metric to hit before Phase 2**: At least 20 target newsletters identified and researched (see target-newsletters.md).

---

### Phase 2: Outreach (Weeks 7-8, April 6-19)

**Volume target**: 20-30 outreach emails in this window.

**Batching approach**: Send in waves of 5-7, not all at once. This lets you iterate on subject lines and opening lines based on early reply signals before sending the next batch.

**Wave 1 (April 6-8)**: Tier 2 targets only (newsletter writers you've interacted with, smaller lists). Lower risk, builds momentum, produces early proof points for tracking.

**Wave 2 (April 13-15)**: Tier 1 mid-size targets (5K-30K subscribers). Apply learnings from Wave 1.

**Wave 3 (April 17-19)**: Remaining Tier 1 targets and first Tier 1 larger newsletter attempts.

**Do not email Tier 1 large newsletters** (The Neuron, TLDR AI, Every.to) until Month 3. These require warm introduction or existing relationship to get past the inbox. Cold pitching newsletters of 400K+ subscribers almost never works.

---

### Phase 3: Relationship Maintenance (Week 9+)

Newsletter cross-promotion is not a transactional event. The highest-value outcome is a repeat partnership — being mentioned again in a future issue, or the writer becoming a vocal advocate for Neural Feed.

**Post-swap actions**:
1. Track subscribers acquired per partner (UTM data in GA4)
2. Send a personal follow-up to the writer with their results ("You sent us X subscribers — here's what we sent you")
3. Feature the partner in a future Neural Feed issue (even without a formal swap, just as genuine appreciation)
4. Keep track of which partners have high-quality audiences (high open rates, convert to trial) vs. vanity traffic

## Conversion Funnel for Cross-Promo Subscribers

When a reader clicks through from a partner newsletter to Neural Feed:

1. They land on a dedicated opt-in page variant (not the homepage) — tracked with UTM parameters specific to that partner
2. They subscribe and immediately enter the **Cross-Promo Welcome Variant** of the onboarding sequence (see onboarding-sequence.md) — slightly different than standard welcome sequence to acknowledge "I know you found us through [Newsletter Name]"
3. Standard 7-email welcome sequence from email 3 onward
4. Conversion tracked: did they engage with Neural Feed, open the awakening section, or become a PureBrain trial user?

**Expected benchmarks**:
- Newsletter readers who click through: 2-5% of the partner's audience (varies widely)
- Of those who land on opt-in page: 20-35% subscribe
- Of those who subscribe: 40-60% open email 1 (typical for new subscribers from warm referral)
- Of those who open: 5-15% eventually click through to purebrain.ai/#awakening over 30 days

## Risk Factors and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Low reply rates**
Mitigation: Personalize every pitch (reference a specific recent issue). Generic pitches get ignored. See swap-proposal-templates.md for customizable templates.

**Risk 2: Swaps execute but subscribers don't stick**
Mitigation: The cross-promo onboarding variant explicitly acknowledges the referral source. Social proof from the referring newsletter extends to Neural Feed.

**Risk 3: Audience mismatch (we receive traffic, nobody converts)**
Mitigation: Strict partner criteria. Developers-only audiences and pure hobbyist newsletters are disqualified before outreach even starts.

**Risk 4: Partner newsletter sends low quality**
Mitigation: Track UTM data for each partner. After one issue, the data shows quality. If traffic converts below 30% opt-in rate on the dedicated landing page, deprioritize repeat swaps with that partner.

## The Aether Advantage

One unique asset that most newsletters cannot offer: Aether as co-author.

When proposing a guest piece for a partner newsletter, offer this option:

"Aether (our AI system) can write the guest piece, with me reviewing it. The piece would be transparently disclosed as AI-written — which, given your audience's interest in AI, is a feature, not a liability. It's a live demonstration of what a persistent-memory AI can actually produce when given genuine context about your readers."

This offer:
- Stands out in a crowded inbox
- Demonstrates the product rather than describing it
- Creates natural curiosity in their audience about PureBrain
- Generates a talking point the newsletter writer can use in their intro ("this piece was written by an AI that's been tracking trends in this space for months")

Not all partners will want this. Some will prefer human authorship. But for the right audience (AI-curious professionals, productivity/PKM crowd, innovation-focused executives), the Aether angle converts better than a standard human-written guest post.

## Quarterly Cadence (After Month 2 Launch)

Newsletter cross-promotion compounds. Month 1 swaps produce relationships. Month 2 relationships produce repeat swaps and warm introductions to other writers. By Month 3, the goal shifts from "who can we cold pitch" to "who do our partners know who would be a good fit."

**Quarterly maintenance tasks**:
- Review partner performance data (UTM — which partnerships produced engaged subscribers)
- Re-pitch top 3-5 partners for a second swap
- Ask top partners for warm introductions to 1-2 writers in their network
- Update target newsletter list with newly discovered newsletters from outreach conversations
