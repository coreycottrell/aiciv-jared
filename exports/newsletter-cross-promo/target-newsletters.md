## How to Use This Document

Each newsletter is scored on three factors:
- **Audience Fit (1-5)**: How closely their readership matches Neural Feed's ICP (business professionals using or adopting AI)
- **Approach-ability (1-5)**: How realistic it is to get a yes — smaller newsletters, active writers, prior AI content = higher score
- **Estimated Reach**: Subscribers exposed to our pitch if swap is accepted

**Tier assignments**:
- Tier 1 (Feature Swap): 5K-100K subscribers, strong audience fit
- Tier 2 (Shoutout Swap): 2K-10K subscribers, moderate-high fit
- Tier 3 (Relationship Build Only): 100K+ subscribers, valuable as long-term goals but not realistic cold pitches

**Contact approach**: In all cases, email first. Never DM on LinkedIn or Twitter as the primary outreach — it feels transactional. Use LinkedIn DMs only after a prior email exchange, as a warm follow-up.

## Research Checklist Before Outreach

Before sending any swap pitch, verify:

- [ ] Checked the newsletter's last 5 issues (know what they write about)
- [ ] Noted one specific piece they published that you found genuinely valuable
- [ ] Confirmed subscriber count via public statement, media kit, or Beehiiv/Substack stats
- [ ] Confirmed they publish at least 2x per month (active newsletters only)
- [ ] Confirmed no recent pause or hiatus (check last issue date)
- [ ] Confirmed they are not a direct competitor (other persistent-memory AI products)
- [ ] Found the writer's email or LinkedIn for outreach
- [ ] Drafted a personalized opening line referencing a specific issue
