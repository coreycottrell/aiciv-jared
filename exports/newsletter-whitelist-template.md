# Newsletter Whitelist Instruction Template

**Version**: 1.0
**Created**: 2026-02-19
**Usage**: Paste one of these blocks near the TOP of every issue of The PureBrain.ai Pulse

---

## How to Use This Template

Pick one of the three versions below based on context:

- **Version A** - Use for ALL issues (standard default, always include)
- **Version B** - Use for the first 2-3 issues after a subscriber joins, or whenever the spam problem resurfaces publicly
- **Version C** - Use as a standalone LinkedIn post (not inside the newsletter itself) when you want to address the Gmail issue publicly

Copy the block exactly. Do not edit the core instructions - the specific steps are what make it work for non-technical readers.

---

## VERSION A: Standard Block (Use in Every Issue)

Paste this above your opening section, before the first content heading:

---

**Gmail user? Quick fix:** LinkedIn newsletters sometimes end up in spam or trigger a safety warning. This is a known issue with LinkedIn's email system - not specific to this newsletter. To fix it permanently: find this email, click the three dots in the top right, select "Report not spam" or "Looks safe," then add **newsletters-noreply@linkedin.com** to your Gmail contacts. You'll only need to do this once.

---

**Character count**: approximately 370 characters. Short enough to not distract from content.

## VERSION B: Extended Block (For New Subscribers or Active Issues)

Use this when you want to be more explicit, such as after a subscriber joins for the first time or during a period when you're actively asking followers to whitelist:

---

**Important for Gmail users (takes 30 seconds, only once):**

If you're reading this in spam, or if Gmail showed you a "dangerous message" warning, here's what happened and how to fix it:

LinkedIn newsletters across Gmail are currently affected by a system-wide spam issue. This is not a problem with this newsletter's content - it is a known issue with LinkedIn's email infrastructure. Gmail flags messages from LinkedIn's servers more aggressively than most email providers.

**To fix it permanently:**

1. Find this email (check your Spam folder if needed)
2. Click the three dots menu in the top right corner of the email
3. Select "Report not spam" or "Looks safe" or "This is not phishing"
4. Optional but recommended: Click the sender name at the top of the email, then click "Add to contacts"

After doing this once, future issues will arrive normally in your inbox.

## VERSION C: Standalone LinkedIn Post (Not Inside the Newsletter)

Post this as a regular LinkedIn post - not as a newsletter article - when you want to proactively reach subscribers who may have missed issues due to spam filtering:

---

Gmail users who subscribed to The PureBrain.ai Pulse: you may have missed some issues.

LinkedIn newsletters are currently triggering a Gmail "dangerous message" warning across the board. This is a known infrastructure issue affecting many LinkedIn newsletter creators - it is not a content problem.

Here's how to fix it in 30 seconds (one time only):

1. Find any issue of The PureBrain.ai Pulse in your Gmail (check Spam if needed)
2. Click the three dots in the top right of the email
3. Click "Report not spam" or "Looks safe"
4. Then click the sender name and "Add to contacts"

That's it. Future issues will arrive normally.

If you haven't subscribed yet and want to avoid this entirely, subscribe directly through LinkedIn and complete the steps above after your first issue arrives.

## Technical Notes (For Reference)

The correct LinkedIn newsletter sending address to add to contacts is:
`newsletters-noreply@linkedin.com`

Some sources also suggest adding:
- `bounce.linkedin.com`
- `e.linkedin.com`

For Gmail filter power users, they can also create a filter:
- Search: `from:linkedin.com`
- Action: "Never send to spam"

This is more aggressive than needed and may bring in unwanted LinkedIn marketing emails, so it is not recommended as the primary instruction. The "Report not spam" + "Add to contacts" path is the right default for most subscribers.

## Placement Rules

1. Always place the whitelist block ABOVE the fold - before your first content heading
2. Version A should appear in every single issue without exception
3. Do not bury it at the bottom - subscribers who found the email in spam and need to act on these instructions will not scroll to the bottom
4. Keep it visually distinct from your content - a horizontal rule before and after works well on LinkedIn's newsletter formatting
