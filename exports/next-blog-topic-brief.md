## Thesis

Everyone's focused on AI capabilities. The real bottleneck is trust. Companies will let AI automate emails but won't let it inform strategy. This trust gap - not technology - is why 95% of pilots fail and only 8.6% of companies have AI agents in production.

The solution isn't better AI. It's a fundamentally different relationship with AI.

## Key Data Points

1. **HBR (Feb 2026)**: "Why AI Adoption Stalls" - performance gains plateau because employees experiment but don't integrate
2. **Alteryx Survey**: 50% trust AI for repetitive tasks, only 28% for decision-making
3. **Industry Data**: Only 8.6% have AI agents in production; 63.7% have no formalized AI initiative
4. **AmEx Case Study**: AI assistant failed due to poor data foundation (infrastructure, not intelligence)
5. **Our "95% Fail" Post**: Already established the pilot failure narrative - this extends it

## PureBrain Angle

The trust gap exists because most companies treat AI as a tool (you tell it what to do, hope it does it right). When you treat AI as a partner (it understands your context, grows with your team, earns trust through demonstrated competence), the trust gap closes naturally.

**This is PureBrain's entire thesis**: The gap between "AI assistant" and "AI partner" is exactly the trust gap.
