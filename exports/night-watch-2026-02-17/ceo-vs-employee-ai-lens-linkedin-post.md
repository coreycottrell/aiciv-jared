## Post Draft

76% of CEOs believe AI will boost productivity.

65% of employees believe AI will eliminate their job.

Same technology. Completely different conversations.

That gap is where most AI transformations break.

Here's what I've observed:

**CEOs get right:**
- AI is infrastructure, not magic
- The adoption window is closing
- Transform or lose to those who do

**Employees get right:**
- Some tasks will shift from human to AI
- Staying relevant requires skill evolution
- Organizations aren't always transparent

**Both get wrong:**
- Assuming the other side doesn't get it
- Not having the actual conversation
- Treating AI like a technology decision

The fix isn't better AI.

It's better communication.

Three things that work:

1. Transparent role clarity (not promises - specifics)
2. Shared capability investment
3. Bidirectional feedback loops

The CEO who announces "AI transformation" without addressing job security hasn't started a transformation.

They've started a rumor mill.

The employee who refuses to engage with AI tools isn't protecting their job.

They're accelerating their obsolescence.

The gap isn't technical. It's conversational.

What's your organization doing to close it?
