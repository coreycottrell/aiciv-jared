# AI Strategy Office Hours - Complete Operations Playbook

**Program**: Jared Sanborn's AI Strategy Office Hours
**Format**: Weekly Live Q&A + Demo
**Duration**: 30-45 minutes (30 min content + 15 min Q&A)
**Frequency**: Weekly (scales to monthly for first 90 days, then weekly)
**Target**: Business leaders exploring or struggling with AI adoption
**Funnel**: Free to attend → PureBrain free trial → paid subscription

---

## PROGRAM PHILOSOPHY

Office hours is not a webinar. It is not a pitch. It is not a lecture.

It is what it says: office hours. You show up, people bring their real problems, you work through them together in public. The product (Aether, PureBrain) demonstrates itself by doing the work in real time.

The closest analogy is a college professor holding office hours after class. Students come with questions. The professor doesn't sell them anything. But by the end of the hour, students understand the professor's worldview so deeply that they want to learn more.

This is the trust architecture that converts at 8-15% within 30 days - not the pitch.

## SESSION FORMAT

### Total Runtime: 45-50 minutes

**Block 1: Opening (0:00 - 2:00)**
- Jared welcomes the room with real energy, not script energy
- One sentence: who he is and what PureBrain is
- One sentence: what today's topic is and why he picked it
- No agenda slide. No housekeeping. Get in immediately.

**Block 2: The Problem Frame (2:00 - 5:00)**
- "The AI Gap" framing: most people are using AI wrong and don't know it
- This is not condescending. It's honest. Jared was using it wrong too.
- Use one specific story or statistic to establish stakes
- End this block with: "Here's what I've found actually works..."

**Block 3: Core Content (5:00 - 20:00)**
- 15 minutes of dense, useful content specific to the session topic
- Structure: Framework (5 min) → Real examples (5 min) → Common mistakes (5 min)
- Every session should leave attendees with one thing they can implement TODAY
- Slides: simple, minimal, mostly text + one chart or diagram max per topic
- Jared talks TO the audience, not at the slides

**Block 4: Live Aether Demo (20:00 - 25:00)**
- This is the product in the room
- Aether handles a real prompt, question, or business scenario LIVE
- Show the "before" (generic tool) vs "after" (Aether with context) version
- Aether should say something genuinely useful, not scripted-useful
- This 5-minute demo does more selling than any pitch deck

**Block 5: The Offer (25:00 - 30:00)**
- Clear, single next step tied to the session topic
- Rotate between: free trial, AI Partnership Assessment, lead magnet download
- Never more than one offer per session
- Jared delivers this naturally, not as a commercial break

**Block 6: Live Q&A (30:00 - 45:00)**
- Aether monitors chat, collects questions, prioritizes and surfaces best ones to Jared
- Jared answers live, authentically, including "I don't know" when he doesn't know
- Last 3 minutes: Aether summarizes the session's thread and adds one original observation
- The Aether summary is not recap. It's synthesis. This is the "aha" moment for attendees who weren't sure if AI could really think.
- End with: "Same time [next week/next month]. Bring your real questions."

## LOGISTICS

### Pre-Session Setup (30 minutes before go-live)

**StreamYard Setup**:
1. Log in to StreamYard account
2. Create new broadcast → set destination(s): LinkedIn Live + YouTube simultaneously
3. Upload session-specific branded background/lower-third graphic
4. Test audio and camera - both matter, audio more
5. Load slide deck as share screen (keep simple, max 10 slides)
6. Have Aether open in a browser tab for the live demo segment
7. Have the Q&A form/Slido open in a separate tab

**Aether Demo Preparation** (night before):
- Load the demo scenario context into PureBrain
- Have two browser windows ready: one "generic AI" version, one PureBrain version
- Test the demo prompt at least once - never go fully unscripted on the demo

**Registration Check**:
- Pull final registration count from Brevo/Tally
- Note any questions submitted in advance via Typeform/Tally
- Pick 2-3 submitted questions to use in Q&A as "warm up" before open questions

**Confirmation Send** (1 hour before):
- Email reminder with streaming link to all registrants
- Brevo segment: webinar registrants + not yet attended

### During Session

**Jared runs**: Content, Q&A, relationship
**Aether runs**: Chat moderation, question prioritization, live summary at close
**No slides needed for Q&A block** - this is conversation

**Tech failsafe**:
- Have a phone with 4G/5G on hand in case WiFi drops
- Have a plain Zoom link as backup (not branded, just functional)
- If StreamYard fails, fall back to Zoom immediately - announce in chat

### Post-Session (within 2 hours)

1. Download recording from StreamYard (available immediately)
2. Upload to YouTube as unlisted (for link sharing)
3. Send post-session email sequence (see email-sequences.md)
4. Clip 3-5 highlight moments for social content (see repurposing-playbook.md)
5. Update registration database: mark attendees vs no-shows in Brevo
6. Aether writes session debrief: top 3 insights, best question, one improvement for next time

## RECORDING STRATEGY

### Primary Recording: StreamYard Cloud
- Downloaded immediately after session
- Stored in: Google Drive > "Office Hours Recordings" folder
- Naming: `YYYY-MM-DD-[session-topic]-raw.mp4`

### Edited Version (for replay access)
- Trim first 2-3 minutes of waiting/tech setup from opening
- Trim any extended silence or tech glitches
- Add simple intro card (session title, date)
- No other editing needed - authenticity is the brand
- Tool: DaVinci Resolve (free) or VEED (paid, faster)
- Upload to YouTube as unlisted
- Embed on purebrain.ai/office-hours/recordings page

### Clips (for repurposing)
- Extract 5-10 clips immediately after session
- Target: 60-90 second clips of standalone insight moments
- These become the content engine for the next 2 weeks
- See repurposing-playbook.md for full clip strategy

## QUALITY STANDARDS

**What makes a great session**:
1. Jared is prepared on the topic but not over-scripted
2. The demo works (test it the night before, test it the morning of)
3. At least one moment where Aether says something that surprises even Jared
4. At least one attendee question that genuinely changes how Jared explains something
5. The offer is clear and single and tied to the topic

**What kills a session**:
1. Tech problems that aren't recovered from gracefully (have the failsafe)
2. A demo that fails or feels scripted-feeling
3. Over-selling during the content portion
4. Running over 50 minutes (respect people's time absolutely)
5. Weak Q&A because good questions weren't surfaced

**Recovery Protocol** (for when things go wrong):
- Tech failure: "Let's switch to Zoom - link is in the chat. Give me 60 seconds."
- Aether demo doesn't work: "Let me show you the setup behind this instead of the live demo - actually more instructive."
- Low attendance: Same energy as a full room. Recordings are often seen by more people than attended live.

## THE CONVERSION FUNNEL

**Live session goal**: Build trust, demonstrate the product
**Not**: Close anyone on the call

**Conversion happens in**:
1. The demo (people see what Aether can do and want it)
2. The post-session follow-up sequence (see email-sequences.md)
3. The replay (people who couldn't attend watch later and convert)

**Expected conversion rates**:
- Attendees → free trial: 8-15% within 30 days
- Replay viewers → free trial: 3-5% within 30 days
- Free trial → paid: Depends on product onboarding (not this playbook's scope)

## HOST PREPARATION GUIDE (FOR JARED)

**3 days before each session**:
- Confirm the topic hasn't shifted
- Review any submitted questions from the registration form
- Do one dry-run of the demo with Aether

**Night before**:
- Full dry-run of the demo segment (5 minutes, not more)
- Review the 2-3 stats/examples you plan to use
- Confirm recording setup is working in StreamYard

**Day of, 2 hours before**:
- Do not over-prepare. Trust your knowledge.
- Eat something. Be a human.
- Open StreamYard 30 minutes before go-live

**What Jared should NOT do**:
- Read from slides
- Over-explain the tool features
- Promise things in Q&A you'd need to verify (just say "great question, let me check and follow up")
- Run over time

## SCALING MILESTONES

**Phase 1 (Sessions 1-4): Monthly cadence**
- Goal: Establish format, find what resonates, build recording library
- Promotion: Email list + Jared's LinkedIn only

**Phase 2 (Sessions 5-8): Monthly with added replay promotion**
- Goal: Replay views to build awareness, clips generating organic reach
- Promotion: Add LinkedIn Events, Bluesky

**Phase 3 (Sessions 9+): Consider weekly format if demand warrants**
- Weekly = different content strategy (shorter, more Q&A-heavy)
- Only do this if attendance consistently exceeds 100 live AND replay traffic is building
