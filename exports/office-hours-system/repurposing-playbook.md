# Office Hours Content Repurposing Playbook

**Core Principle**: One 45-minute office hours session = 8+ pieces of published content over 14 days.

This is not about squeezing content out of sessions. It is about recognizing that a good office hours session already contains everything you need. The frameworks, the examples, the Q&A moments, the Aether synthesis — these are finished ideas delivered live. Repurposing is just giving them a second audience.

## ASSET 1: SESSION CLIPS (5-10 clips, 60-120 seconds each)

### What to Extract

**Priority clip types** (in order of impact):

1. **The Demo Moment** (always clip this)
   - The 60-90 seconds where Aether is demonstrably solving something
   - This is the product ad that doesn't feel like an ad
   - Target: 90 seconds or under

2. **The Reframe Moment**
   - When Jared says something that shifts how you think about a familiar problem
   - These are the clips that get shared
   - Target: 60-90 seconds

3. **The Best Q&A Answer**
   - The one question where Jared gave a genuinely surprising or useful answer
   - Not the softballs — the one that made the room get quiet
   - Target: 90-120 seconds

4. **The Aether Synthesis**
   - The 60-second moment at the end of every session when Aether synthesizes the session
   - This is the "what AI partnership feels like" moment in pure form
   - Always clip this. Always.

5. **The Honest Moment**
   - If Jared said "I don't know" or challenged an assumption or told an uncomfortable truth
   - These are the trust-builders
   - Target: 45-90 seconds

### How to Extract Clips

**Step 1**: Watch the raw recording with timestamp notes (Aether does this — creates a timestamp document with clip candidates and descriptions)

**Step 2**: Jared reviews the timestamp document and marks which clips to extract

**Step 3**: Extract using StreamYard's clip tool (built-in) or VEED

**Step 4**: Add minimal branding — lower third with session title and date. Nothing else.

**Step 5**: Upload each clip to Loom (for link sharing) and YouTube Shorts (for algorithm reach)

### Clip Distribution

**Each clip gets**:
- Uploaded to Loom with descriptive title
- Shared in the post-session emails as "highlights"
- One clip per week shared on LinkedIn for 3-4 weeks after the session
- Clips posted to YouTube Shorts with keyword-optimized titles

**Do not dump all clips at once.** Spread them over 2-4 weeks. One clip per week keeps the session alive in the feed long after it happened.

## ASSET 2: LONG-FORM BLOG POST (1,000-1,400 words)

### Strategy

The blog post is NOT a transcript of the session. It is the best 30% of the session's ideas, written for someone who wasn't there and will never watch the recording.

Think of it as: what would Jared write if he were explaining this topic from scratch, with the benefit of having just taught it live and heard what the audience struggled with?

### Structure Template

```markdown
# [Session Topic Title - SEO Optimized]

[One-paragraph intro that puts the reader in the situation this session was designed for.
Not "in today's blog post I'll cover..." — start with them, not you.]

---

## [Framework Section 1 - the core reframe]

[3-5 paragraphs. The main idea. Use concrete examples, not generic ones.]

---

## [Framework Section 2 - the practical application]

[3-4 paragraphs. How to actually do the thing.]

---

## [The Part That Surprised People]

[1-2 paragraphs. Pull from the actual Q&A — the question that shifted the room's
understanding. This is exclusive content that didn't exist before the live session.]

---

## [What This Means for Your Business]

[2-3 paragraphs. Tie back to the reader's situation. Specific, not general.]

---

## [Action Step]

[One clear thing the reader can do today. Tied to the session's offer/lead magnet.]

[CTA Button: → Try PureBrain Free / → Download the Template / → Take the Assessment]

---

## [FAQ]

[3-5 questions that actually came up in the session's Q&A. Answer them in the blog.
This serves SEO and gives Q&A questions a second life.]
```

### Publication

- Publish to purebrain.ai/blog AND jareddsanborn.com/blog (dual publish per standing rule)
- Internal link to: AI Partnership Assessment, related blog posts from content arc
- Add FAQ section with structured data for SEO/AEO (schema markup)
- Include recording embed or "watch the session" CTA in the blog

## ASSET 4: LINKEDIN CAROUSEL

**When to use**: For sessions with a clear framework (5+ components, steps, or items)

**Format**: 8-12 slides
- Slide 1: Hook (session topic as a problem statement)
- Slides 2-7: One framework point per slide, with visual representation
- Slide 8: The "Aether demo" moment (screenshot or visual of output comparison)
- Slide 9-10: Action step + soft CTA
- Last slide: "Next Office Hours: [DATE]" with registration link

**Design**: Canva, dark background consistent with PureBrain brand, orange accents

**Distribution**: Post to LinkedIn as document/carousel format

## ASSET 7: PODCAST EPISODE (Optional - if podcast format exists)

**Format**: "Office Hours Audio" — the raw Q&A block from the session edited down to 20-25 minutes

**Why this works**: The Q&A portion of office hours is already a podcast. It's two people talking about a real topic with real questions from a real audience. The preparation is done.

**Production steps**:
1. Extract the Q&A block from the recording (minutes 30-45)
2. Remove long silences and tech glitches
3. Add brief intro: "This is the Q&A from AI Strategy Office Hours [DATE]. Topic was [TOPIC]."
4. Add brief outro: "Next session is [DATE]. Register at purebrain.ai/office-hours."
5. No music, no production — authenticity is the brand
6. Upload to podcast platform (if exists) as "Office Hours" episode

**Episode title**: "AI Office Hours: [Session Topic] — Live Q&A [DATE]"

## QUALITY STANDARDS FOR ALL REPURPOSED CONTENT

**What to include**:
- Specific examples, not generic statements
- The Q&A moments — these are the most valuable, most shareable content
- The Aether synthesis — include the actual words from the session
- Honest moments (including when Jared didn't have a perfect answer)

**What to avoid**:
- Transcription dumps (nobody reads these)
- "Today we discussed..." opener on anything
- Over-produced clips with too many lower thirds and graphics
- Any repurposed piece that requires watching the recording to understand — each piece should stand alone

**Freshness rule**: Never publish a repurposed piece after Day +14. If it's not distributed within two weeks, it's stale. The recordings are evergreen; the content calendar moves on.

## CLIP EXTRACTION: AETHER'S TIMESTAMP PROTOCOL

After every session, Aether creates a timestamp document in this format:

```markdown
# Office Hours Clip Timestamps - [DATE]

**Full recording location**: [PATH / URL]
**Total runtime**: [HH:MM:SS]

---

## Clip 1: [Description]
**Timestamp**: [HH:MM:SS] - [HH:MM:SS]
**Duration**: [X seconds]
**Type**: Demo / Reframe / Q&A / Aether Synthesis / Honest Moment
**Why this clip**: [1 sentence on why this is worth clipping]
**Suggested use**: LinkedIn / Bluesky / Email / YouTube Shorts

---

## Clip 2: [Description]
[Same format]

---

[Continue for all clips]

---

## Recommended Priority Order
1. [Most important clip - extract first]
2. [Second priority]
3. [Third priority]
[etc.]
```

Jared reviews this document and marks clips for extraction within 24 hours of the session.
