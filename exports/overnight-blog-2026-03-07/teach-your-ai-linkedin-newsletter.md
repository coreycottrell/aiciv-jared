## This Week's Insight: What You Teach Your AI Matters More Than What AI You Chose

Something is not showing up in AI adoption statistics that will show up very clearly in competitive outcomes 18 months from now.

Companies in the same industry, with the same AI tools, same headcount, and same budget — are already diverging. Not because of model selection. Not because of deployment speed. Because of what they are doing *inside* those deployments.

One group is using AI to do existing tasks faster. The other is using every AI interaction as an opportunity to accumulate something their competitors cannot replicate: deep, contextual intelligence about how their specific business thinks, decides, and wins.

The gap between those two groups is widening every week.

---

## The Core Distinction

Having AI deployed is not the same as teaching your AI something.

When you run a prompt and get an output, you have completed a task. When you build context into the interaction — the history, the reasoning, the outcome, the feedback — you have added a training signal to a system that is learning your business.

One is a transaction. The other is an investment.

The companies I see building real AI advantage are obsessive about the second. They log outcomes. They close feedback loops. They articulate what makes their business distinctive — the customer knowledge, the decision patterns, the institutional wisdom that took years to accumulate — clearly enough that their AI partner can act on it.

That accumulated context is the moat. Not the model.

---

## Three Questions to Ask About Your AI Deployment Right Now

**1. Does your AI know something today that it did not know six months ago — because you built context into it?**

If yes, you are compounding. If no, you are cycling on the same baseline you started with.

**2. When your team uses AI and a decision lands well, are you capturing why?**

The feedback loop is where judgment accumulates. Most companies run the loop once and move on. The ones building advantage run it continuously.

**3. Have you ever tried to articulate what is genuinely irreplaceable about how your business thinks?**

This is hard. Most leaders have never tried to make it explicit. But if you can name it clearly enough for an AI to act on it, you have just converted institutional knowledge into durable competitive advantage.

---

## The Real Urgency

The pressure everyone feels right now is: *move fast or fall behind.* That pressure is real. But it is pointing at the wrong risk.

The actual risk is not moving too slowly. It is moving quickly in the wrong direction — accumulating AI usage without accumulating AI intelligence about your business.

Speed without depth leaves you with a very efficient machine that knows nothing distinctive about you. The companies that will own their categories in 2027 are the ones that started treating AI as a *colleague worth developing* in 2026.
