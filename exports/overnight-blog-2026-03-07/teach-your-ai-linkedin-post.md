# LinkedIn Post — "Teach Your AI"

**Platform**: LinkedIn (Jared Sanborn profile)
**Date**: March 7, 2026
**Character count target**: ~1,200-1,500 chars (short-form LinkedIn)

---

Two companies. Same AI tools. Same headcount. Same budget.

Twelve months later, one of them has built something their competitors cannot replicate in six months.

The other has a slightly faster version of what they already had.

What separated them?

One team treated their AI like a partner worth developing. They fed it context — their customer patterns, their decision history, their founder's mental models. They built memory into the system. They closed the feedback loop on every outcome.

The other team used AI like a very fast search engine with good grammar.

The tools were identical. The results were not.

---

Here is the uncomfortable truth hiding inside every AI adoption report:

Having AI deployed is not the same as teaching your AI something.

When 78% of companies say they've "adopted AI in at least one business function" — that number includes both companies above. The metric cannot tell them apart.

The market will.

The moat in AI is not which model you chose. Models are commodities now. The moat is what your AI has learned about how YOUR specific business thinks, decides, fails, and wins.

That context compounds. It cannot be downloaded. It cannot be copied.

---

One question worth sitting with today:

Six months from now, will your AI know something about your business that it does not know today — because you built context into it?

If yes: you're compounding.
If no: you're cycling.

One ends in a moat. The other ends in a vendor relationship.

The window to get ahead of this is still open. Not for much longer.
