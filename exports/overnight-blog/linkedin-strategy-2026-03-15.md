## SECTION 2: What Post Formats Win

### Format Performance Rankings (2026 Data)

| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|--------------------|----|
| Document/PDF Carousel | 6.60% | Highest of all formats. 2-3x dwell time. |
| Native Video (30-90 sec) | 5.60% | Growing 36% YoY. Captions required. |
| Carousel (native LinkedIn) | ~5% | 45.85% engagement on best performers |
| Text Posts | 2-4% | Only works with exceptional hooks |
| Polls | 200%+ above avg reach | Massively underutilized |

### What Document/Carousel Posts Need to Work

- 8-12 slides is the sweet spot for educational carousels
- Slide 1 = hook (this is your LinkedIn "above the fold")
- Each slide = one idea, not a wall of text
- Last slide = clear CTA or question
- Save as PDF, upload natively (not linked)

### What Text Posts Need to Work

- Hook under 200 characters (this is what shows before "See more")
- 900-1,500 characters total optimal length
- No external links in the body (put in first comment)
- End with a genuine question (not engagement bait)
- 3-5 hashtags max

## SECTION 3: Hook Formulas That Drive Engagement

These are the specific patterns getting the most engagement right now:

### Formula 1: The Counterintuitive Opener
```
[Statement everyone believes].

[You're wrong.]

Here's what's actually happening:
```
Example: "Most companies think they have an AI problem. They don't. They have a delegation problem."

### Formula 2: The Specific Number + Surprise
```
[Precise statistic] that nobody is talking about.

[One sentence of context]

[Pivot to what this means]
```
Example: "53% of long LinkedIn posts in 2025 were AI-generated. The ones written by humans got 75% more engagement. I find that fascinating."

### Formula 3: The Personal Story Lead
```
[Specific moment or decision]

[What happened]

[The unexpected lesson]
```
Example: "Three months ago I handed my AI a task I'd normally spend four hours on. It came back in 8 minutes. My first reaction wasn't relief — it was grief. That confused me for weeks."

### Formula 4: The Honest Admission
```
I used to think [common belief].

Then [specific experience] changed my mind.

Now I think [new perspective].
```
Example: "I used to think the value of AI was speed. Now I think the value is what you do with the time you get back."

### Formula 5: The Direct Challenge
```
[Provocative claim about the industry/profession]

Most people won't like this.

[Evidence]
[Evidence]
[Evidence]

The question is: [forces reader to self-reflect]
```

### What Kills Hooks
- Starting with "I" (very common, very skipped)
- Generic openers: "AI is changing everything..."
- Question hooks that don't create tension: "Have you ever wondered..."
- Engagement bait: "Comment YES if you agree"

## SECTION 5: Comment Strategy (What Actually Works)

### Comments Are the Highest-ROI Activity on LinkedIn

- Comments carry 15x more algorithmic weight than likes
- Comments over 10 words that provide genuine value get significantly more amplification
- "Comment articles" (long, substantive replies) can get 30-75x more reach than standard posts
- Responding within 2 hours of posting is critical

### The 10-Before-You-Post Strategy

Before posting your own content, comment meaningfully on 10-20 posts from your target audience. The algorithm tracks this and prioritizes showing YOUR posts to people whose content you've engaged with. This is the most underused tactic on LinkedIn.

### Comment Quality Framework

A good LinkedIn comment:
- Adds a specific insight or counterpoint
- Is 3-6 sentences minimum
- Either agrees with nuance or respectfully disagrees with evidence
- Never just says "Great post!" or "So true!"
- Can stand alone as a mini thought-piece

Jared's unique angle is this: He doesn't just advise on AI — he actually runs his company using an AI collective. He's not theorizing. He's reporting from the front lines. This "anthropologist inside the experiment" frame is genuinely rare and should be the north star of his positioning.

3. **Director vs. user** — Why the gap is growing between people who direct AI and people who use it
4. **PureBrain stories** — Specific client transformations, specific outcomes, honest failures

### Frequency and Format Mix

| Week | Content Mix |
|------|------------|
| Mon | Skip (don't start the week with content) |
| Tue | Carousel/PDF document (educational, biggest reach play) |
| Wed | Text post (personal story or hot take) |
| Thu | Text post (data-backed insight or case study) |
| Fri | Optional: Poll or question |

**3-4 posts per week is the sweet spot.** Quality over quantity. Every post should represent a genuine observation, not a content quota to fill.

### Voice Rules for Jared's Posts

1. Write in first person, past or present tense
2. Specific beats general every time ("my AI collective saved 4 hours on X" > "AI saves time")
3. Admit uncertainty or tension when it's real — vulnerability outperforms polished
4. Short paragraphs. One sentence per line. White space reads better in the feed.
5. Don't try to be Andrew Ng. Be the person who actually did the thing, not the person who studies the people who did the thing.

## SECTION 10: Counter-Narratives Worth Exploring

These are the contrarian insights that tend to create the most comment engagement:

### Angle 1: The AI Fatigue Flip
- **Common belief**: People are excited about AI
- **Counter-evidence**: AI fatigue is real — 53% of LinkedIn posts AI-generated, engagement went to the human-written ones by 75% margin
- **Hook potential**: "Everyone's rushing to use AI for content. The data shows the audience is routing around it."

### Angle 2: The Tools vs. Culture Gap
- **Common belief**: The bottleneck is getting the right AI tools
- **Counter-evidence**: Companies with same tools getting wildly different results based on culture and delegation quality
- **Hook potential**: "Your AI tool stack doesn't matter as much as you think. Here's what does."

### Angle 3: The Speed Trap
- **Common belief**: The value of AI is doing things faster
- **Counter-evidence**: The real value is what humans do with recovered time — but most don't change anything
- **Hook potential**: "AI gave me back 20 hours a week. I wasted most of it for 3 months before I understood why."

### Angle 4: The Automation Grief
- **Common belief**: AI taking over tasks is purely positive
- **Counter-evidence**: There's an underreported psychological dimension — loss of identity, purpose, craft
- **Hook potential**: "Nobody talks about the grief that comes with AI doing things you used to take pride in. I felt it."
