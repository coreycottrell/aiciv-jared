# LinkedIn Post — "The AI That Knows You Before You Even Speak"

**For**: Jared Sanborn / @jaredsanborn
**Tone**: Peer-to-peer, direct, thought leadership
**Hook type**: Provocative question → real cost → clear contrast

---

Every week, smart people spend hours briefing AI tools that already "worked with them" yesterday.

Re-explaining the company.
Re-establishing context.
Re-introducing themselves to something that was supposed to make them faster.

This is not an AI problem. It is a design problem.

The tools most people use were built to serve millions of users adequately. Not to serve you specifically and exceptionally.

So every session resets. Every conversation starts at zero. Every result you get is only as good as what you managed to paste in before you started.

There is a word for the cost this creates: compounding — but in reverse.

Every week you spend briefing AI, you are not building anything. You are cycling.

The organizations building genuine AI partnerships — where the AI accumulates understanding of the business the way a real team member does — they are compounding. Every week, the gap widens.

The leading indicator is not how many AI tools you use.

It is whether any of them actually know you.
