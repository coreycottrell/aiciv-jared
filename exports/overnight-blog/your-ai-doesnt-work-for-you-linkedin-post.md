## POST COPY

Most business leaders are managing their AI more than their AI is managing their workload.

They spend hours every week prompting, re-prompting, re-briefing, and editing.

They have the tool. They have the subscription. They go through the motions.

And they are still exhausted.

Here is what I have realized: if your AI requires that much management, you have the relationship completely backwards.

Think about how you manage a high-performing employee.

You do not re-explain your company every morning. You do not brief them on your tone preferences before every task. You onboard them once — and then you let them run.

Over time, they learn you. They anticipate what you need. They grow into the role.

That is what an AI partnership should feel like.

But most AI tools are designed to start from zero every single session. No memory. No context. No understanding of who you are, what your business does, or what you actually care about.

So you do all the work.

The business leaders who are genuinely winning with AI in 2026 figured out one thing:

They stopped treating AI like a subscription and started treating it like a relationship.

They invested in onboarding. They built systems that get smarter over time. They asked: "What does my AI need to know to stop needing my management?"

And then they stopped managing it.

That is the difference between a tool and a partner.

New post on this today — including three diagnostic questions to tell you if you have the relationship inverted, and what the correctly calibrated version actually looks like.
