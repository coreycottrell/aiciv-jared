## Blog Post Summary

**Hook**: Meta's "rogue AI" story is being misread. Those agents aren't rogue — they're contextless.

**Core argument** (CEO vs. new hire lens):
- Brilliant new employee makes a costly decision week two: not rogue, just inexperienced
- AI agents face the same problem, at machine speed, but most systems can't learn and retain
- The gap between "what you gave the agent" and "what it needs to know" is your real AI risk
- Persistent memory closes that gap — not just faster prompts, but genuine organizational knowledge

**PureBrain integration**: Natural, in the solution section. Positioned as the architecture that closes the context gap, not as a pitch.

**Honest caveat included**: Memory isn't a perfect shield — it narrows the space of surprising decisions rather than eliminating error.

**FAQ**: 6 questions covering the rogue/contextless distinction, scale of the problem, memory vs. system prompts, model training limitations, self-diagnosis test, and first steps.
