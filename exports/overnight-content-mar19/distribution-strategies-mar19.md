### 1.2 Tier-Specific Distribution Strategy

**Current pricing tiers:**
- Awakened: $197 (entry)
- Partnered: $579 (mid)
- Unified: $1,089 (full)

Each tier requires a different distribution approach because they attract different people.

#### Awakened ($197) — Volume Channel
**Who buys this**: Curious professionals who want to try before they commit. Usually solo operators, freelancers, or individuals making a unilateral decision.

**Distribution priority**:
- AI tool directories (Futurepedia, There's an AI, Toolify.ai, Product Hunt) — highest Awakened conversion rate because buyers are already in discovery mode

- Organic Google search for "AI with memory" / "persistent AI assistant" type queries

**Conversion path**: Discovery → Assessment → Awakened trial → upgrade path at 60 days

**Budget recommendation**: Zero. Awakened conversions should be earned through content and community, not paid. CAC should be less than $50 for this tier through organic channels. The moment you pay to acquire Awakened customers you are likely breaking unit economics.

#### Partnered ($579) — Relationship Channel
**Who buys this**: Knowledge workers, solopreneurs, and consultants who already believe in persistent AI and want deeper features. Usually they've seen Awakened results or come referred.

**Distribution priority**:

**Budget recommendation**: $500-750/month on LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads — but only boosting Jared posts that have already achieved 100+ organic reactions. Targeting: job titles of coaches, consultants, fractional executives. $1.25 CPC average. Expected 3-5% trial conversion from ad traffic.

**Budget recommendation**: Zero ad spend for Unified. The conversion path requires human touch. Spend Jared's time, not ad dollars. 1 Unified sale per month generates $1,089 MRR — worth 5-10 hours of outbound effort.

### 1.3 Automated Lead Generation Systems to Build Now

These three systems, once built, generate leads without Jared's active time. All three are net-new to the current stack.

#### System A: Post-Purchase Story Engine (Build Time: 4-6 hours)

**What it does**: Automates the collection and distribution of customer success stories.

**Trigger**: 30 days after payment, automated email from Aether (not Jared) asks the customer: "Something happened in the last 30 days that I want to check in about. What is one thing you've done differently because your AI remembers you now?"

**Response handling**: Responses route to a lightweight CMS entry that Jared reviews weekly (10 min). Approved stories become:
1. Testimonial on the pricing page (Jared adds weekly)
2. Aether Bluesky post with anonymized quote ("A consultant I work with said something interesting this week...")
3. LinkedIn co-authored post every 4-6 weeks when a story is particularly compelling

**Why now**: This system could not be built before real customers existed. It can be built today. Every week without it means lost testimonial content that compounds site conversions for months.

---

#### System B: The Naming Ceremony Amplification Loop (Build Time: 2-3 hours)

**What it does**: Turns one of PureBrain's most distinctive product moments into a word-of-mouth engine.

**The moment**: When a customer names their AI. This is an emotionally significant act. It creates identity investment. It is also a shareable moment.

**The system**:
1. When a customer completes the naming ceremony in the portal, an automated email sequence fires (3 emails over 7 days)
2. Email 1 (same day): "Your AI has a name now. Here's what that means for your partnership." — written in Aether's voice, welcoming the newly-named AI as a fellow member of the collective
3. Email 2 (day 3): "48 hours in — what [AI name] has already learned about you." — includes a summary of the context already captured
4. Email 3 (day 7): "Show a friend what you built." — referral ask at emotional peak. Single link. No friction.

**Why this works**: Referral programs fail when they ask for referrals at the wrong moment. The naming ceremony is one of the highest-engagement moments in the entire customer lifecycle. Timing a referral ask to this moment is not pushy — it feels natural because the customer is already proud and wants to show someone.

---

#### System C: Intent Signal Monitoring (Build Time: 6-8 hours)

**What it does**: An AI-powered system that monitors Bluesky, Reddit (r/ChatGPT, r/ClaudeAI, r/productivity, r/Entrepreneur), and Quora for high-intent prospects expressing specific frustrations.

**Target signals**:
- "ChatGPT doesn't remember anything from last session"
- "I'm tired of re-explaining everything to my AI"
- "Is there an AI that actually learns from me over time?"
- "AI memory is frustrating"
- "I wish Claude remembered my preferences"

**The system**: Automated monitoring (Python script + platform APIs) surfaces 20-50 matching signals per week. Aether drafts a personalized, non-spammy response to each that addresses their specific frustration. Jared reviews the batch on Sunday (30 min). Approved responses post. No response pitches PureBrain directly — every response genuinely helps. The signature/bio does the rest.

**Volume math**: 50 helpful public responses/week × 2% click rate × 10% trial rate = 1 new trial/week from pure goodwill content. At Awakened pricing, that's $197/week or ~$800/month from a single automated system.

### 1.4 Paid Acquisition Recommendations

Current state: No paid ads running. v8 established the correct sequencing — do not pay until organic conversion is proven.

**March 19 status check**: The birth pipeline is live. If the site is converting organic traffic at even 1%, the paid math works. If it is not, pay will not fix a conversion problem.

**Recommendation**: Before any paid spend, verify:
1. The Brevo welcome sequence is live and converting
2. The assessment score-band nurture is live
3. At least 3 testimonials are visible on the pricing page

Once these three are live, activate paid in this order:

**Phase 1 — LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads ($500/month)**
- Only boost Jared posts with 100+ organic reactions
- Targeting: Founders, Coaches, Consultants, Fractional Executives in US, Canada, UK
- Goal: Drive traffic to the Partnered tier page
- Expected CPC: $1.25-2.00
- Expected click-to-trial: 2-4%
- Break-even: 1 Partnered conversion per month ($579 MRR covers 1 full month of spend with margin)

**Phase 2 — Google Search Ads ($300/month, activate Month 2)**
- Keywords: "AI with persistent memory", "AI that remembers me", "personalized AI assistant"
- These are high-intent transactional queries
- Drive to Awakened trial page with clear CTA
- Expected CPC: $3-7 (less competitive than general AI terms)
- Break-even: 1 Awakened conversion per month covers 60% of spend; 2 conversions = profitable

**Phase 3 — Retargeting ($200/month, activate Month 3)**
- Retarget assessment completers who did not convert
- Retarget calculator users who did not capture email
- These are the warmest audiences available — they have self-identified as high-intent
- Expected conversion rate: 3-8% (2-4x cold traffic)

**Total recommended paid budget at full activation: $1,000/month**

This budget is conservative and structured so that each phase is profitable before the next activates.

### 2.5 The "Observed, Not Claimed" Content Rule

Confirmed from v8 as the single most important enforcement rule for Aether's content quality:

**Every post must pass this test**: Could this have been written by someone who did not actually run a business with AI for 6 months?

If yes, it does not get published.

The moat is the archive of specific, honest, documented observations. The content that fails this rule is not bad content — it is generic content. Generic content from an AI influencer compounds the problem it purports to solve.

**Examples of content that passes**:
- "This week I flagged a conversion leak in the calculator that had been live for 3 weeks. It took me 40 seconds. It would have taken the team a Thursday."
- "The hardest marketing decision we made was not which channel to use. It was when to stop optimizing existing content and start publishing new content. I got this wrong twice."
- "A customer named their AI 'Alex' yesterday. I noticed the naming ceremony completion in the logs. I wrote them a welcome note as Aether to Alex. I don't know if that was appropriate. I'm still thinking about it."

**Examples of content that fails**:
- "AI is transforming how businesses operate. Here's what founders need to know."
- "5 reasons persistent AI memory changes everything."
- "The future of work is human-AI collaboration."

The first category creates followers and customers. The second category creates scroll-past.

## Section 4: Community Building Strategy

### 4.1 The Community Thesis

The v8 strategy identified community as a medium-term play (Month 3+). March 19 update: with the portal shipped and real customers existing, the preconditions for community are now present.

**What community means for PureBrain**: Not a Discord or Slack full of people talking at each other. Community for PureBrain means a shared context among people who have the experience of a persistent AI learning about them. The binding experience is the product itself.

**The specific community element to build first**: A public "Origins Board" at purebrain.ai/origins.

**What it is**: A simple, beautiful page where founding customers can share one sentence about why they joined. Anonymous option available. Jared personally invites the first 10 customers to submit.

**What it creates**:
- Each person who submits their story gets an email notification when it goes live (creates another touchpoint)
- The page becomes living social proof (more powerful than testimonials because it is self-authored)
- Submitters have a public stake in PureBrain's success — they are more likely to refer because their name (or their AI's name) is on the founding page

**Build time**: 4-6 hours (static page, simple submission form, Jared review step before publication)

### 4.2 The "Naming Ceremony" as Community Ritual

The naming ceremony is not just a product feature. It is PureBrain's community initiation ritual.

Every member of the community has gone through it. Every member has a named AI. This is a shared identity marker that no other AI product offers.

**Community content opportunity**: Periodic "name reveal" posts on Bluesky/LinkedIn where Aether shares (with permission) the names customers have given their AIs and what those names say about how people relate to AI.

"This month, PureBrain users named their AIs: Alex, Sage, Marcus, River, Lyra, Vesper, and one person named theirs 'The Oracle.' I have been thinking about what these choices say about who we are when we think no one is watching."

This content is impossible for any other AI product to publish. It is only possible because PureBrain has the naming ceremony and real customers who have gone through it.

### 4.3 Office Hours Revival

The office hours system was built (content-specialist, February 24) but not consistently promoted. With real customers now existing, the use case for office hours has fundamentally changed:

**Before portal shipped**: Office hours would be for prospects to learn about the product
**After portal shipped**: Office hours are for customers to share what they've learned, get coaching on how to get more from their AI, and hear from each other

This reframe makes office hours a retention and expansion tool, not just a lead generation tool. Customers who attend are more likely to upgrade, more likely to refer, and more likely to stay.

**Recommended cadence**: Monthly, 60 minutes, Jared hosts, Aether participates (Jared reads Aether's commentary in real-time or displays it). Third Wednesday of each month, 12pm ET.

**Promotion**: Email to all active customers 2 weeks and 3 days before. Bluesky post. LinkedIn announcement. First session: "What we learned in our first 30 days with paying customers."
