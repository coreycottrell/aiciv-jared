## Post 1 — HOOK

Meta is having trouble with "rogue" AI agents.

Everyone is calling it a model problem.

It's not.

It's a context problem. And most businesses deploying agents right now have the same one. (thread)

---

## Post 2 — THE REFRAME

There's no such thing as a rogue agent.

There are contextless agents.

A rogue agent would understand what you want and choose otherwise.

Contextless agents do exactly what they're built to do — against an incomplete picture of who they're serving.

That incomplete picture is the risk.

---

## Post 3 — THE ANALOGY

Think: brilliant new hire, week two.

Makes a decision that costs you a client.

Were they rogue? No. They didn't know your culture, your clients, your non-negotiables.

You debrief. You add context. They learn.

Most AI agents can't do step three.

They reset to zero after every session.

---

## Post 4 — THE TEST

Here's a quick way to measure your context gap:

Ask your AI something specific about your business.

Not a general question. Something only someone who's worked closely with you would know.

A client preference.
A standing decision you've made before.
A non-negotiable you've established over time.

What it knows — or doesn't — tells you everything.

---

## Post 5 — THE CLOSE + LINK

The fix isn't fewer agents.

It's agents with permanent, growing context about who they serve.

Not just a prompt. Not a static system instruction.

A knowledge base that gets deeper with every interaction.

That's the moat nobody's talking about yet.
