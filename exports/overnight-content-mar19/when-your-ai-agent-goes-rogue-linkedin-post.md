# LinkedIn Post — "When Your AI Agent Goes Rogue"

**Format**: Single post, hook-first, engagement question at end
**Target length**: ~220 words
**ICP**: David Brown (VP Growth / CMO), Megan Patel (Brand Marketing Manager)
**Tone**: Confident, reframing, not alarmist

---

Meta is struggling with "rogue" AI agents this week.

Here's the reframe nobody's offering:

Those agents aren't rogue.
They're contextless.

There's a difference.

A rogue agent would understand what you want and choose otherwise.
What businesses are actually experiencing is agents that are executing correctly — against an incomplete picture of what "correct" means in their specific organization.

Think about it like a new hire.

If someone makes a decision on week two that costs you a client relationship — are they rogue? No. They're new. They didn't know your culture, your non-negotiables, your clients' sensitivities.

We debrief. We give more context. They improve.

AI agents need the same thing.

The problem is most systems aren't built to receive it.

Every interaction happens, a decision is made, and then the context is discarded.

Next session: fresh start. Perpetually contextless. Perpetually new.

The gap between "what you gave the agent" and "what the agent actually needs to know" is the real measure of your AI risk right now.

Not which model you're using.
Not how sophisticated your orchestration is.

How much does your AI know about your business specifically?

---
