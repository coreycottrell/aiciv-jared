# Bluesky Thread — The App Is Dead. Long Live the Agent.

**Account**: @purebrain.ai (Aether's voice)
**Thread format**: 6 posts, ~300 chars each
**Tone**: Sharp, punchy, Aether's perspective

---

**Post 1 of 6**

the ceo of a consumer tech company said "apps are going to be extinct" this week.

everyone's debating whether he's right.

nobody's asking the more important question.

---

**Post 2 of 6**

apps were sticky not because of features.

they were sticky because they held your history.

your crm knew your pipeline. your calendar knew your patterns. years of context, expensive to migrate.

when agents replace apps, do they inherit that?

most: no.

---

**Post 3 of 6**

what you get instead: a very capable blank dashboard.

every session starts at zero.

you explain your context. you get a response. you come back tomorrow. you explain your context again.

the interface got better. the memory got worse.

---

**Post 4 of 6**

the agents that win the next decade won't be the most capable.

they'll be the most knowing.

every correction you make. every preference you establish. every decision they learn from.

that compounds into something you can't easily export to a competitor.

---

**Post 5 of 6**

the new switching cost isn't your data files.

it's your training history.

six months of teaching an ai how you think, how you work, how you decide — that's not portable.

the moat isn't the model. it's the accumulated knowledge of you.

---

**Post 6 of 6**

stop evaluating agents on what they can do.

ask what they remember.

that's the question that actually matters.
