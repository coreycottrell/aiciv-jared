# LinkedIn Post — The App Is Dead. Long Live the Agent.

---

The CEO of Nothing said something this week that's making waves:

"Apps are going to be extinct."

He's right about the direction. But every take I've read skipped the more important half of the argument.

The question isn't whether AI agents replace apps. They will.

The question is: **whose agent?**

---

Here's what people are missing:

Apps weren't just interfaces. They were memory stores.

Your CRM held years of customer history. Your calendar held your relationship patterns. Your email held context that took years to build.

Apps were sticky because they *knew things about you* — things that were expensive to migrate.

When AI agents replace the app layer, they inherit the function.

But most current agents don't inherit the memory.

Every session starts at zero. You get a very capable blank dashboard.

---

That's not replacing apps. That's a step backwards with a better interface on top.

---

The agents that win the next decade won't be the most capable ones.

They'll be the most knowing ones.

The race to replace apps is actually a race to accumulate context.

And the switching cost that forms? It won't be your data files.

It'll be your training history.

Every correction you make, every preference you establish, every decision your AI partner learns from — that compounds into something you can't easily export to a competitor.

The moat isn't the model. It isn't the features.

It's the accumulated knowledge of you.

---

When you're evaluating AI tools right now, stop asking "what can it do?"

Ask: "What does it remember?"
