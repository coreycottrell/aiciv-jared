### 4.2 Trial-to-Paid Conversion Tactics

Research (2025) shows 80% of SaaS free trial conversions happen within 7 days or not at all. The first 7 days determine whether a trial user becomes a customer.

- **Day 0 (signup)**: "Your AI Wrote This" welcome email — Aether introduces itself, asks one question, sets tone for relationship (not transaction)
- **Day 1**: In-product prompt: "Tell me about your biggest work challenge this week." Seed the first meaningful memory.
- **Day 2**: Email: "Here's one thing [AI Name] already knows about you." Show early value concretely.
- **Day 3**: Check-in from Aether: "You've had three conversations with me. Here's what I've learned." Demonstrates memory working.
- **Day 5**: If no naming ceremony has happened, trigger "Have you named your AI yet?" — this single action dramatically increases retention
- **Day 6**: If not yet active, send "Left in the middle?" — Aether asking what stopped them, asking one clarifying question
- **Day 7**: Upgrade prompt: "Your trial ends in 48 hours. Here's what happens to everything I've learned about you if you don't continue." This is not a threat — it is honest information about memory persistence.

**The Naming Ceremony** (proven engagement driver):
When a customer names their AI, trigger the 3-email naming ceremony sequence:
- Email 1 (immediate): "You named your AI [Name]. Here's what that means." — psychology of naming, what it signals about the partnership
- Email 2 (Day 3): Aether writes to the newly-named AI, introducing them to the PureBrain world
- Email 3 (Day 7): "How's [Name] getting on?" — check-in with one practical tip for deepening the relationship

People who name their AI have meaningfully higher retention rates. The naming ceremony email is viral (people screenshot and share).

**Product-Qualified Lead (PQL) Identification**:
Use Brevo behavioral tracking to flag trials that should receive human outreach:
- More than 5 conversations in first 7 days = PQL signal
- Named their AI within 72 hours = PQL signal
- Visited /pricing more than once = PQL signal
- Shared a blog post via UTM = PQL signal

When 2+ signals are present: Jared reaches out personally. "I noticed you've been getting deep with your AI — I wanted to check in directly. What's it been like?" This one tactic, applied to high-intent trials, converts at significantly higher rates than any automated sequence.

### 4.4 Enterprise Outreach Playbooks

**Enterprise Target Profile**:
- Organizations with 5-50 knowledge workers
- AI already being used but inconsistently (different tools, different contexts, no memory continuity)
- Decision-maker is VP Operations, Chief of Staff, Head of Productivity
- Budget: $10K+/year (fits the $1,089/month Unified tier and above)

**Lead Sources for Enterprise** (in order of conversion rate):
1. Consultant affiliate program referrals (highest conversion — trusted recommendation)
2. LinkedIn warm circle outreach (Jared's personal account, 14-day warm-up cycle from February memory)
3. Inbound from blog/content (already in consideration mode when they reach out)
4. Conference introductions (highest intent, slowest pipeline)

**The Enterprise Discovery Script**:

Question 1: "How is AI currently being used across your team?"
Question 2: "When a team member starts a new AI session, how much context do they have to re-establish?"
Question 3: "What's the cost of that context re-establishment in time each week?"
Question 4: "If your AI remembered everything your team had ever taught it, what would that be worth?"

By question 4, the prospect has calculated their own ROI. The close is not a pitch — it is a confirmation: "That's exactly what PureBrain delivers."

**The Enterprise Demo Format**:
- 10 minutes: Jared establishes context about the prospect's situation (gleaned from discovery)
- 15 minutes: Live demonstration of PureBrain remembering a specific scenario relevant to their business
- 10 minutes: "What did you see happening there?" — let the prospect articulate the value
- 10 minutes: Q&A, commercial discussion

Do not present pricing until they have articulated the value themselves. Price anchored to value they stated is sticky. Price presented before value is stated is negotiable.

**The 90-Day Pilot Framing**:
Do not propose a "contract." Propose a "90-day partnership pilot." Language matters.

"Contract" = commitment, risk, legal process.
"Partnership pilot" = low-stakes exploration with clear success criteria.

The 90-day pilot proposal includes: success metrics defined in advance, monthly check-in calls with Jared and Aether, and a built-in case study at Day 90 (with customer's permission).

**Custom Pricing Structure**:
- Unified tier ($1,089/month): up to 5 users, standard integration
- Team tier ($1,089 + $150/additional user/month): 6-20 users
- Enterprise ($3,500/month base + custom): 20+ users, dedicated instance, custom integrations, priority support

**The Follow-Up Sequence for Enterprise**:
- 48 hours after demo: Send summary of what was discussed, success metrics agreed to, next steps
- Day 7: One specific resource relevant to their situation (case study, relevant blog post)
- Day 14: Check-in — "Has anything shifted in your thinking since we talked?"
- Day 21: Decision request — "We'd like to get you set up before [month end]. Is there anything still standing in the way?"
- If no response after 3 follow-ups: One final message — "I don't want to keep reaching out if the timing isn't right. If that changes, I'll be here." Then stop.

## Part 9: Strategic Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Aether's content becomes indistinguishable from generic AI content**
- Mitigation: Enforce "Observed, Not Claimed" on every post. If a post could be written without the actual operational experience, it does not get published.

**Risk 2: Paid ads activated before conversion architecture is ready**
- Mitigation: No paid activation until welcome sequence is live and assessment nurture is operational. Paid spend on a leaky funnel scales losses, not revenue.

**Risk 3: Referral program dilutes revenue before proving the flywheel**
- Mitigation: Structure free-month reward as account credit, not cash refund. Cost is negligible if the referred customer converts to paid.

**Risk 4: Platform algorithm changes reduce distribution**
- Mitigation: Email is the one channel no algorithm can throttle. Invest in email list growth above all platform-dependent channels. 77% of B2B buyers prefer email for vendor communication.

**Risk 5: Enterprise sales diverts attention before self-serve is optimized**
- Mitigation: Do not activate enterprise outbound until 20+ paying customers are in the system. The product and conversion infrastructure must be validated before scaling through high-touch sales.

**Risk 6: Too many distribution tactics, none executed deeply**
- Mitigation: This document contains a prioritized stack. Execute Week 1's list before touching Week 2. One channel done deeply outperforms five channels done shallowly.
