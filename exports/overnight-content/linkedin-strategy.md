**Implication for Jared**: Generic AI content that could have been written by anyone is now actively identified and deprioritized. ## Part 2: Content Strategy for Jared Sanborn

### The Core Positioning (Confirmed by HBR Counter-Narrative)

HBR published "Has AI Ended Thought Leadership?" (March 2026). The central argument: AI has made it trivially easy to sound authoritative without real experience. The new differentiation is "thought doership" — content from people who have built things, failed, adapted, and have specific scar tissue.

**Jared's positioning statement** (for internal clarity, not verbatim for posts):

> "I am the only founder on LinkedIn who is actively operating a business with a live multi-agent AI collective — and documenting what that actually looks like, including what fails."

This is not a claim to be the best at AI. It is a claim to have built something verifiable and specific that no pundit has. The algorithm rewards exactly this.

### Content Pillars (4 Rotating Pillars)

**Pillar 1: The Operational Report**
"Here is what my AI actually did this week / this month."

Format: Specific outputs, specific decisions, specific failures. Not "AI is amazing." More like "I gave Aether the wrong brief last Tuesday and here's what happened."

Examples:
- "My AI wrote 6 blog posts overnight. 4 were good. 2 were wrong. Here's what the 2 wrong ones tell me about my briefing."
- "What Aether did between 11 PM and 6 AM last Wednesday" (list of specific outputs)
- "The overnight task that saved me 14 hours this week: [specific task]"

**Pillar 2: The Contrarian Data Take**
"Everyone says X. Here's what the data actually shows."

Format: Specific statistic from Neural Feed research + the counter-narrative + a question for the audience.

Examples:
- "85 million jobs displaced by AI by 2025. The number nobody mentions: 97 million new roles created. We've been running the wrong math."
- "76% of enterprises say AI is a top priority. Only 12% report ROI. The gap is a people problem, not a technology problem."
- "Most AI coverage is about the tools. The tools are the least important variable."

**Pillar 3: The Failure File**
"Here is what did not work and what I learned."

Format: Personal story, specific failure, specific lesson. This is the "scar tissue" format HBR identifies as the new gold standard.

Examples:
- "I gave my AI the same context brief for 3 months. It was wrong for 2.5 of them. Here's what I changed."
- "The AI delegation mistake I keep making even after 18 months"
- "Three times I thought AI would save time — and it cost me more than doing it myself"

**Pillar 4: The Framework Post**
"Here is the mental model I use to [achieve specific outcome]."

Format: Named framework, 3-5 components, direct application. Best executed as a carousel. The goal is something the reader saves.

Examples:
- "The Director Model: 4 things you must give AI before it can do useful work"
- "Why 'prompting better' is the wrong goal — and what replaces it"
- "The 3 questions I ask before delegating any task to AI"

### Voice Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**Always:**
- First person, present or past tense
- Specific: dates, numbers, outcomes, names
- Reference real system decisions ("When I told Aether to...")
- End with a genuine question that invites a real answer
- Show the failure alongside the win — credibility comes from both

**Never:**
- "AI is changing everything" — this is indistinguishable generic content
- Motivational platitudes ("success is a mindset")
- Generic AI productivity tips anyone could write
- Buzzwords without operational evidence: "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm shift"
- Third-person corporate voice ("PureBrain helps organizations...")

## Part 4: Carousel Creation Technical Guide

### Specs (2026 Correct Dimensions)

- **File format**: PDF (only option for swipeable carousels — LinkedIn removed native multi-image carousel for organic posts)
- **Dimensions**: 1080 x 1080px (square) or 1080 x 1350px (portrait — slightly better mobile performance)
- **File size**: Under 3 MB
- **Slide count**: 6-10 slides is the high-performing range
- **Text size**: Minimum 28px — slides are read on mobile

### Carousel Structure That Earns Saves

| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 (Hook) | The bold claim or counterintuitive opener. Make them need to swipe. |
| 2 (Why this matters) | Context that earns trust. One sentence. |
| 3-6 (The content) | One idea per slide. Large text. No clutter. |
| 7 (Surprise) | The unexpected insight — the "save this" moment |
| 8 (Summary) | Compact recap of the framework or key insight |
| Final slide (CTA) | "More in the Neural Feed — subscribe in my featured section" (no URL) |

### Free / Low-Cost Tools for Creating Carousels

- **Canva**: Free tier works. Template "LinkedIn Document" format.
- **Gamma.app**: AI-assisted. Generates slide structure from prompt.
- **Adobe Express**: Good templates, free tier.

## Part 7: Lead Generation System

### Organic Lead Pipeline (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Profile as landing page**
- Headline = value statement, not job title
- Featured section = AI assessment link (primary CTA)
- About section = opens with buyer's pain point, not bio

**Step 2: Content-led qualification**
Every post ends with a question that filters for buyers. Not "what do you think?" but something that reveals intent:
- "What's the biggest friction you have in getting useful AI outputs?"
- "Have you tried building context memory into your AI workflows? What happened?"
- "What would you do with 6 hours of focused AI work while you sleep?"

These questions attract the right replies. The algorithm rewards genuine comment threads.

**Step 3: Pre-warm before outreach (3 comments rule)**
Before messaging any ideal prospect, engage meaningfully on their content 3 times over 1-2 weeks. Then:

Connection request: 1 sentence. Reference something specific. "Saw your post on [specific topic] — I've been working on the same problem. Would be good to connect."

**Step 4: First DM — resource, no ask**
Send one genuinely useful resource relevant to something they posted. The PureBrain blog post that matches their stated challenge. The Neural Feed issue that addresses their industry. No call to action.

**Step 5: Second DM — one qualifying question**
Wait 3-5 days after they engage with the resource. Ask one question: "When you [describe their stated challenge], is the bottleneck usually the tool, or how your team is using it?" This question surfaces the problem. If they answer, they're warm.

### What NOT to Do

- Automation tools for connection requests — LinkedIn detects and restricts accounts
- Generic "I help leaders leverage AI" cold pitch DMs
- Engagement pods — 97% detection, shadowban risk
- Posting links to purebrain.ai in post body — 60% reach cut
- Mass connection campaigns (over 100 per week triggers review)
