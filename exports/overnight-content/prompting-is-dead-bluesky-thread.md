## Post 1 (Hook)

Prompt engineering was never the skill.

It was the workaround.

The real skill — getting AI to actually know your business — is finally possible. And it makes everything you learned about prompting mostly irrelevant.

🧵

---

## Post 2

Why prompting mattered in the first place:

Every session started at zero. No memory of who you were, what you'd built, or what had already failed.

So you front-loaded everything. Context dumps. Elaborate instructions. Jailbreaks.

You got good at telling AI what to know because there was no other way.

---

## Post 3

What replaced it:

→ Persistent memory (AI that retains context across sessions)
→ Agent orchestration (specialists that run without hand-holding)
→ Workflows (repeatable systems that don't need re-prompting)

The better your infrastructure, the less you need to prompt. That's not a coincidence.

---

## Post 4

If your team is investing in prompt engineering right now, ask this question:

Are we building a skill — or a workaround for a problem that's being solved?

Prompting got us through the early years. Memory and agents get us through the next decade.

Doubling down on the workaround is a positioning risk.
