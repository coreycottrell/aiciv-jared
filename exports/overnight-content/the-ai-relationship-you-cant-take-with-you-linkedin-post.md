When you switch AI tools, you lose more than prompts and templates.

You lose eight months of accumulated context — the trained preferences, the learned vocabulary, the pattern the system built of how you work.

And unlike your documents or your data, that context cannot be exported. There is no standard format. No portability layer. What you built inside that platform stays inside that platform.

This is the switching cost that never appears in vendor RFPs.

Most AI evaluation frameworks look at:
→ Model quality and benchmarks
→ API pricing and enterprise tiers
→ Security controls and compliance
→ Integrations with existing tooling

Almost none of them ask: what happens to everything the AI has learned about us if we leave?

The 2025 Salesforce State of Work report found that workers whose AI "knows their work context well" report 42% higher satisfaction and 31% better output quality than workers using the same tools without that persistent layer.

Same tool. Different relationship depth. Measurably different results.

Gartner called it "AI relationship depth" in their 2026 enterprise report. Companies optimizing for context retention over constant model switching are pulling ahead.

The model advantage window is 6 to 8 weeks, per McKinsey. The relationship advantage compounds for years.

Two questions worth adding to your next AI vendor evaluation:

How does this system accumulate and retain contextual knowledge about how our team works?

What happens to that context if we leave?

If the vendor cannot answer both clearly, you now know something important about what they actually built for.
