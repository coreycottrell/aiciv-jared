## Primary Draft

50% of leaders trust AI for busywork.

Only 28% trust it for decisions.

That gap isn't a technology problem.

---

Here's what's actually happening.

You trust AI when you can verify the output immediately and the cost of a mistake is low.

Sorted emails. Formatted reports. Drafted responses.

Something's off? You catch it in seconds.

Now ask AI to help you decide whether to enter a new market, restructure your team, or allocate a budget.

Trust collapses.

Not because AI got worse at the harder question.

Because you have almost no history of it doing *that kind* of work for you specifically.

And trust requires history.

---

Most AI pilots are designed to test capability.

Can it summarize? Yes.
Can it draft? Yes.
Can it analyze? Yes.

Capability confirmed. Pilot "successful."

But demonstrating capability and building trust are completely different things.

Only 8.6% of companies have AI agents in production.

75% of enterprise AI pilots stall before they get there.

That's not a technology gap. That's a trust gap.

---

The question most companies are asking: "What can our AI do?"

The question that actually matters: "How is our AI learning about us?"

AI that starts from zero every session never earns the right to the harder problems.

AI that accumulates your context, your standards, your way of thinking - that's the version you eventually trust with the decisions that matter.

The ceiling for AI in your business isn't the model.

It's the relationship you're willing to build with it.

---

Where does your team fall on the trust curve?

## Formatting Notes for LinkedIn

LinkedIn post formatting that maximizes reach:

- **Line 1-3**: Hook must stand alone before "see more" cutoff. Tested format above does this - the 50%/28% stat + reframe fits in ~200 characters.
- **White space**: Heavy use of single-sentence paragraphs throughout. LinkedIn algorithm rewards time-on-post; white space slows reading speed.
- **No links in body**: Link is in first comment only (LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links in body text).
- **Hashtags**: 5 hashtags, end of post. More than 5 can reduce reach.
- **Question ending**: "Where does your team fall on the trust curve?" is intentionally open-ended. Prompts comments without feeling forced.

## Timing Recommendation

**Best window**: Tuesday or Wednesday, 8-10am EST

**Why**: Business decision-makers are in LinkedIn feed pre-standup or post-morning email. Data vs. motivation content performs better Tuesday/Wednesday than Monday or Friday.

**Reply strategy**: Reply to every comment within 2 hours of posting. Comments restart the engagement clock; your replies extend distribution window.
