## SECTION 1: Research Findings — LinkedIn Algorithm 2026

### Algorithm Priority Stack (ranked)

1. **Early engagement velocity** — The first 60 minutes after posting determines whether a post gets broadly distributed. Saves, comments, and shares in that window are the signal. Likes matter less than ever.

2. **Comment weight** — The algorithm weights meaningful comments (3+ words, substantive replies) above all other engagement forms. A post with 10 genuine comments outperforms one with 100 likes.

3. **Zero-click content** — LinkedIn actively suppresses posts that push users off-platform. Value must be delivered IN the post. External links belong in the first comment, not the post body.

4. **Content format score** — Carousel PDFs and native video receive the highest format bonus. Text-only posts receive the lowest. Image posts fall in the middle.

5. **Creator consistency signal** — Posting 4+ times per week creates a momentum signal that compounds. Accounts posting daily for 30+ days receive priority placement in follower feeds.

6. **Niche authority score** — LinkedIn tracks which topics you consistently post about and builds a "niche graph." Staying in lane (AI, business transformation, leadership + AI) builds compounding authority.

### Format Performance Benchmarks (2025-2026 data)

| Format | Relative Engagement | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Carousel (PDF) | 6-8x text baseline | Highest reach, save-worthy content |
| Native video | 5x text baseline | LinkedIn Live achieves 24x |
| Image post | 2-3x text baseline | Single image outperforms galleries |
| Text post (hook-driven) | Baseline | Strong for conversation, low for reach |
| Post with outbound link | 0.5-0.8x baseline | Algorithm actively suppresses |
| Poll | 1.5x baseline | High impression count, lower signal quality |

### What Gets Penalized

- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree")
- Posting more than once per day (suppresses both posts)
- External links in the post body
- Posts with 6+ hashtags
- Copying/pasting content from other platforms
- Generic AI-generated content with no personal voice

### Optimal Posting Times (Sprout Social, 2.7B engagement dataset)

- **Best overall**: Tuesday–Thursday 8AM–2PM
- **Top single slot**: Wednesday 8AM and 12PM
- **Strong**: Thursday 9AM, 1PM
- **Acceptable**: Friday 7AM–2PM, Monday 11AM–12PM
- **Avoid**: Weekends, evenings after 6PM

## SECTION 2: AI Thought Leader Patterns — What's Working

### The Winning Archetype in 2026

The highest-performing AI thought leaders on LinkedIn share a consistent pattern:

**Pattern 1: The Practitioner Reveal**
Not "here's what AI can do" — but "here's what happened when we tried it with a real client." Specificity + outcome = share-worthy. These posts drive 3-5x the engagement of general AI commentary.

**Pattern 2: The Contrarian Take**
The AI space is crowded with "AI will change everything" content. The accounts breaking through lead with friction: "Why 95% of AI pilots fail" (Jared has this angle). Contrarian hooks get saves and comments because they invite debate.

**Pattern 3: The Framework Post**
Numbered frameworks perform extremely well in AI content — "3 things that separate AI tools from AI partners." These become reference posts that users save and return to. Carousel format amplifies this format massively.

**Pattern 4: The Human Cost / Human Gain Story**
Content that centers the human experience of working with AI — not the AI itself. What did it feel like for the client? What was the before/after? This plays into LinkedIn's shift toward authentic, human-centered narratives.

**Pattern 5: The Prediction with Stakes**
"By end of 2026, every CMO without an AI memory system will be at a competitive disadvantage." Predictions with specific timeframes and consequences generate comment debate and shares. High-risk, high-reward format.

### What Top AI Voices Are NOT Doing

- Not posting generic "AI is transforming [industry]" content — this is now commodity
- Not sharing news articles with a one-line take — suppressed by algorithm
- Not hiding behind corporate speak — the platform rewards personal voice
- Not posting once a week — all high-performers post 4-5x weekly minimum

## SECTION 3: B2B SaaS LinkedIn Strategies

### The Three Pillars That Drive Pipeline

**Pillar 1: Authority Content**
Establishes why Jared/PureBrain is the expert. Blog repurposes, frameworks, contrarian takes, client results. This content rarely drives immediate DMs but creates the "I keep seeing this person, they must know what they're talking about" effect that precedes inbound conversations.

**Pillar 2: Social Proof Content**
Client wins (anonymized or named), milestone posts, "here's what our customers told us" content. B2B buyers need to see that others have taken the leap. This category is systematically underused by most B2B SaaS companies.

**Pillar 3: Invitation Content**
Direct asks — to read the newsletter, to book a demo, to take the AI assessment. Most B2B marketers are afraid to mix pillars 1 and 3. The data says mixing it in at 1-in-5 posts does not damage authority; it drives conversions.

### B2B Posting Frequency Data

- Companies posting weekly see 2x the engagement of companies posting monthly
- Consistency over 90+ days creates a compounding follower growth effect
- The optimal split for a founder's personal account: 60% Pillar 1, 20% Pillar 2, 20% Pillar 3

### Company Page vs. Personal Profile Priority

For companies Jared's size, the personal profile always outperforms the company page for reach and engagement. The algorithm gives 5-10x more organic reach to personal posts. The company page's role is credibility, not reach.

**PureBrain.ai Company Page should focus on**:
- Credibility and social proof (the "due diligence" stop)
- Reposts of Jared's high-performing content
- Case studies, product updates, job postings
- Employee advocacy content

## SECTION 4: LinkedIn Newsletter Growth — Neural Feed

### Key Platform Facts

- Newsletters on LinkedIn bypass the feed algorithm — subscribers receive a notification directly
- Links in newsletter articles are NOT downranked (unlike post body links)
- LinkedIn controls the subscriber list — emails are NOT accessible without Premium tools
- 150 followers minimum required; Creator Mode must be enabled

### Growth Tactics That Work

**Tactic 1: The Launch Post**
A dedicated post announcing the newsletter (not just a share — a full hook-driven post explaining WHO it's for, WHAT they'll get, and WHY they should subscribe now). This single post, when done well, can generate 100-300 subscribers on day one.

**Tactic 2: Cross-Platform Newsletter Mention**
Every Bluesky thread, email footer, and blog post should mention Neural Feed with a direct subscription link. LinkedIn makes the subscribe link shareable.

**Tactic 3: The "First Issue" Preview**
Post a teaser of the newsletter content in the feed — a condensed version — with "full issue in the newsletter" as the CTA. This creates FOMO and is compliant with LinkedIn's algorithm (no outbound link penalty).

**Tactic 4: Guest Swap**
Identify 3-5 non-competing AI/marketing newsletter creators and propose a subscriber swap — mention their newsletter in Neural Feed, they mention Neural Feed in theirs. This is the fastest organic growth method at Jared's audience size.

**Tactic 5: Consistency Signal**
Weekly publishing on the same day (e.g., every Thursday) creates a habitual open behavior. Inconsistent publishing kills open rates. The recommendation: Thursday 8AM publish time.

### Monetization Path for Neural Feed

Neural Feed → AI Assessment CTA → PureBrain demo → Client conversion

Every subscriber should see: 1 editorial issue → 1 value-add issue → 1 CTA issue → repeat. The CTA issue should drive to the AI Partnership Audit lead magnet or directly to a calendar booking.

## SECTION 6: Engagement Tactics

### Hook Formulas That Work in 2026

LinkedIn engagement is front-loaded in the first 2 lines (the "see more" threshold). These structures perform consistently:

- **Contrarian**: "Everyone's talking about AI. Nobody's talking about why 95% of AI projects fail in 90 days."
- **Observation**: "I've had 47 conversations with CMOs this quarter. The same thing comes up every time."
- **Result-first**: "We helped a brand marketing team reduce their AI tool costs by 60% without losing a single workflow."
- **Bold claim**: "The next wave of B2B differentiation won't come from your product. It'll come from who your AI knows."
- **Relatable frustration**: "Your team adopted Copilot. Now they're using it to write emails faster and nothing else."

### Comment Strategy

Comments are as valuable as posts for building authority. 10 thoughtful comments per day (on posts from ideal clients, industry leaders, and complementary creators) generates profile views, connection requests, and inbound DMs.

**The 5-5-10 Comment Rule**:
- 5 comments on posts by ideal clients/prospects
- 5 comments on posts by industry peers (AI, marketing)
- 10 minutes maximum total — use templates adapted to context

### Hashtag Strategy

- 3-5 hashtags maximum per post
- Mix: 1 broad (#artificialintelligence), 1 mid (#aimarketing), 1 niche (#brandAI or #purebrain)
- Do NOT use the same hashtag set on every post — vary them
- Branded hashtag: establish #PureBrain or #NeuralFeed as a searchable content hub
