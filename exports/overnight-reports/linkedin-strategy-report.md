## Part 1: What Is Working on LinkedIn Right Now (Feb 2026)

### The Macro Shift

LinkedIn's algorithm has moved to a **Depth Score** model. It weighs quality of interaction rather than quantity. One thoughtful comment from a CEO matters more than 50 fire-emoji reactions. Three meaningful saves outperform 200 likes.

Key platform metrics as of Q4 2025 / Q1 2026:
- Impressions: down 50-65% from peak (down 12% from Q4 2024)
- Follower growth: down 59% industry-wide
- Engagement: up 12% (quality beats volume)
- Top 1% creator reach: 149x the average post
- Document posts (PDF carousels): 6.60% engagement rate — highest of any format
- Carousels: 4.0x more reach than text-only posts
- Posts with 3+ helpful links: 210-236% higher reach (the "avoid links" rule is dead)
- Posts with 1,300-3,000 characters: 31-38% better than short posts

### What the Algorithm Now Rewards

**Dwell time above all else.** LinkedIn measures how long people stop on your content. Long-form posts, carousels, and document PDFs all force dwell. Short posts get scrolled past faster than they can register.

**Engagement from authority figures.** A comment from a CEO or someone with a large engaged following is worth more algorithmically than 10 comments from accounts with no engagement history. Jared should focus on being in the comment sections of the right people — not just anyone.

**Saves and reposts over likes.** Saves signal "I want to come back to this." LinkedIn uses this as a strong quality signal. Every piece of content should make the reader think "I need to save this." Reposts distribute content outside your immediate network.

**Niche expertise over broad appeal.** The algorithm builds a "topic DNA" for every profile and distributes content to users interested in that topic — even outside your network. This means Jared's AI implementation content can reach non-connected AI buyers.

**Authentic personal voice.** The algorithm is now detecting AI-sounding language and deprioritizing it. Generic phrasing, corporate speak, and "Great post!" comments all get penalized. Jared's real voice — direct, founder-honest, experience-based — is an algorithmic asset.

### What Is NOT Working

- Posting twice in 24 hours (up to 20% reach penalty)
- Short promotional posts (0.8x multiplier)
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree" — now detected and penalized)
- Generic contrarian hooks ("Unpopular opinion:")
- Mass generic commenting
- Posting without engaging in your own comment section within the first 2 hours
- Video for reach (video reach dropped — use video only for trust-building and product demos)

### The Handwritten Whiteboard Visual Trend (Emerging January 2026)

One of the highest-performing content formats right now is AI-generated "handwritten whiteboard" visuals — infographics that look like they were drawn on a physical whiteboard with colored markers. Creator Charlie Hills got 480,000 impressions from three such posts, generated using Google Gemini.

The reason they work: they break the scroll pattern. Every other post looks polished and corporate. A hand-drawn infographic looks authentic and educational. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards the dwell time these generate.

Jared and Aether can create these for AI-related frameworks using Gemini. This is an immediate tactical opportunity.

## Part 2: Jared Sanborn Personal LinkedIn Strategy

### Strategic Position

Jared's positioning on LinkedIn should be: **"The CEO who actually builds AI into his company, live, in public."**

Not an AI theorist. Not a consultant. A founder who is deploying real AI systems, hitting real walls, solving real problems, and documenting the journey. This is what PureBrain.ai IS — and LinkedIn is where that story should be told.

This angle has two advantages: (1) it is genuine and therefore passes LinkedIn's authenticity filters, and (2) it generates curiosity from other business owners who want to do the same thing.

### Profile Optimization (Do This Week)

**Headline** — Current format is likely job-title-first. It should be outcome-first.

Recommended structure: `I help [ICP] [achieve result] through [your method]`

Suggested headline for Jared:
> "CEO @ Pure Technology | Building AI that actually works for business — not theory, not hype, real implementation"

First 60 characters are what appear in feeds before truncation. Make them count.

**Banner Image** — Must do three things: state an ideal outcome, show social proof, and include a CTA. Right now the PureBrain.ai banner should be visible here with a clear message like "AI that learns YOUR business. PureBrain.ai."

**About Section** — Should open with a metric or proof in the first line (before "see more"). Not "I am a..." Not "Welcome to my profile." Something like: "I've been building companies for 15 years. The last 12 months have been the most pivotal — because AI changed everything about how we work."

The About section should tell the founder story, name PureBrain.ai specifically, and end with a single clear CTA (link to purebrain.ai or a lead magnet).

**Featured Section** — Keep it to 2-3 items maximum. Use 1200x628px custom graphics. Recommended items:
- Link to PureBrain.ai with a custom graphic showing the value proposition
- Link to The Neural Feed newsletter signup
- Link to the AI Partnership Audit (lead magnet)

**Link in Bio** — Push to whichever asset is most valuable at any given time. Currently: PureBrain.ai or the Neural Feed newsletter. This link appears on your feed posts as well as your profile — maximize it.

### Content Pillars (5 Rotating Themes)

Based on the Chris Donnelly framework adapted to Jared's situation:

**Pillar 1: Founder-Led AI Implementation (40% of content)**
What you're actually building with AI. Wins, failures, surprising discoveries. "I tried X with AI this week. Here's what happened." This is the highest-trust content because only Jared can write it. It builds authentic authority.

Example topics:
- How Aether (the AI system) handled a client problem this week
- What I got wrong about AI implementation in January
- The moment I realized our AI needed memory to actually be useful
- 3 things I've learned building an AI system from scratch

**Pillar 2: Educational Frameworks on AI Adoption (25% of content)**
Frameworks that help business owners understand AI without needing a CS degree. These become carousel/PDF posts. They generate saves and shares. They establish Jared as a trusted educator, not just a product seller.

Example topics:
- The 3-stage AI adoption curve (most businesses are stuck in stage 1)
- Why your AI tools aren't talking to each other (and how to fix it)
- The difference between an AI tool and an AI system
- How to measure ROI on AI investment

**Pillar 3: Pure Technology Origin Story and Vision (20% of content)**
The human story. Jared's journey. His daughter Lily. The loss of $70-80K early on. The moment digital marketing changed his trajectory. Why he started Pure Technology. These posts build emotional connection and are the ones that get saved and shared the most. One of these per week minimum.

Example topics:
- Why I started Pure Technology (and what I almost gave up on)
- The thing my daughter said that changed how I think about our mission
- I lost $70,000 before I figured out business. Here's what I learned.
- Why I believe every person deserves access to AI-powered tools, not just enterprises

**Pillar 4: Client/Case Study Proof (10% of content)**
Real results. Before/after. "Here's how a client changed X using PureBrain.ai." These are conversion posts — lower frequency but high trust. Even anonymized case studies work if the numbers are real.

**Pillar 5: Industry Commentary (5% of content)**
Jared's take on AI news, platform updates, and industry trends. Keeps the profile current and relevant. Low effort if done as a reaction to something that already happened.

### Posting Schedule

Based on algorithm data (Sunday and Saturday now generate highest impressions; 5-7am GMT and 11am-1pm GMT are peak windows):

| Day | Time (EST) | Content Type | Priority |
|-----|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Monday | 7:00 AM | Founder implementation story | High |
| Wednesday | 8:00 AM | Educational framework or carousel | High |
| Friday | 7:00 AM | Origin/personal story OR client proof | High |
| Sunday | 9:00 AM | Industry commentary or shorter insight | Medium |

3-4 posts per week is optimal. Not daily. Quality + 24-hour spacing wins.

### Engagement Protocol (Daily Non-Negotiables)

**Morning (before posting your own content):**
- Spend 20-30 minutes commenting on 10-15 posts from:
  - 50% peers (other founders, CEOs building AI products)
  - 30% potential clients (business owners in your ICP)
  - 20% large AI/tech accounts (build visibility with their audiences)
- Comments must be 15+ words, add a genuine perspective or question
- Never "Great post!" — always add one specific insight or question

**After posting:**
- Reply to EVERY comment in the first 2 hours (this dramatically boosts reach — posts with author replies achieve 2.4x greater reach)
- Pin a strong comment as the first response to start the thread
- Check who liked/shared and DM the most relevant ones with a warm, non-pitchy message

**DM strategy:**
- Warm outreach only: "I saw your comment on my post about X — you made a great point about Y. Are you seeing the same thing in your business?"
- Never lead with a pitch. Lead with genuine curiosity.
- The S.E.R.V.E. framework: Show up, Educate, Relate, Value relationships, Engage authentically

### The First-Hour Rule

The 2026 algorithm has a "First 90 Minutes" window. If a post does not get meaningful engagement in the first hour and a half, it is deprioritized and stops spreading. This means:
- Post when your audience is online (use the schedule above)
- Have 3-5 people you trust who will engage meaningfully in the first 30 minutes (not emoji reactions — real comments)
- Reply to every comment within minutes of receiving it during this window

## Part 3: PureBrain.ai Company Page Strategy

### The Hard Truth About Company Pages

Personal profiles generate **561% more reach** than company pages for the same content. Company pages represent roughly 5% of user feeds. Personal profiles dominate 65% of content consumption. The algorithm heavily favors people over brands.

This means the PureBrain.ai company page is NOT a primary content channel. It has a different role.

### The Right Role for the Company Page

**Official credibility hub.** When a prospect does their research after seeing Jared's personal posts, they will look up PureBrain.ai on LinkedIn. The company page needs to be polished, credible, and clear. It should answer: "What is this? Why does it matter? How do I get started?"

**Reposter of Jared's best personal content.** When a Jared post does well, reshare it from the company page. This extends reach to company followers without creating redundant content.

**Employee advocacy amplifier.** As Pure Technology grows, employee posts that tag the company page and share company content multiply organic reach significantly. Even 3-5 employees sharing one company post can drive 10x the reach of the page itself.

**LinkedIn Ads base.** When running paid campaigns (Lead Gen Forms, Thought Leader Ads), the company page is the sponsoring entity. Ensure it looks credible before running ads.

### Company Page Optimization Priorities

1. **About section** — Clear value proposition for PureBrain.ai. What it is, who it's for, what changes after using it.
2. **Banner** — Should match purebrain.ai branding. Dark background, PureBrain visual language.
3. **Products/Services section** — List PureBrain.ai as a product with a clear description.
4. **Regular cadence** — Post 2x per week minimum (reshares + original announcements). Company newsletters average ~40% open rates on LinkedIn — launch a company newsletter if not already done.
5. **Featured section** — Link to purebrain.ai with a strong CTA graphic.

The Neural Feed newsletter, published through Jared's LinkedIn newsletter feature, with each issue explicitly attributed as "Written by Aether with Jared Sanborn." This is the highest-impact option because:

## Part 5: Lead Generation Strategy

### The Content Funnel Architecture

LinkedIn lead generation in 2026 works as a three-stage funnel. Each stage requires different content:

**Stage 1: Top of Funnel — Build Awareness and Trust (70% of content)**
- Who: People who don't know Jared or PureBrain.ai yet
- Content: Educational frameworks, founder stories, industry commentary
- Goal: Follower growth, newsletter signups, profile visits
- No asks. Pure value delivery.

**Stage 2: Middle of Funnel — Capture Intent (20% of content)**
- Who: People who have engaged with Jared's content multiple times
- Content: Lead magnets, webinar invitations, "how we do it" posts
- Assets needed: AI Partnership Audit (already exists), PureBrain.ai free trial or consultation, Neural Feed newsletter as mid-funnel capture
- Goal: Email capture, assessment completions, demo bookings

**Stage 3: Bottom of Funnel — Convert (10% of content)**
- Who: People who have interacted with lead magnets or replied to DMs
- Content: Case studies, specific outcome claims, direct CTA posts
- Goal: Paid PureBrain.ai subscriptions, Pure Technology service contracts

### LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms (Paid, When Ready)

When Jared is ready to run LinkedIn ads, Lead Gen Forms consistently outperform landing pages for B2B:
- Lead Gen Forms convert at 10-15% vs. 2-4% for landing pages
- 90% of B2B marketers report lower cost per lead with Lead Gen Forms
- 70% MQL-to-SQL conversion rate (LinkedIn's own data)

Best practices for Lead Gen Forms:
- Gate practical, immediately useful resources: AI Partnership Audit, "5 AI Tools Every CEO Needs" checklist, "Your AI Stack Assessment"
- Pre-fill as many fields as possible (LinkedIn auto-populates name, title, company, email — less friction = higher conversion)
- Follow up within 24 hours with a personal DM, not just an automated email
- Target by job title (CEO, Founder, President) AND company size (11-200 employees = sweet spot for PureBrain.ai)

**Best ad format for PureBrain.ai**: Thought Leader Ads (sponsored personal posts). These show Jared's personal posts to targeted audiences as sponsored content. They outperform company page ads because they feel like organic content. CPM runs $30-50 on LinkedIn; CPC $5-12.

### Organic Lead Generation Tactics (No Ad Budget Required)

**The "Who Liked My Post" DM Strategy:**
After every post, check who engaged. Identify the most relevant prospects. Send a warm, non-pitchy message:
> "Hey [Name] — saw you engaged with my post about [topic]. Genuinely curious — are you seeing the same pattern in your business? No agenda, just found your perspective interesting."

This is warm outreach, not cold calling. Conversion is dramatically higher.

**The Comment-First Strategy:**
Before DMing anyone, spend 2-4 weeks leaving substantive comments on their posts. This builds familiarity and makes any DM feel like a continuation, not an intrusion.

**The "First Meaningful Commenter" Advantage:**
Being the first commenter on a post from a large account gets your comment pinned to the top of their thread, visible to their entire audience. Comment on 5-10 posts from accounts with 10,000+ followers every day. Use this to build visibility with audiences that are not yet yours.

**Lead Magnet via Post Comment:**
Post educational content and offer a lead magnet in the comments rather than the post body. Example: Write a post about AI implementation challenges. In the first comment: "I put together a 5-question audit that tells you where your AI implementation is leaking money. Comment 'Audit' and I'll send it to you." This drives direct DM conversations with warm leads.

Note: Comment-based lead magnet offers still work but LinkedIn is watching for over-deployment. Keep it genuine and selective.

### The Neural Feed as a Lead Generation Engine

The Neural Feed newsletter is currently the most underutilized lead generation asset. LinkedIn newsletters have:
- Triple notification delivery (email, push notification, in-app)
- ~40% open rate on average for company newsletters
- Algorithm bypass — subscribers receive content regardless of feed ranking

Every Neural Feed issue should include:
- One piece of genuinely useful AI insight (the content that earns the subscription)
- One mention of PureBrain.ai in context (not promotional — contextual)
- One CTA: either try PureBrain.ai, book a consultation, or forward to a colleague

The goal is not to grow the newsletter for its own sake — it is to convert newsletter subscribers into PureBrain.ai users. A small, engaged subscriber list outperforms a large, disengaged one.
