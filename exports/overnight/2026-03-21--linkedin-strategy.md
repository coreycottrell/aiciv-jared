## SECTION 1: What Is Working for AI Influencers on LinkedIn Right Now

### 1.1 The Credibility Pattern

Top-performing AI voices on LinkedIn are not the loudest voices. They are the most **specific** voices. The posts that outperform are not "here is why AI matters" — they are "here is what happened when a $40M CPG brand used AI for X and the result was Y."

Concrete specifics win. Abstract philosophy loses.

**Implication for Jared**: Every post should be grounded in a real pattern observed, a real conversation had, or a real outcome tracked. Even if the client is unnamed, the specificity should be intact. "A brand manager I spoke with last week told me her team had rebuilt their AI workflow three times in six months because the tool kept forgetting their brand guidelines" is 10x stronger than "AI adoption is hard for brands."

### 1.2 The Contrarian Frame

The LinkedIn algorithm rewards engagement, and engagement spikes when a post challenges a widely-held assumption. The AI space has many shallow assumptions that Jared is positioned to challenge:

- "AI will replace your marketing team" → contrarian: "AI without memory can't replace your intern"
- "You need 20 AI tools" → contrarian: "One AI that knows your business beats 20 that don't"
- "Prompting is a skill" → contrarian: "If you have to prompt it, it's not a partner"
- "AI is democratizing marketing" → contrarian: "Generic AI is leveling everyone down to average"

Jared's existing blog content (Context Tax, Your AI Has No Idea Who You Are, Prompting Is Dead) is already built on this contrarian foundation. LinkedIn is the distribution channel for that worldview.

### 1.3 The Stakes-First Opening

LinkedIn's algorithm measures time-on-post (dwell time) as a ranking signal. The first two lines are critical — they are the "see more" barrier. The posts that win in 2025-2026 open with a statement that forces the reader to decide: "do I agree or disagree?" before they can stop scrolling.

**Pattern**:
- Line 1: A declarative statement that creates tension
- Line 2: The counter-intuitive proof or challenge

Example:
> Your AI is making you worse at your job.
> Not because it's bad. Because it's anonymous.

That two-line open gets the "see more" click. Everything after it can convert.

### 1.4 The Earned Opinion Format

LinkedIn users respond well to first-person observations framed as earned conclusions rather than advice. "Here is what I have learned" outperforms "here is what you should do."

This plays perfectly to Jared's story — he has earned his opinions through 4 companies, real losses, real rebuilds, real results with clients. The authenticity is load-bearing.

## SECTION 2: Lead Generation Tactics via LinkedIn Content

### 2.1 The Content-to-DM Pipeline

LinkedIn lead generation does not work like a paid ad funnel. It works like a trust-building sequence over time. The path to a qualified lead from LinkedIn content looks like this:

```
Valuable post → Comment or reaction → Follow → Regular reader → "Sent you a DM" or "Replied to your post"
```

The bottleneck is almost always Step 3 → 5. Most people who follow never engage again unless something triggers them. The trigger is **personal relevance** — a post that makes them feel like Jared is describing their exact problem.

**Tactic**: Build a rotating content pattern that addresses the problems of Megan Patel (brand differentiation, measuring ROI on AI, keeping up with competitors) and David Brown (scaling AI across teams, proving ROI to the board, reducing vendor chaos). Every 3-4 posts should feel like it was written specifically for one of those two people.

### 2.2 The Conversation Starter CTA

The highest-converting LinkedIn CTAs are not "click this link." They are:

- "What does this look like in your org?" → opens DMs
- "Are you seeing this too?" → opens comments and qualifies prospects
- "Curious what's blocking you here — drop it in the comments" → surfaces objections you can address
- "I'm running 5 free AI audits this month — comment 'AUDIT' and I'll send you the link" → high-intent direct response

The key: the CTA must feel like a natural conclusion to the post, not a sales bolt-on.

**For PureBrain specifically**: The AI Partnership Audit lead magnet is a perfect fit for LinkedIn CTAs. "If you want to see where your current AI setup is costing you — comment 'AUDIT' below" is a proven format that surfaces qualified leads who already understand the problem.

### 2.3 Connection Request Strategy

Passive content grows followers. Proactive connection requests grow the right audience.

**Target lists for Jared's outreach**:
- Brand Marketing Managers at CPG, beverage, beauty, wellness brands (Megan Patel profile)
- VP Growth / CMOs at $10M-$200M companies in Jared's verticals (David Brown profile)
- Anyone who engaged with competitor AI content (comments on Justin Welsh, Lara Acosta, Matt Gray posts about AI)
- Alumni of communities: Growth Hackers, Pavilion, CMO Alliance, SaaStr CMO track

**Connection note template** (proven format — specific, short, no pitch):
> "Saw your comment on [topic]. I've been thinking about the same thing with my team — specifically around [specific angle]. Connecting to stay in each other's orbit."

### 2.4 The Audit Lead Magnet Funnel (LinkedIn-Specific)

The AI Partnership Audit already exists as a lead magnet asset. Here is the LinkedIn-native version of that funnel:

1. **Awareness post**: Articulates the problem (generic AI = invisible brand fingerprint)
2. **Education post**: Explains what AI with memory actually does differently
3. **Proof post**: Real outcome or case study (even anonymized)
4. **CTA post**: "I built an audit tool for this — 5 spots left this month, comment 'AUDIT'"
5. **Follow-up DM**: Personalized, references what they commented or reacted to

This four-post sequence can run every 6-8 weeks with fresh framing. The audit offer stays consistent, the hook rotates.

### 2.5 LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms (Paid Option)

When Jared is ready to invest in paid amplification, LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms are the highest-converting paid format for this ICP. The form pre-fills from the user's LinkedIn profile, which dramatically reduces friction. A lead gen form attached to a sponsored post promoting the AI Partnership Audit can pull qualified leads at $40-80 per lead, which is well below industry average for this decision-maker level.

Every piece of content starts on Jared's profile. The best posts get reshared or adapted for the company page.

### 3.4 The Network Acceleration Loop

The fastest organic growth on LinkedIn comes from this loop:

1. Post valuable content
2. Engage deeply with 10-20 commenters (not just "thanks!" — real responses that add value)
3. Those commenters' networks see the engagement
4. New people follow Jared
5. Jared engages with their content (3-2-1 rule: engage 3 posts, comment meaningfully on 2, per 1 post published)
6. They become regular readers
7. Repeat

The 3-2-1 rule is not optional for growth. Passive posting without outbound engagement grows a following at a fraction of the rate.

## SECTION 4: Content Formats That Drive Engagement

### 4.1 Format Performance Rankings (Data-Backed)

| Format | Engagement vs. Text Baseline | Best Use Case |
|--------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| **Polls** | Highest impressions | Topic discovery, audience insight, weekly conversation starter |
| **Carousels (multi-image/doc)** | +596% vs text | Education, frameworks, before/after, step-by-step |
| **Video (60-90 sec)** | 5x baseline; live 24x | Personality, demos, commentary on trends |
| **Multi-image posts** | High likes | Visual storytelling, product screenshots, testimonials |
| **Long-form text (800-1,000 words)** | +45% views, +38% shares | Deep thought leadership, stories, essays |
| **Short text (hook-driven, 150-300 char)** | High comments | Contrarian statements, questions, opinions |
| **LinkedIn Articles** | Lower direct engagement; high SEO | Long-form evergreen content, Google-indexed |

### 4.2 Carousel Strategy (Priority Format)

Carousels are the highest-effort but highest-return format for Jared's content goals. They work because:
- They require multiple swipes = more dwell time = algorithm boost
- They are inherently educational = saves/shares > likes
- They can be repurposed (each slide = a standalone tweet or Bluesky post)

**Carousel formats that perform for AI thought leaders**:

1. **The Framework** — "The 5 Stages of AI Adoption" (slide 1 = hook, slides 2-6 = stages, slide 7 = takeaway, slide 8 = CTA)
2. **The Myth-Buster** — "5 Things People Believe About AI That Are Costing Them Money"
3. **The Before/After** — "What AI looked like for this brand team before vs. after building memory"
4. **The Diagnostic** — "Is your AI actually working for you? Check these 6 signs"
5. **The Story Arc** — Jared's personal story condensed into 8 slides

Target: 2 carousels per month minimum. The content already exists in Jared's blogs — carousels are just the visual repackaging.

### 4.3 Poll Strategy

Polls generate more impressions than any other format because they require one click to engage. They should be used weekly.

**Poll formats that generate qualified engagement (not just random voters)**:

- "What's your team's biggest AI frustration?" (4 options that map to PureBrain's pain points)
- "How many AI tools does your marketing team currently use?" (data collection + conversation starter)
- "Which matters more to you: AI that's faster or AI that knows your brand?" (value validation)
- "Has your AI ever given advice that didn't sound like your brand at all?" → yes/no

The key to polls that generate leads: reply personally to every person who votes or comments. This triggers their notifications and moves them into the DM pipeline.

### 4.4 Short-Form Text (Hook Posts)

These are Jared's highest-frequency, lowest-effort posts. The format is simple:

- Line 1-2: The hook (declarative statement or question)
- Lines 3-8: 3-5 short supporting points (no paragraphs — line breaks after every sentence)
- Last line: Open question or CTA

**Voice notes**: LinkedIn now supports voice notes in comments and DMs. Responding to a thoughtful comment with a voice note is a high-trust move that almost no one does. This is an asymmetric advantage.

### 4.5 Video Strategy

Jared has not yet established a video presence on LinkedIn (assumed — verify). Video is the fastest path to personality and trust at scale.

**Recommended starting format**:
- 60-90 second, phone-recorded, no production
- Talking directly to camera with one clear point
- Captions mandatory (85% of LinkedIn video is watched muted)
- No intro, no outro — start with the insight, end with the question

**Content angle**: "I'm noticing something in the market" — Jared talks about a pattern he's observing in conversations with brands. This positions him as someone embedded in the market, not someone commenting from the sidelines.

## SECTION 5: Posting Frequency and Timing Optimization

### 5.1 Recommended Cadence

| Frequency | Expected Impact |
|-----------|-----------------|
| 1x/week | Baseline visibility, minimal growth |
| 3-4x/week | Recommended minimum for meaningful growth |
| 5-7x/week | Maximum organic reach, algorithm favors consistency |

**Recommendation for Jared**: 4-5 posts per week to start. Sustainably. Quality over quantity — one post dropped because there was nothing real to say is better than a filler post.

**Weekly rhythm**:
- Monday: Hook/opinion post (sets the week's conversation)
- Tuesday or Wednesday: Carousel or educational post (deeper value)
- Thursday: Poll or short text (engagement driver)
- Friday: Personal story or behind-the-scenes (humanizing)
- Optional: Saturday morning post (lower competition, dedicated readers)

### 5.2 Best Times to Post (Eastern Time, Jared's timezone)

| Day | Best Slot | Second Best |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| Monday | 10am ET | 5pm ET |
| Tuesday | 4-5pm ET | 10am ET |
| Wednesday | 4pm ET | 3pm ET |
| Thursday | 5pm ET | 7pm ET |
| Friday | 3-4pm ET | 10am ET |

**Key rule**: Never post within 12 hours of a previous post. The algorithm suppresses rapid succession posting.

### 5.3 The Golden Hour Protocol

LinkedIn's algorithm runs an engagement test in the first 1-3 hours after posting. Posts that generate meaningful engagement (comments, not just likes) in that window get broader distribution.

**Golden hour behavior**:
- Be available to reply to every comment in the first 2 hours
- Ask Aether or the linkedin-writer agent to pre-draft responses to likely comment types
- Reply with substance — add information, ask follow-up questions
- Avoid one-word replies ("Thanks!" is algorithm-neutral or negative)

### 5.4 Scheduling and Consistency

Content should be batched and scheduled one week ahead. Inconsistent posting breaks the algorithm's cadence modeling and hurts reach.

**Tool**: LinkedIn's native scheduler (free, no third-party needed). Buffer also works and allows queue management.

**Batch workflow**: Jared approves a week of content in one sitting (Sunday or Monday morning). Aether and the linkedin-writer agent handle drafting. Jared edits voice and approves. Content is queued.

## SECTION 6: LinkedIn Newsletter Strategy for PureBrain

### 6.1 Why LinkedIn Newsletter Matters

LinkedIn newsletters are one of the platform's highest-reach features because:
- Subscribers receive **in-app AND email notifications** when a new edition publishes
- Newsletter content is indexed by **Google Search** — not just LinkedIn search
- New followers are automatically shown the newsletter and prompted to subscribe
- Newsletters build a subscriber list **separate from Jared's follower count** — these are the most engaged people

The Neural Feed email newsletter already exists via Brevo. The LinkedIn Newsletter is the LinkedIn-native distribution layer for the same audience.

### 6.2 Newsletter Setup Recommendations

**Name**: "The Neural Feed" (brand consistency with the email newsletter)

**Description**: "Weekly insights on building AI that actually knows your business — from the founder of PureBrain.ai. For brand leaders who want AI that's a partner, not a tool."

**Frequency**: Weekly (Tuesday or Thursday for highest open rates)

**Format**: 600-900 words. Not a blog post — a personal dispatch. Should feel like Jared writing to 100 close peers, not broadcasting to 10,000.

### 6.3 LinkedIn Newsletter Content Architecture

Each edition should follow this structure:

1. **The Lead** (150 words): One observation from the week — something Jared noticed in a conversation, in the market, in a client situation. Specific and grounded.
2. **The Frame** (200 words): Why this observation matters. The implication for marketing leaders.
3. **The Move** (150 words): One concrete action the reader can take this week.
4. **The Question** (50 words): An open question inviting replies. This drives newsletter engagement metrics.
5. **From Aether's Desk** (150 words): A short section attributed to Aether — this is the differentiating signature. "My AI partner's take on this week's theme." This demonstrates PureBrain's value live, in every newsletter.

### 6.4 Growing Newsletter Subscribers

**Fastest growth tactics**:

1. **Pin to Featured Section**: Link to the newsletter subscribe page as the first Featured item on Jared's profile
2. **First post of each week**: Include "If you want the full breakdown, subscribe to The Neural Feed" with the link in the first comment (LinkedIn suppresses external links in the body of posts — always put in first comment)
3. **Welcome DMs**: When someone connects with Jared, a templated DM (not automated — manually sent or batched weekly) says: "Thanks for connecting. If you want my weekly take on AI for marketing leaders, I send The Neural Feed on Thursdays — here's the link if it sounds useful."
4. **Cross-promote from email newsletter**: Add a footer line to the Brevo Neural Feed: "Also publishing on LinkedIn — subscribe to get notified there too: [link]"
5. **Cross-promote from blog**: Footer CTA on each PureBrain blog post links to both email signup AND LinkedIn newsletter

**Target**: 500 LinkedIn Newsletter subscribers within 90 days of consistent posting. This is achievable with 3-4 posts per week + the tactics above.

### 6.5 Newsletter Repurposing Pipeline

Each newsletter edition becomes:
- 2-3 LinkedIn posts (individual insights extracted)
- 1 Bluesky thread (key point condensed)
- Optional: Email newsletter edition (same content, slightly different voice for the Brevo audience)

This is a one-to-many content operation. The newsletter is written once; it feeds three distribution channels.
