# PODCAST PITCH FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE
## Jared Sanborn | PureBrain.ai
## Three-Email Sequence After Initial Pitch

*This sequence is designed to follow up without being annoying. The goal is to stay visible, add value on each touch, and make it easy to say yes. Three emails maximum. If no response after three contacts, move on - this show is not the right fit at this time.*

---

## THE PROTOCOL

**Initial pitch**: Day 1 (Tuesday-Thursday preferred)
**Follow-up 1**: Day 10-12 after initial pitch (brief, warm, no pressure)
**Follow-up 2**: Day 25-28 after initial pitch (short, value-add, clear close)
**Archive**: Day 90 (move to "revisit later" list, no more contact)

**Non-negotiables**:
- Never follow up on Monday or Friday
- Never send the same message twice
- Each follow-up should add something new (new angle, new asset, new context)
- Make every follow-up shorter than the one before

---

## FOLLOW-UP EMAIL 1
### Day 10-12 After Initial Pitch
*Tone: Warm, brief, genuinely curious about whether this is a fit*

**Subject line options**:
- "Re: Guest pitch — Jared Sanborn + AI partnership"
- "Quick follow-up on the AI co-founder pitch"
- "Following up — wanted to add one thing"

---

If timing is off or the topic doesn't fit your current direction, no worries at all. If it does, I'd love to find 15 minutes to talk through whether there's a version that works.

Either way, keep making great episodes.

[Jared Sanborn]
jared@puretechnology.nyc
purebrain.ai

---

*Notes for personalizing Follow-Up 1*:
- The [BRIEF, SPECIFIC UPDATE] should be a genuine, recent development - something Aether did, a business decision that involved AI, a content piece that landed unusually well, a new episode outline you've developed. It signals that you're actively building, not recycling a stale pitch.
- Keep the entire email under 150 words.
- No attachment in this follow-up. Keep it light.

## FOLLOW-UP EMAIL 3 (OPTIONAL - ONLY IF THEY'VE SHOWN INTEREST)
### Use only if: host opened emails multiple times, engaged with content, or gave a soft "not now" response
*Tone: Re-engagement, new angle, low pressure*

**When to use**:
- Host responded with "good pitch, wrong timing - check back later"
- Host engaged with Jared's content or social media after the pitch
- A new development has occurred that changes the pitch significantly (podcast appearance elsewhere, significant news, new product feature)

**Subject line options**:

- "Re-opening the conversation if the timing is better"

## RESPONSE HANDLING GUIDE

**If they say YES (interested / want to schedule)**:
1. Reply within 24 hours
2. Offer 3 specific time slots (their timezone, their format preference)
3. Attach the relevant episode outline
4. Ask: "Would it be helpful to have a 15-minute pre-call to align on angles, or do you prefer to go straight to recording?"
5. Send a calendar invite with Zoom link and agenda

**If they say NOT NOW / CHECK BACK**:
1. Reply warmly: "Appreciate you letting me know - I'll revisit in [X months]. Is there a better time or format to check back?"
2. Add to a 90-day re-engagement list with specific date
3. Continue engaging with their content genuinely in the meantime

**If they say NO**:
1. Reply graciously: "Completely understood - thanks for the honest reply. I'm a fan of what you're doing and will keep listening."
2. Do not pitch again for at least 12 months
3. Continue engaging with their content authentically - relationships have long-term value

**If no response after all three emails**:
1. Archive the pitch
2. Add to a 6-month re-engagement list
3. In 6 months, check if they've covered AI topics recently and send a fresh, completely new pitch if relevant

---

## WHAT NEVER TO DO

- Never mention "I sent you X emails" - it creates pressure and resentment
- Never apologize for following up - it signals weakness
- Never send a fourth contact if there's been no response to three
- Never pitch a different topic in a follow-up - stay consistent, just add context
- Never send follow-ups Friday afternoon or Monday morning
- Never follow up within 7 days of the initial pitch - it's too fast

---

## TRACKING YOUR SEQUENCE

For each pitch, log:
- Date of initial pitch
- Date follow-up 1 should go out (Day 10-12)
- Date follow-up 2 should go out (Day 25-28)
- Response status at each stage

Recommended tool: A simple spreadsheet with a "Next Action Date" column sorted by date. Check it every Monday.
