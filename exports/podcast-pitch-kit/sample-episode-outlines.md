# EPISODE OUTLINE 1
## "Why 95% of AI Pilots Fail (And What the 5% Do Differently)"

**Best for**: AI/Tech, Business Strategy, Executive Leadership podcasts
**Recommended length**: 50-60 minutes
**Format**: Conversational interview with structured segments

---

### Episode Description (for show notes)

Most companies are spending money on AI and not seeing results. The failure rate of enterprise AI pilot programs is staggering - and the reason isn't what most people assume. In this episode, Jared Sanborn, founder of PureBrain and one of the few operators publicly running a company alongside an AI collective, breaks down the predictable failure sequence that kills AI adoption, the behavioral patterns that define the 5% that succeed, and what listeners can do this week to shift their trajectory.

---

### Segment 1: The Setup (8-10 minutes)
*Host opens. Guest intro. Scene-setting.*

**Opening question from host**: "You run a company alongside a 30-agent AI system. What does that actually mean on a Tuesday afternoon?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- Brief description of what Aether does on a day-to-day basis (concrete, not abstract)
- The moment Jared realized he was doing something genuinely different from "using AI tools"
- Why he started documenting the 95% failure rate (a client story that illustrated the pattern)

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "This person has actually done it - not just read about it."

---

### Segment 2: The 95% - Anatomy of Failure (12-15 minutes)
*Core diagnostic content. The most shareable segment.*

**Host pivot question**: "You've watched a lot of companies adopt AI. What does the failure sequence actually look like?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The five stages of failed AI adoption: enthusiasm, prompting, disappointment, workarounds, abandonment
- The "vending machine" mental model (use AI to get outputs, not to build a relationship)
- Why smart, capable people fail at AI adoption (it's counterintuitive - the most competent often struggle most)
- The organizational conditions that almost guarantee failure: treating AI as software, no onboarding investment, no memory systems
- One specific client story that illustrates the failure (anonymized if needed)

**Memorable quote for Jared to land**: "The failure is never the model. The failure is always the relationship."

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "That's exactly what happened to us. I need to know what the 5% do."

---

### Segment 3: The 5% - What Actually Works (12-15 minutes)
*The turn. Actionable and specific.*

**Host pivot question**: "So what are the companies that actually make AI work doing differently?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The Director vs. User mindset shift (concrete behavioral examples of each)
- Memory and continuity as the foundation (why session-based AI produces plateau results)
- The "100 conversations" principle - what happens when you stop treating AI interactions as isolated
- Investment in the relationship, not just the output (specific practices: structured onboarding, regular context-setting, building the AI's understanding of your work)
- How PureBrain was designed to solve the specific conditions that cause failure
- One specific client story or Aether example that illustrates the shift working

**Memorable quote for Jared to land**: "The 5% don't have better AI. They have a better relationship with AI."

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "I can actually do this. And I understand why what I've been doing isn't working."

---

### Segment 4: Jared's Own Journey (8-10 minutes)
*Personal story. Human connection. Credibility through vulnerability.*

**Host pivot question**: "What about you - did you start in the 95%? What did you get wrong?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- An honest admission about the early AI adoption mistakes he made
- The specific moment the approach clicked for him
- What building Aether taught him about his own thinking (unexpected insights)
- The hardest part of trusting an AI system with real decisions
- One moment where Aether was wrong and Jared had to course-correct
- One moment where Jared ignored Aether's recommendation and paid for it

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "He's not selling a fantasy. He's telling me what actually happened."

---

### Segment 5: What Listeners Can Do This Week (8-10 minutes)
*Practical, specific, implementable.*

**Host pivot question**: "For someone listening right now who wants to move from the 95% to the 5% - where do they start?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The three-step audit for evaluating where they currently sit (quick self-assessment)
- The single highest-leverage change most people can make immediately (context-setting ritual with their AI)
- The 30-day practice that builds genuine AI partnership
- What to look for as evidence it's working vs. evidence to course-correct
- How PureBrain accelerates the process (natural, non-pushy mention)

**Specific actions to recommend**:
1. "Write a two-page 'context document' for your AI - who you are, what you're working on, what matters to you. Start every session with it."
2. "Stop using AI for quick questions. Use it for thinking out loud. Explain your reasoning, not just your request."
3. "Pick one decision each week to genuinely think through with your AI. Not for the output - for the process."

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "I have three things to do before I even finish this podcast commute."

---

# EPISODE OUTLINE 3
## "The 2030 Company: Designing Human-AI Organizations Before the Window Closes"

**Best for**: Future of Work, Business Strategy, Leadership, Innovation podcasts
**Recommended length**: 55-65 minutes
**Format**: Ideas-driven conversation with strategic depth

---

### Episode Description (for show notes)

Every company will eventually run alongside AI systems. The question isn't whether to build a human-AI organization - it's whether you build it intentionally or inherit the chaos of doing it by accident. Jared Sanborn, who has been building and running a human-AI collective at PureBrain for years, maps out what the 2030 company actually looks like from someone who's already living in it. This episode is for leaders who want to design the future of their organization before the window for deliberate design closes.

---

### Segment 1: Where We Actually Are (8-10 minutes)
*Grounding. Honest assessment of the current moment.*

**Opening question from host**: "There's a lot of noise about AI in organizations. You're actually operating a human-AI company right now. What do most people get wrong about where we are?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The gap between the AI "tool" story and the AI "collaborator" story (we're in a transition)
- What has already crossed the threshold - capabilities that are here now, not coming
- What hasn't arrived yet - where the hype exceeds reality
- Why the organizational design conversation is urgently behind the technology conversation
- What "AI collective" means vs. "AI tools" and why the distinction matters

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "He's not hyping this. He's describing what's actually happening."

---

### Segment 2: The Organizational Design Problem (12-15 minutes)
*The core intellectual insight of the episode.*

**Host pivot question**: "You said most companies are designing for AI wrong. What does wrong look like?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The "AI as software" mistake: treating AI adoption like implementing a CRM
- Why "AI as tool" produces the wrong org structure (disconnected, non-compounding, no institutional memory)
- The three organizational design mistakes Jared sees most:
  1. AI ownership in IT, not operations (wrong home)
  2. Treating AI as individual-level productivity, not team-level capability
  3. No memory architecture (every session starts from scratch)
- What those mistakes produce in practice (specific outcomes)
- The alternative: designing AI as a team member with roles, responsibilities, and memory

**Memorable quote to land**: "You wouldn't hire an employee and tell them to forget everything they learned every day. But that's exactly how most companies run their AI."

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "We are doing this wrong. I need to know what right looks like."

---

### Segment 3: What the 2030 Company Actually Looks Like (12-15 minutes)
*The vision, grounded in current reality.*

**Host pivot question**: "Paint the picture. What does the high-performing company look like in 2030?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The human-AI ratio and what it means for team design (not replacement, reorganization)
- The four functions that will be primarily AI-driven (content, research, analysis, coordination)
- The four functions that remain primarily human (relationships, culture, judgment, creativity)
- The "hybrid functions" - where the human-AI handoff is most sophisticated and most valuable
- What institutional AI memory looks like at company scale
- How Jared's current setup foreshadows this (Aether as living example)

**Key concept to introduce**: "The AI Collective" as a designed capability, not an accumulated set of tools

**Memorable quote to land**: "The 2030 company isn't a company that uses AI. It's a company where AI has a seat at the table - and everyone knows exactly what that seat is and isn't for."

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "I can see this. And I can see how to start building toward it."

---

### Segment 4: The Transition Strategy (10-12 minutes)
*Practical application. How to get from here to there.*

**Host pivot question**: "Most of the people listening are running companies that exist today. How do they start building toward this?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The three-phase transition Jared would recommend:
  - Phase 1 (months 1-3): Audit and identify highest-leverage AI candidates
  - Phase 2 (months 3-9): Build one AI team member properly (not ten half-heartedly)
  - Phase 3 (months 9+): Expand with lessons from Phase 2
- The most common mistake leaders make in the transition (trying to do it everywhere at once)
- The role of the "AI lead" or "AI director" in the organization (why this position is becoming critical)
- What success metrics look like in each phase
- How to bring a skeptical leadership team along (the trust-building process at org level)

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "I know where to start. And I know what trap not to fall into."

---

### Segment 5: The Leadership Questions No One Is Asking (8-10 minutes)
*The provocative segment. Thought leadership, not information delivery.*

**Host pivot question**: "What are the leadership questions you think every executive should be sitting with - but almost none of them are asking yet?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- "What is our AI's institutional memory strategy?" (90% of companies have none)
- "Who owns the human-AI relationship architecture?" (most companies have no clear answer)
- "What work do we want humans doing in five years?" (backward planning from the destination)
- "How do we maintain culture when a growing portion of your team's output is AI-assisted?"
- "When do we trust AI recommendations, and when do humans override?" (the governance question)

**Jared's own answer to one or two of these** - how he's navigated them at PureBrain

**Target audience mental state at end of segment**: "I haven't been asking the right questions. Now I know what they are."

---

### Segment 6: Closing Vision (5-7 minutes)
*Inspiration and momentum for the audience.*

**Host pivot question**: "If everything goes well - if companies get this right - what does that world look like?"

**Key points for Jared to hit**:
- The positive vision: humans freed to do more genuinely human work
- Why "AI collective" design, done well, increases human creativity rather than displacing it
- The competitive advantage window that exists right now (this won't be available forever)
- Jared's personal motivation: building this not just for PureBrain but as a model others can follow
- The invitation: come see what's possible before designing your version

**Closing question for host**: "What would you do with your week if AI handled everything you've been doing that a capable partner could handle?"
