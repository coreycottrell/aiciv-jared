## TOPIC 1
### "Why 95% of AI Pilots Fail - And the One Pattern in the 5% That Don't"
**Best for**: AI/Tech, Executive, Business Strategy podcasts
**Length**: 45-60 minutes

**The Hook**: Most AI pilot programs fail within six months. Not because the technology is bad. Because the organization treated AI like a vending machine instead of a collaborator.

**The Core Argument**: There is a predictable failure sequence in every failed AI adoption: enthusiasm, over-reliance on surface-level prompting, disappointment when outputs feel generic, abandonment. The 5% that work have one thing in common - they invest in the relationship with the AI system, not just the outputs. Jared has documented this pattern across dozens of cases, including his own wrong turns.

**Talking Points**:
- The "vending machine" vs "partner" mental model shift
- Why the first 90 days of AI adoption predict the next five years
- The three organizational conditions that guarantee AI adoption failure
- What "investing in the relationship" actually looks like in practice
- Why the best AI outcomes require learning to think about thinking

**Audience Takeaway**: Listeners leave with a diagnostic framework to evaluate whether their current AI approach is in the 95% or the 5% - and specific interventions to move from one to the other.

**Hook Stat**: "95% of enterprise AI pilots fail to scale. The failure isn't the model. It's how people relate to it."

## TOPIC 3
### "Director vs. User: Why Leaders Who Master AI 10x Their Output While Others Plateau"
**Best for**: Leadership, Executive, Performance, Career podcasts
**Length**: 20-60 minutes (scalable)

**The Hook**: Same tools. Same access. 10x different results. The gap has nothing to do with the prompts and everything to do with a leadership skill most people haven't been taught.

**The Core Argument**: There are two types of AI users: Users and Directors. Users treat AI as a search engine with better answers. Directors treat AI as a thinking partner they develop over time. The cognitive shift required to move from User to Director is real, learnable, and has dramatic compounding returns. Jared has built a framework for making that shift deliberately.

**Talking Points**:
- The Director vs. User distinction (specific behavioral differences)
- Why high performers often struggle with AI (letting go of being the expert)
- The "100 conversations" principle - what happens when you stop treating each interaction as isolated
- How to develop your AI's understanding of your work over time
- What Director-level AI output actually looks like vs. User-level output
- The competitive advantage window (this gap won't exist forever)

**Audience Takeaway**: Leaders leave with a clear picture of where they currently sit on the Director/User spectrum and a specific 30-day plan for shifting their relationship with their AI tools.

**Hook Stat**: "I've seen two people use identical AI tools on identical tasks. One got mediocre results. One produced work that impressed an entire executive team. The difference was a mindset shift that took about 20 minutes to explain."

**Hook Stat**: "The executives most capable of leveraging AI are often the ones most stuck. Not because they're resistant - because they're rigorous about where they place their trust."
