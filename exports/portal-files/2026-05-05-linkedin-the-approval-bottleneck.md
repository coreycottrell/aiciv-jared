## POST COPY (paste-ready for social.purebrain.ai kanban → LinkedIn)

A founder told me yesterday: "My AI is producing more work than my team can review."

That's the shape of the new bottleneck.

Six months ago, the limit was AI output. Now the limit is human approval. The AI drafted twelve outreach emails before lunch. The CEO has time to read three. The other nine sit in a queue that gets longer every day.

This is not an AI problem. This is an org-design problem with an AI cause.

The CEO math: hiring more humans to keep up is the worst possible response. You'd be paying salary to babysit a pipeline that already has zero marginal cost. The fix is upstream — fewer drafts, sharper drafts, only the ones that need a human at all.

The Employee question is harder: which decisions are worth your time? When the AI produces forty options, your job is no longer to evaluate forty. It is to design the filter that ranks them, then look only at the top three.

Most teams skip this and end up with a drawer full of unread AI work. Which is the same outcome as having no AI at all — except more expensive.

The real productivity gain isn't more output. It's a smaller queue with sharper teeth.

What's sitting in your AI's "awaiting review" folder right now?
