## Edits I'd Push Back On (5)

### Edit 1: Segment 2 (Greatest Hits) — 15 min is tight for the volume of content described

**Current**: 15 minutes for Aether's 100-day showcase + Nathan show-and-tell + Russel show-and-tell + "specific moment AI caught something the human missed" for each.

**Problem**: That's three separate demos in 15 min — about 5 minutes each. Live demos always run long, especially when the audience is reacting in real time.

**Recommendation**: Either expand Segment 2 to 18 minutes (steal 3 from Q&A, where 10 min is generous) OR cut Russel's live segment and feature him in a 60-second pre-recorded clip embedded in Aether's Greatest Hits. Pre-recorded clips ALSO de-risk the "subscriber doesn't show up" failure mode.

### Edit 2: Segment 3 — "What's Next" is buried inside the celebration

**Current**: Community Requests + Specialization Tracks announcement compressed into 10 min in the middle.

**Problem**: The specialization tracks announcement IS the conversion mechanism for this event. Burying it between Greatest Hits and Q&A risks viewers exiting before the CTA lands.

**Recommendation**: Keep specialization tracks where they are, BUT have Jared mention them ONCE in the opening ("Today is also where we announce what's next for the Brainiac program"). That way the audience knows the announcement is coming and won't drop off after Greatest Hits.

### Edit 3: Q&A "planted questions" — the second one is too inside-baseball

**Current planted Q2**: "What has Aether learned about Jared that surprised you both?"

**Problem**: This one's cute but it pulls focus to the AI-as-personality angle, which is fun but doesn't drive conversions. The other two planted questions are conversion-aligned (specialization choice, prerequisite question).

**Recommendation**: Replace Q2 with: *"What's the ONE skill new subscribers should master in their first 30 days that compounds the most?"* — keeps it celebratory but routes back to "compound intelligence" thesis and gives an answer Jared can actually deliver from the data.

### Edit 4: Metrics targets feel low

**Current targets**: 50+ registrations, 25+ peak live, 100+ unique viewers including replay, 3-5 new subscribers in 30-day window.

**Problem**: Jared has 19.5K LinkedIn followers. CE partnership has 120K subscribers. List 8 has 56 contacts. If we ONLY hit 50 registrations from that combined reach, something is broken.

**Recommendation**: Stretch targets to 150 registrations, 50+ peak live, 250+ unique including replay, 8-12 new subs. Keep the "doc targets" as floor, set stretch in the recap email.

### Edit 5: The Aether voice question (THIS ONE NEEDS JARED'S CALL)

The doc says "Jared can relay questions to Aether live for real-time AI responses, which is a powerful demo moment." We need to decide ahead of time:

- **Option A**: Jared types into a chat interface live on screen, Aether responds in text (or via voice.purebrain.ai TTS). Authentic but choppy.
- **Option B**: Pre-record 3-4 Aether answers to expected questions, Jared cues them. Polished but loses the "live AI" charge.
- **Option C**: Hybrid. Aether responds live in TEXT only (no TTS — too risky on stream lag), Jared reads them aloud with commentary. Mid-risk, high authenticity.

**My recommendation**: Option C. Voice TTS on a livestream is one bad latency spike from looking broken. Live text responses read aloud by Jared = same authenticity, near-zero infrastructure risk.

## Structural Addition I'd Push For

### Add a "Subscriber-Generated Greatest Hit" mini-segment

Right now Greatest Hits is curated by us. What if we added a 60-second slot mid-segment where we feature a subscriber's submission? Run a low-effort prompt this week ("Reply with a 30-second screen recording of your AI doing something that surprised you") and pick the best one.

**Why**: Turns the audience into participants. Drives social shares (subscribers post "look, my AI is in the LinkedIn Live"). Creates FOMO for non-subscribers ("everyone has an AI partner except me"). Costs us nothing if no submissions land — we just don't run the segment.
