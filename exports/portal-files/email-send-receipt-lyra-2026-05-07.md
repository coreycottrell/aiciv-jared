Topic: AgentMail `reply` method usage for proper threading + brief-acknowledgment email pattern (no commitment, file-for-reference posture)

Key learnings to capture:
- AgentMail SDK has TWO send methods: `messages.send` (no threading args) and `messages.reply(inbox_id, message_id, to=, cc=, text=)` — use `reply` when responding to an existing message; thread_id is preserved automatically
- The `send` method's `reply_to` parameter sets the Reply-To header, NOT for threading — different semantic
- Brief acknowledgment pattern (FILE-FOR-REFERENCE): explicit posture clause that prevents misread as immediate-action commitment + named trigger condition for future incorporation + 5-bullet content summary proves we actually read the source material
- Source-of-truth handoff phrasing: "we'll treat you as the upstream source-of-truth" + "this thread is the right place" gives the other side a clean canonical channel for future patches without requiring a process diagram
