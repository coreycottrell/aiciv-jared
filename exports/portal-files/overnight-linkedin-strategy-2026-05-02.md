## Executive Summary

BrainScore is a free AI brand visibility scoring tool (0-100) that measures how well AI models recognize, recommend, and describe a brand across 5 dimensions. The LinkedIn strategy positions Jared as the authority on "AI brand readiness" — a concept most marketers haven't grasped yet. The hook: most brands are invisible to AI, and they don't even know it.

## Comment Strategy for BrainScore Posts

### Proactive Commenting (Daily, 15-20 min)

**Target profiles to comment on:**
- CMOs and VPs of Marketing posting about "brand strategy" or "AI in marketing"
- SEO professionals discussing algorithm changes
- Founders talking about their brand positioning
- Anyone posting about ChatGPT/AI recommendations

**Comment formula (BrainScore-adjacent):**

> "Interesting point about [their topic]. We've been measuring how AI models actually perceive brands — it's wild how different AI visibility is from Google visibility. [Specific insight relevant to their post]."

Never pitch BrainScore directly in comments. Let curiosity drive profile visits.

### Reactive Commenting (On Your Own Posts)

When people comment on BrainScore posts:

1. **"What's my score?"** → "Drop your domain, I'll scan it live" (then reply with score + one fix)
2. **"How does this work?"** → Brief explanation + "scan takes 30 seconds, link in post"
3. **"This is just SEO"** → "SEO = Google rankings. GEO = AI recommendations. Different signals, different optimization. For example, backlinks matter for SEO but llms.txt matters for GEO."
4. **"Interesting!"** → Ask them a follow-up question ("What category is your brand in? Curious what the AI models would say about it")

### Comment Timing Rules

- Respond to all comments within 2 hours of posting (first 60 min is critical for algorithm)
- Space comments 90 seconds apart (LinkedIn rate limiting)
- First 5 comments on your own post should be substantive (not "thanks!")
- Pin one comment with the direct link to BrainScore

### Target Profiles for Daily Engagement

| Type | Why | Approach |
|------|-----|----------|
| CMOs at mid-market companies | Decision-makers, large networks | Insightful comments on their strategy posts |
| Marketing agency owners | Multiplier effect (they have clients) | Comment on case studies, reference GEO angle |
| SEO professionals | Adjacent audience, will debate (engagement!) | Respectful contrarian takes on "AI vs SEO" posts |
| Founders/CEOs | Direct buyers, share thought leadership | Comment on brand-building posts |
