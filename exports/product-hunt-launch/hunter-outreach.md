## Hunter vs. Maker — What You Need to Know

On Product Hunt, a "hunter" is the person who submits and promotes a product. The "maker" is the person who built it. They can be different people.

**Why a hunter matters**:
- Hunters with large followings send launch notifications to all their followers the moment the product goes live
- A hunter with 5,000 followers can drive 300-800 extra upvotes on launch day from their network alone
- Products with prominent hunters consistently rank higher due to algorithmic weighting of the hunter's credibility score
- The right hunter signals quality to the Product Hunt community before they've read a single word of your description

**Why you still need to be the maker**:
- Makers can post in the comments, engage directly, and get credit for building the product
- Maker status matters for the profile and community relationships you build long-term
- You want Jared's name and face on this launch permanently

**The ideal setup**: Jared is listed as Maker. A credible, active hunter submits the product on launch day.

**The backup plan**: If no strong hunter confirms by April 12, launch as maker-only. Many outstanding Product Hunt launches have done this — it is not a failure.

## Finding the Right Hunter

### Where to Research

1. **Product Hunt Leaderboard**: producthunt.com/leaderboard
   - Filter by "Top Hunters" and by recent activity (last 90 days)
   - Note: follower counts, types of products hunted, comment engagement

2. **Previous AI Tool Launches**: Search Product Hunt for top AI tools and see who hunted them
   - Look at who hunted: Perplexity, Notion AI, Otter.ai, Copy.ai, Jasper, Loom, Superhuman

## Outreach Strategy

### Timing

- **Start**: Week 6, March 30
- **First response window**: Give 5-7 days to respond
- **Second batch**: If no response after 7 days, reach out to next 3 hunters
- **Decision deadline**: April 12 — by this date either a hunter has confirmed or you proceed maker-only

### Channel Priority

1. **Product Hunt direct message** (highest response rate — they are active on the platform)
2. **Twitter/X DM** (second choice — most hunters are active here)
3. **LinkedIn message** (third choice — more formal, lower response rate for PH requests)
4. **Email** (last resort — hard to find and often filtered)

## What to Offer Hunters

Hunters do this voluntarily. Most do it because they genuinely find the product interesting. But here is what you can offer:

**Always include (non-negotiable)**:
- Full product access (paid tier if applicable, for free)
- All assets delivered in one package — make it zero effort on their end
- A clear brief on what the product does and who it's for

**Offer to also include (based on what they care about)**:
- Co-promotion: "I'll mention you by name in my launch email to [X] subscribers"
- Social amplification: "I'll share your post announcing the hunt with our [X] Bluesky/LinkedIn followers"
- Product feedback: "I'd love your honest take on the product — your perspective matters to me"

**Do NOT offer**:
- Payment (violates Product Hunt's policies and community norms)
- Anything that feels like purchasing the hunt (ruins authenticity)

## If No Hunter Found By April 12

Launch as maker-only. This is a legitimate path. Several top products of the day and week have launched without prominent hunters.

Compensate with:
- Heavier email list activation (see `upvote-community-strategy.md`)
- LinkedIn pre-launch buildup starting May 1
- Bluesky "building in public" thread starting April 27
- More aggressive personal outreach to existing customers for day-of upvotes

Document the outcome and for any future launches, begin hunter relationship building 90 days in advance rather than 30.
