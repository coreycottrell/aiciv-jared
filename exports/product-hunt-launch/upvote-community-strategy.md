## The Ethical Framework

The only upvotes that matter are genuine ones. Product Hunt's algorithm is specifically designed to detect inauthentic upvote patterns — coordinated voting from new accounts, vote-buying, and coordinated bulk upvoting all trigger suppression.

More importantly: fake upvotes do not generate real customers. The purpose of a strong PH launch is to surface PureBrain to the right people, create authentic social proof, and generate real signups. None of that happens if the upvote list is manufactured.

**What we are doing**: Systematically reaching genuine people who have already interacted with PureBrain and asking them, personally and honestly, to support the launch.

**What we are NOT doing**:
- Purchasing upvotes (Product Hunt bans violators permanently)
- Asking people to create PH accounts just to upvote (new accounts with no history have near-zero weight in the algorithm)
- Coordinating bulk upvoting at a specific time (flags as manipulation)
- Incentivizing upvotes with discounts or gifts (explicitly against PH rules)

## What "Genuinely Ethical" Looks Like in Practice

The test: Would Jared be comfortable if Product Hunt saw exactly how he asked for every single upvote?

If yes: proceed.
If no: don't do it.

Honest asks from genuine supporters are the foundation of a launch that creates real customers, real community, and real reputation — not just a high-upvote vanity metric that converts to nothing.

The businesses that build the most credibility on Product Hunt are the ones who engage authentically. The maker comment is personal. The founder is actually in the comments. The product demo is real. The upvotes come from people who have genuinely used the tool.

That is what we are building toward.
