## 1. Setting Up Google Drive

### What You Need

- A Google account (Gmail address). If you have one already, you are set. If not, create one at accounts.google.com.
- 15 GB of free storage comes with every Google account. Most PureBrain customers will not need more than this for documentation purposes.

### Accessing Google Drive

1. Open a web browser and go to **drive.google.com**
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. You will see the main Drive interface — a file browser similar to what you see on your computer's desktop

### Creating Your PureBrain Root Folder

Before anything else, create a dedicated root folder. This keeps all your PureBrain-related files organized and separate from personal Drive content.

**Steps:**

1. Click the **+ New** button (top left, blue button)
2. Select **New Folder**
3. Name it: `PureBrain — AI Partnership`
4. Click **Create**

This is your home base. Everything in this guide lives inside this folder.

### What Goes Where

**01 — AI Partnership Notes & Learnings**
This is your knowledge journal. Every time your AI session produces a meaningful insight, decision framework, or strategic revelation, it gets captured here. Think of it as your partnership's memory bank.

- *Monthly Insights*: A single document per month capturing the most valuable things you learned working with your AI partner
- *Breakthrough Moments*: Individual notes capturing specific "aha" moments — when the AI helped you see something in a new way
- *Strategy & Direction*: Documents capturing major strategic decisions that came out of AI conversations

**02 — Conversation Exports & Session Logs**
Raw session exports, conversation summaries, and important chat histories. Organized by year and month so you can always find what was said when.

- Export important conversations as PDFs or text files and drop them here
- Use monthly folders to keep volume manageable
- Move anything older than 12 months to Archive

**03 — Business Documents Shared with AI**
Documents you share with your AI partner as context. The AI works better when it knows your business — keep those documents here so they are easy to reshare or update.

- *Company Overview*: Mission, vision, products, team, positioning
- *Products & Services*: Detailed descriptions, pricing, features
- *Customer Data (Anonymized)*: Personas, research, feedback (remove any personally identifiable information)
- *Strategic Plans*: Goals, roadmaps, priorities

**04 — Generated Content**
Everything your AI partner helps you create. Organized by content type so nothing gets lost and everything is reusable.

- Blog posts: Full text plus any images or related assets
- Social media: Posts, threads, captions organized by platform
- Reports: Analysis documents, summaries, research outputs
- Email campaigns: Sequences, individual emails, templates

**05 — Training Materials**
The collection of information that helps your AI work better for your specific business. This is one of the most valuable folders over time.

- *What AI Knows About My Business*: A master document you keep updated with key facts, preferences, and context about your business — paste this into new sessions to get up to speed faster
- *Prompts That Work*: Copy and save prompts that produce excellent results so you can reuse them
- *Workflows & Processes*: Step-by-step processes you have developed for using AI effectively in your business

**06 — Backups & Archives**
Periodic snapshots of your entire system and completed project archives. More detail on this in Section 6.

**07 — Templates**
Blank templates for documents and prompts. When you find a format that works well, save it here.

### Creating the Folder Structure

To create all these folders quickly:

1. Double-click your `PureBrain — AI Partnership` root folder to open it
2. Click **+ New > New Folder** for each top-level folder
3. Double-click each folder to open it, then create the subfolders inside

This takes about 10-15 minutes to set up once. After that, the system runs itself.

## 3. Setting Up Drive for Desktop (Automatic Sync)

Google Drive for Desktop keeps a folder on your computer automatically synced with Google Drive in the cloud. When you save a file to your Drive folder on your computer, it uploads automatically. No manual uploads required.

### Installation — Mac

1. Go to **google.com/drive/download**
2. Click **Download Drive for Desktop**
3. Open the downloaded `.dmg` file
4. Drag the Google Drive icon to your Applications folder
5. Open Google Drive from Applications
6. Sign in with your Google account
7. Follow the setup prompts

A Google Drive folder will appear in your Mac's Finder sidebar. Any file you place in this folder automatically syncs to Google Drive online.

**Recommended**: During setup, select **Stream files** rather than **Mirror files** if you have limited disk space. Stream mode shows all files in Finder but only downloads them when you open them. Mirror mode keeps a full copy on your hard drive.

### Installation — Windows

1. Go to **google.com/drive/download**
2. Click **Download Drive for Desktop**
3. Open the downloaded `.exe` installer
4. Follow the installation wizard
5. Sign in with your Google account when prompted
6. Choose your sync preferences

A Google Drive folder will appear in Windows Explorer under **This PC**. Files sync automatically.

### Accessing Your PureBrain Folder from Your Computer

After setup, navigate to your Google Drive folder and you will see `PureBrain — AI Partnership` listed. You can drag and drop files directly into the appropriate subfolder from your computer, and they sync to the cloud within seconds.

## 4. Setting Up Drive on Mobile

Mobile access lets you view, share, and add to your knowledge base from anywhere — useful when you want to capture a thought immediately after a meeting or check something while traveling.

### iOS (iPhone / iPad)

1. Open the App Store
2. Search **Google Drive**
3. Install the official Google Drive app (publisher: Google LLC)
4. Open the app and sign in with your Google account
5. Tap the hamburger menu (three lines, top left) to navigate your folders

To upload a file from your phone:
- Tap the **+** button (bottom right)
- Select **Upload** to upload a file from your phone's storage, or **Use Camera** to photograph a document directly

### Android

1. Open the Play Store
2. Search **Google Drive**
3. Install the official Google Drive app
4. Open and sign in
5. Navigation works the same as iOS

**Tip**: On mobile, use the **Star** feature to mark your most-used folders as starred. Starred items appear on the Drive home screen for quick access. Star your `01 — AI Partnership Notes & Learnings` and `04 — Generated Content` folders for fastest access.

## 5. Documentation Best Practices

Having the folder structure is half the work. Knowing what to put in it — and how — is the other half.

### What to Document from Your AI Partnership

Not everything needs to be saved. Focus on:

**Always save:**
- Strategic decisions that came out of AI conversations
- Content the AI generated that you actually used or plan to use
- Prompts that produced exceptional results
- New frameworks or ways of thinking about your business
- Anything the AI said that changed how you approach a problem

**Save if significant:**
- Research the AI conducted for you
- Competitive analysis or market insights
- Customer persona developments or refinements

**Skip:**
- Exploratory conversations that did not produce actionable output
- Failed experiments (unless there is a specific lesson worth capturing)
- Duplicate content (keep the final version, not every draft)

### How to Organize Conversation Insights

When you want to capture insights from a session:

1. **Immediately after a valuable session**, open a new Google Doc in `01 — AI Partnership Notes & Learnings > Monthly Insights`
2. Use this format:

```
Date: [Date]
Topic: [What the session was about]
Key Insights:
  - [Insight 1]
  - [Insight 2]
Action Items:
  - [What you are going to do as a result]
Saved Prompts:
  - [Any prompts worth reusing]
Files Generated: [Link to any content saved in 04 — Generated Content]
```

3. Add to the same document throughout the month rather than creating a new one every time

### Building a Knowledge Base Over Time

The most powerful thing you can build is a master document about your business that you keep updated and share at the start of new AI sessions. This document is your business's context file.

**Start this document now in:** `05 — Training Materials > What AI Knows About My Business`

Include:
- Your business name, industry, and what you do
- Your target customer (who they are, what they care about, what problems they have)
- Your products or services with brief descriptions
- Your tone of voice and brand personality
- Your current priorities and goals
- What you do NOT want your AI partner to suggest (saves time)
- Your preferred content formats

Update this document every month. It becomes more valuable over time. At the start of any new AI session, share this document as context.

### Consistent Naming

Consistency in how you name files makes search fast and navigation intuitive. See Section 11 for the full naming convention reference.

## 6. Backup Strategy

### What to Back Up and How Often

| Content Type | Frequency | Method |
|---|---|---|
| AI Partnership Notes | Ongoing | Auto-sync via Drive for Desktop |
| Conversation Exports | After each significant session | Manual upload |
| Generated Content | Whenever approved/used | Auto-sync or manual upload |
| Business Documents | When updated | Auto-sync |
| Monthly Snapshot | Once per month | Manual folder copy |

### Automatic Sync vs. Manual Uploads

**Automatic (recommended for most content):**
Once Drive for Desktop is installed, any file you save to your Google Drive folder on your computer syncs automatically. This covers most of your day-to-day file saving with no extra work.

**Manual uploads (for conversation exports):**
When you want to export and save a specific AI conversation:
1. Use the export or copy function in your AI interface to copy the conversation
2. Paste into a Google Doc or save as a text file
3. Upload to the appropriate folder in `02 — Conversation Exports & Session Logs`

### Monthly Snapshot Process

Once per month, create a snapshot of your current state. This gives you a point-in-time backup you can return to if anything is accidentally deleted or reorganized.

**Steps:**

1. Open `06 — Backups & Archives > Monthly Snapshots`
2. Create a new folder named: `Snapshot — YYYY-MM` (example: `Snapshot — 2026-03`)
3. Right-click your `PureBrain — AI Partnership` root folder
4. Select **Make a copy**
5. Move the copy into the snapshot folder

This preserves exactly what your knowledge base looked like at month-end.

## 7. Version History & Recovery

Google Drive automatically saves version history for Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides. For uploaded files (PDFs, Word documents, text files), Drive saves previous versions when you re-upload a file with the same name.

### Viewing Version History

For a Google Doc:
1. Open the document
2. Click **File** in the top menu
3. Select **Version history > See version history**
4. A panel opens on the right showing all saved versions with dates and times
5. Click any version to preview it
6. Click **Restore this version** to revert to that state

### Re-uploading to Preserve Version History

When you update a file that already exists in Drive:
1. Right-click the existing file in Drive
2. Select **Manage versions**
3. Click **Upload new version**
4. Select your updated file

Drive keeps the old version and makes the new one current. You can always go back.

### Recovery Procedures

**If you accidentally delete a file:**
1. Look in the **Trash** (left sidebar in Google Drive)
2. Files stay in Trash for 30 days before permanent deletion
3. Right-click the file and select **Restore**

**If you delete a file and empty the Trash:**
Contact Google Support immediately. Recovery is not guaranteed after Trash is emptied, which is why monthly snapshots are valuable.

**If you need to recover a previous version of a document:**
Use the Version History process above.

## 8. Sharing & Permissions

Google Drive makes it easy to collaborate on documents. Understanding permissions prevents accidental over-sharing.

### Permission Levels

| Permission Level | What They Can Do |
|---|---|
| Viewer | Read only. Cannot edit or comment. |
| Commenter | Can leave comments. Cannot edit. |
| Editor | Can edit and reorganize files. |
| Owner | Full control. Can delete, share, transfer ownership. |

### Sharing a Single File

1. Right-click the file
2. Select **Share**
3. Enter the email address of the person you are sharing with
4. Select the permission level from the dropdown
5. Uncheck **Notify people** if you do not want an email sent
6. Click **Share**

### Sharing a Folder

Sharing a folder grants access to everything inside it. Be intentional about which folders you share.

The **03 — Business Documents Shared with AI** folder, for example, should stay private unless you have a specific reason to share it with a team member or advisor.

### Creating a Shareable Link

For one-time sharing (such as sending a report to a client):
1. Right-click the file
2. Select **Share**
3. Under **General access**, change **Restricted** to **Anyone with the link**
4. Set the permission to **Viewer**
5. Click **Copy link**
6. Share the link

**Always set links to Viewer unless the recipient specifically needs to edit.**

### Revoking Access

To remove someone's access:
1. Right-click the file or folder
2. Select **Share**
3. Find the person in the list
4. Click the permission dropdown next to their name
5. Select **Remove access**

## 9. Security & Privacy

### Recommended Security Settings

**Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your Google account:**

This is the single most important security step you can take. With 2FA enabled, someone who learns your password cannot access your account without also having your phone.

1. Go to **myaccount.google.com**
2. Select **Security** from the left menu
3. Under "How you sign in to Google", click **2-Step Verification**
4. Follow the setup steps — Google will walk you through the options (SMS, authenticator app, or hardware key)

**Recommended**: Use the Google Authenticator app rather than SMS. It is more secure.

### What NOT to Store in Google Drive

Google Drive is excellent for documents, content, and business records. It is not appropriate for:

- Passwords (use a dedicated password manager such as 1Password or Bitwarden)
- API keys or developer credentials
- Private keys or security certificates
- Customer credit card information or banking details
- Anything your industry regulators prohibit from cloud storage

If you are in a regulated industry (healthcare, finance, legal), review your compliance requirements before storing client-related documents in Google Drive.

### Understanding Google Drive Encryption

Google Drive encrypts files:
- In transit (between your device and Google's servers) using TLS encryption
- At rest (while stored on Google's servers) using AES-256 encryption

This means your files are protected from external attackers intercepting them in transit and from unauthorized access to Google's storage systems. Google itself, however, can access your files as outlined in their Terms of Service.

For most business documents, this level of protection is appropriate. For highly sensitive legal or financial documents, consult your attorney or compliance officer about whether additional protection (such as encrypting files before uploading) is warranted.

### Reviewing Shared Access Periodically

Every quarter, audit who has access to your folders:

1. Open your `PureBrain — AI Partnership` root folder
2. Click on it once to highlight it
3. Click the information icon (top right, looks like a lowercase i in a circle)
4. Select the **Sharing** tab
5. Review all users and their permissions
6. Remove anyone who no longer needs access

## 10. Storage Management

### Your Storage Quota

Every Google account starts with 15 GB free. This is shared across Gmail, Google Drive, and Google Photos.

For typical PureBrain customer use — documents, PDFs, exported conversations, and text-based generated content — 15 GB is ample. Issues arise if you store large video files or thousands of high-resolution images.

### Checking Your Storage Usage

1. Go to **drive.google.com**
2. Look at the bottom left of the screen — it shows your current usage (example: "3.2 GB of 15 GB used")
3. Click the storage indicator to see a breakdown by category

### Managing Storage

**If you are running low:**

1. Check Gmail — old emails with large attachments consume significant space. Delete old attachments.
2. Review Google Photos — videos are large. Download and delete if not needed in the cloud.
3. Empty the Trash — deleted Drive files still count against your storage until Trash is emptied.
4. Look for duplicate files in Drive and delete extras.

**Upgrading storage:**

If you consistently need more than 15 GB, Google One plans start at $3/month for 100 GB. For most PureBrain customers, 100 GB is more than sufficient for years of documentation.

### Archive vs. Delete

When a project is complete or content is outdated:

- Move it to `06 — Backups & Archives > Project Closures` rather than deleting it
- Keep a note in the folder explaining what the project was and when it closed
- Only delete content you are certain you will never need

Storage is inexpensive. Lost knowledge is expensive.

## 11. Naming Conventions Reference

Consistent naming makes files instantly identifiable without opening them. Use these conventions throughout your PureBrain folder structure.

### Standard Format

```
YYYY-MM-DD — [Category] — [Descriptive Title]
```

**Examples:**

- `2026-03-08 — Blog Post — How AI Changed My Sales Process`
- `2026-03-01 — Session Export — Q1 Strategy Planning`
- `2026-03-15 — Insight — New Customer Persona Framework`
- `2026-02-28 — Training — Business Context Master Document v3`

### Why Dates First

Starting with the date (in YYYY-MM-DD format) means files sort chronologically when listed alphabetically. The newest file always appears at the bottom. The oldest appears at the top.

### Version Numbers

When a document has multiple versions, add a version suffix:

- `2026-03-08 — Blog Post — How AI Changed My Sales Process v1`
- `2026-03-09 — Blog Post — How AI Changed My Sales Process v2 FINAL`

Mark the approved final version clearly so there is no ambiguity.

### Folder Naming

Use the numbered prefix system (01, 02, 03, etc.) for top-level folders so they stay in order. Subfolders do not need numbers — alphabetical or chronological organization works well.
