# PureBrain Google Drive Quick-Start

**Get your backup system running in 30 minutes.**

---

## Step 1 — Create Your Root Folder (2 min)

1. Go to **drive.google.com** and sign in
2. Click **+ New > New Folder**
3. Name it: `PureBrain — AI Partnership`
4. Click **Create**

---

## Step 2 — Build Your Folder Structure (10 min)

Open your new root folder, then create these 7 subfolders:

```
01 — AI Partnership Notes & Learnings
02 — Conversation Exports & Session Logs
03 — Business Documents Shared with AI
04 — Generated Content
05 — Training Materials
06 — Backups & Archives
07 — Templates
```

Inside **02**, create a subfolder for the current year (e.g., `2026`), then subfolders for each month.

---

## Step 3 — Install Drive for Desktop (10 min)

This keeps your Drive synced automatically — no manual uploads.

1. Go to **google.com/drive/download**
2. Download and install **Google Drive for Desktop**
3. Sign in with your Google account
4. Choose **Stream files** (saves disk space)

After setup, your `PureBrain — AI Partnership` folder appears directly in Finder (Mac) or Windows Explorer. Save files there and they sync automatically.

---

## Step 4 — Install the Mobile App (3 min)

Search **Google Drive** in the App Store or Play Store and install. Sign in with the same Google account.

**Tip:** Star your most-used folders for quick access from your phone.

---

## Step 5 — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (5 min)

Protect your account before storing anything important.

1. Go to **myaccount.google.com**
2. Click **Security**
3. Click **2-Step Verification**
4. Follow the steps (use an authenticator app, not SMS, if possible)

## What Goes Where — At a Glance

| Folder | What to Put Here |
|---|---|
| **01 — Notes & Learnings** | Insights, decisions, breakthroughs from AI sessions |
| **02 — Conversation Exports** | Exported or copied AI conversation logs |
| **03 — Business Documents** | Company overview, product info, strategy docs |
| **04 — Generated Content** | Blog posts, social copy, reports, emails |
| **05 — Training Materials** | Your business context doc, winning prompts, workflows |
| **06 — Backups & Archives** | Monthly snapshots, closed project archives |
| **07 — Templates** | Document and prompt templates |

---

## First Thing to Build: Your Business Context Document

**This is the highest-value thing you can create.**

In `05 — Training Materials`, create a Google Doc called `What AI Knows About My Business`.

Include:
- What your business does and who it serves
- Your products or services (brief descriptions)
- Your tone of voice and brand personality
- Your current priorities and goals
- What you do NOT want AI to suggest

Paste this at the start of new AI sessions. It gets your AI up to speed instantly. Update it monthly.

---

## File Naming Convention

Use this format for everything:

```
YYYY-MM-DD — [Category] — [Title]
```

Examples:
- `2026-03-08 — Blog Post — How AI Changed My Sales Process`
- `2026-03-01 — Session Export — Q1 Strategy Planning`
- `2026-03-15 — Insight — New Customer Persona Framework`

Files sorted alphabetically will automatically be in chronological order.

---

## Your Monthly Rhythm

**Ongoing:** Save files to your Drive folder on your computer — sync is automatic.

**After each valuable AI session:**
- Add key insights to `01 — Notes & Learnings > Monthly Insights`
- Save any content you generated to `04 — Generated Content`
- Copy winning prompts to `05 — Training Materials > Prompts That Work`

**Once per month:**
- Create a snapshot folder in `06 — Backups & Archives > Monthly Snapshots`
- Make a copy of your root folder and move it there
- Update your business context document with anything that has changed

---

## Security Reminders

- **Store in Drive:** Documents, content, insights, business records
- **Never store in Drive:** Passwords, API keys, credit card numbers

For passwords, use a dedicated password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass).

---

## Storage

Your Google account includes **15 GB free** — more than enough for years of text-based documentation. Check your usage at the bottom left of drive.google.com.
