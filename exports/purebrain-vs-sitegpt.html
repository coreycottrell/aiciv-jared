Context First

Different Products for Different Problems

Comparing SiteGPT to PureBrain is like comparing a receptionist to an executive team. Both are real jobs. Both provide value. They're just not the same thing.

SiteGPT does one specific thing: it reads your website content, then answers visitors' questions about it. That's a legitimate product that solves a real problem — repetitive support tickets, 24/7 availability, basic FAQ automation.

PureBrain is a different category of product entirely. It's 23 AI department heads operating across your entire business — marketing, legal, finance, recruiting, strategy, engineering, sales, and yes, customer support too. It's not a chatbot. It's an operating system for your company.

Why we're telling you this upfront: If you only need a website support chatbot, SiteGPT is a reasonable choice and significantly cheaper. We'd rather you know that now than buy PureBrain for a job it's overbuilt for. This page exists so you can make an informed decision.

Being Genuinely Fair

Where SiteGPT Wins

SiteGPT has real strengths. If these are what you need, acknowledge it:

Price for Support-Only Use

At $39/mo, SiteGPT is the right tool if your only goal is automating website support. PureBrain starts at $179/mo — that's a meaningful difference if you don't need the extra scope.

Setup Speed

Paste your URL, wait 10 minutes, embed the widget. That's genuinely fast. PureBrain has a real onboarding process because it's learning your entire business — not just your FAQ page.

No-Code Simplicity

Non-technical teams can get SiteGPT running without any developer involvement. If your team has zero technical capacity, that frictionless setup matters.

Multilingual Support

SiteGPT supports 95+ languages natively. If you're serving a diverse international audience with support queries, that breadth is useful out of the box.

Existing Support Stack Integration

SiteGPT connects to Crisp, Intercom, and Zendesk. If those tools are already your support backbone, the native handoff to human agents is convenient.

Proven at Reducing Tickets

Users report real results — one team went from 43 to 24 weekly support tickets. For the specific problem of repetitive inbound questions, SiteGPT delivers.
