## Executive Summary

PureBrain needs a second high-converting lead gen asset beyond the AI Tool Stack Calculator. This document recommends building an interactive "Use Case Finder" page that lets visitors self-identify by role or challenge, then delivers a personalized AI use case report in exchange for their email. The page targets marketing professionals and social media managers as primary verticals — the same audiences who are being actively misled by platform-native AI recommendations from Google and LinkedIn. PureBrain's positioning is the unbiased AI advisor that works for the marketer, not the ad platform.

The expected outcome: 15-30% email capture rate from visitors who complete the tool (vs 1-3% from static pages), a scalable content asset that compounds over time, and a clear differentiating message that platforms cannot replicate.

## Part 1: Competitive Intelligence — What Otter.ai Is Doing

### How Otter Positions Itself

Otter.ai built its lead gen around a single, specific use case: **meeting notes and action items**. They did not try to be all things to all people. Their homepage hero says "AI notetaker" — not "AI productivity platform" or "enterprise intelligence solution."

Key observations from their approach:

**What they do well:**
- Hyper-specific problem statement ("stop missing meeting action items")
- Quantified outcome ("save 4+ hours per week")
- Freemium as primary CTA (low friction, massive volume)
- Social proof from recognizable names (Tim Draper, Tony Robbins)
- Integration-first storytelling (Zoom, Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot, Greenhouse)
- Three clearly defined verticals: Business, Education, Media — each gets their own landing experience

**What they do NOT do:**
- They do not serve marketers specifically
- They do not offer unbiased strategy advice
- They do not compete on personalization or memory
- Their AI gives the same output to everyone

**The opening Jared identified:** Otter owns "meeting tasks." Nobody owns "marketing tasks" and "social media tasks" as a dedicated AI use case verticals with unbiased advice. That is PureBrain's territory.

### The Unbiased Advisor Angle (The Core Differentiator)

This is the positioning insight worth building the entire page around:

**Google Ads recommends you increase your Google budget.**
**LinkedIn Campaign Manager recommends you expand your LinkedIn targeting.**
**Facebook Ads Manager recommends you broaden your audience.**

These platforms are not giving you objective advice. They are giving you advice designed to maximize their ad revenue. Marketers know this at some level but have no alternative — the only "advisor" they have access to is the platform itself.

**PureBrain is the AI advisor that works for you, not for the platform.**

This framing:
- Creates immediate resonance with any marketer who has wasted budget following platform recommendations
- Is 100% true and defensible (PureBrain has no financial interest in any ad platform)
- Cannot be claimed by Google, LinkedIn, Meta, or any platform-native AI tool
- Positions PureBrain as the independent expert, not just another AI tool

## Part 4: Marketing and Social Media Deep Dives

### 10+ Marketing Use Cases in Detail

---

**USE CASE M-1: Email Subject Line A/B Testing at Scale**

**The Problem**: Writing 10 subject line variants takes 45-60 minutes. A/B testing with statistical significance takes 2-3 weeks and most marketers never test more than 2 variants.

**The Platform Bias**: Brevo, Mailchimp, and HubSpot all offer subject line "optimization" — but they optimize for their aggregate open rate data, not your specific subscriber list. A subject line that works for a mass consumer list underperforms for a niche B2B audience.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Generate 15-20 variants across emotional angles (curiosity, urgency, social proof, specificity, controversy), rank them by fit to your specific audience's prior behavior, and identify which angles you've over-used vs under-tested.

**The ROI Angle**: AI-personalized subject lines increase open rates by 41% (Salesforce). For a list of 10,000 with a current 20% open rate, this means 820 more emails read per send.

---

**USE CASE M-2: Campaign Budget Reallocation Across Channels**

**The Problem**: Most marketers allocate budget by habit, history, or internal politics — not by actual ROI performance. This is the single largest source of marketing waste.

**The Platform Bias**: Google tells you Google is working. LinkedIn tells you LinkedIn is working. Neither shows you the cross-channel picture. They literally cannot: each platform only sees its own data. Even when you give them the same goal (conversions), they optimize for conversions on their platform, not your total marketing ROI.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Map your conversion data across all channels against your CRM outcomes. Identify which channel-to-segment combinations produce your best customers (not just clicks), and recommend reallocation based on your business outcomes, not platform metrics.

**The ROI Angle**: Companies report 22% higher ROI with AI-optimized budget allocation. On a $50,000/month ad budget, that is $11,000 in additional return per month.

---

**USE CASE M-3: Email Deliverability Diagnosis**

**The Problem**: When emails land in spam, it's a black box. Open rates decline gradually. Most marketers don't know if the problem is subject lines, sender reputation, content, or list health — and their email platform doesn't tell them.

**The Platform Bias**: Email platforms flag deliverability as a problem but point you toward their paid deliverability tools. The incentive is to sell you warming services, deliverability monitoring, and premium support — not to give you a transparent diagnosis.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Analyze your email content, sending patterns, list hygiene metrics, and bounce/unsubscribe data to isolate the specific cause. Recommend the sequence of fixes that will recover deliverability fastest.

**The ROI Angle**: Deliverability at 85% vs 98% means 13% of your list never receives your emails. On a 20,000 person list, that is 2,600 people who paid to subscribe but never hear from you.

---

**USE CASE M-4: Content Repurposing Operation**

**The Problem**: A well-researched 2,000-word blog post takes 4-6 hours to write. Then it sits. Turning it into a LinkedIn carousel, a Twitter/X thread, an email newsletter, a YouTube script, and a podcast episode takes another 6-8 hours of skilled writing time.

**The Platform Bias**: No platform bias here — this is pure time cost. Most platforms want you creating native content on their platform anyway and don't support repurposing workflows.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Feed one source piece into PureBrain once. Get formatted output for 6-8 destinations in 20 minutes. Each version maintains the source argument but adapts to the platform's format, audience, and content norms.

**The ROI Angle**: 75% faster campaign production with AI content ops. For a marketing team spending 20 hours per week on content creation, this recovers 15 hours per week — $750-$1,500/week at typical content marketing rates.

---

**USE CASE M-5: Competitive Intelligence Monitoring**

**The Problem**: Tracking what competitors are doing — new messaging, product updates, pricing changes, campaign pivots — is impossible to do manually at any useful frequency. Most teams do quarterly reviews at best.

**The Platform Bias**: SEO tools like Semrush and Ahrefs track keyword rankings but not messaging or strategic pivots. They are not advisors; they are data repositories.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Monitor competitor blog, social, and ad content on a weekly cadence. Identify when messaging shifts, surface new claims or offers, flag pricing changes, and recommend proactive responses.

**The ROI Angle**: First-mover advantage on competitor moves. Specific ROI depends on competitive intensity, but the alternative cost is launching a campaign the same week a competitor pivots and not knowing until two weeks later.

---

**USE CASE M-6: Voice of Customer Extraction**

**The Problem**: Your customers tell you exactly why they buy, why they stay, and what they wish was different — in reviews, support tickets, survey responses, and cancellation feedback. This data exists but takes days to analyze and most teams never read it systematically.

**The Platform Bias**: Review platforms show you ratings. Survey tools show you raw responses. Neither synthesizes the language patterns that make your copy resonate.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Process 500+ customer responses in 30 minutes. Extract the exact phrases customers use to describe their problem, the language they use to describe the outcome they want, and the friction points they mention most frequently. These phrases become your homepage copy, your ad creative, and your email subject lines.

**The ROI Angle**: Copy written in the customer's language consistently outperforms copy written by marketers about their product. Specific lift depends on implementation, but this is the methodology behind most high-converting SaaS homepage rewrites.

---

**USE CASE M-7: Landing Page Copy Optimization**

**The Problem**: Landing page copy is written once and rarely tested. Most marketing teams A/B test ad creative but leave the landing page static — which means they're optimizing the first 3 seconds of attention but not the next 60.

**The Platform Bias**: Google and LinkedIn Ads platforms now offer "asset suggestions" for your landing page. These suggestions optimize for ad quality scores — metrics that make platforms happy, not metrics that make your customer act.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Generate multiple headline and subheadline variants for your specific offer and audience, ranked by psychological conversion principle (specificity, urgency, social proof, contrast). Identify the structural gap between your current page and the question your customer is asking when they arrive.

**The ROI Angle**: 15-35% CTR improvement on landing pages is achievable through systematic headline and structure optimization. On 10,000 monthly visitors at 3% current conversion, a 25% lift means 75 additional conversions per month.

---

**USE CASE M-8: Email Sequence Architecture**

**The Problem**: Welcome sequences and nurture sequences are written once, never revisited, and leak leads at predictable points that no one is tracking.

**The Platform Bias**: Email automation platforms (Brevo, HubSpot, Klaviyo) will show you open and click rates by email. They will not tell you which emails are causing unsubscribes, which are failing to advance the relationship, or which sequence architectures convert best for your product type.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Audit your existing sequence for logical gaps, pacing issues, and missing trust-building moments. Recommend a sequenced architecture based on your product's purchase consideration length. Write or rewrite underperforming emails.

**The ROI Angle**: Automated email sequences generate 320% more revenue than non-automated campaigns. A well-architected 7-email welcome sequence vs a poorly structured one can produce 3-5x difference in conversion rate.

---

**USE CASE M-9: Audience Segmentation Refinement**

**The Problem**: Most marketing lists are segmented by acquisition source (how they came in) rather than by behavior, intent, or fit. This means every segment gets the same message, and the message is written for no one in particular.

**The Platform Bias**: Email platforms offer demographic segmentation. Ad platforms offer behavioral targeting within their platform. Neither connects your marketing segments to your actual customer success patterns.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Remap your list segments by behavioral signals and customer outcome data. Identify which segments convert, which churn, and which produce your best customers. Recommend segment-specific messaging that matches each group's actual journey.

**The ROI Angle**: Segmented email campaigns produce 50-70% higher revenue than non-segmented campaigns (DMA). The segmentation itself is free; the revenue gain compounds every campaign.

**USE CASE M-10: Campaign Brief Generation and Creative Briefing**

**The Problem**: Writing a comprehensive creative brief for a campaign takes 2-4 hours. It requires documenting target audience, key message, proof points, tone, competitive context, success metrics, and channel-specific requirements. Most briefs are incomplete, which produces creative that misses the mark.

**The Platform Bias**: No direct bias here — platforms don't help with brief-writing. This is a pure efficiency gap.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Generate a complete campaign brief from a 15-minute input conversation. The AI asks the questions a senior strategist would ask, captures the answers, and structures them into a document any agency, freelancer, or in-house creative can execute from.

**The ROI Angle**: 75% reduction in brief-writing time. Better briefs produce better creative on first submission, reducing revision cycles. On a $10,000 agency engagement, one fewer revision cycle saves $1,000-$2,500 in agency time.

### 10+ Social Media Use Cases in Detail

**USE CASE S-1: LinkedIn Ad Campaign Optimization (The Conflict of Interest Case)**

**The Problem**: LinkedIn Campaign Manager's optimization recommendations are designed to maximize LinkedIn's ad revenue, not your return on ad spend. Their standard recommendation: expand targeting, increase budget, use their automated bidding. Average LinkedIn CPL ranges from $75-$200+ across B2B industries.

**The Platform Bias**: This is the clearest example in marketing of a conflict of interest. LinkedIn's business model requires you to spend more. Their AI is trained on data that reflects what spending more does for their metrics, not what it does for your conversions.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Analyze your LinkedIn campaign data against your actual CRM conversion outcomes. Identify which job titles, company sizes, and industries are converting to customers (not just leads). Recommend targeting restrictions that reduce volume but improve quality. Calculate your true cost-per-customer, not cost-per-click.

**The ROI Angle**: Marketers who constrain LinkedIn targeting to proven converting segments report 20-40% reduction in CPL. On a $5,000/month LinkedIn budget producing 50 leads at $100 CPL, a 30% efficiency gain produces 15 more leads per month at the same cost.

**USE CASE S-2: Google AdWords Spend Optimization (The Other Conflict of Interest)**

**The Problem**: Google's Smart Campaigns and Performance Max campaigns are designed for ease of use — which means Google's algorithm makes the decisions. The decisions Google makes maximize Google's revenue. Experts in 2025 consistently document that following Google's own optimization recommendations blindly can be counterproductive, as these suggestions prioritize Google's revenue rather than advertiser objectives.

**The Platform Bias**: Performance Max specifically hides keyword-level data, making it nearly impossible to audit where spend is going. This opacity benefits Google, not the advertiser.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Audit your Google Ads account against your actual conversion and customer data. Surface the specific campaigns, ad groups, and keywords where spend is producing no downstream revenue. Identify the 20% of spend producing 80% of results and recommend shifting budget accordingly. Flag negative keywords that are consuming budget.

**The ROI Angle**: Average small business wastes 25-40% of their Google Ads budget on unqualified clicks. On a $3,000/month budget, recovering 30% waste = $900/month returned. Every month.

**USE CASE S-3: Content Calendar Strategy (Not Just Scheduling)**

**The Problem**: Content calendars are built from what topics are "interesting this week" or from a vague sense of what the audience wants. The result is sporadic, reactive, and disconnected from any strategic goal.

**The Platform Bias**: LinkedIn and Instagram's algorithm rewards consistency and native content. They are not wrong about consistency — but their definition of "good content" is whatever keeps you posting natively on their platform. They do not help you connect posting activity to business outcomes.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Build a content calendar from three inputs: your business goals for the quarter, your highest-performing past content, and your audience's documented interests. Map each post to a specific content pillar, audience segment, and funnel stage. Flag gaps. Identify the 4 types of posts you need each week (educational, engagement-bait, social proof, direct offer).

**The ROI Angle**: Consistent posting increases organic reach 3-5x vs sporadic posting (platform data). For an account with 2,000 followers, moving from sporadic to consistent posting alone adds 500-1,500 organic impressions per week without spending a dollar.

**USE CASE S-4: Content Curation for Thought Leadership**

**The Problem**: Sharing relevant third-party content is one of the fastest ways to build audience trust and demonstrate expertise — but finding genuinely valuable content takes 2-3 hours per week and most curated content is generic industry news.

**The Platform Bias**: LinkedIn's "content suggestions" and Twitter/X's trending topics surface what is popular broadly, not what is specifically relevant to your niche audience.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Define your content sources (specific publications, authors, newsletters) and your audience profile. AI monitors those sources daily, scores each piece against your audience relevance criteria, and surfaces the top 3-5 pieces per week with pre-written commentary in your voice.

**The ROI Angle**: Time savings of 4-6 hours per week. Qualitative improvement in content quality. Thought leadership positioning compounds over time — difficult to quantify single-use ROI but foundational to audience growth.

**USE CASE S-5: Social Media ROI Attribution**

**The Problem**: Every social media platform shows you engagement metrics. None of them shows you revenue. Proving that social media spending produces business outcomes is the #1 frustration for marketing teams justifying their budgets.

**The Platform Bias**: Platforms have a financial interest in you believing social media drives revenue. They will show you the attribution model that makes their platform look best. "View-through attribution" is a particularly platform-favorable metric that most advertisers should ignore.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Build a first-party attribution model using UTM parameters, CRM tagging, and customer acquisition source data. Map social activity to pipeline stages and closed revenue. Identify which platform, content type, and audience segment produces the highest lifetime value customers — not just the cheapest leads.

**The ROI Angle**: First-party attribution model typically reveals that 1-2 channels produce 80% of actual revenue. Reallocating budget to those channels and reducing spend on vanity-metric channels produces immediate ROI improvement.

**USE CASE S-6: Organic Hashtag and Timing Strategy**

**The Problem**: Instagram and LinkedIn both offer "insights" about when your followers are online. These insights are aggregate and delayed. They do not tell you which hashtag combinations produce reach vs engagement, or how those patterns change seasonally.

**The Platform Bias**: Platform insights are designed to encourage you to boost posts (paid) rather than optimize organic strategy. Better organic performance means less paid promotion, which conflicts with platform revenue goals.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Analyze your actual post performance against timing, hashtag usage, content type, and format to build your personal optimization model. Identify the specific posting windows that produce the most organic reach for your account (not averages across all accounts).

**The ROI Angle**: 20-44% engagement lift from timing optimization. For an account producing $1,000 in monthly referral traffic, a 30% engagement improvement adds $300/month in organic traffic value.

**USE CASE S-7: Social Listening for Competitive Intelligence**

**The Problem**: Monitoring what your competitors post across LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X, YouTube, and TikTok simultaneously is a full-time job. Most companies check competitors occasionally rather than systematically.

**The Platform Bias**: Each platform shows you your own analytics. No platform shows you cross-platform competitive intelligence. Native tools like LinkedIn Analytics do not show competitor performance.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Monitor defined competitor accounts across platforms on a weekly cadence. Identify their highest-performing content types. Surface new product claims or positioning shifts. Flag when they launch a campaign, so you can decide whether to counter, ignore, or amplify.

**The ROI Angle**: Early detection of competitor campaigns enables proactive response. In content marketing, being second to market on a topic is significantly less valuable than being first. Competitive intelligence prevents reactive positioning.

---

**USE CASE S-8: Influencer Identification and Vetting**

**The Problem**: Finding the right influencer for your brand requires manually reviewing dozens of profiles, checking follower authenticity, evaluating audience demographics, and estimating engagement quality. This takes 10-20 hours per influencer search and most marketers do not have a systematic process.

**The Platform Bias**: Instagram's and TikTok's native creator marketplaces prioritize their own creators and surface influencers with high follower counts regardless of audience quality. The platform benefits from high-volume influencer transactions; you benefit from high-quality fit.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Define your audience criteria, content niche, and engagement quality threshold. AI scores potential influencers against all three, flags accounts with suspicious follower growth patterns, and surfaces micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) who often produce better CPE than macro influencers.

**The ROI Angle**: Average influencer campaign produces 5-11x ROI when audience-fit is strong. When audience-fit is weak, influencer campaigns can produce negative ROI. The identification step is where the value is created or destroyed.

---

**USE CASE S-9: Comment Response and Community Management at Scale**

**The Problem**: Responding to 50-200 social comments per day individually takes 2-4 hours. Quality deteriorates as volume increases. Most brands either ignore comments (damaging) or respond with generic replies (neutral at best).

**The Platform Bias**: Platforms reward accounts that engage with comments through algorithmic reach boosts. They do not help you do so at scale; they just benefit when you do.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Draft personalized, on-brand responses to incoming comments. Categorize by type (question, complaint, compliment, spam, competitor mention). Flag comments requiring human judgment. Queue approved responses for one-click sending.

**The ROI Angle**: 44% of customers expect social media response within 24 hours. Response rate is a measured factor in LinkedIn and Instagram algorithm scoring. Quality community management is among the highest-ROI social activities with the lowest tool support.

---

**USE CASE S-10: Cross-Platform Content Adaptation**

**The Problem**: The same information should be shared across LinkedIn (professional long-form), Twitter/X (short + hooks), Instagram (visual + caption), TikTok (short video script), and newsletter (narrative). Writing each from scratch is 3-5x the time. Copying and pasting the same content to every platform performs poorly everywhere.

**The Platform Bias**: Every platform prefers native content written specifically for their format. This is actually correct — it is not bias, it is audience behavior. The problem is the time cost of writing natively for 5 platforms.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Write one source document. AI extracts the core argument and supporting evidence, then reformats it for each platform's specific conventions: LinkedIn essay with bullets and a question at the end; Twitter thread with hook and numbered insights; Instagram caption with an emotional opening; TikTok script with a spoken hook; newsletter with a narrative arc.

**The ROI Angle**: 3-5x content output from same creative input. For a solo marketer spending 6 hours per week on content, this produces 18-30 hours of equivalent content output at 6 hours of effort.

---

**USE CASE S-11: Social Media Crisis Detection**

**The Problem**: Brand sentiment on social media can shift from neutral to negative in hours. By the time a social media manager sees the problem, it has been compounding for 4-8 hours. Response lag is the primary amplifier of social media crises.

**The Platform Bias**: Platforms notify you when you are mentioned. They do not tell you when sentiment is shifting, when a thread about your brand is gaining traction, or when a competitor is using your brand name in their content.

**The PureBrain Solution**: Continuous monitoring of brand mentions, sentiment scoring, and escalation triggers. Alert when negative sentiment clusters appear. Provide initial response recommendations within minutes of detection.

**The ROI Angle**: A brand crisis that is caught and responded to in the first 2 hours produces 60-80% less reputational damage than one that surfaces to leadership 12-24 hours later. The cost of not having this system is impossible to calculate in advance; the cost of having it is near-zero.

## Part 5: Competitive Landscape — Use Case Lead Gen in AI

### Who Is Doing This Well

**Jasper.ai**
Jasper focuses on marketing use cases with role-specific landing pages (CMO page, content team page, agency page). Their approach: show a specific use case per audience, demonstrate with examples, offer free trial. They do not gate a personalized report; they use the free trial itself as the lead capture. **Copy.ai**
Copy.ai uses an interactive workflow builder as their primary lead gen mechanism. You can literally try workflows before signing up. They then gate the full workflow library behind email capture. Writesonic shows use cases by content type (blog post, ad copy, social post) but not by industry or challenge. Their lead gen is straightforward freemium. No personalization.

## Part 6: Page Design Principles

### What Made the AI Tool Stack Calculator Work

The calculator succeeded because:
1. It gave a genuine, specific answer (not a generic recommendation)
2. The output was useful whether or not the visitor bought anything
3. It demonstrated expertise through the depth of the comparison logic
4. The email gate came after users had seen enough value to want more

The use case finder should follow the same principle: demonstrate value before asking for contact information.

### Conversion Psychology Framework

**Step 1 (Role selection)**: Identity commitment. "I am a Marketing Manager" creates psychological investment in the results.

**Step 2 (Challenge selection)**: Problem acknowledgment. Selecting a challenge creates a micro-commitment: the visitor is saying "yes, this is my problem." This increases perceived relevance of the result.

**Step 3 (Company size)**: Context provision. Makes the result feel tailored, not generic. Also qualifies the lead for Brevo segmentation.

**Step 4 (Preview)**: Value demonstration. Show 2-3 use cases before the email gate. This is the trust moment — visitors decide if the output is worth trading their email for. Make this genuinely useful.

**Email gate**: Reciprocity moment. They have seen 3 use cases; they want the other 5. Email feels like fair exchange for genuine value already demonstrated.

**Post-gate report**: Exceeded expectations. Deliver more than promised. The full report should be more comprehensive than the preview suggests.

**CTA**: Natural next step. After reading 8 specific use cases, "See how PureBrain handles these for you" is a logical invitation, not a sales pitch.

### Email Nurture Sequences by Role

After capturing email, trigger a role-specific 5-email sequence:

**Marketing Manager sequence:**
1. Email 1 (immediate): Your Use Case Report + 1 quick win to implement today
2. Email 3 (day 2): The Google Ads conflict of interest (educational, no pitch)
3. Email 5 (day 4): Case study or example of a marketing team using AI effectively
4. Email 7 (day 7): "What would your first week with PureBrain look like?" (personalization moment)
5. Email 10 (day 14): Offer + urgency (soft close)

**Social Media Manager sequence:**
1. Email 1 (immediate): Your Use Case Report + platform bias cheat sheet
2. Email 3 (day 2): LinkedIn vs your business interests (educational)
3. Email 5 (day 4): Content calendar template + AI workflow
4. Email 7 (day 7): "What does an AI-assisted social media operation look like?"
5. Email 10 (day 14): Offer + urgency (soft close)

Each sequence should be built in Brevo with role tagging to enable segmented follow-up.

## Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Use cases are too generic to feel personalized**
Mitigation: Use specific data (statistics, percentages, dollar amounts) in every use case. Generic use cases do not justify email capture; specific, benchmarked use cases do.

**Risk 2: The tool completion rate is too low**
Mitigation: Keep the flow to 4 steps maximum. Each step should take under 10 seconds. Preview value before the email gate. If completion rate is below 20%, shorten the flow.

**Risk 3: Email nurture sequences do not convert**
Mitigation: Educational sequences (giving useful content without pushing a sale) outperform promotional sequences in this audience. The first 3 emails should be pure value. The pitch comes in email 4-5.

**Risk 4: Competitive platforms copy the concept**
Mitigation: PureBrain's persistent memory creates a moat that no generic AI tool can replicate. A use case report from a tool that doesn't know you is interesting; a use case implemented by an AI that remembers your goals from month to month is transformational. The page is the top of a funnel that leads to a genuinely differentiated product.
