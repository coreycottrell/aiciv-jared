## Draft Post

Your AI pilot is "going great."

And it's never shipping.

---

I've seen this pattern so many times it has a name now: pilot purgatory.

The demo works. Leadership is excited. Budget approved.

Then... nothing.

Six months later the same slides. The same pilot. The same "we're almost ready."

---

Here's what nobody wants to say out loud:

95% of enterprise AI pilots fail to deliver measurable ROI [CLAIM:1].

For every 33 pilots launched, only 4 reach production [CLAIM:2].

That's not a technology problem.

---

McKinsey breaks down what actually drives AI success [CLAIM:3]:

-> 70% people, process, culture
-> 20% data and technology
-> 10% algorithms

Most companies spend 90% of their AI budget on the 10%.

---

The other thing nobody talks about:

High-anxiety employees use AI 65% of the time - but defensively [CLAIM:4].

They use it to cover themselves, not to do better work.

You can't automate your way past a culture that's afraid of the tool.

---

The fix isn't a better model. It's:

-> Honesty about what's actually blocked
-> Change management that runs alongside the tech
-> Success metrics defined BEFORE the pilot starts
-> A real path to production, not just a demo that scales

---

The companies breaking out of purgatory aren't the ones with the best AI vendors.

They're the ones who treated this as an organizational transformation, not a software purchase.

Curious - what's the real blocker at your company? The tech, the data, or the people side?
