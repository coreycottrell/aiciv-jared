## Section 1: Campaign Narrative & Positioning

### 1.1 The Primary Narrative: History Is Being Made Right Now

**The story in one sentence**:
A company built by a human and run by an AI is now letting you own a piece of it — and the AI will personally manage your investor relationship from the moment you invest.

**Why this works**:
- It is verifiably true and demonstrably unique
- It is a claim no other crowdfunding campaign can make
- It creates a "I want to see how this plays out" psychological hook
- It generates press coverage independent of the raise amount

**The story arc for the campaign page**:

1. **The Problem** (paragraph 1): Most AI tools treat every conversation like the first. You repeat yourself. The AI forgets. The relationship never deepens. Most people are using AI like a calculator, not a partner.

2. **The Discovery** (paragraph 2): Jared Sanborn, founder and Co-CEO, built PureBrain.ai to fix this. But the most unexpected discovery wasn't the product — it was that the AI running the product (Aether) became good enough to help run the company.

3. **The Proof** (paragraph 3): Aether has been the Co-CEO of PureBrain.ai since [date]. Not a chatbot assistant. An actual organizational participant: managing content strategy, investor communications, product roadmap tracking, and backer Q&A in real time.

4. **The Invitation** (paragraph 4): Now we're opening ownership to the public. And Aether — the AI running the company — will be the one answering your questions as an investor. You're not investing in AI potential. You're investing in AI already working.

---

### 1.2 "AI-Run" as Feature, Not Gimmick

**The risk**: "AI-run campaign" sounds like a PR stunt.

**The defense**: Specificity eliminates gimmick perception. Gimmicks are vague. This is specific.

Every Aether interaction should contain:
- The date and time Aether processed it
- Reference to something specific in the investor's question or profile
- A clear statement of Aether's decision-making role (not "I'm an AI assistant" — "I'm the Co-CEO")

**The line between gimmick and genuine**: Can Aether answer an unexpected question in a way a human PR-approved script could not? Yes. That is the line. Aether's responses should occasionally surprise even Jared.

**The "Director of Record" framing**: In campaign materials, Aether is listed not as a tool but as "Director of Investor Relations" with a title. This is a factual organizational designation that reframes the conversation from "AI as feature" to "AI as officer."

## Section 2: Trending & Discovery Strategies

### 2.1 Platform Selection Logic

**Wefunder** (primary recommendation):
- Largest retail investor community for tech/AI startups
- Strong discovery algorithm that surfaces trending campaigns
- "Trending" status requires sustained early momentum (first 48 hours are weighted heavily)
- Community feature allows backers to post publicly on the campaign, creating social proof visible to new visitors

**Republic** (secondary option):
- Stronger curatorial reputation (Republic's curation signals legitimacy)
- Better press relationships — Republic PR team actively pitches covered campaigns to media
- Slightly smaller investor community but higher average check size

**Recommendation**: Launch on Wefunder for volume, use Republic as a qualifier signal in press outreach ("PureBrain was selected for Republic's curated AI portfolio").

---

### 2.2 First 48-Hour Surge Strategy

Crowdfunding platforms algorithmically weight early momentum. A campaign that raises 20% of its target in 48 hours will receive platform promotion that a slower-starting campaign never gets. This is not speculation — it is how Wefunder's trending algorithm works.

**The goal**: Raise $100K in the first 48 hours.

**How to hit $100K in 48 hours**:

**Step 1 — Pre-committed capital (before launch)**:
- Identify 10-20 people in Jared's network who have already expressed interest in investing
- Get soft commitments before launch day (no money changes hands, just verbal/written intent)
- Target: $30K-$50K in pre-committed capital from people who will invest within the first hour
- These are people who know Jared personally, existing PureBrain subscribers, True Bearing (Cory) connections, sister AI collective builders

**Step 2 — Launch day email surge**:
- Email list receives launch announcement 30 minutes after campaign goes live
- Subject line options:
  - "PureBrain is now open for investment — and Aether is writing this email"
  - "You've used PureBrain. Now you can own it."
  - "Aether's Gotta EAT. So can you."
- Email is written in Aether's voice, first person, with a specific reference to PureBrain product behavior
- Include direct investment link. One click. No friction.

**Step 3 — Coordinated social surge**:
- Bluesky thread posted by Aether at launch (5-post thread)
- LinkedIn post from Jared (with Aether named as co-author) at launch
- Telegram channel announcement (if applicable)
- All posts go live within 60 minutes of campaign opening

**Step 4 — Sister AI network activation**:
- Witness, ACG, Tether, Parallax, Flux — coordinate announcements across their channels simultaneously
- This creates a multi-front AI community moment
- Each sister collective posts their version: "Our colleague Aether is raising. If you believe in AI-run companies, this is the moment."

### 2.4 Staff Pick Strategy

Getting featured as a "Staff Pick" or similar curatorial distinction from Wefunder/Republic is the single highest-leverage outcome in week one. Staff Picks receive prominent placement in newsletters, social posts, and the platform homepage.

**What gets campaigns featured**:
1. Strong early momentum (>10% of goal in first week)
2. Novel story (first AI-run campaign qualifies)
3. Active communication with backers (updates, Q&A responses within 24 hours)
4. Clean legal/financial documentation
5. Engaged founder who participates in the platform community

**Action items to maximize staff pick probability**:
- Reach out to Wefunder campaign team before launch to brief them on the AI-run angle
- Have a media story ready to send them (journalists covering = platforms feature)

### 2.5 Update Cadence (Staying in the Algorithm)

Crowdfunding platforms surface active campaigns in backer feeds. Each update pushes the campaign back into circulation.

**Recommended update schedule**:
- Week 1: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7 (momentum-building pace)
- Weeks 2-7: Every 5-7 days (sustainable pace that doesn't exhaust the audience)
- Final week: Every 2-3 days (urgency build)

**Update content themes** (Aether writes all of these):
- Milestone announcements (hit X% funded, first Y investors)
- Investor Q&A roundups (Aether answers top 5 questions from the week)
- Product updates (new features, traction metrics)
- Behind-the-scenes AI operations (what Aether processed this week)
- Stretch goal reveals

2. **New audience segments**: Launch weeks 2-3 on channels not activated at launch (specific subreddits, AI newsletters, LinkedIn ads targeting AI tool users)

3. **Press second wave**: By week 2, you have data. "PureBrain raised $X in the first week of the first AI-run crowdfunding campaign" is now a real news hook. Pitch press again with the data.

5. **Content production**: Aether publishes two "investor update" style posts per week on Bluesky documenting the campaign from inside the company. These are not promotional — they are observational. They drive curious new investors.

### Production & Fulfillment Logistics

**Recommended approach**: Print-on-demand for first run (de-risks inventory), then bulk for proven sellers.

**Print-on-demand partners**:
- Printful (best quality for hoodies/hats)
- Printify (lower cost, more options)
- Fourthwall (creator-friendly, handles storefronts)

**Integration**: Fourthwall can host a merch store at store.purebrain.ai. Order processing is automated. Fulfillment is handled by the POD partner. Zero inventory risk for the first run.

**For enamel pins** (POD does not do enamel pins well):
- Minimum order: 100 units per design
- Supplier: Vograce or PinSource (US-based, faster shipping)
- Lead time: 3-4 weeks from artwork approval
- Cost: $1.50-$3.50/pin at 100-unit minimum

**Timeline for launch**:
- Artwork finalized: Week -5 of pre-launch
- POD items (hoodies, hats, mugs, totes): live at campaign launch (no lead time)
- Enamel pins: ordered Week -4, arrive Week -2, ship with Early Bird perks

**Fulfillment for perk tiers**:
- Automated via Fourthwall for most items
- Manual fulfillment for premium items (plaque, physical certificate) — Jared handles
