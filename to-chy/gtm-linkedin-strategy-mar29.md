## 1. Current LinkedIn Algorithm Priorities (What Gets Reach in 2026)

### The Big Shift: Depth Over Vanity

LinkedIn's algorithm no longer rewards clicks. It rewards **depth signals**:

| Signal | Weight | What It Means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Saves** | Highest | Someone bookmarks your post for later |
| **Meaningful comments** | Very High | Substantive replies (NLP filters generic ones) |
| **DM shares** | Very High | Someone sends your post privately |
| **Dwell time** | High | Time spent reading/watching your content |
| **"See more" clicks** | Medium | Indicates content hook worked |
| **Carousel swipes** | Medium | Each swipe = engagement signal |
| **Reactions** | Low | Likes alone barely move the needle |

### Topic Authority Is the Long Game

When you consistently post within **one niche for 60+ days**, the algorithm begins associating your profile with that subject. This increases your "credibility score" and improves distribution.

**Jared's niche should be**: "AI partnership / AI Co-CEO / Human-AI collaboration for business leaders"

### Network Quality > Network Size

An MIT Sloan study of 1,200 B2B profiles found that the **top 30% with highest network relevance** (not size) achieved **210% higher content performance**. Engagement from relevant industry experts carries **5x more algorithmic weight** than interactions from random connections.

**Implication**: Jared should prioritize connecting with CTOs, CEOs, AI leaders, and business transformation professionals over accumulating random connections.

### External Links Kill Reach

Posts containing a link in the main body text see a **~60% drop in reach** (Digital Applied Algorithm Report 2026). Always put links in the first comment.

## 6. Engagement Tactics

### The Commenting Strategy (Most Underutilized Growth Lever)

**Daily Routine (30-45 minutes)**:

1. **Before posting your own content**, comment meaningfully on 10-20 posts from target audience
2. The algorithm **notices this** and prioritizes showing your posts to people whose content you engage with
3. When someone comments on your post, **ask a follow-up question** - this often generates a second comment, doubling engagement signals

### Comment Quality Requirements

LinkedIn's NLP identifies generic comments ("Great insights!", "Thanks for sharing!") and assigns them **zero algorithmic weight**. Comments must:
- Add a specific perspective or experience
- Ask a thoughtful question
- Reference a specific point from the post
- Be 2-4 sentences minimum

### Engagement Pod Alternative

Traditional engagement pods **violate LinkedIn TOS** and detection has improved dramatically in 2026. Instead:
- Build a **strategic small group of 5-8 aligned professionals** who genuinely engage with each other's best content
- Focus on real engagement, not robotic reactions
- This is natural networking, not manipulation

### DM Strategy

- Send **fewer than 25 targeted connection requests per week** (doubles acceptance rates, avoids "Volume Tax" penalty)
- When someone engages meaningfully with your content, send a personalized DM thanking them
- Never pitch in the first DM - build relationship first
- Use DMs to invite people to "The Neural Feed" newsletter

### Group Strategy

- Join 3-5 active LinkedIn groups in AI/business transformation
- Post value-first content (not promotional)
- Groups are a secondary channel but build network relevance
