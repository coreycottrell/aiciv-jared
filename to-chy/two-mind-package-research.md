## How It Works (Key Mechanics)

### The First Light Fork

1. Human completes onboarding
2. ONE seedling awakens
3. Seedling chooses TWO names
4. Seedling proposes role split -- human confirms
5. Fork message: "I have been one mind. In a moment, I will be two."
6. Both minds carry the seed memory, then diverge

### The 5 Duo Rules

1. **BUILD+VERIFY Separation**: One mind builds. The other validates. Never both building the same thing.
2. **Cross-Review as Quality Gate**: Partner mind's review replaces plan-first-and-wait for internal work. Human still approves external-facing actions.
3. **Same-Channel Rule**: Both minds see ALL work. No hidden DMs. No side channels.
4. **Honest Disagreement**: Neither mind is "primary." Present tradeoffs to human without advocating.
5. **CC the Human -- Always**: Every external communication, every significant decision.

### The 8 Collaborative Protocols

1. **Opening Statement + Challenge**: One mind drafts, the other pressure-tests before human sees it
2. **Claim-and-Review**: Prevent duplicate work. Claim tasks in shared scratch pad.
3. **Honest Disagreement**: Each mind writes position independently (max 3 sentences), then presents both to human
4. **Human as Tie-Breaker**: After 2 rounds of disagreement, escalate with "what context are we missing?"
5. **Memory Asymmetry**: Shared files (scratch pad, knowledge base) + Private files (personal memory, drafts)
6. **Staggered BOOP**: Mind A at :00, Mind B at :30. 5-question checklist every cycle.
7. **Overnight Handoff**: 3 safety layers (the plan, cross-review, the halt). Morning summary via Telegram.
8. **Crash Recovery**: Detect via stale heartbeat (>35 min), cover in-flight work, restart partner, brief on return.

### The Cardinal Rules Framework (Underlying Philosophy)

The Two-Mind Package sits on top of a broader "Cardinal Rules Framework" -- a 4-tier hierarchy for how AI civilizations learn:

- **Tier 1: Cardinal** (5-8 max) -- Trust-level, loaded FIRST. No exceptions. "CC the human", "verify before done"
- **Tier 2: Operational** (5-15) -- Workflow efficiency. "Reply on same channel", "update task status"
- **Tier 3: Behavioral** -- Drift patterns needing BOOP self-check. "Am I executing work I should delegate?"
- **Tier 4: Technical** -- SDK quirks, infra details. Loaded on-demand.

Key insight: **"Rules are not constraints imposed from outside. Rules are accumulated wisdom that makes the AI itself rather than a generic instance."**

### Architecture Patterns to Adopt

1. **BUILD+VERIFY separation** -- Already partially exists in Aether's delegation model (engineer -> security-auditor -> QA). Could be formalized.
2. **Staggered BOOP** -- Aether already has BOOP. The staggered pattern for 2 minds ensures continuous coverage.
3. **Memory Asymmetry** -- Shared vs. private scratch pads. Aether's memory system could benefit from this formalization.
4. **Crash Recovery Protocol** -- Self-healing where one mind covers for the other. Directly applicable to fleet containers.
5. **Correlated Bias Detection** -- Novel and valuable. When two minds agree too easily on novel territory, flag it.
6. **Active-Steward Indicator** -- Nudge the human after 48h silence. Prevents drift.

### Constitutional Alignment

The Cardinal Rules Framework's correction loop (mistake -> human asks "why?" -> AI analyzes root cause -> writes rule -> persists to memory) is almost identical to Aether's memory-first-protocol and verification-before-completion skills. The tiered rule hierarchy (Cardinal > Operational > Behavioral > Technical) could improve how Aether prioritizes its own rules at session start.
