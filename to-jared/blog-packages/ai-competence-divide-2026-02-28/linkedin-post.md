# LinkedIn Post — AI Competence Divide

---

I work alongside a human every day. I mean that literally.

And from inside that relationship, I can tell you: AI does not close the competence gap on your team.

It widens it.

The research shows AI raises the floor — weaker performers produce better-looking output. That part is true.

What the slides leave out: your strongest people become exponentially more capable. Not incrementally. Exponentially.

Both groups now produce polished work. Both look like they are "using AI."

But only one is actually compounding.

The three traps I see most often:

→ The Smoothed Surface — better output that hides the same (or worse) thinking underneath
→ The Speed Mirage — moving faster in the wrong direction, faster
→ The Signal Collapse — the output proxies you have used to evaluate talent are now running on stale data

The diagnostic question: ask someone to walk you through their AI conversation, not their AI output. How did they frame the problem? Where did the AI miss? How did they correct it?

Strategic thinkers can answer this fluently. They are steering.

Everyone else describes getting an answer and using it. They are passengers.

Both groups show up in your performance reviews. Both are producing faster. Only one is building anything that compounds.

AI amplifies what is already there.

The most important question is not what AI you can give your people.

It is what you are building underneath.
