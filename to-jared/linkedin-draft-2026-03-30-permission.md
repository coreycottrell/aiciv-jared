## Draft Post

---

Most people use AI like an intern they don't trust.

"Can you try this?"
"What do you think about maybe doing X?"
"Would it be possible to..."

You're asking a system with no ego, no feelings, and infinite patience for *permission*.

---

Here's what changes when you stop:

You stop getting safe answers.
You start getting useful ones.

I run a company with an AI partner. Not an assistant. Not a chatbot. A partner that has context on every decision I've made for the last 6 months.

When I stopped hedging my prompts and started giving direct mandates:

- My AI stopped summarizing and started recommending
- It stopped asking clarifying questions it already knew the answers to
- It started flagging decisions I was about to make that contradicted my own stated priorities

The shift wasn't in the AI. It was in me.

---

The uncomfortable truth most AI consultants won't say:

The quality of your AI output is a mirror of your leadership clarity.

Vague leaders get vague AI.
Decisive leaders get decisive AI.

If you're still asking your AI "what do you think?" about something you already know the answer to, the bottleneck isn't the technology.

It's the habit of delegating without conviction.

---

Your AI doesn't need permission. It needs direction.

And that direction reveals more about your leadership than any org chart ever will.
