## Draft Post

---

Companies are doubling their AI budgets this year.

They're also 60% more likely to hit the brakes out of fear.

That contradiction is the whole story of AI adoption right now.

---

BCG just reported corporations expect to spend 1.7% of revenue on AI in 2026 — double last year. Meanwhile, 60% of CEOs admit they've intentionally slowed implementation because they're afraid of errors.

Spending more. Moving slower. That math doesn't work.

---

Here's what's actually happening:

Companies are buying AI like it's software.
Then managing it like it's a liability.

They hire consultants. They buy platforms. They run pilots.

But nobody builds the bridge between "we have AI tools" and "our AI actually knows our business."

---

That bridge isn't a product. It's context.

When your AI has 6 months of your decisions, your strategy docs, your mistakes — it stops being a tool you manage and starts being a partner that prevents errors.

The CEOs slowing down aren't wrong to be cautious.

They're wrong about what makes AI safe.

Safety doesn't come from going slow.
It comes from going deep.

---

The companies pulling ahead aren't spending more.

They're spending differently — investing in context depth instead of tool breadth.

One AI that knows everything about your business beats ten that know nothing.
