## Draft Post

---

86% of companies are increasing their AI budgets this year.

Only 18% have actually adopted AI.

Read that again.

---

The Federal Reserve just published new data. After years of hype, pilot programs, and executive retreats about "AI transformation" -- fewer than 1 in 5 US firms have adopted AI.

Meanwhile, nearly three-quarters of CEOs now say they're their company's main AI decision maker. Double the share from last year.

So CEOs are taking the wheel on something most of their companies haven't started.

---

Here's the pattern I keep seeing:

Leadership declares AI a top priority.
Budget gets approved.
Tools get purchased.
Then... nothing changes.

Not because the tools are bad.
Because nobody taught the AI anything about the business.

---

You can't adopt AI the way you adopt software.

Software works out of the box. AI works out of context.

The 18% who've actually adopted? They didn't just buy tools. They invested in building AI that understands their decisions, their customers, their mistakes.

The other 82% bought subscriptions.

---

The Fed data tells us what most boardrooms won't admit:

Adoption isn't a purchase order.
It's a relationship.

And most companies haven't even had the first real conversation.
