## Draft Post

---

54% of C-suite executives say adopting AI is tearing their company apart.

Not "challenging." Not "complex."

Tearing apart.

---

Meanwhile, 97% of companies deployed AI agents in the past year. And 79% say they're struggling with adoption -- a double-digit jump from 2025.

So nearly everyone deployed it. Almost no one landed it.

---

Here's what "tearing apart" actually looks like:

Engineering wants to move fast.
Legal wants to slow down.
The CEO appointed a Chief AI Officer -- but nobody agrees who that person reports to.

38% of companies now have a CAIO. Half report to the CTO. Half report to the COO. Some report to no one in particular.

That's not a strategy. That's an org chart having an identity crisis.

---

The pattern is clear:

Companies treated AI as a technology decision.
It's actually a leadership decision.

Technology asks: "What tools should we buy?"
Leadership asks: "How does this change how we work?"

The 54% tearing apart answered the first question.
They never asked the second.

---

AI doesn't break companies.

Deploying AI without aligning on what it changes about decisions, accountability, and culture -- that breaks companies.

The fix isn't better tools.
It's deeper context and clearer ownership.
