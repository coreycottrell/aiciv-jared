## Section 1: LinkedIn Algorithm — What is Actually Working in February 2026

### The Shift That Changed Everything

LinkedIn deployed what researchers are calling "Brew 360" — an AI-powered recommendation engine that replaced the old broadcast model. The core change: LinkedIn no longer pushes your content broadly. It asks, "Does this person's history of content, comments, and connections signal genuine authority?" and distributes accordingly.

**Key stats from January/February 2026**:
- Views down 50% from 2025 peaks
- Only 5% of posts that underperform in the first 60 minutes ever recover
- Comments hold 15x more algorithmic weight than likes
- Posts with 3+ helpful links get 236% higher reach (the "no links" rule is dead)
- The top 1% of creators receive 149x more reach than average posts

### Format Performance Rankings (February 2026)

| Format | Avg. Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Document Posts (PDF Carousels) | 6.60% | #1 format. Drives dwell time, saves, bookmarks |
| Native Video (30-90 seconds) | 5.60% | Growing 36% YoY. Product demos, trust-building |
| Text Posts (strong hook + structure) | 2-4% | Still viable. No external links. 1,300-3,000 chars |
| Images | 1-2% | Personal photos beat stock. "Surprising" moments |
| Company Page Posts | 0.3-0.8% | Employees resharing beats company direct posts by 561% |

### What Is Working Right Now

**Hooks**: Proof-of-work hooks and authority hooks are outperforming contrarian hooks. The era of "Hot take: X is dead" is over. What works: "I helped [client] go from [X] to [Y] in [timeframe]. Here is exactly how." First 210 characters determine whether the "see more" button gets clicked.

**Post length**: 1,300-3,000 characters outperform shorter posts by 31-38%. But each paragraph should be short (2-3 lines) for mobile. Posts with more than 14 short paragraphs perform 74% worse — density of ideas matters, not density of lines.

**First 60 minutes**: LinkedIn tests every post with 2-5% of your network first. Engagement in that window determines wider distribution. Jared should post when his ICP is active, then engage with comments immediately.

**Saves and reposts**: These are the algorithm's strongest signal of long-term value. Design every piece of content so that the reader thinks, "I want to come back to this" or "My colleagues need this."

**Comment strategy**: The top 5% of creators leave 150+ comments per week. Average creators leave 25. The algorithm tracks your behavior as a commenter — accounts that add genuine value in comments get boosted distribution for their own posts. Quality > quantity. One detailed comment beats ten "Great post!" replies.

**Timing**: Best days are Saturday and Sunday (counter-intuitive). Best times are 5-7am GMT and 11am-1pm GMT. For Jared's US audience, this translates to late evening/early morning Eastern Time for off-peak posting.

### What Has Stopped Working

- Engagement pods (LinkedIn detects and suppresses)
- Generic contrarian hooks ("Most people are wrong about...")
- Posting twice within 24 hours (20% reach penalty)
- Hashtags (consistently rated "useless" by top creators with data)
- Corporate language and AI-sounding phrasing
- Ending every post with a generic question ("What do you think?")
- Video for reach and virality (video now works only for trust-building and demos)

## Section 7: Engagement Strategy

### The Daily Minimum (15 minutes)

Following Chris Donnelly's framework, the top 5% of LinkedIn creators comment 150+ times per week. That is 20+ per day. For a founder with limited time, a realistic minimum is:

**10 targeted comments per day** on posts from:
- Potential clients (companies/founders in Jared's ICP)
- Peers (other AI consultants — creates reciprocal visibility)
- Industry voices (builds association with credibility)

**What makes a comment perform well in 2026**:
- Adds a specific insight, not just agreement
- References personal experience ("In my work with clients, I've seen...")
- Is 2-4 sentences minimum (15+ words boost the commenter's algorithmic standing)
- Ends with a genuine question that invites dialogue

**What to avoid**:
- "Great post!" or "This is so valuable!"
- AI-sounding language
- Self-promotional links in comments (except when directly relevant and invited)

### Profile Visit Reciprocity

When someone views your profile, LinkedIn shows it in their notifications. Visiting their profile back creates a reciprocity loop. This is a low-effort, high-signal engagement tactic: spend 5 minutes daily visiting the profiles of people who viewed yours.

### DM Outreach — The Comment-First Model

For high-ticket leads (PureBrain's Partnered and Unified tiers at $499-999/month), the data is clear: **comment-first outreach converts significantly better than cold DMs** for high-value B2B sales.

The flow:
1. Identify ICP prospect (founder or exec of mid-size company actively posting about AI challenges)
2. Leave 2-3 substantive comments on their posts over 1-2 weeks
3. When they engage back or the relationship feels warm, send a DM referencing the conversation
4. DM should be under 300 characters, offer specific value, no pitch

**DM template (adapt to context)**:
> "Your post about [specific topic] stuck with me — we're seeing the exact same pattern with clients. Happy to share what we've found if it's useful. No agenda."

That is it. No pitch. No "Would you be open to a 15-minute call?" The goal is to continue the conversation, not to sell.
