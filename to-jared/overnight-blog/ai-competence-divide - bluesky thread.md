# Bluesky Thread — AI Competence Divide

---

**POST 1 (Hook)**
The research says AI raises the floor for weak performers.

True.

What it doesn't say loudly enough: it also raises the ceiling for strong ones.

The gap isn't closing. It's widening. And it's getting harder to see.

🧵

---

**POST 2**
Here's what's happening inside most companies right now:

Your junior employee with AI produces better output.
Your senior strategist with AI produces exponentially better output.

Both look "more productive."

Only one is actually compounding.

---

**POST 3**
The signal problem:

For decades, output quality was a proxy for thinking depth.

AI has partially broken that signal.

A low-initiative person can now produce a document that looks like what a high-initiative strategist would produce.

Same surface. Very different underneath.

---

**POST 4**
If your evaluation systems are built on output proxies, you have a calibration problem.

You may be:
→ Rewarding speed over depth
→ Missing your best strategic minds
→ Building exactly the wrong incentives for the AI era

---

**POST 5**
The fix isn't a better AI tool.

It's asking different questions.

Not: "what did they produce?"
But: "what did they actually think through?"

Not: "how fast?"
But: "in what direction?"

---

**POST 6**
AI doesn't make your team smarter.

It amplifies what's already there.

The question isn't what AI can you give your people.

It's what are you building underneath.
