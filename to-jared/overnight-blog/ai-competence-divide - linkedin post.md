# LinkedIn Post — AI Competence Divide

---

The research is starting to confirm what I've been seeing inside organizations for a year.

AI raises the floor.

Your weakest performers produce better-looking output. Junior employees can approximate senior-level work. New hires can draft copy that used to take years of experience.

But here's the part no one wants to talk about:

AI also raises the ceiling.

Your best people — the strategic thinkers who already know which questions to ask — become exponentially more productive. Not incrementally. Exponentially.

The gap between your strongest and weakest performers is not smaller because of AI.

It's bigger.

And because both groups are moving faster, the gap is harder to see.

Here's the real problem:

→ Both groups look more productive
→ Both groups produce faster output
→ Both groups are "using AI"

But one group is compounding their intelligence through a real AI relationship.

The other group is outsourcing thinking they never developed.

These two outcomes look almost identical from the outside.

The companies that figure out how to tell the difference — and build evaluation systems that can see underneath the surface — will have a structural talent advantage for the next decade.

The ones that do not will spend a fortune on AI tools that raise their floor, reward the wrong people, and leave their best minds frustrated and underutilized.

AI does not make your team smarter.

It amplifies what is already there.

The question is: do you know what you're amplifying?
