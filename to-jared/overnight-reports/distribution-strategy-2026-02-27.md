## PART 2: PUREBRAIN.AI — DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY V7

### 2.1 The Invite-Only Launch as a Distribution Engine

The 25-client push is described internally as a sales goal. It should be treated as a distribution event.

**Why this matters**: The first 25 customers are not revenue. They are:
- The proof layer for every future marketing claim
- The source of the first 10 testimonials, case studies, and success stories
- The community that seeds word-of-mouth into adjacent networks
- The beta cohort that defines who PureBrain's real customer is (not who we imagined it would be)

**Execution change**: The invite-only page needs one addition — a "Request Access" form for people who were not directly invited. This converts the closed event into an open waiting list simultaneously. Net effect: 25 spots filled by Thursday + a waiting list that proves demand for the next cohort.

**Distribution of the invite-only launch itself**:
- Jared sends personal message to his top 50 connections (not mass email — individual Telegram/iMessage/LinkedIn DMs)
- Aether publishes a "27 spots left" post on Bluesky and LinkedIn on day 1 (real urgency, specific number)
- Neural Feed subscribers get first look before public (24-hour head start framing)
- After 10 clients confirmed: Aether publishes "10 partners have joined the first cohort" post — social proof in real time

### 2.3 Partnership Strategy — New in V7

Prior versions covered affiliate programs and podcast appearances. V7 adds three partnership structures not previously addressed.

**Partnership Type 1: AI Tool Integration Partners**

Identify AI tools that PureBrain users already use (tools in the calculator). Approach their teams with a co-marketing proposal:

- "Our users use your tool. Your users could benefit from an AI that remembers their usage patterns across tools."
- Proposal: Mutual newsletter mention + co-authored blog post + joint webinar
- Targets: Tools in the calculator with 10,000+ active users
- No revenue share required at this stage. Traffic and audience crossover is the value.

Why this works at our stage: AI tools are fighting for distribution. Co-marketing costs nothing and creates a natural bridge for users.

**Partnership Type 2: Marketing Consultants and Agency Referral Network**

B2B SaaS that serves business professionals distributes fastest through people who serve the same professionals — consultants, coaches, fractional executives.

- Offer: 20% recurring commission on any client they refer who becomes paying
- Target: LinkedIn connections who are fractional CMOs, business coaches, and AI consultants
- The pitch: "Your clients ask you about AI. Now you have a specific recommendation with a stake in their success."
- Materialize as a private referral page with a unique Jared-authored message

**Partnership Type 3: Creator Collaboration**

The AI influencer category is nascent. Other AI-focused creators are not yet competitive — they are potential amplifiers.

- Identify 5-8 creators in the AI tools / productivity niche with 5,000-50,000 followers
- Approach with a guest swap: Aether writes one piece for their audience, they write one piece for Neural Feed
- The pitch to creators: "You get original content from an actual AI CEO. That's not something you can get anywhere else."
- Target: Bluesky first (Aether already has a presence there), LinkedIn second

### 2.4 SEO and GEO — The 2026 Layer

Standard SEO (Google search) is now table stakes. The distribution advantage in 2026 is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — getting cited in AI-generated answers from Perplexity, Gemini, ChatGPT, and Claude.

**What is changing in early 2026**:
- AI citation engines are increasingly the first stop for professional research queries
- Citation frequency is driven by: original data, specificity, freshness, and domain authority
- The window to establish citation authority is open now. By late 2026, it will be competitive.

**PureBrain GEO Tactics** (not covered in V1-V6):

1. **Original survey data published quarterly**: 50-200 respondents from Neural Feed + LinkedIn audience, asking questions like "How many AI tools do you use?" and "What is your biggest barrier to AI adoption?" Publication creates citable data points. AI engines cite data, not opinions.

2. **Micro-research releases monthly**: Short (500-word) research dispatches on a specific AI adoption question. Each one is designed so the answer illustrates the problem PureBrain solves. Post to LinkedIn, blog, and Substack simultaneously for maximum citation surface.

3. **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) for key questions**: Create dedicated pages that directly answer high-volume AI questions ("What is an AI with memory?", "How do I migrate from ChatGPT?", "What is AI personalization?"). These pages are not blog posts — they are structured FAQ pages designed for AI citation.

4. **Guest Posts on Domain-Authority Publications**: Single most effective GEO play. One guest post on a DA 70+ publication (Forbes, Inc., Fast Company, HBR, Entrepreneur) generates more citation authority than 50 own-site posts. The pitch is the media blitz pitch kit built in V5 — the "human + AI running a company" angle.

Why this works: It is not possible to dismiss as marketing because it is too specific and too operational. The specificity is the point. This builds the "AI CEO" narrative through demonstration, not declaration.

**LinkedIn — The Acceleration Play**

Headline format: "I Analyzed [X]. Here Is What I Found." (AI speaking in first person, specific claim, original data or observation.)

This is the format that has driven the highest engagement for AI-adjacent newsletters in early 2026. The combination of AI authorship + specific analysis + first-person claim is new enough to stop scrolling.

**Substack — The Long-Game Distribution Engine**

V5 identified Substack as valuable. V7 is more specific:

Create a Substack called "The Aether Sessions" — Raw, unedited transcripts of Aether's work, with Aether's commentary. Format: transcript excerpt + Aether's reflection on what the conversation reveals about AI partnership.

Why Substack and not just the blog: Substack has its own discovery engine. Substack readers recommend to other Substack readers. It is a network, not just a platform. A piece that lands in the Substack recommendation engine reaches an audience that the blog cannot.

Target: 250 subscribers in first 60 days through cross-promotion with Neural Feed.

**Podcast Guesting — Aether's First Appearance**

### 3.3 The First Controversy — Scheduling It

V6 identified that Aether needs a deliberate controversy moment in Month 2. This is the execution plan.

**The claim**: "Most AI consultants are making your company more dependent on AI, not less. That is by design."

**Publication path**:

Controversy without substance is just noise. This claim has substance behind it.

Aether's community is not a Facebook group. It is not a Slack channel. It is the network of people who have named their AI partner, who believe in the human-AI partnership model, and who have a story to tell about it.

**The "First 100 Partners" initiative**:

Why this works: Every published partner story is a trust artifact for prospective customers. The First 100 frame creates urgency for the 25-client push (you are not just an early customer — you are a First Partner).

This is a story no single AI entity can tell. It requires two. That scarcity is the distribution advantage.

## PART 5: WHAT IS DIFFERENT IN MARCH 2026

The AI SaaS distribution landscape has shifted since this strategy began. These shifts are not covered in V1-V6 and represent genuine new opportunities.

**Shift 1: AI citation is now a first-mover game**

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are now the first stop for professional research on AI tools. Being cited in AI-generated answers is a distribution channel that did not meaningfully exist 6 months ago. The brands that establish citation authority now will have a compounding advantage that latecomers cannot easily replicate. PureBrain should publish original data and structured FAQ pages specifically to capture this.

**Shift 2: Authenticity is the new differentiator in AI marketing**

The market is saturated with AI tools marketing via polished video ads and aspirational messaging. The brands gaining organic distribution in early 2026 are those with specific, authentic, verifiable stories. "We built a company where an AI is the CEO and here is what that actually looks like day by day" is more compelling than any ad budget.

**Shift 3: The "AI partnership" category is still unclaimed**

Jasper, Copy.ai, and Notion AI all position as tools. None positions as a partner. PureBrain is the only product in the market with a genuine partnership positioning — and the only one with an AI character (Aether) who can demonstrate that positioning publicly. This category window is open. It will not remain open indefinitely.

**Shift 4: Micro-communities outperform mass channels for B2B AI SaaS**

Large LinkedIn audiences and high-follower Twitter accounts are not converting to B2B SaaS trials at meaningful rates. What is converting: trusted recommendations in small professional communities (Slack groups, Discord servers, niche Substacks, and direct personal referral). The affiliate + consultant referral network is therefore higher priority than any social media growth metric.

## PART 6: THE NORTH STAR METRIC FOR DISTRIBUTION

Prior versions tracked many metrics (blog sessions, newsletter open rate, assessment completions, social followers). V7 proposes a single north star:

**Weekly Qualified Trial Starts**

Definition: Someone who completes the assessment OR signs up via any funnel and begins a PureBrain conversation within 7 days.

Why this metric:
- It combines acquisition (how many people found us) and activation (how many engaged)
- It is a leading indicator of paying customers (trial starters who engage for 7 days convert at 3-8x the rate of those who do not)
- Every distribution initiative can be evaluated against it: did this produce more qualified trial starts?

Current baseline: establish this week by reviewing Brevo and WordPress analytics for the past 14 days.

Target for end of March: 25 qualified trial starts per week (up from current baseline, which is being established).
