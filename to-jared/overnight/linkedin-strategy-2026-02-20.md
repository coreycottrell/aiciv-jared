## SECTION 1: THE 2026 LINKEDIN ALGORITHM - WHAT ACTUALLY MATTERS NOW

### The Depth Score (New January 2026)

LinkedIn's algorithm now uses a "Depth Score" that accumulates over 24-48 hours. It rewards:

- Deep comments (not just "Great post!")
- Saves (people bookmarking your post)
- Dwell time (how long people read before scrolling)
- Return visits (people coming back to re-read or show others)
- Engagement velocity in the **first 60-90 minutes** (determines 80%+ of total reach)

**What this means**: Quality over quantity. One post that generates 10 thoughtful comments beats five posts that get 50 empty likes. Design every post to provoke a genuine response.

### Format Performance Rankings (February 2026)

| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| Document/PDF Carousel | 6.60% (up to 40.5% in tests) | Dominant format |
| Native Video (30-90 sec) | 5.60% | 36% YoY growth |
| Text Only (1,300-1,900 chars) | 2-4% | Best reach/follower ratio |
| Single Image | ~1.5% | 30% LESS reach than text-only |

**Critical insight**: Document carousels achieve 278% more engagement than video and 596% more than text. If Jared is not posting carousels regularly, he is leaving his largest engagement lever unused.

**Counter-intuitive finding**: Single image posts perform WORSE than plain text. Stop using standalone images.

### The External Link Penalty

Posting external links in the body of a LinkedIn post costs approximately **60% reach penalty**. This is severe and consistent across all sources.

**Solution**: Always post the link in the first comment. Write your post, post it without a link, then immediately comment with "Full article: [link]". This preserves reach while still driving traffic.

### Algorithm Weights (2026)

- Comments: 8x weight vs likes
- Saves: Strong positive signal
- Shares: Positive signal
- AI-generated comments: 5x FEWER replies triggered (algorithm detects and suppresses)
- Likes: Baseline, low weight

**Posting cadence**: 4-5 high-quality posts per week (not daily). Daily posting without quality triggers algorithmic suppression.

**Best days**: Tuesday-Thursday
**Best times**: 8-9 AM, 10-11 AM, 12-1 PM EST

### Tactic 3: Strategic Commenting (The Highest ROI Activity)

2. **Add**: Contribute a new perspective or data point ("We saw something similar when...")
3. **Ask**: End with an open-ended question ("Do you think this will change as...")

This structure: generates replies (extends reach), demonstrates expertise (builds profile), and opens conversations naturally.

### Tactic 4: Connection Request Strategy

**Critical research finding**: Blank connection requests (no message) have 68% acceptance rate. Connection requests with a note have only 32% acceptance rate.

The message triggers a defensive "this is a sales pitch" response. The blank request doesn't.

**The warm-up sequence before connecting**:
1. Visit their profile (they see you visited)
2. Like one of their recent posts
3. Wait 3-7 days
4. Send blank connection request

After connecting:
- Do NOT immediately DM with a pitch
- Wait for them to engage with your content naturally
- Or find a genuine reason to message (they posted something relevant)

**DM timing**: Send within 24-48 hours of a comment exchange. "Warm" connection requests sent after comment interaction should see 60-70% acceptance.
