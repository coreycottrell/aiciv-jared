## SECTION 4: HOOK LIBRARY

### The Rules for Hooks in 2026
- First 210 characters determine if anyone reads the rest
- 60-70% of potential readers are lost at the "see more" decision point
- The algorithm's NLP model detects and penalizes engagement bait phrases ("Comment YES if...")
- The best hooks feel like something a real person would say, not a headline
- Avoid AI-sounding phrasing (flagged as lower quality content)

### Hook Formulas That Work (with AI/PureBrain examples)

**Formula 1: The Contrarian Claim**
Template: "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's what actually works."
Jared examples:
- "Most AI consultants will tell you to start with a pilot. I think that's backwards."
- "The AI tool comparison guides are lying to you. Here's what the comparison should actually be."
- "'Build an AI roadmap first' is the advice that keeps companies stuck. Here's why."

**Formula 2: The Specific Number**
Template: "I [specific action with number]. Here's what I learned."
Jared examples:
- "I've run 47 AI implementation reviews this year. The same mistake shows up in 34 of them."
- "We analyzed 200 hours of AI team interactions. The pattern that emerged surprised everyone."
- "3 years ago I had zero AI in my business. Today I run a company with 15 AI team members. Here's the honest summary."

**Formula 3: The Vulnerability Hook**
Template: "[Failure or struggle] happened. Here's what I did."
Jared examples:
- "Aether got the brief completely wrong this week. Here's what I learned from the breakdown."
- "I almost lost a client because I let AI handle something it shouldn't have. The lesson still stings."
- "For 6 months, I thought AI was making my team more productive. I was measuring the wrong thing."

**Formula 4: The Unexpected Observation**
Template: "The thing nobody talks about with [topic] is..."
Jared examples:
- "The thing nobody talks about with AI implementation is the identity crisis it creates for your existing team."
- "Everyone's talking about AI efficiency. Nobody's talking about what happens to company culture when half your decisions are AI-assisted."
- "The hardest part of running an AI team isn't the technology. It's the communication."

**Formula 5: The Challenge to Common Wisdom**
Template: "Stop [common behavior]. Here's why it's hurting you."
Jared examples:
- "Stop measuring AI ROI in hours saved. You're measuring the wrong thing."
- "Stop asking 'will AI take my job?' Start asking 'what decisions should I never delegate to AI?'"
- "Stop comparing AI tools. Compare your AI implementation strategy. The tool barely matters."

**Formula 6: The Before/After**
Template: "[State before] → [State after]. Here's what changed."
Jared examples:
- "12 months ago: Every blog post took 3 days. Now: 6 hours. Not because AI writes them. Because of how we use AI in the process."
- "Before: Our team had 23 tools. After AI implementation: 9 tools, doing more. Here's what we cut and why."

**Formula 7: The Question that Triggers Self-Reflection**
Template: "Quick question: [question that makes them think]"
Jared examples:
- "Quick question: If your best employee left tomorrow, how much tribal knowledge would walk out the door?"
- "Quick question: If you had to explain your AI strategy in one sentence, what would it be? (Most leaders can't.)"
- "If an AI could do 80% of your team's current work, what would you have them focus on instead?"

## SECTION 7: POSTING SCHEDULE OPTIMIZATION

### Optimal Posting Times (Feb 2026 Data)

- Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday: 8-11 AM (audience's local time)
- Peak within that window: Wednesday 9-10 AM
- B2B posts published Tue-Thu 8 AM-12 PM: 60% more clicks, 80% more comments vs weekends

**Avoid**:
- Friday afternoon (decision-maker disengagement begins)
- Weekends (60%+ reach reduction in B2B)
- Monday morning (email-clearing mode, not content-browsing mode)

**Jared's recommended weekly schedule**:

| Day | Format | Type | Topic Focus |
|-----|--------|------|-------------|
| Monday | Text post | AI Reality (uncopyable content) | Honest observation from previous week |
| Tuesday | Native video (optional) | Behind-the-scenes | How Jared works with his AI team |
| Wednesday | Document carousel | Framework/case study | AI implementation insight |
| Thursday | Text post | Leadership/thought leadership | AI strategy angle |
| Friday | Poll | Engagement/debate | Hot take that invites responses |

**Frequency rule**: 4-5 posts per week is optimal for founders. Quality degrades fast above that. Three excellent posts per week outperforms seven mediocre ones in the 360Brew algorithm.

**The Golden Hour protocol** (60 minutes after posting):
1. Post goes live
2. Immediately engage with any early comments (within 10-15 minutes)
3. Reply to EVERY comment in the first 60 minutes
4. Replying creates a conversation thread (counts as multiple engagements)
5. Responding within 60 minutes triggers 2.4x higher reach amplification
6. Only 5% of posts that underperform in the first hour recover

**Implementation note**: Schedule the post for when Jared or Aether can respond to comments immediately. A post going live at 9 AM while Jared is in meetings until 11 AM wastes the Golden Hour.
