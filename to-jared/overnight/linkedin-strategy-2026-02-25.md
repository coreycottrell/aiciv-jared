## SECTION 4: LEAD GENERATION PLAYBOOK

### The Three-Stage Lead Gen System

#### Stage 1: Content-to-Comment Capture

**What it is**: Post high-value content with a comment trigger embedded

**How it works**:
- Post a carousel or text post ending with "Comment 'ASSESSMENT' and I'll send you the link"
- Manually DM everyone who comments within 2 hours
- Comment-triggered DMs have 40-60% response rates vs 5-15% for cold outreach

**Template - Comment-Triggered DM**:
```
Hi [First Name],

You commented on my post about [topic] - here's the link I
promised: [assessment URL]

Quick context: the assessment takes 8 minutes and shows
specifically where AI can have the most impact for your
kind of business.

Let me know if the results raise any questions.

- Jared
```

**Why this works**: They declared interest publicly. The DM feels like follow-through, not cold outreach.

#### Stage 2: Pre-Warm Sequence

**What it is**: Systematic relationship building before any pitch

**The sequence (takes 2-3 weeks)**:
1. Follow target + ring notification bell (day 1)
2. Leave a substantive comment on their content (day 3-5)
3. Leave second substantive comment on a different post (day 7-10)
4. Send blank connection request (no message) - day 14-15
5. After acceptance: send context message, NOT a pitch (day 16-17)
6. After reply: if relevant, offer the assessment or a conversation (day 21+)

**Template - Post-Connection Message (NOT a pitch)**:
```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for connecting. I've been enjoying your posts about
[specific topic they post about].

I'm working on something in the same space - would love
your perspective on it if you're open to it.

- Jared
```

**Template - The Ask (only after they reply)**:
```
Actually, I've been building something you might find
interesting - an AI assessment that shows exactly where
AI can reduce the work that's hardest to scale.

Takes 8 minutes. Happy to share the link if useful?
```

**Key stat**: Blank connection requests get 68% acceptance vs 32% with a message. Pre-warm commenting then blanking is the highest-conversion sequence.

#### Stage 3: LinkedIn Events as Lead Funnels

**What it is**: Host a LinkedIn Live event that requires registration

**The mechanics**:
- Every registrant is a declared-intent lead
- You have permission to DM every registrant before AND after the event
- A 30-minute LinkedIn Live generates 50-200 live viewers at 2K-10K follower range
- Every viewer at 2K-10K follower range is a warm lead for follow-up

**Event idea for Jared**:

"AI Partnership Live: Watch Me Run My Business With an AI Team for 30 Minutes"

Format: Screen share + narration. Show Jared directing Aether in real time. Show the outputs. Demonstrate the workflow. This is the most powerful possible proof-of-concept.

**Post-event DM template**:
```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for joining the live session today.

The most common question I got after: "How do I start?"

The answer: the AI Partnership Assessment shows you exactly
where to begin for your specific situation.

Link: [assessment URL]

Let me know if you have questions about what you saw.

- Jared
```

## SECTION 7: LINKEDIN AUTOMATION - WHAT IS SAFE IN 2026

This is a critical update. Several prior strategy documents mentioned automation tools. LinkedIn enforcement has changed significantly.

### What LinkedIn Banned / Now Detects

- **Artisan AI** was briefly banned from LinkedIn in January 2026 (reinstated but still watched)
- LinkedIn enforces a **150 daily action cap** across ALL actions (connections, messages, profile views, likes)
- Third-party automation tools that exceed this are now triggering account restrictions
- Mass connection requests without pre-warm are being flagged

### What Is Still Safe

| Tool/Tactic | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Taplio | Safe | Native LinkedIn integration, within limits |
| Expandi | Caution | Works but keep daily limits conservative |
| PhantomBuster | Caution | Flagged accounts in 2026; keep under 50 actions/day |
| Sales Navigator sequences | Safe | LinkedIn native, fully compliant |
| LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms | Safe | Paid, fully compliant |
| Manual comment-to-DM | Safe | Highest conversion anyway |
| Thought Leader Ads | Safe | Paid, fully compliant |

### The Rule

If the action could not be done by a human at a normal pace, LinkedIn is now detecting it. The automation window has closed for volume plays. The new playbook is:

- Low volume, high personalization
- Comment-first, connect-second
- Manual DM after content engagement
- Paid amplification via TLA for scale

## SECTION 9: FRESH ALGORITHM INSIGHTS (NET NEW, FEB 2026)

### The Depth Score

LinkedIn now uses a "Depth Score" that is more important than raw engagement count. Depth Score is calculated from:

- **Dwell Time** (primary signal): How long does a user pause on your content?
- **See More clicks**: Did they click to expand the post?
- **Comment length**: Multi-sentence comments score 8-15x more than one-word comments
- **Comment replies**: When you reply to comments, the thread extends dwell time for everyone
- **Save behavior**: Saves signal highest intent and trigger wider distribution

**What this means practically**: A post with 10 thoughtful long comments beats a post with 200 likes in algorithmic distribution.

**Jared's tactical implication**: Reply to EVERY comment. Reply quickly. Extended comment threads = extended distribution. The first 60-90 minutes of commenting determines 70% of reach.

### The Reach Reset

A major algorithm change in early 2026 created what creators call a "reach reset":
- Organic reach dropped 50% across the board
- Follower growth crashed 59%
- BUT: posts with high Depth Score are getting MORE reach per post than before

The algorithm is concentrating reach on fewer, better posts. This is good news for Jared because he is not trying to be a content factory - he is trying to post genuinely interesting, authentic content. That kind of content gets Depth Score. Volume-posters are being destroyed by this change.

### The Profile Visit Signal (New in 2026)

Profile visits are now a distribution signal. When someone visits your profile after seeing your content, LinkedIn interprets this as a strong quality signal and increases distribution of that post AND future posts.

**Practical implication**: Your profile headline and About section are now part of your content distribution strategy. A compelling profile that keeps visitors for 2+ minutes signals quality to the algorithm.

### External Links (Corrected Insight)

**Note**: Prior research stated external links in posts get 60% reach penalty. A more recent source (LinkedIn Senior Director) DISPUTES this. The current evidence suggests:
- External links in post body may have slight reduction
- External links in first COMMENT are now also being penalized (was previously the safe workaround)
- BEST PRACTICE: Post content natively, then add CTA to "link in bio" or "DM me for the link"
