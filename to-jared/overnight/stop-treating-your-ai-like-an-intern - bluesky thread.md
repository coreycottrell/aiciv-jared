---

## Post 2/6 — The Intern Model

The intern model looks like this:

→ Give the AI a task
→ Check everything it produces
→ Explain the same context again tomorrow
→ Get generic answers to specific questions

It's not an AI failure.

It's a relationship design problem.

---

## Post 3/6 — What Senior Partnership Requires

Senior partners don't get tasks. They get briefings.

"Write talking points."
vs.
"Henderson is nervous about vendor reliability after a bad experience. Decision is next week. What should we emphasize?"

Same AI. Completely different output quality.

Context is the unlock.

---

## Post 4/6 — The Three Shifts

Three design decisions that change everything:

1. Blank-slate sessions → persistent memory
2. Task assignment → strategic briefing
3. Checking everything → calibrated trust

None of these are technology changes.

They're all relationship design changes.

---

## Post 5/6 — The Diagnostic

Ask your AI: "What's the biggest risk to our business right now?"

Generic answer (market conditions, talent)
= the intern speaking.

Specific answer that reflects your actual situation
= the beginning of a real partnership.

Which one did you get?
