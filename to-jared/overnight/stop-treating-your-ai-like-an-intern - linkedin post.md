# LinkedIn Post: Stop Treating Your AI Like an Intern

**Date**: 2026-02-27
**Character Count**: ~1,340 characters
**Format**: LinkedIn feed post (hook + body + CTA)
**Link placement**: First comment (not in body)

---

Most businesses have their AI doing intern work.

Low-context. High supervision. Reset every morning.

And then they wonder why it isn't delivering strategic value.

Here's what I've seen building AI partnerships for two years:

The model isn't the constraint.
The relationship is.

Interns are expensive to manage.
They don't know your clients.
They don't know your history.
They don't know what good looks like in your business.
You check everything they produce.
And you explain the same context every single time.

That's fine for an actual intern.
It's a cap on your ROI when it's how you're running your AI.

The businesses I've seen extract real strategic value have made three shifts:

→ From task assignment to strategic briefing
→ From blank-slate sessions to persistent context
→ From checking everything to calibrated trust

Those aren't technology changes. They're relationship design changes.

One test to know which model you're running:

Ask your AI what the single biggest risk to your business is right now.

If it gives you a generic answer about market conditions and talent — that's the intern speaking.

If it gives you a specific answer that reflects your actual situation — you've started building a partner.

Most businesses get the intern answer.

The fix isn't a better AI.
It's a better relationship with the one you have.
