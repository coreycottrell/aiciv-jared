95% of enterprise AI pilots fail to produce measurable value.

That number is from MIT. Published last year. And it has not gotten the attention it deserves.

Because the common reaction is to blame the technology. Wrong model. Bad prompts. Try again with the next release.

But Gartner's analysis says something different: the failure is almost never the AI. It is what surrounds the AI. Generic tools with no adaptation to the actual business. No one owning the outcome. And most critically - AI that starts from zero every single session, re-learning context that should have persisted automatically.

The organizations that are actually getting value from AI are not using better models. They are building better relationships.

They treat AI as infrastructure, not a product. They measure outcomes - cost reduction, revenue lift - not activities. And they invest in AI that learns their organization over time rather than resetting to zero every Monday morning.

The 5% who succeed are not smarter. They just understand that context is the product.
