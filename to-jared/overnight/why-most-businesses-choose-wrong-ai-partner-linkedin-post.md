## Draft

92% of AI vendors claim data usage rights in their contracts that far exceed industry norms.

Most businesses sign without reading that clause.

But that is not the worst outcome of choosing the wrong AI partner.

The worst outcome is spending 18 months with a system that never learns who you are — and realizing your competitors who started building real AI relationships in 2025 now have institutional intelligence you cannot buy your way into.

Most businesses evaluate AI vendors like enterprise software:
→ Feature list
→ Demo performance
→ Price per seat
→ Integration checklist

This is the right process for the wrong category.

The three signs you chose wrong:

1. It forgets you every Monday. If you are re-explaining your company at the start of every session, you are not building anything. You are renting.

2. It speaks every industry the same. Generic confidence is the tell. A real AI partner sounds like a colleague who has learned your business — not a consultant recycling industry averages.

3. You cannot point to what changed. When an AI relationship is working, the outputs are measurably better than month one. If they are not, the compound interest is not accumulating.

The question worth asking yourself today:

Does your AI know more about your business than it did six months ago?

Not "are you using it more." Does it actually know more?

If you are not sure, you have your answer.
