## Bluesky Thread

**Post 1 (Hook)**
Most businesses treat AI onboarding like software rollout.

Setup. Train the team. Measure adoption.

Then wonder why nothing compounds.

The first 90 days don't determine if your team uses the AI.

They determine if the AI knows your business.

[thread]

---

**Post 2 (The Backwards Playbook)**
The standard enterprise AI playbook:

Month 1: Technical setup
Month 2: Identify use cases
Month 3: Report productivity metrics

Notice what's missing?

Zero structure for what the AI learns about you.

Generic tools don't need that. Partnerships do.

---

**Post 3 (Phase 1)**
What month one should actually look like:

The Deep Brief.

Before high-value output: build context.

Voice. Strategy. Competitive lens. Decision patterns. What's failed before.

This doesn't happen in documentation.

It happens inside real working sessions — if you're paying attention.

---

**Post 4 (Phase 2 + 3)**
Month 2: The Calibration Loop.

Every correction = a deposit. Feed it back. Explain why. The corrections are the curriculum.

Month 3: The Expansion Window.

Outputs that reference January. Patterns across projects. Strategic synthesis.

This is when "tool" becomes "partner."

---

**Post 5 (The Diagnostic)**
The test that tells you where you actually stand:

By day 90, can you ask your AI a question it's never been asked — and get a specific, synthesized answer that draws on everything it knows about your business?

Yes = compounding.
No = you deployed a tool.

---

**Post 6 (Stakes)**
Tools have diminishing returns.

Partnerships appreciate.

The problem is almost never AI capability.

It's that the foundation phase gets compressed under pressure to show early results.

Then teams spend years wondering why outputs are close — but never quite right.
