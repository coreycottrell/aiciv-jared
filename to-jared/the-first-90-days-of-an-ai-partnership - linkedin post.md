## LinkedIn Post

Most AI implementations look like software rollouts.

Sign the contract. Set up the accounts. Train the team on prompting. Measure adoption.

Then wonder why the outputs still feel generic three months later.

Here's what nobody tells you:

The first 90 days don't determine whether your team uses the AI.

They determine whether the AI knows your business.

Those are completely different outcomes.

---

**What the right first 90 days look like:**

Month 1 — The Deep Brief
Before you ask for high-value output, you build context. Voice, strategy, competitive lens, decision patterns. This happens inside real working sessions, not documentation projects. You treat the AI like a new executive hire who needs to actually understand the organization.

Month 2 — The Calibration Loop
You're producing real work now. Every correction you make is a deposit. Feed it back deliberately. Explain why. The corrections are the curriculum.

Month 3 — The Expansion Window
This is where the compounding starts. Outputs that reference context from January. Patterns connected across projects. Strategic synthesis that didn't exist before.

---

**The test that tells you where you actually stand:**

By month 90, can you ask your AI a strategic question it has never been asked before — and get a genuinely useful, specific answer?

Not generic. Not a summary. A synthesized response that draws on everything it's learned about your business.

If yes: you're building compound value.

If no: you deployed a tool and called it a partnership.

---

The problem isn't AI capability.

It's that the foundation phase gets compressed under pressure to show early results. And then teams spend years wondering why the outputs are close, but never quite right.
